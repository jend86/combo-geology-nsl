**Kipshak Group of Coal Occurrences**

The first signs of coal-bearing capacity within the investigated territory were established by A.M. Pavlik (1932). In 1955, the Churubai-Nurinsk geological prospecting party of the "Kazuglegeologiya" trust (Lyagif-Zade, 1955) carried out coal exploration work on the northeastern limb of the Sary-Donkal anticline. Four boreholes were drilled here to depths of 400–500 m. No commercial coal seams were identified in the boreholes.

The coal occurrences are associated with the sediments of the Kipshak Formation, with its lower and upper Kipshak sub-formations, which we correlate with the Karaganda and Nadkaraganda formations of the Karaganda coalfield.

The formation of the Kipshak Formation sediments took place in a coastal-marine environment, as evidenced by the presence in its section of a large number of limestone interlayers with marine fauna. The coals associated with them belong to the paralic type.

The coal occurrences are confined to different structures. The northern group of occurrences is located on the northeastern limb of the Sary-Donkal anticline, and the southern one — on the northern limb of the Kipshak syncline. These groups form the Kipshak coal-bearing district, which includes coal occurrences Nos. 18, 19, 21, 22, 23–28.

The coal-bearing sequence of the Kipshak coal-bearing district contains 8–45 carbonaceous interlayers, the main part of which is represented by carbonaceous argillites and seams. All of the most significant interlayers of carbonaceous rocks are located within the northeastern limb of the Sary-Donkal anticline. The most extensive and thick coal seam here is the "slozhnyi" ("complex") seam, named so because of its complex structure. It represents an alternation of coal interlayers, carbonaceous argillites, and "waste" rocks. Along strike, the "complex" seam is traced for 9.5 km. Its thickness is 2.6 m, of which clean coal is 0.97 m, carbonaceous argillites with coal laminae 0.25 m, weakly carbonaceous argillites and gray siltstones 1.38 m. On the western and eastern flanks of this seam, a reduction in coal thickness to 1.16 m and 2.4 m respectively is noted. The coal of the "complex" seam is bituminous in composition, weakly metamorphosed, and corresponds to grade "K" (Besedina, 1950).

Comparing the coal-bearing character of the Kipshak Formation of the northeastern limb of the Sary-Donkal anticline and the northern limb of the Kipshak syncline, it should be noted that the deposits of the upper Kipshak submember, distributed in the area of the Sary-Donkal anticline, are more coal-saturated. In terms of coal quality, the coals of the Sary-Donkal anticline are close to hard (bituminous) coals, whereas in the Kipshak syncline (boreholes 146, 147, 148), carbonaceous argillites are predominant. Both in the Sary-Donkal anticline and in the Kipshak syncline, coal-bearing character has been studied at the stratigraphic level corresponding to the Nadkaragandinsk Formation in the Karaganda Basin. The majority of geologists-coal specialists consider the Kipshak coal district to be unpromising; however, it must be noted that the coal-bearing prospects at the level corresponding to the Karaganda Formation have not been studied. In the geological re-investigation at a scale of 1:50,000, which is projected here for the next five-year plan, the assessment of the coal-bearing character of this stratigraphic level must be given special attention.

**Metallic Mineral Resources**

Among metallic mineral resources, numerous occurrences of copper have been identified, with rare occurrences of polymetals, gold, silver, and aluminum. Other useful elements associated with copper, lead, zinc, and silver do not form independent occurrences.

**Non-Ferrous Metals**

**Copper**

Copper is the principal mineral resource in the metallogeny of the Teniz depression. Copper-bearing deposits within this structure are confined to various parts of the section of Upper Paleozoic terrigenous variegated formations *(chert No. 105)*. Copper mineralization is distinguished in deposits of the Middle–Upper Devonian, Upper Devonian, Carboniferous, and Permian. The Carboniferous and Lower Permian deposits are most saturated with copper occurrences. Despite the wide stratigraphic range and the presence of numerous copper-ore occurrences, no industrial copper deposits have been identified within the depression. The copper occurrences belong to the formation of stratiform cupriferous sandstones.

Copper mineralization has been established in sandstones, siltstones, and pelitomorphic limestones at various stratigraphic levels. Copper mineralization is localized in deltaic, alluvial, alluvial-lacustrine, and lacustrine plain facies, on the slopes of uplifts. Copper occurrences associated with alluvial, alluvial-lacustrine, and lacustrine facies are assigned to the Pre-Ural type, while those associated with deltaic facies are assigned to the Dzhezkazgan type. In addition, the geological structure of the investigated area is favorable for exploration of copper occurrences of the Mansfeld type. These are confined to the base of shallow-marine sediments that transgressively overlie the red beds.

**Middle–Upper Devonian Stratigraphic Level.**
The mineralization of the Middle–Upper Devonian stratigraphic level is associated with red-colored sediments of the Middle–Upper Devonian and is confined to beds of green-gray sandstones. At the surface, the ores are composed of secondary copper minerals: malachite and azurite. The richest ores are observed in sandstones containing plant detritus. The ore bodies have the form of low-capacity, rapidly pinching-out lenses, traced along strike for 100–200 m at a visible thickness of 10–20 cm. The copper content in oxidized ores reaches 1.0%. The ore occurrences of this stratigraphic level are: Sovetskoye, Nayrakty, Akimovskoye, as well as a small number of copper occurrence showings. All copper occurrences of the Middle–Upper Devonian stratigraphic level are characterized by insignificant dimensions and low copper grades in the ores.

**Shayrakty Occurrence No. 9** is located in the eastern part of sheet M-42-XIV, 4.8 km northwest of elevation mark 398.0 m.

In 1960, it was briefly described by O.V. Minervin; in 1966–69 it was studied by the Shabdar Party of the PSE (Kolesnik, 1969). The ore occurrence is composed of a Middle–Upper Devonian sequence of interbedded brown, gray, medium- and coarse-grained sandstones, conglomerates, siltstones, and limestones. The strike of the rocks is sublatitudinal, with dip toward the north-northeast at an angle of 18–25°.

In structural terms, the ore occurrence is situated in the core of the Shabdar brachyanticline.

Ore mineralization is confined to beds of greenish-gray conglomerates, gravelites, sandstones, and rarely siltstones. Four mineralized interbeds have been identified at the surface. The ore interbeds are highly discontinuous along strike and represent copper-bearing rock layers that laterally grade into red-colored sandstones and conglomerates. Along strike, the ore interbeds can be traced for 40–80 m, rarely up to 150 m. One of the ore bodies has a length of 400 m. The thickness of the copper-bearing interbeds ranges from 0.8 to 1.0 m. Copper content by spectral analysis ranges from 0.03 to 0.8%, lead from 0.001 to 0.003%, and zinc from 0.001 to 0.02%.

Ore mineralization is represented by disseminations, coatings, and streaks of malachite and azurite.

In 1967, the mineralization was studied at depth by drilling exploration boreholes. Five boreholes were drilled to depths of 70 to 90 m (Gostev, 1968). The boreholes intersected thin beds of brownish-gray and light-gray sandstones and conglomerates with no visible copper mineralization. According to spectral analysis data of core samples, the copper content in the said rocks is 0.01–0.05%, lead 0.01–0.02%, and zinc – 0.001%.

Due to the insignificant dimensions of the ore bodies and low copper grades, the Shayrakty ore occurrence has been deemed unpromising.

facies. Terrigenous sediments are characteristic of the Dayrin Formation, composed of alternating medium- and coarse-grained sandstones, gravelites, and conglomerates. Carbonate rocks are represented by a sulfipherous horizon — grey limestones, dolomites with subordinate interlayers of grey sandstones and siltstones.

Copper mineralization is localized in grey sandstones at the transition from the carbonate facies to the terrigenous facies.

The mineralization formed during the transgressive stage of development of the marine basin and probably belongs to the Mansfeld metallogenic type. At this level, copper occurrences of Bakalyadyr and Nokdombak (borehole No. 02) have been identified. The first of these has been evaluated from the surface and at depth. Commercial copper grades in the ores have not been established. In deep borehole No. 02, drilled on the southwestern limb of the Sary-Donkal anticline, ore zones with copper grades of 0.44 and 2.76% have been established at this same level. Although the mineralization has been established at considerable depths, the high and consistent copper grades in the ores and the favorable facial setting (presence of "red bed" in the footwall and a carbonate roof) define this stratigraphic level as prospective for copper exploration. Thus, there exists a potential possibility of discovering commercial ore deposits in Upper Devonian-level sequences. The most prospective for copper exploration are the deposits of the Dayrin Formation, distributed within the Shabdar brachyanticline. The Upper Devonian carbonate-terrigenous formation also controls polymetallic mineralization localized in the limestones and dolomites of the sulfipherous horizon. The prospects of the formation are conditioned by the lithological composition of the ore-hosting rocks and by the presence of dispersion haloes of lead, zinc, and barium. All of the above indicates that the Upper Devonian metallogenic level is prospective both for copper deposits and for polymetallic deposits (Atasuy type?).

**Lower Carboniferous (Spassky)**
**Stratigraphic Horizon**

Copper occurrences confined to the deposits of the Spassky suite have been assigned to this stratigraphic horizon. The Spasskoye copper ore occurrence and the ore occurrence identified during the process of additional study in borehole MT-295 are localized within these deposits. The copper mineralization is localized at the transition from grey-colored carbonate facies to red-colored terrigenous facies, i.e., the formation of copper mineralization took place during the transgressive stage of development of the marine basin.

According to published data, it is known that the Mansfeld type of mineralization is confined to the base of the marine transgressive series and is characterized by elevated content of carbonaceous matter in the ore-hosting rocks with small thickness of ore bodies. The mineralization confined to the sediments of the Spassky suite is concentrated in rocks containing an insignificant quantity of carbonified plant remains. It is possible that this accounts for the local concentration of copper within the Spasskoye ore occurrence and borehole MT-295.

The ore bodies at the Spasskoye ore occurrence have a lenticular form and limited extent both along strike and down dip. They are facially replaced by barren red-colored sandstones and conglomerates. The copper content in primary ores varies from 0.01 to 0.3%; the maximum value is
