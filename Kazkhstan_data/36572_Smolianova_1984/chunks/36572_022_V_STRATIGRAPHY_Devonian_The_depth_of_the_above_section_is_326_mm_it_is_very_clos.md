The lithological composition in the rocks of the Dairin formation is
monotonous. The main part of its section consists of conglomerates,
gravelites, sandstones and siltstones. In addition, there are single interlayers of
dolomites and tubogenic rocks.

conglosherates and gravellite are
characteristic of the lower and middle parts of the section, the color
of the rocks depends on the color of the cementing sand and varies from
red to light gray. Upward along the section, there is a decrease in the size
of the pebble material, the displacement of conglomerates by granite,
Pebbles, as a rule, are well rounded, the composition of gravel-pebble
fragments is polymictic, siliceous and metamorphic rocks, volcanites
of basic, medium and acid composition, and products of granitoid
decomposition are present. Upwardly, the composition of the rock changes
naturally in the direction of decreasing the shedding of volcanic debris.

Sandstones and siltstones are
rocks common at all levels of the Dairin formation section. The color
of the sandstones varies from red to light gray and almost white.
The macrotextures are diverse: lenticular-layered, oblique-layered,
Characterized by a slab-like separation, the dimension of the
clastic grains of sandstones varies from fine to coarse. Due to
the usually weak pyroclastic sorting, rocks of mixed granulometric
composition (fine-grained, coarse-grained , etc.) are common, the
roundness of the clastic grains is generally poor, the composition
of the fragments is peculiar, Quartz abruptly prevails in most
areas, and its flaking in the rock, especially in the upper parts of
the region ., rarely less than 50%, in some cases rising to 80%-90%.

Feldspars comprise from 50% to 10-15% of the rock, represented as

Plagioclases and kalishpates, and often kalishpate completely displaces
plagioclases (section for borehole 150). Rock fragments rarely amount
to more than 15-25" of rock and quite often, especially in the upper
parts of the section, act as impurities. The petrographic composition
of the rock fragments is the same as in the conglomerates.
There is a decrease in the shedding of lithoid fragments down the
section and, on the other hand, an increase in the shedding of
clastic grains of Late-Middle Devonian volcanics in them. Impurities:
ore minerals, chloritized biotite (often covers a significant part
of the rock), epidote, circol.

Sandstone cement is diverse both in the nature of
cementation and in composition. Clay, hydro-mica-chlorite cement
predominates in grades from film to semi-basement. Thus , the
pore, semi-saline and bivalent carbonate cement is widespread.
Carbonate, consisting of 50% to 10% of the rock volume, is represented
by calcite and dolomite. Dolomitic cement is more typical for the upper
parts of the section, which emphasizes the gradual transition
of the Dairin formation to calcareous-dolomitic facies overlying
Famennian and Early carboniferous deposits. Carbonate is usually
coarse- grained crystalline, and often corrodes clastic grains.
Regenerative cements are generally not typical, although they are found
in some sludge.

Siltstones are usually colored in red and purple tones.
They are characterized by poor sorting of clastic material,
significant clay admixture, Compared with sandstones in them,
quartz and feldspar are usually more pronounced in fragments,
clay cement, ferruginous and, less often, hydro-sulphide-
chlorite, spotting is often noted due to the different
composition of cement (fibrous in spots and silty in the main
surface) gander.

Deposits of the Sulfciferian horizon come to the surface along the
southern side of the Kipshak trough. In addition, they were uncovered by
the reference parametric wells 02 and 06 (fig. 49, sheet 10) drilled by the
Dzhezkazgan Geological Development in the northern part of the trough on the
north-western wing of the Sarydonkal anticline.

The exposure of the Sulfciferian horizon is good. The section is
represented by thin terrigenous rocks with lenses of limestones and
dolomites. The lens thickness is typically 1-2 m, rarely up to 5-7 m.
Their length is l-l,5, less often up to 2 km. This structure of the section
determines that the outcrops of rocks of the Sulfciferian horizon to
the surface usually form a weakly undulating plain, on which there
are low and unstrung ridges and hills with bedrock outcrops, and
more often with eluvial ruins of limestone and dolomite rubble.

The nature of the rocks coming to the surface also depends on the angle of
incidence of the layers. With a relatively flat occurrence , the
relief character corresponds to that described above. With a steeper occurence , the
nature of the outcrops is different: one or two wide ridges are
observed on the surface , on which strips of limestone and dolomite rubble
deposits stand out.

The decipherability of the Fameni deposits is also different.
When lying flat, they produce an expressionless gray mottled phototone
with white stripes and spots (Fig. 13). With a steep occurence
, the phototone is somewhat different: wide ridges stand out as long light
gray and white stripes against a gray background (Fig. 14)

In terms of photon, the Famen deposits differ sharply from the underlying sandstones of
the Dairinsky trench, and they practically do not differ from the overlying
rocks of the Simorinsky ridge.

The lower boundary of the division is conditional and is based on the
replacement of red-colored terrigenous rocks of the Dayara thicket with variegated
and gray-colored terrigenous-carbonate rocks.
