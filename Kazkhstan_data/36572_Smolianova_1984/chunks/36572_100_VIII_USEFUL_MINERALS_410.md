**V l a d i m i r o v s k a y a g r o u p o f c o p p e r o c c u r r e n c e s** (3,4,5–13) is located within map sheet M-42-XXXIi on the left bank of the Hobai River, 1.5–7.5 km to the east of the village of Vladimiroka. This group combines 13 copper occurrences with fairly intensive copper mineralization. Some of the copper occurrences were discovered by V.A. Plygin and V.M. Popov in 1929. In 1940, geological survey work was carried out by geologists V.G. Li and G.P. Bogomазов. In the same year, four boreholes to a depth of 100 m were drilled at the "Main" site. Two copper occurrences were discovered by I.A. Zlatochurova (Zlatochurovo, 1952), while the remaining ones were identified by V.V. Dmitrovsky (Dmitrovsky, 1969) and geologists of the IGN named after Satpaev, Academy of Sciences of the Kazakh SSR (Seifulin, 1976).

Mineralization covers a large area of terrigenous sediments extending 6 km at a width of 3.0 km.

The Vladimirovsky group of occurrences site is composed of deposits of the upper Tersakan subformation — brown, lilac-red, grey fine- and medium-grained sandstones with brown and grey siltstones, with rare interlayers of intraformational conglomerates and limestones.

In structural terms, this group of copper occurrences is situated on the northern limb of the Vladimirovsky syncline, where the strata dip toward the southwest (190–200°) at an angle of 5–10°.

Copper mineralization is localized in grey-colored sandstones and siltstones and is represented at the surface by disseminations, admixtures, and coatings of malachite and azurite.

The first ore body occurs in the lower part of the upper Tersakan subformation. It has been traced along strike by trenches for 1.0 km at a variable thickness of 0.6 to 2.5 m. To trace the ore body down-dip, four boreholes were drilled in 1940, of which one was ore-bearing (B-1). In borehole B-1, at a depth of 75 m, an interlayer of greenish-grey sandstones with a thickness of 5 m was intersected, of which 2.5 m contained disseminations of bornite and chalcopyrite. Copper content in oxidized ores ranges from 0.2 to 2.27%. In primary ores it reaches tenths of a percent. To trace this ore interlayer at depth, borehole D-146 was drilled 800 m from the outcrop down-dip. It intersected an interlayer of grey sandstone with a thickness of 1.9 m at a depth of 40.1 m. The thickness of the ore portion of the interlayer is 0.5 m at a copper content of 0.25%. This same ore horizon was established by borehole D-147 at a depth of 182.9 m, located 2.0 km from borehole D-146 down-dip. Mineralization is represented by sparse disseminations of chalcopyrite and bornite. Thickness of the ore interlayer is 0.45 m at a copper content of 0.2%.

Thus, the first ore body down-dip has been traced by boreholes D-146 and D-147 for 2.8 km. With depth, a decrease in the thickness of the ore horizon is observed: from 2.5 m at the 75 m horizon to 0.42 m at the 182.9 m horizon.

The second ore body is located 1.8 km down-dip from the first. It has been traced along strike with an interruption of 3.0 km. The richest oxidized ores are concentrated within a lens-shaped ore body of 400 m extent at a thickness of 1.5 m. The seventh ore body is located 1.6km to the south-southeast of the first ore body. The ore mineralization is confined to grey-colored sandstones, which along strike transition into "barren" grey siltstones. The extent of the ore body is 700m with an average copper content in oxidized ores of 0.94%, over a thickness of 0.65m.

**B o g o r o d s k o y e o r e o c c u r r e n c e** (Nos. 17, 18, 20) is located on the left bank of the Atymek River, 4 km to the north of the village of Bogorodka.

Within the Bogorodskoye ore occurrence, deposits of the upper Teresakkan sub-formation are widespread, represented by red-coloured and grey-coloured sandstones, siltstones, and argillaceous limestones. In structural terms, the ore occurrence is situated on the northeast limb of the Vladimirovskoye syncline. The strata dip to the southwest at an angle of 5–10°.

Three horizons of cupriferous sandstones have been established. The first horizon can be traced along strike with interruptions over 1000 m; two other lens-shaped ore bodies have an extent of 50–60 m. The visible thickness of mineralisation at the surface reaches 1.5–2.0 m. The mineralisation is represented by crusts and smears of malachite and azurite. The average copper content in grab samples is 2.0% (Pan, 1966).

In 1973, the Tselinograd GRE of PGO "Tsentrkazzgeologiya" (Posadsky, 1980) drilled boreholes No. P-221 and P-229, located 2 km down-dip from ore occurrence No. 17. Borehole P-229 at depths 314.7–316.0; 339.0–342.2; 423.0–423.7 m intersected fine- and medium-grained grey sandstones with disseminations of chalcopyrite. Copper content by spectral analysis data is 0.13; 0.43 and 0.12%. In borehole P-221, drilled 3.5 km to the northeast of borehole P-229, the number of ore interlayers is considerably reduced: in the interval 60.6–60.9 only one interlayer of sandstones was intersected with copper content by spectral analysis of 0.3%.

**P o l t a v s k a y a G r o u p o f C o p p e r O c c u r r e n c e s (34, 35, 37, 38, 39, 42)** is located 5 km to the southeast of the village of Spaskoye, on the left bank of the Mabai River at its confluence with the Sarkyrама River. It was discovered by V.M. Popov in 1928, and studied in detail during geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000 by the Atbasar Party (Dmitrovskiy, 1968).

The ore field is underlain by deposits of the upper Tersakkanian substage, represented by brown and grey sandstones and siltstones with rare interlayers of grey intraformational conglomerates and pelitomorphic limestones. Structurally, the mineral occurrence is confined to a monocline complicated by a fault of northeast strike. The displacement amplitude of the rocks is 20–30 m, and the dip of the rocks is 8–10°.

Copper mineralization is localized in grey sandstones and siltstones, rarely in conglomerates and limestones. The copper-bearing rocks extend in a chain from north to south, forming lenticular ore bodies. Here, twenty ore bodies of varying extent and thickness are exposed at the surface. Some of them have been traced along strike by trenches (the larger ore bodies are shown on the mineral deposit map as separate copper occurrences Nos. 35, 37, 38, 39, 42).

One of the ore bodies has been traced along strike for 1.95 km with minor gaps at an average thickness of 0.2 m. The remaining lenticular ore bodies have lengths of 150–500 m. The thickness of the ore bodies ranges from 0.5 to 1.0 m. The maximum thickness of an ore body was established in trench No. 106 and amounts to 1.35 m.

The ore mineralization is represented by malachite and azurite; chalcopyrite and chalcocite are observed less frequently. As a rule, the ore minerals are concentrated near plant remains, sometimes forming pseudomorphs after them. The copper content in hand specimen and groove samples ranges from 0.39 to 1.53%. With the aim of intersecting ore horizons at depth in the northwestern part of the Poltavskoye mineral occurrence, the Tselinograd GRE (Peshevskiy, 1980) drilled borehole No. P-211 to a depth of 492.8 m. Copper mineralization was intersected by it in the interval 50.3–51.6 m and is associated with silicified lime-

**P r o l e t a r s k a y a g r o u p o f c o p p e r o c c u r r e n c e s** (4,7,8,12,15,17,19–22,26,28) is located within sheet M–42–[illegible], on the left bank of the Zhilandinka River. The group of copper occurrences under consideration was identified as a result of geological survey work at scales 1:200,000 (Babichev, 1964) and 1:50,000 (Dmitrovsky, 1968). It combines thirteen copper occurrences: Proletarskoye Nos. 4,7,12,15,17,19–22; Ladmilovskoye No. 3, Smirnovskoye Nos. 26,28 and Intermediate No. 8. The Proletarskoye ore field is composed of deposits of the upper Tersakkan sub-suite — interbedding of grey, greenish-grey, brown siltstones and obliquely-bedded fine-grained sandstones. Lenses of pelitomorphic porphyritic limestones and thin interlayers of intraformational conglomerates have a subordinate significance in the section. Structurally, the area is confined to the Proletarskaya anticline. The dip of strata is gentle, 4–6°.

Copper mineralization in the form of crusts, coatings, and disseminations of malachite and azurite is confined to twelve horizons of grey sandstones.

In plan view, the majority of ore-bearing horizons consist of isolated lens-shaped bodies, shifting en échelon along strike. At surface, the copper mineralization can be traced along strike for tens to the first hundreds of meters. The maximum extent of ore bodies varies from 400 to 950 m, their thickness ranges from 0.2 to 0.9 m. The copper content in grab and channel samples according to spectral analysis data varies from 0.4 to 1%, and by chemical analysis reaches 4.68%. For the purpose of intersecting the ore horizons at depth, individual boreholes were drilled.

**D o r o g i n s k o e o r e o c c u r r e n c e** (Nos. 23, 25–29, 31) is located 3.0 km from the village of Doroginka (map sheet No. N–42–XXXIII), discovered by the Archalin party in 1970–1971 (Dmitrovsky, 1972). At the ore occurrence, deposits of the upper Terskakkan sub-suite are developed, represented by grey, brown sandstones, siltstones with subordinate interbeds of limestones.

E.V. Dmitrovsky identified seven ore layers.

The first ore layer is confined to the base of the upper Terskakkan sub-suite, represented by light-grey sandstones with sparse impregnation of malachite, less commonly chalcopyrite. Along strike, the ore layer is traced for 10–15 m with a thickness of 2.0 m. The copper content in lump samples, according to spectral analysis data, is 0.4%.

The second, third, and fourth ore layers are established in the section of the Terskakkan suite stratigraphically above the first. Each of them is composed of grey fine-grained sandstones with carbonaceous plant detritus. Along strike, the second ore layer is traced for 15–20 m with an apparent thickness of 1.5 m. The copper content in oxidized ores is 2–3.0%. By borehole D-1, it was intersected at a depth of 86.6–90.0 m, where it is represented by grey fine-grained sandstones with impregnation of pyrite and chalcopyrite. The copper content by spectral analysis is 0.4% at a thickness of 1.1 m and 0.75% at a thickness of 0.4 m. The third and fourth ore layers in terms of extent, thickness, and copper content are identical to the above-described one.

The fifth, sixth, and seventh ore beds are confined to the upper part of the Upper Tersakan subformation. Each ore bed consists of interbedded light and greenish-gray sandstones, siltstones, and intraformational conglomerates. The fifth ore intercalation has been traced along strike for 2.5 km. The thickness of the ore portion of the intercalation is 0.4–3.0 m, with copper content in oxidized ores ranging from 0.6 to 1.5%. Borehole D-2 intersected it at a depth of 282.0–288.5 m, where sparse dissemination of chalcopyrite was identified in the cement of gray sandstones. The copper content, according to spectral analysis data, does not exceed 0.1%. The sixth ore intercalation is located in section 55.0 m above the fifth; along strike it is traceable with interruptions for 2.2 km. The ore intercalation has a complex structure. It is represented by sandstones, siltstones, argillites, and fine-pebbly conglomerates. The variegated lithological composition and local distribution of carbonized plant remains in the ore intercalation has caused uneven distribution of mineralization within it, represented by secondary copper minerals — malachite and azurite. The thickness of the ore intercalation is 1.0 m. The copper content in oxidized ores, according to spectral analysis data, reaches 2.38%. Borehole D-2, drilled down-dip 1000 m from the outcrop of the sixth ore intercalation, intersected gray siltstones with sulfide dissemination in the interval 154.0–168.0 m. The copper content in core samples, according to spectral analysis data, does not exceed 0.08%.

The seventh ore intercalation lies 10 m above the previous one, was intersected by Borehole No. 2 at a depth of 132.0–144.5 m, and is represented by gray, dark-gray siltstones with sparse dissemination of pyrite and chalcopyrite. The copper content in primary ores from core samples does not exceed 0.04%; in oxidized ores it reaches 1.5%.

In 1980, on the Doroginsky ore field area of the Tselinograd GRE PTO "Tsentrkazgeologiya," Borehole No. P-237 was drilled to a depth of 681.0 m. The borehole, in the intervals 86.2–87.5 m and 266.9–267.3 m, intersected gray fine-grained sandstones with thin disseminated impregnation of chalcopyrite and chalcocite. The copper content according to spectral analysis is 0.2 and 0.4%, respectively. According to data from previous investigators (Dmitrovsky, 1972; Pavadsky, 1980) and our own data, the prospects of the Doroginsky copper occurrence are very limited.
