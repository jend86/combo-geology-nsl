in the lower part of the section are the Tereskei suite of the Upper Paleocene-Lower Eocene age.

DETAILED-STRUCTURAL-DEVELOPMENT-OF-THE-LOWER-PALEOCENE-OLIGOCENE-SECTION. The Lower Paleocene (Q IV) combines diverse populations, balks, grooves, lake shore lines and shows various transitions to alluvial-proluvial deposits of the first terraces of the national terrain. The complex is distinguished by considerable variability and frequent shows diagonal flatness.

Below is presented a section breakdown according to well 354 (Floor 1-42-YU, Gerenin B.V., 1967) surveyed in m:

1. Light, marl-series sands                                1.0
2. Loose sands                                             2.6
3. Sands and marl-series                                  0.7
4. Sandy marl-green-series with gravel, pebble and coarse pore              0.4
5. Sands and marl-series                                  0.3
6. Sands silty sandy-series                               2.4

Total in the above section - 8.4 m. Generally it does not exceed 5-6 m.

In other studied sectional-productive formations, similar populations and variations are noted only in the lower portions and individual sections.

The age assigned by the upper stratigraphic-contemporary deposits is based on the geological-paleontological analysis, which revealed in the section of the second terraced terraces, fauna related to the first national terraces, clearly these established them.

Results of spore-pollen analysis (Dimitrovich N.V., 1976; Smirnov S.I., 1976, 1972) do not contradict the assigned age.
