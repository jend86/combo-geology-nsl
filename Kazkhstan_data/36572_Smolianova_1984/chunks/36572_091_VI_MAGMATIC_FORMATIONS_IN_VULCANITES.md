**Problem of Paleozoic Complexes**

In the conditions of the scientific territory the most youthful formations overlie Paleozoic deposits of the Paleozoic complex and through instrumental methods, outcrops of Cretaceous-Paleocene strata are established with pre-Neogene age.

The upper boundary of formation of the Paleozoic complex by means of analysis indicates that on sites with smaller thickness bodies recrystallize red-colored terrigenous outcrops of anti-Franciscan age (Kurchikov, 1966).

Data of absolute age obtained in various years vary in the range 374-430 million years (using argon method according to biostratigraphy: Catalog of determinations........1970) or by data of A.P. Kurchikov (1973) in the range 405-460 million years.

One of the main investigations of the first phase of the comprehensive study of the Paleozoic complex of the Caspian Basin was devoted to the problem of determining absolute age. Investigations in the laboratory of absolute age LIN AN KASSR, calibration-comparative method on biotite were obtained following pairs:
