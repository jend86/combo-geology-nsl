At the surface it is exposed in the form of small hills with eluvium of red-colored sandstones. The exposure quality of the sequence is very poor. Across the entire vast area of sheet M-42-XXXY, where the syncline occupies a large part of the western half of the sheet, there are only 4–5 outcrops of these rocks at the surface. It has not been studied by boreholes either.

The sequence is overlain by variegated sediments of the undivided upper member of the Upper Torsakkansk Subformation and the lower member of the Kayrактinsk Formation. The identification of the latter is likewise carried out by correlation, but now with the Archalinsk reference section.

The lithological composition of the sequence and the physical properties of its rocks are analogous to those of the Torsakkansk Formation.

The age of the sequence is determined by its position between the Lower Carboniferous and Permian deposits, as well as on the basis of correlation with adjacent areas. It should be emphasized that neither in the Archalinsk syncline nor in the Oksanovsk syncline are these deposits traced continuously. No organic remains permitting determination of the age of the sequence have been encountered within it.

The physical properties of the rocks of the undivided Middle–Upper division of the Carboniferous System are similar to the properties of rocks of the Kireysk and Torsakkansk Formations. Magnetite-bearing sandstones are absent from the sequence. The thickness of the sequence is negligible and it is not mappable in gravimagnetic fields.

In accordance with instructional requirements, the Teniz Formation should be described below, but for a correct understanding of its nature and the logic of its identification, its description will be given after the description of the deposits of the Kayrakt Formation.
