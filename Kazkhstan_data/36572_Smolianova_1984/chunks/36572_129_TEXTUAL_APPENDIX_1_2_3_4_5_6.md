**Column 5 — Characteristics of occurrences:**

Coal occurrences are associated with deposits of the Kishak Formation. The northern group of coal occurrences is represented by six occurrences: Kishakskoe I, II, III, IV, Buruk, and Domgileksor. The southern group of coal occurrences was identified by boreholes No. 146, 147, 148. In the area, 45–40 interbeds of coal, carbonaceous argillites, and carbonaceous siltstones were identified. The most extensive seam is the "Slozhny" seam; it extends for 3.5 km. The seam thickness is 2.6 m, of which pure coal accounts for 0.97 m, carbonaceous argillites with coal lenses 0.25 m, weakly carbonaceous argillites and grey siltstones 1.38 m. On the western and eastern flanks, the thickness of the complex seam decreases to 1.16 m and to 0.24 m. Boreholes No. 146, 147, 148 intersected at various depths 15 interbeds of carbonaceous siltstones and argillites. Their thickness varies from 0.1 to 0.4 m, rarely 0.7 m.
