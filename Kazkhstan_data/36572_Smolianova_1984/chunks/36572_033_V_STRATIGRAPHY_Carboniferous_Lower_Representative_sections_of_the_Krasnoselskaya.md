Representative sections of the Krasnoselskaya formation are also
exposed in the Archaly riverbed (the northern wing of the Archalinskaya
mulda) and on the northern wing of the Vladimirovskaya Mulda. #Technically,
the formation is sufficiently maintained for the most part along the area of its
distribution, usually only the thickness of the thickness changes (see
comparison diagrams , Drawing 49). Noticeable facies variability is
observed mainly in the immediate vicinity of such paleoprobes as
Marinovskoya (for example, well I66) and Atbasarskoye (for example
, well 152). It manifests itself, on the one hand, in the increasing
role of terrigenous rocks in the context, and, on the other, in
the widespread occurrence of coastal-marine carbonate
facies, such as oolite-bearing, often sandy, limestones. In
the above wells, these limestones form significant
section intervals.

*                    *                    *

Terrigenous rocks are not typical for deposits of the
Krasnoselskaya formation, as noted above. They are represented by
small-scale interlayers of sandstones, siltstones and mudstones, and
we omit their description, since they are practically similar to those
of the Spasskaya formation, being marked only by finer-grained clastic
material.
Sandstones and siltstones with a high (up to 50%) content
of biogenic fragments and oolites form transitional differences to
the corresponding limestones.
Among the limestones of the Krasnoselskaya formation, which
are the main rocks of this stratigraphic unit, biomorphometrite,
biodetrite, and detritus- containing limestones are the most
widely represented. They are identical to the rocks described
in the sections on the Rusakovsky, Ishim, and Yagovkinsky
horizons, both in lithology and in a set of faunal remains, so
we omit their detailed description. Let us only note that in the
reference section of the Archaly River, oncolite-containing differences
in biomorphic detrital limestones that had not been previously encountered
were noted (sections A-103; A-100). Another peculiar variety of
limestones of this group has been described from the core of
well I66 (shl. I66-170). Biomorphic fragments in this rock are cemented

mostly not with carbonate, but with coarse-crystalline barite. It
should also be noted that there are no spiculate limestones in
sections of the Krasnoselskaya formation, although isolated spicules
were noted when describing individual slabs.

The second widespread type of limestones of the
Krasnoselskaya formation are oolitic and oolite- bearing limestone.
The area of their maximum distribution (as noted above) covers
the zones adjacent to the protrusions of the Basement of the
depression (Marinovskoe uplift), which were non-flooded land
areas in the Early-Middle Visean period. These rocks form thick interlayers
among the sediments of the Krasnoselskaya formation. These
gray, massive and lenticular-layered limestones are composed
of 50%-70% oölites and well-rounded biodetritic fragments
("pseudoliths"), the sizes of which vary from 0.2 to
0.8mm cemented with sparite calcite cement. Granulation
rims on the periphery of pseudoliths are characteristic.
Terrigenous admixture (quartz-feldspar) from (to 15 .
There are many transitional varieties to ordinary
biomorphic-detrital limestones. Oolitic limestones with distinct
porphyritic deposits composed of a zeolite-like mineral are described
from the core of well 152 . Porphyritic secretions replace both the
cementing mass and oölites.

Presumably algal limestones have been described from the core
of well 166 (shl. S- 166-167 ,5). This is pelitomorphic limestone
with a clear wavy-layered microtexture, due on the one hand to
varying degrees of crystallization of the layers, and on the other
- to varying degrees of manifestation of algal (?) structures.
The bottom ones are formed by a combination of micro-spaces
and micro-voids of a peculiar shape, made of crystalline calcite (a
reticular- clot structure; Atlas of Rock-forming Organisms,
1973).

Porphyritic limestones were found from the core of well l69 (shl.C-l69-96.5)
and in the reference section along the Archaly River (shl.A-30).They
are of the usual type (see the description of the Dainensky horizon).
The size of porphyritic secretions is up to 3 mm. The isolations were
performed with either secondary micrograined calcite or zeolite-like mineral

researchers in different ways: from the late tour to the middle vise, based
on numerous collections of organic residues from various sources.

From the sections, some of which are shown in Tables No. 20 and
No. 21, it can be concluded that the age of the formation corresponds
to the early and middle sections of the Visean century. This is
mainly confirmed by the brachiopod fauna. Significant Late Visean
forms appear only in the uppermost, lower-left parts of the Krasno-
rural formation.

The ostracod fauna does not provide a definite answer to the
question of the age of the described formations. According to S.B.Mamutova, the
appearance of such forms as Actuaria sp., lavatius sp. and single
representatives of the genus Darwinula (section along the Konur River, pp.
12-15) does not allow us to determine the age of the intervening rocks
more accurately than the Vise-Serpukhov stage. Considering the above,
we take the age of the Krasnoselskaya formation as an early-to-middle
age and allow for the possibility of sliding up its upper boundary. Practice
shows that with detailed layered collections of fauna, it will be possible to
identify analogues of both the Ishim and Yagovkin horizons of the Kipshak
formation. However, this boundary runs
through in the Krasnoselskaya formation. However, this boundary runs
in the middle of a stratum of monotonous limestones and is unsuitable for
flat-scale mapping.

Dalnensky horizontal_( C, de )

The deposits of the Dalnensky horizon will be isolated
within the Kipshak trough and the Teniz depression, where
they occupy significant areas.

The horizon is composed of a variegated, significantly terrigenous
thickness with rare low-thickness limestone interlayers, which are
concentrated in its middle and upper parts. The thickness is mostly red-
colored, only in the middle and upper parts it is gray-colored.

The exposure and decipherability of the Dalnensky horizon
is poor (Fig. 17, 18). Clasts of terrigenous rocks do not form well-
defined ridges. This is usually a poorly exposed flat area with small,
low manes that are weakly pronounced in relief. On the surface, the
section of the Dalnensky horizon is described along the riverbed
