The deposits of the Teniz Formation are distinguished for the first time. They represent the facial analogue of the upper part of the Terekkanski Formation and the lower part of the Kayraktinian Formation.

From the description of the Kayraktinian Formation it follows that, in the direction to the east and southeast from the stratotypic section, it was continually replaced by coarser-grained sediments, among which an increasing quantity of red-colored varieties appeared, the number of limestone interlayers decreased, and their thickness diminished. Thus, the lithological and litho-facial characteristics of the Kayraktinian Formation gradually disappeared. In the central and eastern parts of the area of sheets M-42-XY, the Kayraktinian Formation was already distinguished mainly by marker limestone beds —

limestones, which continued to be traced in this structural-facial zone. East of the West-Teniz flexure, which runs along the western shore of Lake Teniz in a direction from Lake Kypshak in the south to the mouth of the Bepesai stream in the north, the changes in the lithological composition of the Kayrakt Formation became so great that the previously traced marker horizons cease to be traceable into the new structural-facial zone. At their equivalent levels in the new zone, we cannot find new markers that could be traced eastward from the studied territory.

East of the West-Teniz flexure, new marker horizons appear, bounding at the top and base a bed of rocks which we propose to distinguish as a new local stratigraphic unit — the Teniz Formation.

The lower marker bed, above the roof of which it is proposed to draw the lower boundary of the Teniz Formation, is represented by polymict medium-to-coarse-grained conglomerates. This conglomerate bed corresponds to the conglomerate bed in the roof of the lower member of the Lower Tersakkansk Submember (see drawing 49, sheet 3). Thus, approximately the seventh part of the thickness of the Tersakkansk Formation belongs to the Teniz Formation, which in volume corresponds, apparently, to half a stage. Therefore, in accordance with the "Stratigraphic Code," the lower, underlying Tersakkansk Formation to the east of the studied territory may be preserved, but its upper boundary must be considered stratigraphically sliding, with the volume of the Tersakkansk Formation east of the West-Teniz flexure reduced in comparison with the stratotypic section.

The upper boundary of the Teniz Formation is proposed to be drawn at the base of a series of closely spaced nodular limestones, which corresponds to the base of the upper member of the Upper Kayrakt Submember. Thus, the Teniz Formation will include a bed of rocks corresponding to 2/3 of the volume of the Kayrakt Formation. Therefore, east of the West-Teniz flexure, in accordance with the "Stratigraphic Code," it will apparently be necessary to distinguish above the Teniz Formation a new grey-coloured formation,

The southeastern part of sheet M–42–XV and the area of sheet M–42–XVI are proposed as the stratotype region for the newly distinguished Teniz Formation. Unfortunately, the outcrop exposure of the Teniz Formation deposits is so poor that we are currently unable to propose a stratotypic section at the surface. As its stratotypic section, the section from a key borehole projected here, which is to be drilled by the Dzhezkazgan Geological Survey Expedition, may be accepted. Its location on the area of sheet M–42–XV is indicated on drawing 34. The section from exploration boreholes 143, 144 is proposed as the hypostratotype, which is presented on drawing 49, sheet 3, and in greater detail in the core logs of boreholes 143, 144 (drawings 64, 65).
