**Upper Division**

**S H O P T Y K U L S K A Y A F O R M A T I O N**

By the resolution of the Stratigraphic Conference of 1971 in Alma-Ata, the Shoptykul Formation is distinguished in the section of the Teniz Depression above the deposits of the Kiiminskaya Formation. This stratigraphic unit was first identified by N.V. Litvinovich in 1949. The deposits of the formation were described by her in the central parts of the Kiiminskaya, Ladyzhenskaya, and Agabazskaya synclines. The Shoptykul syncline was proposed as the stratotype region, in the center of which, on the area of sheet M-42-II, lies Lake Shoptykul.

During follow-up study in this area, a large number of traverses were made with the aim of re-describing the stratotypic section. The entire area is very poorly exposed. At the present time all the land around Lake Shoptykul has been ploughed up; even from the eluvium it is not possible to compile a section.

When identifying the Shoptykul Formation, N.V. Litvinovich emphasized the presence of porphyritic limestones in its section as its defining characteristic. At the same time, the nature of the porphyritic texture of the limestones was not described in detail. Therefore, later, when "cryptoporphyritic" limestones were discovered even in the uppermost beds of the Kiiminskaya Formation, the definitiveness of the lithological characteristics of the Kiiminskaya and Shoptykul formations disappeared. "Cryptoporphyritic" limestones on a weathered surface (Fig. 60) produce a sufficiently distinctive, striking structure such that many researchers began to regard their appearance as marking the boundary of the Shoptykul Formation. Thus, on the area of sheet M-42-II, E.A. Doubts arose regarding the validity of the formation's identification. They were repeatedly expressed by various researchers; thus, S.A. Nesmeyanov (1959), who studied the red-colored deposits in the southern part of the depression, came to the conclusion that only one red-colored sequence existed — the Kiiming one — and considered the Shoptykul Formation to be the upper part of the Kiiming Formation. Therefore, N.V. Litvinovich (1974) later re-examined the criteria by which the formation should be distinguished. They were defined by her as follows: greater saturation with sandstones than in the Kiiming Formation; the appearance of "porphyritic" limestones; abundance of organic remains.

In 1970–72, E.M. Smolyaninova described the hypostratotype section of the formation in the Achili syncline. In describing it, in addition to the relatively well-exposed surface, boreholes were used that penetrate the formation's deposits along the northeastern limb of the syncline. In this section, the lower boundary of the formation was drawn primarily on the basis of the younger microfauna, fauna, spores and pollen complex, as well as by changes in the color of the formation's rocks and the abundance of sandstone interbeds. The criterion of porphyritic character was not taken into account when identifying the formation in this structure, since "cryptoporphyritic" limestones and "porphyritic" concretions were first noted not only in the Shoptykul but also in the Kiiming Formation precisely during the description of the Achili–Aksui section.

During later work on the area of sheet M-42-IX (Smolyaninova, 1980), the parastratotype section was re-described in the Aukelbek syncline. During its description, as well as in the course of conducting routes in the central part of the Aukelbek syncline, the lithological characteristics cited below were identified as typical for the Shoptykul Formation.

During the additional study comparing the sections of the Shoptykul Formation in the Shoptykul, Aukelbek, and Achili synclines — the main areas of development of the formation — considerable attention was paid and it was established that previously the lower boundary of the formation in all structures had been drawn using different lithological criteria and, apparently, at different stratigraphic levels.

the geological section of the Shoptykul syncline, the parastratotype section of the Aukelbek syncline, and the hypostratotype section of the Ashilin syncline. From our point of view, only on the maps attached to the report is the lower boundary of the formation drawn in all these structures according to the same lithological criteria and, possibly, at an isochronous level. The Shoptykul Formation throughout the studied territory is characterized by the following features:

1. By the appearance of more thick beds (from 0.2 to 0.5 m) of distinctly porphyritic limestones, whereas in the underlying upper sub-member of the Upper Keymin sub-formation only concretions and thin, laterally discontinuous lenses of distinctly porphyritic limestones are encountered.

2. By saturation with sandstones. The sandstones are variegated in color, and in various structures either greenish-grey or red-colored varieties predominate.

3. By a lesser quantity of carbonate concretion-nodules in siltstones compared to the underlying Keymin Formation.
