In 1977–1980, the Dzhezkazgan Geological Exploration Expedition (Krasiveron, 1980) studied the deposits of the Tersakkenskaya suite within and to the northeast of the Kenenskoye ore occurrence. Borehole No. II-143 intersected, among the red-beds of the eighth horizon at a depth of 78.4 m, an ore body with a thickness of 0.8 m and a copper content of 0.44%. At 8.0 km to the northeast of borehole No. II-143, borehole No. 05 was drilled, which at a depth of 655.8 m intersected an ore interbed with a thickness of 0.5 m and a copper content of 0.89%. The estimated forecast reserves of copper based on borehole No. II-143 amount to 5.1 thousand tons, and for borehole No. II-05 – 6.5 thousand tons. In general, the reserves cited for the Kenen site are small, which allows it to be classified, at the present stage of study, as an ore occurrence. Our recommendations regarding the Kenenskaya group of occurrences will be given below in the chapter on the regularities of mineral deposit distribution.

**COPPER OCCURRENCE KAYRAKTY No. 34**
is located in the northwestern part of sheet M-42-II, 1.3 km to the northwest of elevation mark 365.7 m.

The geological structure of the copper occurrence involves deposits of the Teroakkan Formation. In structural terms, it is confined to the northern limb of the Milandin syncline, where the rocks dip monoclinally to the south-southeast at an angle of 4–5°. The copper-bearing rocks are traced along strike for 980 m, forming an ore-bearing horizon. It consists of two lens-shaped ore bodies. The western one is traced along strike for 420 m with an outcrop width of 20–24 m. The true thickness of the ore body is approximately 2.0 m. The eastern one is situated 340 m from the preceding body, has a length of 200.0 m and a thickness of 2.5 m.

The mineralization is represented by secondary copper minerals — malachite and azurite — developed in the form of films, coatings, veinlets, or as disseminations in the cement of grayish-green sandstones enriched with carbonized plant remains.

In channel samples taken from oxidized ores, spectral analysis established copper contents from 0.1 to 1.0%; lead from 0.0003 to 0.12%; zinc from 0.005 to 0.01%; molybdenum from 0.001 to 0.004%; and silver from 0.0002 to 0.002%.

For the purpose of tracing the mineralization at depth, borehole No. Д-52 was drilled to a depth of 244.0 m, located 560 m from the outcrop of the bed. The ore horizon under consideration was intersected in the interval 35.0–37.5 m, where it is represented by grayish-green sandstones with a bluish tint containing disseminations of chalcopyrite and bornite.

According to spectral analysis data of core samples, the following contents were obtained. Over a thickness of 1.5 m — copper 0.1%, lead 0.002%, zinc 0.006%, molybdenum 0.0001% and silver 0.00004%; over 1 m — copper 0.5%, lead 0.025%, zinc 0.005%, molybdenum 0.02% and silver 0.0003%.

In addition to the ore horizon described above, four more lens-shaped ore bodies have been identified, occurring at the same stratigraphic level. Their extent varies from 40 to 80 m at a true thickness of 0.2–0.3 m.

In chip samples taken from the ore bodies, copper up to 1.0%, lead up to 0.008%, zinc up to 0.001%, molybdenum up to 0.01% and silver up to 0.0005% were established.

**Copper occurrence Khryashchevskoye No. 75** is located in the central part of the map sheet M-42-B, 3.5 km to the southeast of the village of Khryashchevka.

The ore field is composed of deposits of the Tersakkanskoye and Kayraktinskoye suites.

Copper mineralization is localized in grey-coloured sandstones, siltstones and limestones of the Tersakkanskoye suite, as well as in the basal part of the Kayraktinskoye suite. All ore horizons at the surface are identified by secondary dispersion haloes of copper with intensities from 0.008 to 0.01%. Within the distribution of the Tersakkanskoye suite deposits, two lens-shaped ore bodies have been established. One of them has been traced along strike for 100 m with a thickness of 0.5 m. The mineralization is represented by disseminations, impregnations, coatings and films of malachite and azurite. A large concentration of secondary copper minerals is concentrated around plant remains.

The second ore body is located 6 m higher in the section. The extent of the ore zone is 190 m, thickness – 0.4 m. The copper mineralization is associated with grey limestones (0.2 m) and siltstones (0.2 m). According to spectral analysis data of hand specimen samples, the copper content in them ranges from 0.1 to 0.5%.

In the southwestern part, mineralization is established in the basal part of the Kayraktinskoye suite. At the surface it is represented by two lens-shaped ore bodies – western and eastern – separated in plan by barren rocks. The total length of the mineralized and "barren" rocks is 1200 m.

The western lens-shaped ore body is represented by grey-green sandstones with impregnations, films and crusts of malachite, occasionally azurite. Its extent is 460.0 m with a thickness of 0.6 m. On the western and eastern flanks, the mineralized sandstones decrease in thickness and become barren.

**Copper Occurrence Samerskoye**

No. 78 is located in the south-western part of sheet M-42-II, 9.5 km to the south-west of the Samerskiy borehole, and is confined to deposits of the Tersakkanian suite, represented by grey-colored, red-colored sandstones, siltstones with subordinate interlayers of clayey limestones. The grey-colored varieties of rocks are enriched with carbonized detritus.

In structural terms, the occurrence is located in the northern part of the Samerskoye saddle. The dip of the strata is gentle, 2–4°, rarely 5°. Mineralization is confined to light-grey, slightly bluish-tinted sandstones and is represented by scattered disseminations of malachite, less commonly azurite. The mineralized sandstones along strike are clearly controlled by secondary copper dispersion halos. The copper content in dispersion halos ranges from 0.008 to 0.01%.

The ore occurrence in the form of a lens-shaped body along strike has been traced for 1,500 m with a maximum thickness of the ore part of the bed of 0.7 m, and commonly 10–20 cm. The distribution of copper mineraliza-

**Lower Permian Stratigraphic Level.**

Represented by deposits of the Lower Kayraktinsky sub-suite. A large number of copper ore occurrences known in the area are associated with it.

Copper mineralization is confined to the lower variegated part of the sub-suite section; occasionally it is observed in the upper part of its section. Mineralization is localized in beds of grey polymict fine-grained sandstones, siltstones, and limestones enriched with plant detritus. Within the ore bodies, copper mineralization is distributed extremely unevenly and is represented mainly by chalcopyrite; bornite, chalcocite, and galena are occasionally noted. Oxidized ores are composed of malachite and azurite. Ore bodies have a lens-shaped form; their length varies from 100 to 1000 m with a true thickness of 0.2–2.5 m. The copper content in oxidized ores reaches 4.75%, in primary ores — 1.8% (borehole Kh-9). Copper ore occurrences hosted in deposits of the Lower Kayraktinsky sub-suite were formed in

**O c c u r r e n c e A k s u** (No. 6, 7, 8) is located on the Aksui brachyanticline, around the small lake Aksu (M-42-XY) and includes the previously known occurrences Aksu I (7), II (8) and outcrop No. 4110 (6), which were studied in detail by A.D. Gostev (1970). The geological structure of the ore field involves rocks of the lower Kayraktinsky and middle Kayraktinsky subhorizons. Six ore-bearing horizons have been identified on the site. One of them occurs at the base of the lower Kayraktinsky subhorizon and is represented by grey sandstones with a thickness of 2 m and grey breccia-like limestones with malachite admixtures with a thickness of 1.0 m. All the remaining are composed of thin-bedded (0.3–1.2 m) layers of dark-grey pelitomorphic limestones. They are enriched with carbonaceous sludge and fish scales. Secondary copper minerals — malachite and azurite — replace this sludge and scales. The mineralized layers are frequently permeated by a dense network of calcite veinlets with malachite up to 0.5 m thick.

Ore bodies at the surface have an extent of 10–50 m, rarely reaching 650 m. The copper content in oxidized ores based on spectral analysis is 0.05–0.3%. For the purpose of evaluating the Aksu occurrence at depth, borehole G-24 was drilled. In the interval 236.4–237.8 m, a layer of grey sandstone with sparse dissemination of chalcopyrite was intersected. The copper content, according to spectral analysis data, is 0.1%. Due to the small dimensions of the ore bodies and the low copper contents within them, the Aksu ore occurrence is **unpromising**.

**Copper occurrence No. 10** is located on the left bank of the Kokpekty River (M-42-VIII), 3.2 km southwest of elevation mark 283.0 m. Structurally, it is situated on the northeastern limb of the Artykpay dome. The occurrence was identified as a result of borehole drilling by the Zapadno-Teniz party in 1980.

Borehole No. 138 penetrated deposits of the Kayraktin and Tersakkan formations.

Lead mineralization was established in borehole No. 138 at the interval 291.5–292.0 m and is confined to grey limestones of the Kayraktin formation, represented by galena. Lead content: 0.1%. Below the above-mentioned interval, a sandstone interlayer with galena disseminations was penetrated (305.7–306.0 m). The lead content therein, according to spectral analysis data, is 0.2%.

Below the mineralized interlayers with lead mineralization, borehole No. 138 at the interval 310–311.0 m intersected an interlayer of fine-grained mineralized sandstones with carbonized plant remains and chalcopyrite disseminations. The copper content, according to spectral analysis data, is 0.2%. At the base of the above-described interlayer at a depth of 311.0–312.0 m, a dark-grey siltstone with sparse chalcopyrite disseminations was penetrated. The copper content therein is 0.08%.

The next ore interlayer is located 0.5 m below weakly mineralized siltstones and was encountered by borehole No. 138 at the interval 312.5–312.9 m, represented by fine-grained grey sandstones with disseminations of chalcopyrite, bornite, and chalcocite. The copper content, according to spectral analysis data, is 0.6%, and according to chemical analysis results, 0.97%. Through a barren sandstone interlayer 0.1 m thick, an interlayer (interval 313.0–315.0 m) of fine- to medium-grained sandstones with bornite and chalcopyrite disseminations has been established. Copper content: 0.3%. The presence of high copper concentrations in the variegated sandstones indicates the possibility of discovering new ore deposits to the east and northeast of the Artykpay dome.
