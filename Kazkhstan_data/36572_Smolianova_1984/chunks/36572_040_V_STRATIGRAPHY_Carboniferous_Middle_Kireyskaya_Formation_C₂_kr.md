**Kireyskaya Formation (C₂ kr)**

The Kireyskaya Formation, as a local stratigraphic subdivision of the Teniз Basin, was first identified by O.A. Mazarovich (1959) during geological survey at a scale of 1:200,000, on the area of sheet M-42-XU. The name is derived from the Kirey River, where the best surface exposures of the formation are observed within the studied area.

During additional geological study in the area of Lake Kirey and along the Kirey River, several traverses were made for repeated description of the stratigraphic section. The traverses showed that a reference section cannot be compiled here. It is not by chance that O.A. Mazarovich, having named the formation Kireyskaya, provided its incomplete section from a borehole drilled on the left bank of the Kipshak River [illegible], cores from which have not been preserved.

The exposures along the Kirey River permit only a very approximate understanding of the formation's structure: it consists of two roughly equal-thickness parts; the lower part contains numerous interbeds of greenish-grey sandstones with calcarenites, the upper part is saturated with porphyritic limestones; the formation has sufficiently sharp boundaries, and spotted siltstones and silty sandstones are characteristic of it. The thickness of the formation along the Kirey River is 1500–1800 m.

Thus, a well-described stratotypic section of the Kireyskaya Formation does not exist to the present day. This conclusion is all the more obvious if one takes into account the increased requirements for the description of stratotypic sections in connection with the publication of the new "Stratigraphic Code of the USSR."

The section along the Kipshak River proposed by O.A. Mazarovich as the holostratotype is not complete. Only the lower boundary and approximately one quarter of the lower part of the Kireyskaya Formation section are observed here. The section along the Kipshak River may be accepted as stratotypic only for the lower boundary of the formation.

The authors faced the problem of selecting a hypostratotype for studying the formation. As the hypostratotype, the section from reference borehole T-20 is proposed, with a core recovery of 85%; the cores are stored in the core repository of the Turgay GRE in the town of Arkalyk. The area of sheets M-42-XIV, XV, where it has the maximum number of outcrops in a belt approximately 5 km wide from the lake and the Kirei River to the east to Lake Mondykul to the west, may be considered the stratotype region of the Kirei Formation.

In all other parts of the studied territory it has limited distribution and, as a rule, is very poorly exposed, but is penetrated by boreholes in the core of the Kyzyl-Ob anticline, on the Mendyshev dome, in borehole "A" in the central part of the Osakarov syncline, by numerous boreholes in the Tretyakovsk syncline, and on the limbs of the Marinovsk uplift.

The Formation has no distinct deciphering characteristics. Locally, near the top, a bed of variegated, gray-white-black siliceous shales is distinguished, which forms a ridge covered by alluvial deflation features, well traceable on aerial photography materials. In the lower part of the Formation, beds of denser greenish-gray, gray or lilac-gray medium-grained sandstones are well deciphered; these also appear in relief as ridges, easily identifiable on contact prints at scales 1:25,000, 1:17,000.

The Formation lies conformably on the deposits of the Kishpak Formation and is overlain by the Tereakkan Formation.

As noted above, the lower boundary of the Formation is exposed and can be well studied in the bluffs of the lower reaches of the Kishpak River.

Throughout the remaining territory, the lower boundary of the Kirei Formation is drawn according to a series of criteria, the principal of which is the appearance in a continuous unified section of coal-bearing deposits of red-colored rocks. In addition, at the base of the Formation, thick horizons of green, greenish-gray, brownish-greenish-gray sandstones with numerous fossil plant remains are ubiquitously present. Third, though a negative criterion of the lower boundary of the Kirei Formation, is the absence of macrofauna frequently encountered in the upper part of the Kishpak Formation.

In the stratotypic section of the lower boundary of the Kirei Formation (Fig. 18), the transition from the coastal-marine-coal-bearing deposits of the Kishpak Formation to the continental and lagoonal-continental deposits of the Kirei Formation is gradual. Here, as the conventional lower boundary

the base of the formation is taken as the base of a bed of medium-grained greenish-grey sandstones, exposed in quarries on the bend of the river. At the base of the bed, a massive accumulation of calamites is observed, with pseudomorphs after them of malachite, azurite, and iron hydroxides. The bed is clearly traced and was mapped during geological survey at a scale of 1:50,000 to the Teresakkan River.

When drawing the lower boundary in boreholes, in almost every borehole, depending on the thickness of the formation, it is possible to identify an interval of 10 to 70 m within which boundary sliding is possible. The character of this boundary in boreholes is well shown on Drawing No. 49, sheets 1–10.

The upper boundary of the Kireyskaya Formation is drawn at the base of the first massive bed of metaconglomerate-bearing conglomerates of the Teresakkanskaya Formation. This character of the boundary is observed over the area of sheets M-42-XV and M-42-XIV. Over the area of sheet M-42-XIV, in the interfluve of the Miali-Samtao streams, near the roof of the Kireyskaya Formation, a bed of grey-black laminated cherts approximately 20 km long is continuously traced at the surface. These cherts are well identified at the surface, but in boreholes, as a rule, the bed is less clearly expressed. Thus, in borehole 151 (Drawing 71) at a depth of 210 m, a 0.45 m interlayer of grey pelitomorphic limestones with small nodules of black cherts is observed. At the surface, however, the chert horizon here forms a ridge approximately 3 m high and up to 15 m wide. Along the entire ridge, alluvial debris of large slabs of laminated cherts is observed. An equally well-expressed chert horizon is observed in the section along the Maksi-Kairakty River; there it serves as practically the only marker horizon, along the roof of which the upper boundary of the Kireyskaya Formation is drawn, since at the base of the Teresakkanskaya Formation in the north of the studied territory there are no conglomerates, but rather coarse-grained and medium-grained sandstones with lenses and interlayers of gravelites.

Special attention should be paid to the character of the upper boundary in the Tretyanovskaya and Archadinskaya synclines. D.V. Dmitrovskiy, in the Tretyanovskaya syncline during compilation of the geological map at a scale of 1:50,000, drew the upper boundary of the Kireyskaya Formation along the roof of cherts exposed on the eastern limb of the syncline. Drilling of prospecting boreholes in this area revealed the presence of two chert horizons. Thus, boreholes X-108 and X-147 (Drawing 49, sheet 9) intersect two chert horizons at depths of 61, 303 m

and 157, 402 m. We draw the upper boundary of the Kireyskaya suite in both cases at the roof of the lower horizon, since it is immediately below it that the occurrence of spotted fine-grained rocks characteristic of the Kireyskaya suite begins, while beneath the upper boundary lie coarse-grained sandstones with zeolite cement, characteristic of the lower part of the Tereakkansk suite. From our point of view, the second horizon of siliceous rocks corresponds to the level of acid ash tuffs mapped on the area of sheets M-42-XV, XIV in the middle part of the lower Tereakkan sub-suite.

In the Archalinskaya anticline, the upper boundary is drawn at the base of a package of red coarse-grained sandstones with gravel lenses, which V.A. Pashadeckiy [188] assigns to the Ostrogorskaya suite.

The upper boundary of the Kireyskaya suite in the Oksanovskaya anticline is highly conditional. We draw it in borehole "A" at the base of the horizon of intraformational conglomerates at a depth of 640 m (fig. 49, sheet 6). However, the lithological expressiveness of the Kireyskaya suite in this structural unit is lost in the package of rocks which we tentatively correlate with the Kireyskaya suite — the characteristic spotted rocks and porphyritic limestones are absent. In the Molboldinskaya anticline, it is impossible to separate the deposits of the Kireyskaya suite from the Tereakkanskaya suite, therefore in this area we provide an undivided thickness of the middle-upper Carboniferous (C₂₋₃).

Within the studied territory, several types of sections are characteristic for the Kireyskaya suite. In figure 19, a scheme of zoning of the studied area by section types is given.

For the first zone, located within the area of sheets M-42-XII, XIV and bounded to the south by the Kireyskiy fault, and to the north by the Symtasoko-Kyzylobinskiy fault, the following sections are characteristic: studied from exposures along the Kipshak River, streams Madbar, Miali, reference boreholes O1 and T-20. This zone is characterized by maximum thicknesses of the suite and the most complete sections. The thicknesses of the suite in this zone range from 1800 to 500 m; a regular decrease in thickness from east to west is observed. Here the suite is clearly divided into two sub-suites: the lower sub-suite (C₂ₖᵣ₁) is represented by an alternation of green-grey polymictic sandstones, siltstones with violet-brown-grey siltstones and grey pelitomorphic and porphyritic limestones. The upper sub-suite (C₂ₖz₁) is distinguished by a predominance of lilac-brown rocks with abundant interbeds of porphyritic limestones. Over the entire remaining territory, subdivision into sub-suites at a scale of 1:200,000 is impossible, and for some zones with a reduced section it is also inadvisable.

The most complete and well-studied section of the Kireyskaya suite in the southwestern part of the Tenizkaya depression is the section through reference borehole T-20. The borehole was drilled to the south of the settlement of the Titov state farm, between lakes Doinidyakol and Zharkol, on the area of sheet N-42-XIY. In it, the deposits of the Kireyskaya suite are encountered in the interval 1304.6–711 m. The transition from marine deposits of the Lower Carboniferous to the deposits of the Kireyskaya suite here is very gradual. The horizon of sandstones at a depth of 1304 m is taken as the conventional lower boundary. From bottom to top, the suite section is composed of (Fig. 49, sheet 1):
