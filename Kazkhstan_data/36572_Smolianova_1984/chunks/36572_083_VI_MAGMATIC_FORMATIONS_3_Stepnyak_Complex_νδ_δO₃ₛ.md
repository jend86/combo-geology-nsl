3. Stepnyak Complex (νδ – δO₃ₛ)

To the formations of the Stepnyak complex we, as have many previous researchers, refer the Karaagach intrusion, located on the territory of sheet M–42–XXXV, 7 km east of

— 300 —

village of Pushkinka.

The intrusion has an elongated plan shape with a NE–SW orientation of the long axis. The area of the massif is 1.4 km², with dimensions of 2 km × 0.5 km. According to V.D. Yakimenko (1964), the eastern contact of the intrusion has a nearly vertical position, while the western and northwestern contacts dip away from the intrusion at angles of 60–75°. Thus, some widening of the massif with depth takes place.

The Karaagach intrusion cuts through the deposits of the Stepnyak series, with endocontact and exocontact alterations being weakly expressed. The formation of the massif is associated with a deep fault of meridional strike (Yakimenko, 1964). The internal structure of the massif is concentric: the marginal parts are composed of diorites, less commonly quartzose diorites, and the central parts of gabbrooids.

As noted by V.D. Yakimenko, "the intrusion exhibits a high degree of differentiation in the visually apparent absence of contaminated rocks. Undoubtedly, one cannot exclude processes of assimilation of wall rocks; however, this is likely a case of complete anatexis of the host rocks and, accordingly, crystallization of the intrusion from a heterogeneous juvenile melt" (1964).

The age of the rocks of the Karaagach massif is established on the basis of correlation with adjacent areas of the Stepnyak synclinorium. At present, the rocks of the Stepnyak complex, to which the Karaagach intrusion belongs, are regarded as a special tectono-magmatic facies of the Krinikuduk complex (Shulga, Chernopolsky et al., 1976).

In terms of physical properties, the gabbro-diorites are dense (δ = 0.30 g/cm³) and magnetic (χ = 3027·10⁻⁵ c.g.s. units).

An increase in the intensity of the magnetic field is observed over the surface outcrop of the intrusion. Due to its insignificant dimensions, the Karaagach intrusion is not mapped on 1:200,000 scale gravity maps.
