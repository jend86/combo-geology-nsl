In the studied area, from the described deposits and to the southeast of the settlement of the Nakhimov state farm, in boreholes drilled by the Kushanaian geological-geophysical expedition, V.V. Gaem collected bone remains. According to his verbal communication, they were identified by paleontologists N.F. Savanov and G.F. Lynchev. V.V. Gay kindly transmitted their conclusions to the authors.

According to the conclusion of N.F. Savanov, the distal section of the skull with incisors of the ancient pika *Proochotone* sp. may indicate a middle–upper Pliocene age of the enclosing rocks. The rocks containing beaver incisors *Trogontherium minus* New., according to the data of G.F. Lynchev, were formed in the middle to upper Pliocene.

In addition, Yu.V. Dmitrovskim ( ) on sheet N–42–XXXIII from borehole D-116 the following mollusks were collected: *Gyraulus laevis* Ald., *Planorbis planorbis* L., *Valvata piscinalis* Müll., *Limnaea* sp., *Armiger crista* var. *inermis* L., *Pisidium* sp. — inhabitants of stagnant and slow-flowing freshwaters; *Papilla muscorum* L. — a eurygromic form, inhabiting mountains up to no higher than 1500 m and on plains in moist places; *Vallonia pulchella* Müll. — a meadow form; *Snocinea* aff. *putris* L., inhabiting the water's edge in moist meadows.

According to the conclusion of Dyadkina K.A., the age of the deposits enclosing these mollusks is no older than the middle Pliocene.

From this same borehole, ostracods were identified by S.S. Menshikov: *Eucypris clavata* (Baird), *Candona candida* (O. Müller), *Candoniella marcida* Mand., *Limnocythere* aff. *alveolata* Jaz.. The second and third forms have a wide distribution from the Pliocene to the present. The first species is known from Quaternary deposits and is occasionally encountered in Akchagylian deposits (analogue of the Apsheron). *L.* aff. *alveolata* Suz. is very close to Pliocene forms. The age of the deposits in his opinion can be established within the upper Pliocene (Korgosian horizon) – lower Quaternary. Taking into account all of the above, the age of the deposits is considered to be upper Pliocene. The deposits are correlated with the Kushanaian suite and, possibly, with the lower beds of the Kunshilakian suite of the Turgai depression.
