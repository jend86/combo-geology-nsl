In Zone I (Fig. 43), in addition to the reference stratigraphic section of the Kayrakty Formation, numerous core sections from boreholes drilled during geological surveys at a scale of 1:50,000 have been studied. All sections of this zone are presented on drawing 49, sheets 4, 2, 3.

For the area of sheet N-42-XXXIII (drawing 49, sheet 4, 7), the formation section is characterized in general terms as similar to the stratigraphic reference section. Three submembers are also identified within it; they are relatively easy to map. The lower boundary of the Middle Kayrakty submember in the Vladimirovsky syncline is also drawn along the stromatolitic limestone. In the Tretyakovsky syncline it is not recognized in boreholes; the boundary has been drawn along the increase in limestone interbeds. For the area of sheet N-42-XXXIII and the northern half of sheet M-42-VIII, breccia-like, breccia-like-shelly, and lace-like limestones are not characteristic; throughout the section the limestones are argillaceous, pelitomorphic, most commonly of massive texture.

For the area of sheet M-42-II (drawing 49, sheet 1, 2), in addition to the reference stratigraphic section, sections have been studied along the Kiiminsk syncline: one along natural outcrops of the Maksu-Kayrakty River, another from borehole core. Both sections are not very complete in their description; from them one can only infer that the formation section in the area of the sheet does not change in comparison with the stratigraphic one.

The formation sections in this zone are best studied in the area of sheet M-42-VII, in the western half of sheet M-42-IX, and in the north-eastern quarter of sheet M-42-XIV (drawing 49, sheets 2, 5). In connection with the fact that the formation is well exposed in this area, an attempt was made to subdivide the deposits of the Kayrakty Formation in greater detail. A more detailed subdivision was also needed in order to more reliably correlate the sections of Zones I and II.

The Lower Kayrakty submember is not subdivided in the areas of sheets M-42-VII, IX, XIV. Its composition remains sandy, but limestone interbeds in the zone in the direction toward the east and south-east decrease in thickness to 0.1–0.2 m. The limestones sometimes acquire a mottled coloration: grey with brown, grey with greenish-brown spots.

To the west of Zone I, within the Taldyk and, apparently, the Ushkarasui synclines, lies Zone II, characterized by sections studied from key parametric boreholes T-14 A, B, V. These are presented in drawing 49, sheet I.

The deposits of the Kairaktinian suite in this zone are overlain by Cenozoic formations of considerable thickness. A correct understanding of the volume and composition of the suite in this area is currently very important, since the assessment of the area for rhodusite, manifestations of which are associated here with the lower horizons of the Kairaktinian suite, will depend on this to a significant degree. We will not provide a lithological description of the section here. It can easily be read from the drawing. Let us compare the section of Zone II with the stratotypic sections and those of Zone I. They are consistent across all boreholes, easily correlated both with each other and with the sections of Zone I.

Three sub-suites are identified in the boreholes: P₁kr₁ – lower, carbonate-argillaceous, thickness 200–220 m. P₁kr₂ – middle, argillaceous-carbonate, thickness 250–300 m. P₁kr₃ – carbonate-sandy-silty, thickness 165–200 m.

The section as a whole throughout the suite becomes more carbonate in character. Dense calcareous siltstones and dense dolomitized argillaceous limestones begin to play a major role in it. They sometimes form beds with thickness up to 30–40 m. The sandstones and siltstone-sandstones of the suite become fine-grained, silty, also with carbonate dolomite-bearing cement.

In the II structural-facies zone a significantly different character of the section is observed. In this zone it is variable to nearly the same degree as the section of the Terokkansk Formation. It changes from grey-colored to multicolored with a significant content of red beds. The granulometric composition of the rocks also changes. Sandstones transition into medium- to coarse-grained varieties; the sorting of the clastic material within them becomes poorer; interbeds and lenses of gravels appear. The role of intraformational conglomerates increases throughout the section.

The grey coloration of the rocks in the lower part of the formation rises locally up to the marker stromatolitic limestone – the base of the middle Neiraktinsk subformation. This circumstance is proven by the enormous body of factual material from exploration drilling: from a single marker bed of stromatolitic limestones across sheet M-42-XV, many exploration boreholes have been drilled, and despite the uniform rhythmicity and consistency of the section by granulometry, the grey coloration of rocks in zone II rises continuously upward through the section in the north-eastward direction, and east of the Myshak key section, near well 471, red-bed rocks already rise above the stromatolitic limestone.

Among the deposits of the Kairaktinian Formation, gray-colored sandstones, siltstones, limestones, calcareous siltstones, and argillites are predominantly developed. The rocks of the Kairaktinian Formation can be dis-

divide into 2 groups: carbonate-terrigenous, clayey-carbonate-terrigenous and carbonate. The former constitute the main mass of the section, the latter accentuate the character of one or another bed, sublayer.

**Carbonate-terrigenous, clayey-terrigenous rocks** are widely distributed in the section and are represented by polymictic siltstones, silty sandstones, sandy siltstones, siltstone-sandstones and fine-grained sandstones. The rocks have predominantly grey, greenish-grey coloration, less commonly brown, reddish-brown and variegated spotted. The latter are characteristic of the lower Kairaktin sublayer in the Ashilin and Archalin synclines; during surveys at 1:50,000 scale this part of the section was attributed either to the upper transitional bed of the Vladimirova Formation or to the Archalin Formation. Indeed, its composition is similar to the bed of rocks which N.V. Litvinovich identified in the Archalin syncline as belonging to the Archalin Formation. Tracing it over the area showed, however, that it represents the analogue of the lower part of the Kairaktin Formation of the stratotypic Kairaktin reference section.

Textures are massive, parallel-bedded, wavy-bedded, discontinuous-wavy-bedded. Bioturbation traces, brecciation by fracturing, desiccation cracks, and sand dykes are frequently observed.

The composition of the clastic material is polymictic. Fine-grained, fine-to-medium-grained and medium-grained varieties are characteristic of the entire formation. Clasts constitute from 70–85% to 60–70% of rock volume. Clast sizes range from 0.05 mm to 0.2 mm for medium- to fine-grained sandstones; 0.1–0.25 mm for fine-grained varieties; 0.02–0.04 mm for siltstones. Sorting of material is moderate or poor. Grain packing is dense due to the presence of grains of varying size, but less dense than in rocks of the Teroankansk Formation. Roundness is moderate or good; poor roundness is rarely encountered.
