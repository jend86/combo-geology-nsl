In the summary of work received radiometric characteristics of the district and during gamma-logging one anomaly was detected in well No. 169 attributed to a series of limestone deposits.

Maximum gamma-activity from 152 to 106 imp/sec is noted in m.p. 244.0-249.0 and on the background of 25 imp/sec.

According to early core samples from the indicated interval, containing uranium in their composition from 0.001 to 0.03% and thorium up to 0.001%, and in one sample uranium 0.007%, radium 0.01% and thorium no more than 0.001%. Elements present: lead up to 0.005%, zinc up to 0.02%, copper up to 0.002%, molybdenum up to 0.0015% and phosphor from 0.4 to 0.6%.

Anomaly No. 169 has interesting future prospects and represents scientific interest.

It should be noted that it is located in the limits of Jadeyskoe perspective area, recommended for specialized prospecting organization for deep uranium searches.

Commission NOTES:

1. Core sampling was conducted in accordance with the plan and methodology correctly.

2. In the results of radiometric work materials are provided allowing calculation of sections according to lithological composition.

3. Control gamma-logging is performed in sufficient volume with good quality.
