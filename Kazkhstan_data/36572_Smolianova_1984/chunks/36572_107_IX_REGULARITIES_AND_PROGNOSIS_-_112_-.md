**CHAPTER IX. PATTERNS OF MINERAL DEPOSIT DISTRIBUTION AND PROGNOSTIC ASSESSMENT**

**1. Patterns of mineral deposit distribution.**

Geological re-study at a scale of 1:200,000, as well as preceding geological, geophysical, and geochemical investigations, made it possible to establish the following patterns in the distribution of mineral deposits (drawings Nos. 34–42) of the sub-platform cover of this structure within the western part of the Teniz depression.

---

**Coal**

The main factor controlling the localization of coal occurrences is stratigraphic — their confinement to the deposits of the Kipshak Formation. In general, these deposits are of low prospectivity with respect to coal. This is confirmed by the results of coal exploration work carried out by previous researchers and by the data of the West-Teniz Party. The coal prospects within the Kipshak syncline remain unclear, where the coal-bearing sequence has not been studied at depth. The depth to the roof of the productive sequence ranges from 0 to 500 m.

---

**Copper**

Copper-bearing deposits of the Late Paleozoic belong to terrigenous formations formed under sub-platform conditions.

The main factors controlling the distribution of copper occurrences in the given territory are: stratigraphic, lithological-facies, structural, and geochemical.

Within the studied territory, copper ore occurrences are confined to eight stratigraphic levels: Middle–Upper Devonian, Upper Devonian, Lower Carboniferous (Spassky), Middle Carboniferous (Kireysky), Upper Carboniferous (Tersakkansk), Lower Permian, Lower–Upper Permian (Kiyminsky), and Upper Permian (Noptikulskoye) (drawing No. 1D5).

**LOWER–UPPER PERMIAN AND UPPER PERMIAN (Kiiminsk and Shoptykul) STRATIGRAPHIC LEVELS** in the studied area are identified mainly on the basis of geochemical survey data. Verification of the identified copper anomalies by drilling showed that they are associated with interlayers of limestones enriched in carbonaceous detritus. The mineralized limestones pinch out rapidly along strike and dip. The most saturated with copper occurrences are the deposits of the Kiiminsk Formation, distributed within the areas of sheets M-42-II (Altynkazgan, Nima) and M-42-VII (Umkarasu). Deposits of the Kiiminsk Formation and the copper occurrences confined to them are localized in facies of lacustrine plains on the slopes of uplifts; the mineralization is hosted in gray-colored sandstones, siltstones, and limestones. The Shoptykul level is less ore-bearing than the above-described Kiiminsk level. Both levels are assessed as unpromising for copper deposit exploration.

Structural control of mineralization plays an important role in the localization of copper mineralization. It has been established that copper occurrences are localized within positive tectonic structures (anticlinal folds, brachyanticlinal structures, domes, flexural bends) and in transitional zones from positive structures to negative ones.

The overwhelming majority of copper occurrences are situated at the transition from uplifts to synclines. Copper occurrences in the studied territory have a strict confinement to the red-bed formations of the sub-platform sequence of the Teniз depression: they are located mainly at their boundaries, both in cross-section and laterally.

**Lithological-facies control of mineralization** is expressed in the confinement of useful components to certain rock facies types. In all suites, a distinct lithological control of copper mineralization is observed — it is localized in grey-coloured sandstones, rarely in siltstones.

Lithological-facies analysis of rocks of the Dayrinsky and Spassky suites of the Spasskoye occurrence area and borehole No. C2 indicates that they were formed under shallow-water near-shore marine conditions. The features of the geological structure of the Dayrinsky and Spassky suites indicate a connection with them of copper mineralization of the Mansfeld type. The zone of transition from red-bed to grey-coloured marine sediments is considered prospective.

Copper ores in the rocks of the Kireyskaya suite are localized in sandstones, siltstones, and less commonly in limestones, formed in lagoon-continental basins.

According to lithological-facies characteristics, the copper-bearing deposits of the Tersakkanian suite are divided into three facies groups (Fig. 105):

1. Submarine delta deposits: medium- and coarse-grained sandstones, conglomerates, rarely siltstones.

2. Alluvial fine- and medium-grained sandstones, conglomerates, siltstones.

One of the important conditions for ore occurrence in Dzhezkazgan-type deposits, as noted by many researchers, particularly proponents of the sedimentary theory of formation of copper-bearing sandstone deposits, is the ratio of gray-colored and red-colored rocks. Favorable for industrial concentration of copper ores are sequences where the combined ratio of thicknesses of gray-colored and red-colored rocks is equal to or close to unity. Such a ratio has been established in the ore-bearing strata at the Dzhezkazgan deposit. The study of geochemical characteristics of copper distribution and other chemical elements in rocks of the Middle–Upper Paleozoic was conducted on the basis of geochemical sampling of exploratory boreholes, taking into account the results of geochemical work by previous researchers.

It has been established that the behavior of chemical elements, and in particular copper, varies in rocks of different stratigraphic levels.

In rocks of the Middle–Upper Devonian, the average contents of lead, zinc, molybdenum, barium, chromium, and titanium are, as a rule, close to or below clarke values, while the amount of copper in them slightly exceeds the clarke value. For rocks of the Upper Devonian (Davrinsky suite), reduced values of copper compared to clarke values are characteristic.

The average copper content in grey-colored rocks of the Spassk suite within the Magdalinovsk anticline is close to clarke values.

In rocks of the Kireysk suite, the average contents of copper and associated elements are, as a rule, close to clarke values (0.003–0.0148). In rocks of the Terekkansk suite, the average copper content is slightly above clarke values. In this case, in sandstones the amount of copper is slightly higher than in siltstones (0.003–0.0078). This regularity has been established in sections of the Terekkansk suite within the Kokpektinsk uplift, Teyrakasaysk dome, on the northeastern limb of the Vladimirsk syncline, as well as on the western and eastern limbs of the Tretyakovsk syncline. It also extends to analogous rocks of the lower Kayraktinsk sub-suite in the Zhilendinsk, Sipinsk, and Archalinsk synclines, as well as

Among other elements established in the rocks of the study area, silver is of important significance, with elevated quantities being characteristic of the deposits of the Kiiminskaya and Pontikul suites. A comparison of average silver contents in the grey-colored rocks of the above-mentioned suites showed that the highest values of its content are established in limestones. In the axial parts of the Aukelbek and Symtas troughs, the silver content in limestones reaches 24–43·10⁻⁶%, which corresponds to 2–4 clarkes. Similar values of silver content are established in limestones of the Kiiminskaya and Pontikul suites within the Pontikul, Yano-Kiimin, and Algabas troughs. Here, silver occurrences of Pontikulskoye and Achilsay I, II have been identified.

and silver. A highly reliable positive correlation between copper and silver is established practically in all lithological rock types regardless of color and stratigraphic level. It is especially well manifested in gray-colored sandstones and siltstones of the Tersakkansk and Kiiminsk formations, as well as in the Lower Kairaktinsk sub-formation in the Teireksay dome, Kenensky anticline, and in the Vladimirovsky, Archadinsk, Aukelbek, Kiiminsk and Algabas synclines. As copper contents in the rocks decrease, the strength of the copper-silver relationship decreases. Copper also has a notable correlation with lead. Its weakening is noted in red-colored rocks.

Different correlation relationships have been established between copper and zinc. They are more weakened compared to those with lead and silver. In a number of cases, the absence of a copper-zinc relationship is noted in the gray sandstones of the Kairaktinsk formation within the Ailandinsk, Algabas areas, and on the northern limb of the Aukelbek syncline. Sometimes the correlation between copper and zinc has negative values.

Summarizing the above, it can be noted that in the Upper Paleozoic deposits, the highest contents of copper and lead are observed, with lesser amounts of silver and molybdenum. Their maximum concentration is confined to the gray-colored sandy-siltstone rocks of the Tersakkansk formation, the Lower Kairaktinsk sub-formation, and the Kiiminsk formation. It is precisely to these deposits within the investigated area that all known copper ore occurrences are confined.

Analysis of correlation relationships has shown that a close relationship exists between copper and silver. This characteristic is generally typical of copper sandstone-type deposits (A.A. Arustamov, 1978, 1980).
