Based on the description of the reference section of the Ashilinsky anticline, it follows that the Shoptykul Formation on the studied territory is divided into two sub-formations: the Lower Shoptykul and Upper Shoptykul, which in turn are subdivided into packages (bundles) within individual structures. In the Lower Shoptykul sub-formation, two packages are identified, and in the Upper Shoptykul — three packages.

T h e L o w e r S h o p t y k u l S u b - f o r m a t i o n is identified in all structures; its lower boundary corresponds to the base of the formation and is described in detail above. The upper boundary of the sub-formation is drawn at the base of a bed of greenish-grey sandstones with interlayers and lenses of gravelites and coarse-grained sandstones, above which lies a coarser-grained and more variegated package of rocks.

The Lower Shoptykul sub-formation is characterized by the presence of interlayers of "porphyry-like" limestones, the number of which increases significantly in the lower part of the sub-formation. This part of the section is particularly clearly defined in the Ashilinsky and Aukelbek anticlines. In the latter it has been mapped as the lower package of the Lower Shoptykul sub-formation. In the Ashilinsky anticline this package is clearly identified in sections, but it is not mapped at the surface.

In contrast to the underlying Upper Kiiymin sub-formation, the Lower Shoptykul sub-formation (Plate 49, Sheet 2) contains a greater number of interlayers of fine-grained and medium-to-fine-grained sandstones, practically in all sections.

In the Lower Shoptykul sub-formation in the Kiiymin group of anticlines, in contrast to the Upper Kiiymin sub-formation, interlayers of grey-coloured rocks appear. In the Ashilinsky anticline the section of the Lower Shoptykul sub-formation also becomes more grey-coloured than in the Kiiymin Formation, while in the Aukelbek anticline the Lower Shoptykul sub-formation retains its red coloration up to the Upper Shoptykul sub-formation.

T h e U p p e r S h o p t y k u l S u b - f o r m a t i o n differs from the Lower Shoptykul by the significantly greater grey coloration of the rocks and individual packages. Grey-coloured rocks in this sub-formation are probably more abundant than red-coloured ones. The **l o w e r m e m b e r** of the Upper Shoptykulskaya sub-formation is characterized by the presence of interbeds of green-grey medium-grained sandstones, which constitute approximately 30% of the formation section. The limestones in this member are pelitomorphic, or "porphyritic," but the character of the porphyritic limestones in them differs from that in the Lower Shoptykulskaya sub-formation. The porphyritic segregations in these limestones have rounded isometric outlines and are composed of calcite in association with phosphate-apatite. In the interbeds of sandstones and siltstones, a large quantity of fine plant detritus is noted. Siltstones and argillites are grey and contain a large quantity of spores and pollen. Thickness of the members is 100 m. It is identified and mapped in the Ashilinskaya syncline and in the core of the Aukelbeskaya.

The **M i d d l e m e m b e r** of the Upper Shoptykulskaya sub-formation is characterized by the predominance of green-grey sandstones, especially in the lower part of the sub-formation. The lower boundary of the member is drawn at the base of medium-grained sandstones saturated with fish bone remains. These remains are present in large quantities in the lower part of the member. Beds of sandstones and limestones with these remains are well mapped at the surface.

In the roof of the member, exclusively grey-colored, thinly interbedded siltstones and argillites saturated with fine plant detritus are observed. From this part of the section, a large quantity of spores and pollen has been identified and determined.

The **U p p e r m e m b e r** of the Upper Shoptykulskaya sub-formation is distinguished by its siltstone-argillite composition, a significant red coloration of the section, and an abundance of pelecypod fauna. In the composition of rocks of the Shoptykul Formation, the terrigenous varieties do not differ from those of the Kiimin and Kayraktin formations. Perhaps they are distinguished only by better rounding of the clastic material, and by a higher content of plant detritus than in the Kiimin Formation. From the rocks of the Kayraktin Formation they differ in that they contain, even in the limestones, a higher clay component.

The carbonate rocks of the Shoptykul Formation change in its section from bottom to top from predominantly carbonate to argillaceous-carbonate and carbonate-argillaceous.

At the base of the formation there are crystalline fine-grained limestones, composed of a calcite aggregate with interlayers of argillaceous-hydromica composition.

**Porphyritic limestones** appear twice in the formation section. At the base of the formation, the "porphyritic" limestones contain large porphyritic segregations in the form of rhombs, parallelograms, elongated rectangles, and elongated platelets with pyramidal terminations. Their sizes are up to 2.5–3 mm. The saturation of the limestones with "porphyritic" segregations is uneven along the bed. They frequently form clusters and patches. Sometimes they saturate the rock to such a degree that individual segregations merge with one another, forming small nests. The porphyritic segregations are visible both on weathered surfaces and on fresh fractures, and in drill core. Under the microscope they reveal either monocrystalline or aggregate structure, but in both cases are composed of calcite. In the "porphyritic" segregations from the Aukelbek syncline, in individual porphyritic segregations composed of an aggregate of fine-crystalline calcite, minerals from the zeolite group were detected by X-ray structural analysis — phillipsite, natrolite, analcime.

Among the rocks of the Upper Paleozoic, the deposits of the Shoptykul formation are distinguished by the lowest density — 2.50 g/cm³–2.67 g/cm³; on the weighted-average density diagram they are characterized by the lowest density (σ = 2.58 g/cm³) among the Permian deposits.

By magnetic properties, the rocks composing the formation are non-magnetic (magnetic susceptibility varies within 3.40·10⁻⁶ CGS units).

On magnetic field maps over the deposits of the Shoptykul formation, weakly expressed negative fields are observed.

A positive anomaly of intensity up to 200 nT in the southeastern part of sheet M-42-II is caused by an intrusion of granodiorites, which in the gravity field is mapped by negative values.

The positive gravimagnetic anomaly on sheet M-42-IX over the deposits of the Shoptykul formation is conditioned by the underlying sequences of the Bratolyubovsk series.

In the gravity field, the deposits of the Shoptykul formation are not mapped by anomalies; the gravity anomalies over the described sequence are smoothed out.

The age of the deposits of the Shoptykul formation is determined by its stratigraphic position and by the organic remains discovered within them.

The formation lies conformably on the deposits of the Kiymin formation,

-209-

the age of which is considered Lower Permian.

In the Ashilinsky syncline, in the deposits of the Shoptykul Formation from boreholes C-II, 7, 6 during geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000, E.M. Smolyaninova collected numerous samples for palynological analysis in 1971. The results of the analysis, performed by N.I. Stukalova and A.I. Lopatina, were partially not included in the report, since they were obtained after the report had been written. In the present report they are used for the first time to determine the age of the Shoptykul Formation deposits.

The results of the spore-pollen analysis presented in Tables 36, 37 indicate that the spore-pollen assemblages are unquestionably Permian. In them, spores and pollen of ancient conifers are either entirely absent or constitute 1–2%. The assemblages are dominated by cordaitalean, vittatine, and ribbed conifer pollen; pollen of Asonaletes and Florinites is present in significant quantities, constituting 1.5–6.5%. Pollen with ribbed exine predominates over Vittatina. Such a ratio is characteristic of Upper Permian deposits of Bashkiria.

According to N.I. Stukalova, the spore-pollen assemblages from borehole C-II, containing in significant quantities such species as Cordaitina rotata Iab. (5.5–13.5%), Striatopinites bullaeformis Samoil. (9–15.5%), indicate an Upper Permian age of the deposits, while the assemblage from borehole C-7 is somewhat older. The sediments hosting it may belong to the uppermost Lower Permian or lowermost Upper Permian.

From the deposits of the Shoptykul Formation in various structures, a large number of samples was collected for ostracod determination. The ostracod assemblage from borehole 132, drilled in the core of the Aukelbek syncline, characterizes both the Lower Shoptykul and Upper Shoptykul members.

The species Rufloria derzavini (Neub.) appears in the Cis-Urals in Artinskian deposits and ranges up to the top of the Kungurian Stage — base of the Ufimian Stage. In Siberia this species appears earlier. In Kazakhstan it appears already in the Kairaktinsk Formation.

The species Paracalamites decoratus is characteristic of the Lower Permian, but is also known from Ufimian and Kazanian deposits. Based on all of the above, taking into account the resolutions of the Stratigraphic Conference of 1971, the age of the formation is accepted as Late Permian.
