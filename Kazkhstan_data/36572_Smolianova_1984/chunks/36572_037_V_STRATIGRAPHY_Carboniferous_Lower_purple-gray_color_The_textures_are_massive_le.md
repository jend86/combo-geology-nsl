The siltstones and mudstones of the Kipshak
formation are generally identical to those described, for example, for rocks
of the Jagovkins horizon. There have been no special investigations
of them, so we omit their description.

Intraformational gravelines

form low-power lenses among the sandstones of the Kipshak
formation and are similar in nature to the latter. The difference
is that they contain more or less rounded fragments of
mother rocks, usually of uniform composition.

This is the first time that we have used the name "Kipshak
formation" to designate the described part of the section during mapping
work. It was proposed in the article by L.A.Geganova, S.O.Mamutova,
E.M.Smolyaninova , G.V.Rilatova "On the issue of the volume and age of the
Beleutinsky horizon in the southwestern part of the Teniz depression."
The article is in print by the time the report is submitted.

Until recently, the described interval of the Kipshak River
reference section stood out as a stratigraphic analogue of the
Beleuta horizon, the stratotype of which was described by the
Beleuta River in the Sholak basin (Litvinovich, 1958). The age
the horizon in the stratotypic section of the complex, will be determined
in the volume of the Serpukhov century.

A detailed description of the Kipshak River reference section,
accompanied by layer- by-layer collections of paleontological material,
as well as its comparison with the reference section of the Beleutinsky
horizon of the Sholak mulda, which was compiled in 1979 by
one of the authors of this report, led to the conclusion that it
is necessary to isolate the Teniz depression of a new formation
- the Kipshak formation. A detailed lithological comparison of its
section with the section of the Beleutinsky horizon will be given in
the report on topic No. 394. The Kipshak formation in the Kipshak
reference section is divided into two lithological bundles, which
cannot be traced during mapping at m-ba I:200,000, but it is quite
possible to map at m-ba I:50,000 in the southwestern wing. depressions.
