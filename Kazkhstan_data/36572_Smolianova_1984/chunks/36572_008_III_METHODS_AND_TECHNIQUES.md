CHAPTER III. METHODOLOGY AND TECHNIQUE OF GEOLOGICAL WORK

A comprehensive program of geological and geophysical research was carried out over the entire territory of the geological survey area at a scale of 1:200000. Geological observations bore a regional character and constituted a significant portion of the general complex of geological work aimed at studying the deep structure of the territory and clarifying geological problems.

The program of geological research included the following geological tasks: clarification of formations underlying the Quaternary complex, investigation of Upper Paleozoic and Mesozoic deposits, compilation of geological maps of designated stratigraphic complexes, examination of contacts and zone tectonic disturbances. A complex of geological methods, including granulometry and mineralogy, was widely employed within the conditions of the Tengiz depression and was sufficiently effective. Particularly important for successful use of gravimetric data in geological interpretation was the sufficient differentiation of rock densities by genetic types. Here are presented methodology and technique of work on individual geological methods.

Gravimetry

Field gravimetric observations were performed using a gravimeter GK-4 with automatic transport device. Measurements were carried out according to a grid pattern at individual observation points. Each gravimetric profile was drawn, as a rule, from one nodal point (NТ). In this case, individual profiles were plotted between pairs of nodal points. The total length of gravimetric profiles was 717.4 km. Individual profiles between nodal points did not exceed and were maintained at the recommended level. The observations in one cell of the grid were in intervals of 1.5-2 km, which made it possible to clearly outline null-points in the observed field. For accurate interpretation, the notes taken contained information on the entire subsequent quantitative interpretation; the step taken was 200 m.

**Transfer to the nature of the design layout profiling** was carried out by comparing elements (directional well and basement) with reference points of state triangulation.
