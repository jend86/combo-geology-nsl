**68°03' E.L. / 51°59' N.L.**

**32** | **N-42-II** | **Borisovskoe** | **C₁**
68°37' E.L.
51°54' N.L.

Non-industrial limestone deposit Borisovskoe is confined to Lower Carboniferous sediments. Limestones are grey, dense, massive, weakly silicified. Thickness of limestone interbeds is 1.0–2.2 m. According to chemical analysis data conducted in 1956 (Pomurakov, 1957), limestones contain: SiO₂ — 20.34%, Al₂O₃ — 6.3%, CaO — 39.3%, MgO — 0.67%. Clay impurity content in the limestones constitutes 26.64–30.57%. Limestone reserves in category C₁ amount to 115.2 thousand cu.m. They are suitable as building stone and for production of low-quality building lime.

*Studied by V. Pomurakov in 1957. Being developed by local construction organizations.*

**59** | M-42-II | **Sergeyevskoye** | C₁

68°15′ E.
51°42′ N.

Associated with Lower Carboniferous deposits. Limestones are dirty green, grey, marbled and silicified. Thickness of the limestone bed is approximately 40.0 m.

Chemical composition of the limestones is as follows:
SiO₂ — 24.56%; P₂O₃ — 6.01%; CaO — 37.6%; MgO — 0.42%; L.O.I. — 30.44%.
