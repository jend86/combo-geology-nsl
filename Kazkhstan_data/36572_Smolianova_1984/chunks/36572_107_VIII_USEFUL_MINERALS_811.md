**Non-upper-Permian (Kungurian) and upper-Permian (Tatarian) stratigraphic levels.**

The stratigraphic position of Kungurian deposits is characterized by broad dissemination. They contain numerous conglomerate formations and distinctive sedimentary sequences of terrestrial origin. The age distribution across the entire studied territory is uniform and is based on its red-colored facies. The share of Permian-Carboniferous Borod and partly in the range constitutes 10-20% and only for the Nizhne-Kamskaya basin amounts to as much as 50%.

The main occurrence of ores of this level is confined to the Lagen-Baitian, Kungurian and Unkorsutskian assemblies, where Kungurian deposits are represented by red-colored sandstones and siltstones, as well as aleurites and clays. Ore mineralization is confined to sandstones (Unkorsutskiy, lime, Antinozgoten) and aleurites (Altinozgoten). The largest number of points containing secondary ores is localized in palimorphic limestone formations. The least marked sulfidization is noted in sandstones, enriched with organic remains.
