The work was carried out in the western part of the Teniz Depression, which is a populated, easily accessible, economically developed region directly adjoining the Dzhezkazgan ore district, whose mining enterprises are in need of strengthening their raw material base.

The size of the studied area and the scope (stage) of the work performed predetermine the possibility and necessity of presenting and reflecting in the report a medium-scale structural-metallogenic zoning of the studied territory, with the delineation of ore districts or commensurate zoning units, with an assessment of reserves of predicted resources of category P₃.

The high degree of prospecting coverage (the surface has been prospected using a complex of geochemical and geophysical methods at a scale of 1:50,000, large-scale geological surveys of "open" territories have been conducted, and geological follow-up survey at scale 1:50,000 is underway), the completion over parts of the areas of prospecting (detailed prospecting under the old classification), prospecting-evaluation, and even exploration work, makes it possible — given an in-depth processing of the accumulated information — to detail the structural-metallogenic zoning scheme, to delineate ore-bearing areas commensurate with ore fields, and to carry out a calculation of predicted resources of category P₂.

In the materials reviewed, there is no analysis of the existing structural-metallogenic zoning schemes of the territory, nor any proposals for their refinement. There is also no information on previously performed predictive assessments, and it is unclear whether an assessment of predicted resources as of 1.01.83 — which bore the character of a compensatory exercise — was carried out on the area objects, and whether such assessments were performed within the framework of work completed during 1982 and subsequent years.

In view of the fact that the work of the party was begun back in 1978, the geological assignment (which, incidentally, was not appended to the report) set the task of calculating the reserves of predicted resources, and the depth of elaboration of this

the issue was not regulated. With the introduction of requirements for transitioning to the assessment of reserves of forecast resources at all stages of prospecting work, the geological assignment should have been reconfirmed, concretizing the issue of calculating reserves of forecast resources. Not one of the attached NTS protocols clarifying the objectives of work on the project addresses this issue. It is unclear from the "Introduction" whether the "Program..." of work on cupriferous sandstones, an element of which is the completed additional study and compilation of forecast maps, is provided for. If their compilation is provided for, then the calculation of reserves of forecast resources within the framework of the completed work could have been omitted, having made the corresponding reservations in the introduction.

Calculations of reserves of forecast resources are presented in the work under review in the sections "Mineral Resources" and "Regularities of Mineral Distribution and Forecast Assessment" and are reflected on special maps. Specific remarks on these materials are as follows.

1. The authors write about the presence of three genetic types of copper mineralization (Mansfeld type — in the transgressive series of red beds, Dzhezkazgan and Pre-Ural types — in the regressive series), describe the stratified horizons of lead-zinc, silver, and bournonite mineralization. Taking their presence into account, it was necessary to develop (or refine existing) schemes of structural-metallogenic (and mineralogenic) zoning.

2. Within the contours of ore-bearing areas commensurate with ore districts, having localized them in plan and limited them in depth, it was necessary to perform a calculation of reserves of category P₃. The calculation should have been preceded by a characterization of reference ore districts localized in similar or identical formations, of the factors and criteria predetermining the degree of their ore content, and an analysis of their presence and distribution within the forecast areas. Factors are provided only for deposits of the Dzhezkazgan type, but there is no analysis of their presence as applied to specific forecast objects, and the "weights" of these factors in relation to reserves have not been determined.

3. The calculations are methodologically incorrect, in violation of existing methodological guidelines:

a) Forecast resources are categorized as P₃, yet they relate to ore-bearing areas commensurate with ore fields; the quantity of reserves is determined not on the basis of analogy, through the scales of reference standards, but on the basis of specific data, by the thickness and content of bodies of useful

of the mineral within the boundaries of the forecast areas. Formally classified under category P₃, by the nature of the objects counted and the method of calculation, they are reserves of category P₂.

b) The calculated reserves cannot simultaneously be reclassified and approved as category P₂ reserves. The error of the authors lies in the fact that instead of industrially significant reserves they calculate geological ones, without introducing the concept of cut-off grades for the content of the useful component, the thickness of ore bodies and the volume of reserves, and without conducting, in the final analysis, a geological-economic evaluation of the calculated reserves in accordance with existing requirements.

c) It is also impossible to agree with the form of presentation of the reserve calculation: for different types of minerals it is presented in different sections of the report and is not verified. The authors have not departed from the traditional, old form of evaluating the prospects of the territory and have not transitioned to the new one. In the report, the prospective areas are subdivided by order of study (first, second, and third), and, moreover, already outside the framework of this classification, without reference to the provided calculations of forecast resources, recommendations are given for the drilling of boreholes, etc.

Taking into account the above, the provided calculations of forecast resource reserves are not subject to approval. They must either be recalculated in strict accordance with existing requirements, with complete revision of the sections "Mineral Resources" and "Regularities of Distribution of Mineral Resources and Forecast Evaluation," and supplemented with graphics, or the report should be approved in its present form, rejecting the calculated reserves as authored estimates that do not meet the existing requirements for their categorization and methods of calculation.
