Upper Oligocene (P₃ᵒ)

Comparative relations of the upper oligocene are represented by sandy-silt and sandy-silty-carboniferous strata of subregional and sublacustrine genesis. They occur in the greater part of the research territory of the force, and where they are preserved, they are mainly buried under younger sediments.

The upper oligocene strata are characterized by darkening on the core at the site of penetration. In that case, when the core penetration structure is interrupted—the boundary is clear; when the core penetration structure is not interrupted, and the basis is composed of an uncemented heterogeneous mass, the boundary of the upper oligocene strata is determined with difficulty in comparison with the overlying oligocene-eocene strata above.

In the southern Kara-Turgai (southwestern part of the region) upper oligocene strata lie on pores of middle oligocene-eocene strata, and throughout the entire territory—on the white sands forming bases, including breeds of glacial origin.
