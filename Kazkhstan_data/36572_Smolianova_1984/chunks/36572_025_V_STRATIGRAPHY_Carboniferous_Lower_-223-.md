COAL MINING SYSTEM

The deposits of the carboniferous system are widespread
within the studied territory. Their exits are usually confined to the
sides of the Teniz depression and to the cores of large anticlinal
structures in the interior of the depression. Deposits of all departments
of the coal system are represented in the work area.

Lower output

The lower section of the carboniferous system within the studied
territory is represented by a complex of marine, mainly gray- colored
carbonate and carbonate-terrigenous sediments. The formations of
the Tournaisian and Visean tiers are distinguished in its composition

Tournaisian tier

Among the deposits of the Tournaisian stage, we distinguish:
formations of the lower sublayer consisting of the Simorinsky,
Kassinsky horizons and undifferentiated Lower Tournaisian
deposits; formations of the upper sublayer, Rusakovsky horizon,
in some cases subdivided into two lithological bundles. In addition
, for a significant part of the Teniz depression, we identify
undifferentiated sediments of the Spassskaya suite.

According to their lithological composition, the rocks of
the Simorinsky horizon can be subdivided into three large
groups: terrigenous, carbonate, and salt.

Terrigenous rocks, represented by sandstones, siltstones
and mudstones, play a subordinate role in the section of the
Simorinsky horizon. They are confined mainly to the lower parts
of the stratum (Kipshak trough). Their specific weight among the
described deposits increases slightly in the northern direction (reference
parametric well 015).

Sandstones and siltstones are
predominantly heterogeneous. The sizes of clastic grains vary
within 0.08-0.28; 0.04-0.16; 0.15-0.8; 0.08-0.4; 0.2-
1mm. The degree of sorting is different, varying from slot to
slot from bad to good. Grain roundness is average to poor. The
composition is polymictic, quartz grains are most well isolated,
making up in some cases up to 65%-70% of the fragments. Feldspars
and rock fragments, as a rule, are poorly isolated from the
underlying mass. Impurities: carbonate fragments, sometimes
biogenic, oolites, zircon.

        Carbonate rocks are widely represented in all sections
of the Simorinsky horizon of the work area. They form two
large groups closely connected by various transitions:
limestones and dolomites. Among the limestones, three
most common varieties can be distinguished: normal -grained
limestones, biomorphic-detritus limestones; oolitic and
oolite-containing limestones.

Normal -grained limestones

are represented by pelitomorphic and micro-grained differences,
they are characterized by a more or less uniformly grained
structure, sometimes with indistinct lumpiness. A distinctive
feature of limestones of this type are mineral-diverse accretions
and inclusions. Thus, the following were noted: large
(up to 4mm) nests made of coarse-grained calcite; nests
composed of coarse-crystalline carbonate in an association-

The Osoyaitic and oolitic
limestones form separate interlayers among the rocks of the Simorinsky
horizon within the northern margins of the Kipshak trough.

Oolitic limestones are described from the section of borehole
150. The rocks are gray and massive. Oolites make up rocks of
diverse shapes and sizes: round, measuring 0.15mm-0.35mm;
elongated, 0.4mm-1.0mm, large up to one mm and more. They
are composed of radially radiated calcite, usually completely. Less
often, a foreign core is isolated: a fragment of carbonate, feldspar,
or ore. In large ooliths, several fused small ooids can serve as
the core . A slight admixture of echinoderm shell fragments.
Cement is a fine-to-medium-grained, uneven-grained calcite.

Oolite-containing limestones are described from the section
of borehole 015. They are of two types. The first type is
characterized by the presence of rounded organogenic fragments (
crinoid members, algal remains) in the rock in addition to soliths. The sizes of
oolites and fragments are 0.16-0.25mm. In the second type of rocks, soids are
associated with rocks and clumps of pelitomorphic calcite of rounded and irregular

isometric shapes with clear outlines, measuring 0.1-Mm. In
both types of rock, the oolites are well-formed, have multi-layered

Among the rare types of limestones , recrystallized
limestones (the southern wing of the Kipshak syncline) can be
noted; these rocks are entirely composed of "lobed" calcite
crystals (0.06-0.16mm) enclosing small (0.08mm) clumps of
pelitomorphic carbonate and redicts of indeterminate intrusive particles.

The second major group of carbonate rocks of the
Simorinsky horizon is the dolomites. As shown by the results
of a number of chemical, X-ray, and thermal analyses
(see below), microscopic studies and testing using It ,
monocarbonate, pure dolomite rocks in sections
of the Simorinsky horizon are extremely rare. Bicarbonate,
calcareous-dolomite differences are most widespread , This fact,
combined with the complexity of microscopic differentiation
of carbonates of different compositions, determines the vagueness of
the boundaries of the group of "dolomite" rocks and the difficulty
of distinguishing lithological types among them.

According to the prevailing ideas about the genesis of
dolomitic rocks, two large groups can be distinguished
among them: sedimentary dolomites and metasomatic dolomites
(Textbook of Lithology, 1983). Rocks of the first group are
considered as a product of primary chemogenic deposition of
magnesium carbonate; rocks of the second group were formed
either as a result of early diagenetic dolomitization of calcareous sediment,
or at later stages of epigenetic transformations of calcareous
rocks.
