**THE STC NOTES**

In the per-facility plan of TsGPE for 1981, it was envisaged, for the purposes of mapping the Teniz depression, to drill in the Eastern-Teniz Party 10,450 lin. m. of boreholes, and in the Western-Teniz Party — 14,500 lin. m., with a drilling productivity of 1,000 lin. m. per rig per month. In the course of operations, it was found that the actual drilling productivity in the eastern part of the Teniz depression, due to difficult drilling conditions — the presence of quicksands and waterlogged sand-gravel deposits with total thickness up to 100— m. [illegible] — amounts to 490 lin. m. per rig per month.

This circumstance has entailed an extension of the drilling schedule over the work area of the Eastern-Teniz Party, and in connection with this, the necessity arose to reduce the volumes of drilling operations for 1981 in the Western-Teniz GSP. The latter will not result in failure to meet the geological assignment.

**THE SCIENTIFIC AND TECHNICAL COUNCIL RESOLVES:**

1. To authorize financing of the acquisition of aerial photographic materials at actual cost from the reserve fund.

*Uch. 1406f*

- 271 -

2. Authorize the financing of core sample collection and geochemical sampling from borehole cores at the expense of the reserve based on actual volumes. Core samples must be collected from all mineralized zones. Geochemical sampling must be carried out across the entire borehole section for grey-colored varieties at intervals of 1–2 m, and for red-colored varieties — up to 5 m.

3. For the mineral resources map, it is considered appropriate to plot occurrences of cupriferous sandstones as showings with the following parameters: copper content 0.2–0.3% at a thickness of 1.5 m (0.3–0.45 m %), with a detailed description of them in the text and in the occurrence cadastre.

Points of copper mineralization with cadastral contents (< 0.1%) should be plotted on the mineral resources map using different symbols. Their description should be provided in summary form in the report text, organized by stratigraphic subdivisions.

Chief Geologist of the Association
"Tsentrkazgeologiya" — **I.V. Orlov**
