DEVONIAN SYSTEM

Formations of the Devonian sedimentary strata are widely distributed in the adjacent scientific territory. They compose a number of productive and barren layers, approaching and adjoining structural features of the Kirghiz Swell, Ustyurt Anticline, Karatau Syncline and Caspian Depression.

Devonian formations developing along the whole Bashkirian, Bukhara-Khwarezm, territorial and commercial districts, on the basis of distinctions in compositional structure of rocks and stratigraphic relationships, are subdivided into a number of regional suites:

D₁₊₂ - Bashkirian and Bukhara-Khwarezm-bearing formations of the middle-lower sections

D₂₋₃ - territorial formations of the middle-upper sections

D₃ᵤf - territorial formations of the upper section, Derbent suite

D₃ₛₐf - commercial and territorial-commercial formations of the upper section, Semenovsky Gorge, Sulphur-bearing Horizon.

Middle-Upper Sections (D₁₊₂)

Bashkirian-territorial formations of the early-middle Devonian age are distributed sporadically along the entire Ustyurt Field. The main regions of their distribution are the Kirghiz Swell; the districts of Leninsk Kulja and Shankaragandinsky; the sedimentation zone of the north side of Ustyurt Gorge and the Karatau Depression.

The proximity of the middle-upper section to good formations on the whole territory; planing in the northern districts, observing the irregular distribution, forms intermediate fish-like folds and folding with crown-like structures. Territorial and Bashkirian-territorial deposits appear here, as such findings can be seen in Russian river valleys crossing the Devonian section.

The disjunctiveness of suites of both here and in good conditions below is usually observed as construction fields with uniform structure.
