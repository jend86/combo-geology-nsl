Sorting and grading are varied. According to the composition of visible grains, there are two types of variation: polymict (rarely predominant) and oligomictic (less common). The content of quartz in sandstone rarely reaches 10%; usually these are the first processes or single grains. Useful minerals (plagioclase and, rarely, hornblende) comprise 10-25% in polymict varieties and 5-7% in oligomictic sandy sediments. Clay minerals comprise 50% to 90%. According to the composition among sandy sediments, volcanic predominate, less commonly - terrigenous. In some cases metamorphic and cryolitic varieties are found. The cements are represented by clay mineral, chlorite, chloritization biotite, epidote, individual amphibole grains, carbonate, fluorite.

The cement of sandstones is commonly porous, usually porous according to composition, more often cross-laminated calcite or crystalline carbonate (calcareous). Calcium crystallites participate in the pore cement. In cryoparametric sections 015 (sh. 0П-15/165.0; 0П-15/207.5) free-sized sandstone varieties are noted in which cement and significant mineralization from barite group are present, (photo) moreover barite-cementite can replace common grains and form polymict cement. The quantity of calcium in sandstones of the Genizova layer is found, normally within the limits of I-II process - 20-25%. In rare cases it rises to 30-50%; then the variety transitions into the rank of terrigenous-carbonate (clastic-carbonate sandstones - carbonate clastics).

Aerolites and argilites of the Kishak suite in general character resemble those described for the Atyush-sai horizon. We do not conduct special studies of them; therefore we omit their description.

Intraformational gravels form a small band among the sandstones of the Kishak suite and are similar in character to the latter. The distinction is that they contain larger and less rounded clasts of maternal terrace material, as is proper for a single composition.
