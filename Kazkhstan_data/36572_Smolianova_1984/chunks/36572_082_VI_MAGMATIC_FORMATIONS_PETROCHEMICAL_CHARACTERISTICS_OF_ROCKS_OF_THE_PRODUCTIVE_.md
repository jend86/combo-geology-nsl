PETROCHEMICAL CHARACTERISTICS OF ROCKS OF THE PRODUCTIVE COMPLEX

For studying petrochemical peculiarities of rocks of the productive complex, the author used materials of specialized analyses published in "Second Report" and a number of analyses from works conducted by the predecessor investigators (table 47). On the basis of classification of rocks developed by the Petrographic Commission of the Department of Geology of the Academy of Sciences of the USSR, published in 1961 ("Classification.....", 1961). Reference materials of only analyses of Mangystau-Ustyurt deposits in the study of analyses from preceding works remain largely author's work. (table 47). It should be noted that the main attention was paid to studying petrochemical characteristics of rocks of the first and second instrumental gas intervals, as the data on base petrographic instruments the author does not have.

The main petrochemical parameters characterizing rocks and permitting relationships to it and the type to make qualitatively one classification belong to the content of SiO₂ and sum No₂O + K₂O. Analysis of diagrams illustrating relationships SiO₂ - (Na₂O4.O) (see p. 77) permits to identify the following types: 1) rocks of first and second gas intervals of instrumental complex belonging to medium and fine pore rocks with SiO₂ content from 60% to 68% (first interval) and 65%-75% (second interval); 2) by content of alkaline relationships rocks form two series - a series of rocks of normal alkalinity from andesite lavas to normal trachytes and a series of sub-alkaline rocks, preceding in main quartzite composition, succeeding quartzite anorthosite and later through sub-alkaline trachytes. Two special peculiarities are noted in the preceding diagram: a) in practically all mass fractions they are found in rocks of normal type, both in sub-alkaline types, b) "anion" in sub-alkaline area narrowest character for group of medium pores, which at times represents a greater dispersion (with content SiO₂ 64.-63%) which relates to normal type. What, by-and-large, is obscured by manifestation processes, most prominently manifested when forming relatively metanorite varieties of rocks of the productive complex.
