SILTSTONES AND DOLOMITES.

Sandstones and alevrolites confined generally to lower time horizons of the studied sequence. The color of the rock varies in light tones to gray shades. The textures are predominantly massive, less commonly laminated. Sandstones are medium-grained and fine-grained. The composition of detrital minerals varies from calcareous to clay-dominated. Structure of calcareous cemented series ranges from poorly to well-sorted. The composition of carbonate cemented series is predominantly determined by its direction toward the north from the western shore of the Caspian Depression. Thus, for the P. Kipchak characterization, polymictite sandstones with composition ratio 20-40% plagioclase, 30-40% clay minerals and lithoid fragments 20-30%, and in places with scattered quartz grains 0.2 and 0.5 arenite sulfide-cemented horizons already showing quaternization; layered strata remain around 15%, and the porosity formation has a necessary supplement. Cement pore character is predominantly chemical, and clay-pore. According to granular (hydrodynamic, hydrochlorite) designation; regeneration cementless mineral cement. This type is noted together with cementing cementation and dilution and forms the "gliding" appearance of the breed. Calcite cement characterizes alevrolite sequences. Calcite comprises 30-40% of the pore volume and participates in part-linked grain contact. Sometimes it masks detrital grains of lower shales. An increase in carbonate content leads to formation of pore mixed composition, transitional to purely carbonate.

Carbonate breed sulphide-bearing horizons subdivide into two major groups: limestone and dolomite, between which evidence of transitional gradations.

SANDSTONES are most widely distributed in the precincts of the Mangyshlak Depression. In the northern direction (equivalent O₂, O₅) they practically were traced across their terrigenous and dolomitic facies. According to our data, in the section of the Caspian Sinkholes there may be distinguished two groups of limestone (according to the classification of G.A. Theodorovich (1950)): normally-vermiculate limestone and normally-vermiculate limestone with bimorph-detrital remains.

Normalization properties of rock samples with biomorfic-detrital coatings contain in their composition from 10% to 50% biomorphic residues, detritus and silt. When the content of the biomorfic-detrital component exceeds 50%, they transition to the category of biomorph-detrital and detrital rocks. A decrease in the content of organic residues to less than 10% gives rise to the aforementioned type of normal-grain limestone. The sizes of biomorphic residues and detrital particles reach 1.2 mm.

Oiliness and cementation usually weak. Biomorphic residues are predominantly composed of multichamber foraminifers, ostracods and (less commonly?) - serpulopodial formations. Detrital particles are represented by quartz grains, brachiopod fragments, ostracods, and shell material of crinoidal.

Cementing mass is composed of pelitomorphic calcite (micrite), early-diagenetic or slightly consolidated.

Dolomites and dolomitization play a subordinate role in the upper parts of the section of the visible horizon of the lower shale border of the Kipchakian suite and are practically absent in the section of the oxalate O2 and Ob. Considering their greater contrast with analogous, younger strata, their subsequent modification will be conducted below in a section devoted to carbonate Silurian deposits.
