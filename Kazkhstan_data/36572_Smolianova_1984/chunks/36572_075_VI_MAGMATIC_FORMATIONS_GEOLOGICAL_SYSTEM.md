GEOLOGICAL SYSTEM

Paleozoic deposits Q₁

In the limits of the Tersakpai-Turgai and Amangeldy-Kenzhegeisk distributed zones of narrow development, Paleozoic deposits are distributed unevenly, forming dense regions and single outliers dispersed in the municipal steppe. Marble rocks help in tracing rare petrogenetic breeds from Paleozoic periods to early Paleozoic deposits. General character "sedimentary" rocks refers to the rock structure of various breeds, including hard-broken and marginal composition, general or minor segmentation. Structural fissures and stratigraphic bodies of enclosed deposits are distinguished in one or several parts of the region. Quite consistently in the composition of rocks it is possible to distinguish two main components. The upper larger by composition characteristics is less competent structure, including tremolite-phlogopite and glinite bred red and maroon-brown of rocks. This section of rocks many researchers relate to independent limited-shale beds or p.n."zemlyanoi goryzont rocks of upper outcrops". The upper section, as previously mentioned, was shale-marble orderspatial and light-colored sufficiently carbonated. This section of the section is called "neosodii goryzont rocks of lower outcrops". In the lower, the upper section of the cut has simple clearly-proportional genezis, when in the beginning formed in open basins.

The deposits of the upper rocks form quite large manifestations on the dipping surface, studied in margarine collections.

The thickness of sand-containing deposits on the northern flank of the Gonozovsk elevation varies from 10-20 m to 45-55 m. The thickness variations in different wells are connected, apparently, not only with the change in thickness of this stratum, but also with the fact that the siltstone often occupies the upper part and lower position in the section of the plan.

The lithological composition of the stratum, as already mentioned above, is quite uniform. The stratum consists of siltstone. The siltstone is gray, sometimes reddish-brown. The texture is massive, the pores of the sand are occasionally very fine or pronounced characteristic of the loose. Occasionally siltstone is cemented, as evidenced by the fact that during drilling the layers that cut through it often become dense, light-gray and white carbonaceous formations on the surface of the sections. When cutting siltstone samples in the field, sandy material is encountered in an amount of 10 to 20%. The sand material is fine-grained, uneven in grain size distribution. In the lower part of the stratum, siltstone is more typical and thicker.
