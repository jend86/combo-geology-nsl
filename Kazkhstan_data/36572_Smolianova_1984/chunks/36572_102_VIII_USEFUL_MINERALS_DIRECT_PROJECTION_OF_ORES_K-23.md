**Upper Paleozoic (Hercynian) stratigraphic level**

The Upper Paleozoic stratigraphic level is associated with ore bodies of the Terekskoy light. It is distributed widely. The Terekskoy light according to lithologic-facies features, power, and location in different parts of the profile is not uniform. In the northern and central parts of the described territory, the light has acute power, and in the eastern and western parts its power reaches maximum values.

The lithologic composition of the light, as the power of light, varies. The association of serpentine and red-brown rocks varies from 112 to 1120. Peculiarities of structure, composition, texture and position of examined deposits in the space allow relating them to alpinely, alpinely-oceanic, substantially and continental facies. The upper part of the Vladimir group formations is characterized by a large area of terrigenous sediments, extending over 6 km at a depth of 3 km.

The section of the Vladimir group formations shows characteristics of continental-alluvial sedimentation—coarse-grained, fine-grained sands with pebbles, gravels and medium-grained with streaks of interbedded coal and shale.
