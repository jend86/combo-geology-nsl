In the section of the suite, arkosic sandstones light-colored predominate: reddish, gray, pink. According to the size of the clastic material, they range from coarse-grained to fine-grained varieties. In their composition, seeds of gray quartz and white and rose-colored feldspar varieties are present. Occasionally individual bars 2–3 cm in size, composed of clay lenses, clay-iron materials, porfiritam, granites, quartz porphyries. Sandstones are very friable. The size of the clastic material in coarse-grained varieties ranges from 0.5 mm to 8 mm, but approach[ing] smaller size, and more fine grains (from 0.2 to 0.05 mm). The grain material under the microscope is rounded, occasionally fractured, and sometimes angular. Among the terrigenous material, dominant are fully intact quartz, fewer mica veins of feldspar-containing pertisite, and levere partial feldspar strata and mica scales. The percentage content of grains by composition is as follows: quartz – 40–45%, feldspar – 20–25%, cement – 20–25%.
