The West-Teniz party conducted work within the western limb of the Teniz depression, composed of Upper Paleozoic continental deposits of the Vladimirskaya, Archalinsk, Kayraktinsk, and Kiyminsk formations, folded into gentle brachyanticlines. Within the anticlinal folds, deep boreholes at depths of 350–470 m penetrated marine deposits of Lower Carboniferous age.

**The Commission NOTES:**

1. During gamma-ray logging in borehole I35, an elevated gamma-activity of 49 μR/hr was detected against a background of 10–13 μR/hr, at a depth of 457 m. The anomaly is associated with Lower Carboniferous deposits, with grey fine-grained sandstones enriched in plant detritus. The anomaly has no practical significance and is evidently related to sorption by the host rocks.

In boreholes I36 and I40, at the transition from grey-coloured deposits of the Kayraktinsk Formation to the red beds of the Kiyminsk Formation, elevated gamma-activity values of 29–30 μR/hr were identified against a background of 15–12 μR/hr, characteristic of this stratigraphic level, associated with algal limestones and sandstones enriched in plant detritus. These anomalies may be used for geological correlation of sections, as they have a well-defined stratigraphic confinement within the western and south-western limbs of the Teniz depression.

**The Commission RESOLVES:**

1. The target assignment for reconnaissance surveys has been fulfilled.
2. To accept the field materials for reconnaissance surveys of the West-Teniz party for the year 1979 with a rating of **"excellent"**.
