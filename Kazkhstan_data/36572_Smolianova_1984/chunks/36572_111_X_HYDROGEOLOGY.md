**2. Work Methodology.**

Logging of mapping and prospecting boreholes was performed by the PTIS IGPE (operator Meerson D.), the North-Western Logging Party of IGPE (operator Silukin N.), and the Kolymbetsk Logging Party of IGPE (operator Ulyashchenko A.N.). Work was carried out in accordance with the requirements of the gamma-ray logging instruction manual (1975).

Gamma-ray logging of mapping boreholes was conducted using a PRKS-2 instrument with automatic recording on a chart strip at a scale of 1:200. Prior to logging, during tool lowering, monitoring was conducted by ear at T.L.F. ~~All anomalous~~ Gamma-activity values were detailed at a scale of 1:50, and the hoisting speed was no more than 50 m/hr. The logging registration speed was 250 m/hr. Calibration of the PRKS-2 logging radiometer against a radium standard C-41 was carried out twice per month. Sensitivity checks of the instrument were measured before and after logging operations at each borehole against the working standard. The results of these checks were recorded on the chart strip and in the calibration logbooks. More than 10% of the total logging volume was subjected to control gamma-ray logging, which amounts to 900 m. The repeatability of routine gamma-ray logging and control logging is good *(Fig. 86)*.

The methodology of logging operations for prospecting boreholes is described in the chapter "Geophysical Investigations in Boreholes" and is not repeated here. 1800 m were subjected to control gamma-ray logging, which constitutes 10% of the total logging volume.

Gamma measurement of core was carried out in non-logged intervals of boreholes that were in emergency condition and during sampling for uranium in anomalous intervals. **3. Results of Work.**

During the course of drilling operations, deposits of the Cenozoic and formations of the Ordovician, Devonian, Carboniferous, and Permian were studied radiometrically.

Below is provided a radiometric characterization of all stratigraphic subdivisions studied by radiometric methods.

Ordovician deposits were penetrated by boreholes No. 166, 394. They are represented by green, silicified siltstones and sandstones. On the logging diagrams, the radioactivity of the rocks ranges within 6–24 μR/hr.

Middle–Upper Devonian deposits were penetrated by boreholes No. 135, 158, 169, which are located within the Shunkurkol anticline, the Atbasar and Marinovsky uplifts. On the logging diagrams, the radioactivity of the Middle–Upper Devonian rocks varies within 16–25 μR/hr.

Deposits of the Dayrinskaya Suite were penetrated by borehole No. 150 and are represented by red-colored sandstones, siltstones with subordinate layers of grey sandstones and siltstones. The radioactivity of the rocks according to gamma-logging data reaches 18 μR/hr against a background of 15 μR/hr.

Carbonate-terrigenous deposits of the Simorinsky horizon were intersected at a depth of 288.0–320.0 m (borehole No. 150). The gamma activity of the rocks ranges from 6 to 15 μR/hr.

**Cenozoic Group**

The radiometric characterization of Cenozoic group rocks is carried out based on the results of gamma-ray logging of mapping boreholes. A total of 94 mapping boreholes were drilled within the investigated area. The average radioactivity of the rocks according to gamma-ray logging data is: for the weathering crust — 20–30 μR/hr, for Upper Oligocene deposits — 20–25 μR/hr, for deposits of the Teniz Formation — 12.0–20.0 μR/hr, for Middle–Upper Pliocene deposits — 16–20 μR/hr, for Quaternary deposits — 12.0–20.0 μR/hr.

As a result of gamma-ray logging of boreholes No. 174, 183, 239, 253, 254, 257, elevated radioactivity was established in weathering crusts composed of yellowish-brown and variegated clays. At depths of 26.4–28.0; 30.0–31.0; 35.0–36.0 m (borehole No. 174) — 36 μR/hr; in borehole No. 183 (interval 12.4–13.6 m) — 42.0 μR/hr; in borehole No. 239 at a depth of 5.0–6.8 m — 30 μR/hr; in borehole 253 (interval 26.0–27.0 m) — 44 μR/hr; in the interval 22.5–23.6 m (borehole No. 254) — 46 μR/hr; and in borehole No. 257, at a depth of 44.5–45.5 m — 45 μR/hr. Elevated gamma activity, according to gamma-ray logging data of the boreholes, was established in Upper Pliocene deposits. They are represented by ash-grey, dark-grey, and black bituminous clays. In the intervals 20.6–21.4 m (borehole No. 188); 32.0–33.6 m (borehole No. 190); 33.8–35.0 m (borehole No. 200); 12.0–13.0 m (borehole No. 229) and 10.4–11.6 m (borehole No. 246), the radioactivity of the clays ranges from 32 to 48 μR/hr.

The anomalous elevations are of infiltration-sorption type and have no practical significance.
