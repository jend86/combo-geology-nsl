Reference report from R.N. Antsenko on the results of work of the all-Union Western-Tienshan party and the necessity of changing the scope of survey work in the Western-Tienshan party.

NTC STATES:

In the work plan of PGC for 1961 it was envisaged that in the course of the current year the Tienshan division would work in the All-Union-Tienshan party for 4,50 thousand meters and in the Western-Tienshan party for 14,500 thousand meters with drilling productivity of 1000 thousand meters per month. In the course of work it was revealed that actual drilling productivity in the eastern part of the Tienshan division, with favorable drilling conditions - there appeared favorable and combined geological-structural conditions throughout the 100-meter stretch, amounts to 490 thousand meters per month.

This state of affairs necessitated a corresponding extension of drilling deadlines in the work plan of the All-Union-Tienshan party and, in connection with this, arose the necessity to reduce the scope of survey work for 1961 in the Western-Tienshan party. The latter does not prejudge the issues of future planning.

2. Distinguish [between] mineralized vein core samples and geochemical samples from vein excavations based on reserve calculation procedures. In this case, mineralized samples must be separated from all mineral-bearing zones. Geochemical sampling must be conducted along the entire vein excavation for scattered mineralization at intervals of 1-3 m, and for ore-grade mineralization - up to 5 m.

3. In the course of incomplete mineral exploration reports, it is advisable to present median ore occurrence parameters with content of ore 0.2-0.3% at depths of 1.5 m (0.3-0.45 m and 5) with a detailed description in the text and cadastral record of occurrences.

Points of mineralization of ore with cadmium content (0.1%) should be marked on maps of explored mineral occurrences in other sections. Their relationship should be given in a text report on regulatory subsections.
