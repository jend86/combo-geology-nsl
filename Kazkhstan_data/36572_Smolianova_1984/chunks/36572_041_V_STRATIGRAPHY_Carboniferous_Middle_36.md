Thickness of the upper Kireyskaya sub-member is 280m. Total thickness of the formation in this section is 604m.

In the roof of the formation, based on results of VSP (vertical seismic profiling) from reference-parametric well T-20, there is a reflecting horizon T₂. This horizon is the reference horizon for the entire studied territory; the base of the Terekhanovskaya formation is mapped along it. At the base of the Kireyskaya formation, based on VSP results from the same well, there is also a reflecting horizon, but it is insufficiently well traced areally. In any case, it is identified inconsistently during areal seismic exploration work.

To the north of the Syntassko-Kyzylobinskiy fault, throughout the entire northwestern part of the studied territory (Fig. 19, contour II), the Kireyskaya formation has a reduced section. In places, within consedimentation anticlinal structures, the formation is entirely absent. Sections of the Kireyskaya formation characteristic of this area are presented in Drawing 49, sheets 1, 2. Maximum thicknesses of the formation in this area within synclinal structures do not exceed 120–160m. In the core parts of anticlinal structures, thicknesses are reduced to 45m. We will not dwell in detail on the description of sections of this zone, since the character of the sections is easily read from the drawings.

In this area, particular care must be taken in defining the upper boundary of the Kireyskaya formation along the marker horizon of cherts. For it, in addition to the reduced section of the Kireyskaya formation, a reduced section is characteristic, and in places also a red-colored section of the lower Carboniferous, where in the roof of the Yagovkinskiy horizon there is also a very similar in appearance horizon of the same layered cherts. Above the latter, in the sections there may occur both grey-colored and red-colored marine deposits. These necessarily contain, though in insignificant quantities, fauna of atrypids and productids.

The combined occurrence of lower Carboniferous and middle Carboniferous cherts in section was observed in well M-45 on the Mendyskoye dome, and in well Z-13 on the northeastern limb of the Taldinskoye syncline.

On the area of sheet M-42-II, during geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000, V.V. Taranin [209] erroneously identified lower rhombic cherts as Kireyskian.
