PROTEROZOIC GROUP

Deposits of Proterozoic age are the most ancient of all deposits identified within the limits of the scientific territory. Their outcrops are confined to the fundamental and metamorphic basement, forming the bedding and foundation of the Tenizsian depression: in the area of the southern flank of the Kochetchaev mass, Markanagachsky anticlinorium, Kirevisky uplift and Stepnovsky synclinorium, a complex of territorial, vault-shaped and vault-shaped-folded rocks, ranges of Proterozoic rocks, which underlie the basement-sediment series and rocks, distinguished by their stratigraphic position, lithological-petrographic composition and degree of metamorphic transformations, this scheme, proposed in "Explanatory note and geological map of Kazakhstan SSR" at a scale of 1:500000 (1982), based on the stratigraphic breakdown of Proterozoic deposits of the region.

In their composition are identified deposits of the Lower and Upper Proterozoic.

PALEOPROTEROZOIC SUBGROUP
Serebrenichian series (PR₁ sr.)

The beds of deposits of the Serebrenichian series are located only on the north of the region, within the limits of the western flank of the Kochetchaev mass. The series of rocks of this series are located on the left bank of the Sarkara River and extend further to the east to the settlement (territory of sheets A-42-XXXIV). The similarity and differentiation of the rocks of this series, based on the locations of outcrops, in the main, and the role of rivers and rivulets of the granite masses of Paleozoic age.

In the studied territory in the southeastern part, two ridges are clearly distinguished, representing structural elevations of a type similar to the Borovoe and Amangeldy ranges, continuing northward into the internal regions of the Kokchetav uplift. Previously described (Pan, Koshanov, 1966) these ridges are composed of crystalline analogs of Hercynian and Jurassic-age structures, emerging in the southeast series in internal regions of the Kokchetav uplift. However, considering the complete impossibility of correlating these ridges in adjacent areas to the south, we refrain from direct correlation with the above-mentioned structures.

The first ridge forms a row of anticlinal folds in the area of sheet J-42-XXXII on the left bank of the Sarykand River.

From the north the first ridge is traversed by granitoid basement rocks of middle Paleozoic age; in its southern part it is in tectonic contact with rocks of opposite-dipping strata. The core role in the structure of the ridge (Pan, Koshanov, 1966) is played by significant thickening: biotite-muscovite, calc-silicate-biotite gray and gray-white marble. In the upper parts there are significant silicate-carbonate interlayers, and in lower parts there are marble, porphyroblast-bearing rocks and rarely fine-grained light-colored plagioclase-bearing rocks.

Average population density of the first ridge in the described area is 1100-1160 km.

The second ridge forms a row of anticlinal folds in the area of the Bayan-Saiyan region. Relationships of ridges to surrounding and enclosing rock sequences; the single core of the second-generation structures has a tectonic character. Folded ridge biotites, rare biotite-muscovite gneisses and biotite-silicates. Contains a clear very dense, massive plagioclase-biotite, biotite-pyroxene types, rarely fine laminated and especially fine-laminated laumontite-type (Pan, Koshanov, 1966).

Outstanding distinguishing feature of the ridges is intensive fragmentation striving process, which has the character of fractures, and in pristine sections, and very dense folk-like constituent, thickness of 1 to 10 mm.

Population density of the ridge is estimated at 500-600m. The average population density of the associated series in the described area is 1600-1700m.
