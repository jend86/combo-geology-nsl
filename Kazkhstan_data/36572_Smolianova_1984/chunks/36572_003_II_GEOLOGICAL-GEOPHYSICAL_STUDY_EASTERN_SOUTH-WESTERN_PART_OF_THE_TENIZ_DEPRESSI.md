Beginning in 1949, stratigraphic descriptions of Upper Cretaceous deposits of the Teniz field were made by P.P. Lyutkevich (1954, 1956, 1960, 1962), who proposed in 1963 a stratigraphic scheme of Upper Cretaceous deposits in the following form:

1) upper layer (upper member) - light-colored and red with progressing signs of polytrophic organisms;
2) middle layer (middle member) - reddish-brown co-association of sediments and conglomerates;
3) intercalated layer (upper member) - grey-colored sediments, dolomites and limestones;
4) calcareous layer (lower member) - yellow-gray sediments and limestones;
5) municipal layer (lower member) - yellow-gray sediments and limestones.

This scheme was successfully used for many years in the compilation of state geological maps of various scales.

At the Conference on unification of stratigraphic schemes (1956) and in line with new discoveries of organic remains in this scheme, some corrections were introduced. The Karolskaya suite was attributed to the lower member. The lowest stratigraphic suite, located between the Syrymdenskaya and Kairakttinskaya suites, accordingly, was assigned to the upper - lower member.

In 1969, revisions were made to the stratigraphic suite of P.P. Lyutkevich within a new artistic suite. The suite was singled out according to stratigraphic section by it in its last work "Stratigraphy and Petrology of Upper Cretaceous deposits of the western part of Central Kazakhstan" (1974).

a) Partial changeability of peripheral relationships;
b) Absolute organic remains along the entire extent;
