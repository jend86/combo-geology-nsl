1. Insytskyy granite-plutonic complex

Shallow breeds of the granite complex occupy the daily surface on the northern-southern edge of the study area, and in part of the southwestern part of the magmatic anticlinorium. Here they show unique kudo-calcareous structure (zone gor. Kuda-Sary, E-D-VII) and small-scale insytskyy mass (zone rivers insytskyy, E-D-I).

The Kubadaronskii massif represents a sill with isometric structure and dimensions of 0-10km×15-18km extending in a north-south direction. In the adjacent territory, its eastern extension was identified. Judging by the porosity phenomena, it belongs to the kubanschinsk and akkamarrinskii light ores single-stage location.

As a prerequisite for subsequent Kubadaronskii massif investigation, it was considered as an example of a typical middle-paleozoic magmatic association, in the composition of which two phases are distinguished - feldspar and intrusional.

The principal structure of the Kubadaronskii massif is, by contemporary understanding, a kuboscopic chamber coinciding with an offset of the main magmatic chamber. On the second stage, the massif underwent intrusion by later generations of components - ore and post-ore specimens distributed according to mineralogical composition and structural-textural characters. Furthermore, Kubadaronskii vulcano-plutonic structure is distinguished by intrusions, composed of predominantly of subsisting genus and occurring in their composition from hollow embrittlements (essexonites, centrallionites) and concentrations by hollow specimens and granitoscanics.
