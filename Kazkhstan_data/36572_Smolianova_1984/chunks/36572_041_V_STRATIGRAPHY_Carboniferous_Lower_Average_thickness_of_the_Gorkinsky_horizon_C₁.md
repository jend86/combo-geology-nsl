**Average thickness of the Gorkinsky horizon (C₁ joy)**

Outcrops of the Gorkinsky horizon emerge to the surface in the Kishtak depression on the southwestern slope of the Teniz uplift. The Gorkinsky horizon is characterized by a predominance of limestone of gray, cryptocrystalline organogenic-oolitic, algal and pisolithic types. Layers of limestone, and sometimes pisolithic layers, form weakly expressed ridges in the relief of the grian, which geomorphologically manifest themselves in the form of low, gradually descending horizons.

The density of outcrops is generally good. Grians, complicated by cryptocrystalline detritus, are displayed as distinct white bands (fig. 17).

Inclined horizons are conducted along the layer of gray organogenic-oolitic limestones, bounded by water-worn features, with multi-chambered shells. At this level, faunal composition changes occur, manifesting younger fauna forms.

In the same section, described according to the rule in r. Kishtak, two packages were identified: the upper one-terrigenous-carbonate, representing deposits of algal ostracods, limestone and pisolithic layers with exceptional block formations; the lower one-representing transfer of coralstalline pisolithic layers, algae and small mollusks. Single processes of well-distinguished dolomites occur in the blood. The upper layer is bounded well, i.e., small gorges and limestone do not form poorly expressed grian. The boundary of the limestone layers and chalk stratum is formed well in the section proceeding on the shield of grian. The boundary of the layers is conducted along the limestone stratum, marked with oolitic, with shells and limestone crenel formations.

Terrigenous strata of the Agovkinsky horizon are represented by sandstones, siltstones and argilites.

Sandstones — gray, greenish-gray of various textures. Fine-grained predominantly (0.1-0.25 mm) and medium-grained (0.25-0.5 mm) materials predominate in terms of grain size and roundness within broad intervals. The composition of feldspar-bearing sandstones varies within wide limits. In composition of the general grain material, roughly four varieties are distinguished. The first — the most widely distributed sandstone, the second — oligomictic subgraywacke sandstone (less frequently occurring). Subordinate sandstones are composed predominantly of rock fragments (approximately 50% volume of allogenic components) — granitic, granodioritic, effusive. Quartz and feldspar grains are found in subordinate quantities, as are heavy minerals, chlorite, biotite, pyroxene.

Cementing material up to subcrustalline and crystalline, rarely — chloritic with epidote.
