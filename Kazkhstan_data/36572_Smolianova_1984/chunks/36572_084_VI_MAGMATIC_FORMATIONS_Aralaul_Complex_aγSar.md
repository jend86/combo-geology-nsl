**Aralaul Complex ( aγSar )**

Leucocratic, alaskite-type granites, provisionally assigned by us to the Aralaul complex of Silurian age, are not widely distributed within the study area. They form a group

- 301 -

of small bodies in the southern part of the Karatas massif (M-42-XV), with a total outcrop area of about 8 km², and they participate in the structure of the Obalykol massif (~4 km² in the exposed part within the area of sheet M-42-XIV). It should be noted that the name "Obalykol massif" is being applied for the first time, to designate the poorly exposed intrusive body to the east of Lake Obalykol. For the first time, rocks of the Araldul complex are provisionally identified within its composition, by analogy with the rocks of the Karatas massif.

The exposability of the described rocks is satisfactory to poor; outcrop quality is more often poor, rarely moderate. Vein formations are better exposed. In the relief, outcrops of Silurian granites form a gently hilly terrain with elevations on the order of the first meters; valley-like depressions are frequently confined to the areas of their distribution. Within the studied territory, the described formations intrude into Upper Proterozoic sediments (Ultau series) and granitoids of the Krivozudunsk complex, and are in turn intruded by granites of the Borovsky complex of Silurian–Lower Devonian age. However, it should be noted that nowhere within the described territory are the contacts between intrusions of different ages exposed, due to poor outcrop conditions, so that all statements regarding the mutual relationships of the bodies are highly tentative. Tectonic contacts between granites of the Araldul complex and the host rocks are widely developed. The form of the bodies is difficult to determine. According to data from M.S. Grankin, steeply dipping contacts are characteristic of them, with poorly developed exocontact alterations of the host rocks, which, however, does not exclude the flattening of the contact surface with depth (Grankin, 1970). In first approximation, the form of the bodies is close to stock-like.

Among the formations of the Araldul complex, bodies of two generations are distinguished: 1) formations represented by medium-grained and medium- to fine-grained alaskite-like granites. These are predominant in distribution; 2) small dyke- and stock-like bodies, composed of fine-grained granites, alaskite-like, aplite-like, and biotite-bearing varieties (Grankin, 1970).

Regarding the phase affiliation of rocks of the first generation, this will be discussed below, in the section on the age of the described formations. The bodies of the second generation are in all likelihood vein formations of the first stage.
