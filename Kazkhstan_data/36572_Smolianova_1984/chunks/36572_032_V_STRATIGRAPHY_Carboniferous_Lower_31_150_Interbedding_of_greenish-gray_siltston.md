The terrigenous rocks of the Yagovkinsky horizon are represented
by sandstones, siltstones, and mudstones.
They are gray, greenish-gray rocks of various
textures. Mainly fine-grained (0.1-0.25 mm) and
medium-fine-grained (0.25-0.5mm) The sorting and rolling
of clastic grains varies widely. According to the composition
of clastic grains, two varieties are quite clearly established
among sandstones . The first is the most common polymictic
sandstones, the second is oligomictic feldspar sandstones
(rare). Polymictic sandstones are composed primarily of
rock fragments (~50% of the volume of clastic grains)
- clay, siliceous, effusive. Quartz and feldspar are
present in subordinate amounts, in approximately equal
proportions (with a predominance of feldspar). Ore minerals,
chlorite, biotite, and pyroxene are present as impurities.
The cement is porous to semi-basal crystalline calcite
, less often chlorite with epidote.
Oligomictic feldspar sandstones are composed by
more than 90 % of plagioclase fragments cemented with
either regenerative plagioclase, less often quartz cement,
film chlorite cement, or crystalline calcite. In the extreme
case, carbonate so intensively replaces grains of feldspar
that the amount of unsubstituted fragments decreases
to 40%-30%.

The group of carbonate rocks of the Yagovkinsky horizon
is represented by calcareous gravelites and various limestones.
Limestone gravelites (K-176B, K-176A) are, in fact,
the horizon of infraformational washing of carbonate rocks.
The fragments range in size from 0.4 to 4.5 mm and are
composed of variously shaped limestones: oolitic, pelitomorphic
silty, algal (up to bulbous), biomorphic-sludge. Detritus particles
are also present. The edging of the fragments is well
shaped, close to isometric, characterized by granulation
margins, and for some fragments, ferruginous "shirts".
The cement is porous, crystalline calcite, or slightly
calcareous.
The limestones of the Yagovkinsky horizon are
not very diverse. They are mainly biomorphic-detrital or
containing biomorphic fragments, less often algal limestones,
pelitomorphic limestones and limestones with porphyritic
secretions are noted.

Within the northern and northwestern parts of the
depression, north of the Atbasar and Shurumsai uplifts, a pack
of similar limestones with low-thickness layers of siltstones and
sandstones is widely distinguished in the section of the lower coal
deposits, which stands out as the Krasnoselskaya formation.
The sediments of the formation are usually well exposed,
forming a wide, often high ridge, well-defined in relief.
Decryption is usually good. On the contact seal, it
is uniquely identified as a white stripe of different widths.
Despite the fact that the formation is fragmentarily exposed
throughout the north of the depression and is exposed by numerous
wells, it is quite difficult to select a stratotypic section of the formation.
This is explained by the fact that in wells where the features of
the section are clearly visible, it is usually poorly characterized
faunistically. However, on the surface, where the sections are fairly
well characterized faunalistically, due to poor exposure and tectonic
features, it is often not possible to study the section and the
characteristics of the boundaries with underlying and overlapping strata.
