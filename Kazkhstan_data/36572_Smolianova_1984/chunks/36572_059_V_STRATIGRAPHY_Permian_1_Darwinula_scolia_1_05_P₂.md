From the deposits of the Kayraktinsk Formation in the studied territory, both by previous researchers and during the course of further study, spore-pollen complexes were determined.

From reference-parametric borehole 03, a spore-pollen complex [illegible] was identified, which is characterized by a predominance of pollen. Spores constitute an insignificant percentage within it. The most numerous and diverse are striate bisaccate pollen and vittatines. The majority of species have well-expressed ribbing; the number of ribs is small, they are flat and separated by narrow intervals. Such ribbing is characteristic of pollen of the Artinskian and Kungurianstages of the Middle Urals. Forms with a dense band on the distal side are encountered – *Striatodiplopi­nites pseudobulaeformis*. Similar forms are noted in the Kunguriandeposits of the Urals. Vittatines are represented by the species: *Vittatina striata*, *V. vittifer*, *V. subsaccata* –

characteristic of lower Permian deposits. Cordaitean [pollen] plays no role in the complex. Monosaccate pollen is absent. In the complex

Pollen predominates over spores. The spores encountered in the assemblage are transitional and are recorded in middle–upper Carboniferous deposits.

The background of the pollen portion of the spectra is represented by striate pollen; bisaccate and monosaccate pollen of cordaitaleans is noted. Pollen of *Entylissa* is present in almost all spectra; small oval pollen with a broad groove, identified as *Urmites*, characteristic of Artinskian deposits of the Middle Urals, is also noted here. Such an assemblage, in the opinion of A.I. Lopatina, may characterize deposits of the Late Carboniferous – Early Permian.

Here also, on the western slopes of the Urumseisky uplift and in the core of the Mendysky dome, during geological surveys at a scale of 1:50,000 (Smolyaninov, 1980), spore–pollen assemblages were identified from boreholes Л–4 and Л–5. In all spectra, striate pollen predominated; spores derived from the Carboniferous were rare. The age of the deposits, according to the conclusion of A.I. Lopatina, is most likely Early Permian.

The same survey work established a Permian age for the deposits of the Kayraktinsky Formation within the Aukelbek syncline. Spore–pollen assemblages determined by A.I. Lopatina from boreholes Л–93 and Л–109 (Table 27a) are represented mainly by pollen characteristic of Permian deposits, among which the most widely represented is striate pollen with air sacs: *Striatopodocarpites*, *Striatolebachioites*, *Striatohaplopinites*, *Striatodiplopinites*, and vittatins. In addition, pollen of *Azonaletes* plays a significant role in the spectra; pollen of cordaitaleans, ginkgoaleans (*Entylissa caperata*), and gymnosperm pollen *Circeliella marginata*, *Urmites* are encountered. All these species are characteristic of Permian deposits. The content of spores derived from the Carboniferous is insignificant: *Grassispora*, *Punctotisporites*, *Lycospora*, *Schoptipollenites*.

A Permian age was likewise determined for deposits of the Kayraktinsky Formation in the area of sheet М–42–ХУ (Smolyaninov, 1970, 1972), based on spore–pollen assemblages whose species composition was studied by N.I.
