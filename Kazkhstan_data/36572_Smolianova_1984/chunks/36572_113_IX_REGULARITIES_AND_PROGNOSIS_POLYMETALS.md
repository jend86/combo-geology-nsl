POLYMETALS

Polymetallic (zinc-lead) mineralization is noted in the Permian, Upper Carboniferous (Gersakan) and Upper Devonian (Samensky) stratigraphic levels.

1. Permian and Upper Carboniferous stratigraphic levels. Zinc-lead mineralization in the stratigraphic section is found either with lead - in the middle-Cretaceous and Gersakan levels, or is confined to the middle-Cretaceous and Upper Carboniferous subsurface, where it occurs as lead-polymetallic, zinc-lead occurrences and points of mineralization. The highest content overall was identified at the middle-Cretaceous level (sample in DZI - 0.65%) and Gersakan (sample in DZI - 0.4-0.72%). In the middle-Cretaceous and Upper Carboniferous subsurface, according to data from well D-1-4, mineralization with zinc and lead varies from 0.1 to 0.3%. Practical interest in polymetals is presented by middle-Cretaceous and Gersakan levels.

2. Upper Devonian (Samensky) stratigraphic level.

Carbonate formation of Samensky deposits (sulfide-bearing horizon) contains zinc and lead ore, localized in limestones and dolomites of the sulfide-bearing horizon. Primary signs are: presence of total ore swarms and zinc and lead veins; in the former distribution throughout the dolomite and lower part of the sulfide-bearing horizon. They, as a rule, coincide with carbon-bearing formations at the present surface. Zinc-lead mineralization is localized in zones of development of oxidized and limonite-impregnated formations near large structures of the western and north-western construction.
