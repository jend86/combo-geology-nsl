Glauconitic carbonate-glauconite pores are distributed in the Cretaceous strata in a subordinate manner. They form quite sparse interstices among alevrolites, which differ from them in whole descriptions. They are composed of glauconitic-carbonate material of approximately uniform distribution with the admixture of albite-sericite sand-aleurite grain size. This admixture constitutes from 5 to 20% and is represented by quartz, polycrystalline feldspar, mica minerals, circone, epidote. The forms of feldspar grains are typically corrosional.

Glauconitic-carbonate, carbonate glauconitic pores are very iron-rich. The content of iron hydroxides is non-uniform in the pore. In certain cases glauconite pores are overlain by asphalt bitumen.

The texture of glauconite pores is rarely microplastic. The microplasticity is characterized by a unidirectional orientation of glauconite, angular fragments or alternation of layers with a real granulometric composition.

Typical argilites among the pores of Cretaceous strata are rare. They practically do not contain carbonate, feldspar zeolites and are composed in a single-phase manner, weakly permeable by glauconitic material.

Carbonate and terrigenous-carbonate pores of Cretaceous strata are represented by microgranular cryptocrystalline sediments, "strata-morphic" sediments and detrital sediments.

Microgranular sediments are characteristic of lower suites. More rarely they are encountered at the edge of the basin. The structure of the pore is microgranular, equal-granular or unequal-granular. The admixture is rarely aleuritic material (up to 10%). Besides terrigenous admixture, organic residue is almost always present in the pore: oil films, oil coloring, filaments.

For the pores of Cretaceous strata, microcracking is characteristic. Cracks with a microlinear dimension of 1.2 mm, hardness of tens of millimeter.

Rare and such sediments large crystals of calcite in association with barium-celestite. Somewhat more frequently in certain sediments aragonite.
