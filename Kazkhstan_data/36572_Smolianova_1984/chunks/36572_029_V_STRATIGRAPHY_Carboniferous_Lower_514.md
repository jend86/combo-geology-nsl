As noted in the article, the deposits of the Rusakovsky horizon
are represented by a complex of terrigenous and carbonate rocks,
among which a number of lithological differences are distinguished.
Terrigenous rocks are represented by sandstones, siltstones, and
mudstones.

Sandstones and siltstones are
differences that are not widely distributed in the sediments
of the Rusak horizon. They are mainly found in the core
of wells, and in surface conditions they are subjected to
intensive weathering. The rocks are gray, dark gray, and white
when weathered, with massive and lenticular textures. Microscopic
examination shows that in the described group of rocks

A peculiar type of clay rocks, characteristic practically

Carbonate rocks are the most common type of rocks in
the Rusak horizon. In the overwhelming majority , they are
represented by limestones, in isolated cases by dolomites .

Spicule limestones are very widespread in the deposits of

In our opinion, the wide distribution of the Rusakov
horizon of spiculate rocks (argyalites and limestones) is of

the formation of peculiar weathering crusts characteristic of the
distribution areas of rocks of the Rusak horizon.

In the northwestern and northern parts of the depression
, a pack of terrigenous rocks lies at the base of marine Lower
carboniferous sediments, which attracted the attention of many researchers,
since a large Spasskove copper occurrence is associated with it.
           In 1951, when describing the Spasky ore occurrence, P. Sopko' •
(1952) separated this pack of rocks into a suite, naming it Spassky. He
considered the age of the retinue to be Lower Turanian.
           However, the Spasskaya Formation has not been
recognized as a valid local stratigraphic unit.
           The name Spasskaya suite appears in many reports on the
northern part of the depression, but it was used mainly by authors
conducting exploratory work. Thus, the Spasskaya formation (horizon)
was identified in the works of Ryzhov A.I. (1951), Khomchenko P.I.
(1977), Pashadsky V.A. (1980), who attributed the formation to the
Upper Devonian, Lower and Upper Turnian, respectively, Khomchenko
P.I., noted that the age of the formation may be Late Devonian,
and it is probably an analogue of the Dairin suite.
           Dmitrovsky Yu.V. (1969) during filming on a scale
of 1:50,000, a pack of rocks was assigned to the lower Tour.
           As a result of further study, we came to the conclusion that for the northern
part of the depression, this pack of rocks should be separated into a suite, which should
be called the Spasskaya. The stratigraphic section of the formation is considered to
be a section in the area of the village of Spassky along the Khabai River.
           The formation is distributed along the northern and north-western
sides of the Teniz formation, with its area of distribution in the south, along-

The deposits of the Ishim horizon come to the surface
within the Kipshak trough and the southwestern wing of the depression and
were uncovered by well 015 in the central part of the Kokpektin
uplift.

The exposure of the horizon is poor, and at the site
of its exits to the surface, there is usually a decrease in relief
with rare low ridges and strips of gravel, marl, and limestone
deposits.

Decryption is also poor (Fig. 17).

The continuous section of the sediments of the Ishim horizon on the surface
is nowhere completely exposed, therefore it is described fragmentally:
the lower part along the right bank, the upper along the left bank
of the Kipshak peninsula.

In the Kipshak trough, the Ishim horizon is
subdivided into two lithological layers : the lower one is
carbonate and the upper one is carbonate-terrigenous.

The lower boundary of the horizon is drawn along the roof
of a pack of single- shaped marls, the upper part of which contains
a mixed Late Jurassic-Early Visean fauna complex.

This boundary is uniquely determined along the entire area of the Kip-
Shak trough and the southwestern wing of the depression.
