In the first place, a chain of small structures distributed along the **Obelkolsk–Izvilobinskaya fault zone** should be noted. In the west is located the **Obelkolsk anticline**. Its axis has a latitudinal trend. The core is composed of granitoids, the limbs by Lower Carboniferous deposits, and here for the first time faunistically characterized deposits of the Simorinian and Kassinskiy horizons are identified. To the north and northwest on the studied territory they are not identified. To the east, the **East-Obelkolsk anticline** adjoins it, which is regarded by some researchers as a second-order brachyanticline.

The limbs of the Obalikul anticline are complicated by faults, near which the dip angles of the strata increase to 35°.

In the geophysical fields, the Obalikul anticline is recorded at the southern termination of a negative gravity anomaly that maps an intrusive granodioritic massif.

In the magnetic field above the anticline, a mosaic sign-alternating field ranging from -200 to +200 nT is observed. The Caledonian basement is composed of granite and diorite intrusions.

The North-Nabdar (Koschokinsky) anticline is located in an en-échelon arrangement to the east of the Obalikul. The fold is asymmetric, having a very gentle northern limb and a steeper, southward-dipping limb complicated by a flexure and a fault along it. As a result, the fold axis is shifted to the south; its strike is northeast, close to latitudinal. The fold core is composed of red-colored deposits of the lower Tersakkan sub-suite. Above the anticline, a positive gravity and a negative, low-intensity magnetic field are observed. The basement is composed predominantly of deposits of the Bretolubovskoye series. Separated from the Saryobinsky anticline located to the east is the **East-Nabdar (North-Nabdar) brachysyncline**, which is likewise asymmetric, but its asymmetry is the opposite of that of the Koschokinsky anticline. It has a gentle southern and a steeper northern limb; the axis is shifted accordingly toward the latter.

In the geophysical fields, the syncline is recorded by a sign-alternating magnetic field and a positive gravity anomaly. The basement is composed, predominantly, of deposits of the Bretolubovskoye series. The geophysical fields and the structure of the Caledonian basement above the Saryobinsky anticline are the same as those above the East-Nabdar syncline.

**The Saryobinsky anticline** is located to the east of the East-Nabdar brachysyncline. The principal fold axis is elongated in a northeast direction and represents an en-échelon continuation of the Kokpektinsky anticline and the Kokpektinsko-Gopektinsky uplift, which begins to the east of the Saryobinsky anticline. The fold has an oval outline; the limbs are complicated by faults, the most significant of which is a northwest-trending fault separating it from the Kokpektinsky anticline proper. The thickness of the Tersakkan suite in the core of the Saryobinsky, according to drilling data, considerably exceeds the thickness in the Kyzylobinskaya,

The rate of uplift decreases; the thickness of the Terekkanskoye Formation here is only 2.5–3 times less than the thickness at the southern limb of the depression.

The dome is symmetrical: the dip of the southern and northern flanks is 7–10°, and the western and eastern flanks 3–5°. The faults cutting the dome create a radial system.

A **saddle** separating the Pritengiz and Mailikul domes has a complex structure. A dense network of faults of longitudinal orientation has developed on it. Both domes are the youngest folds in terms of time of formation. Their formation began only in the Upper Permian, as evidenced by the analysis of thicknesses and facies.

**East-Shabdar anticline** is separated from the Shabdar anticline by the Shabdar fault, which is apparently one of the faults of the Tasty-Tersakan-Kipchak zone. The fold core is composed of Lower Visean limestones, and the limbs of Lower Carboniferous marine formations of the regression period. The fold axis is oriented concordantly with the fault in the northwest direction. In the physical fields above the anticline, a magnetic field of elevated intensity and a negative gravity field are observed. The character of the structures to the south of the Kipchak flexure is somewhat different from that in the central parts of the Teniz depression. The majority of researchers identify here a structure referred to as the Kipchak trough. The Kirei thrust is considered the southern boundary of the Kipchak trough, while discussions continue regarding the location of its northern boundary. On the studied territory, the most probable northern boundary of this trough is the Mialinsk-Kopkazgen fault of northeast strike. It is precisely in the belt between the Kirei thrust and the Kopkazgen fault that the maximum thicknesses of the lower sequence of the sub-platform cover are observed. The structure of this sequence on the southwest limb of the Teniz depression is quite complex. Here, a series of synclinal and anticlinal structures of northwest strike is identified.
