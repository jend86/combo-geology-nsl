The field assignment of the West-Teniz Field Survey Party for 1980 provided for the compilation of a field geological map at scale 1:200,000 covering sheets M-42-XIV (partial), M-42-XV; M-42-VII (partial). Execution of preceding profile geophysical investigations across the entire work area.

The West-Teniz Field Survey Party in 1980 conducted geological re-study at scale 1:200,000 (6,825 sq. km), study of middle and upper Paleozoic sections in natural outcrops, as well as profile geophysical work: gravity survey 741 lin. km, magnetic survey 741 lin. km.

Having reviewed the submitted materials, the commission notes:

1. Field geological work was carried out methodically and correctly; the materials obtained are characterized by high quality.

2. Core from reference boreholes of third-party organizations has been reviewed and carefully documented.

3. Extensive work was carried out jointly with paleontologists of the expedition on the compilation of composite sections of Upper Devonian, Lower and Middle Carboniferous deposits in natural outcrops with layer-by-layer fauna sampling. A fauna collection has been compiled.

4. Detailed entries in field diaries and borehole documentation logs are accompanied by descriptions and sketches of individual, most interesting outcrops and samples.

**The Commission Recommends:**

1. In view of the fact that a large number of wells have been drilled in the area of the Aksui dome (sheet M-42-54) by third-party organizations, the documentation for which is available in the party records, relocate the projected profile of structural exploration wells in this area to the southeast of Lake Tenis.

2. In future work, study the facies variability of Upper Paleozoic deposits, for which purpose carry out a series of traverses along the strike of structures in the area of sheets M-42-54, M-42-66.

3. In view of the fact that the stratotypic section of the Pontikul Suite is located in an area of poor outcrop exposure and is not accessible for repeated observation, study the section of Upper Paleozoic deposits of the Ashili syncline with the aim of identifying a parastratotype of this suite.

4. Construct structural-tectonic profiles using geophysical survey materials, including seismic data (where possible).

5. Collect glauconite samples from Lower Carboniferous rocks for absolute age determination.

**The Commission Resolves:**

1. Consider the targeted assignment of the party for 1980 to be completed.
2. Accept the field materials of the West-Teniz PSP with a rating of **"Excellent"**.
