**I. Target purpose of work, spatial
boundaries of the object, principal
evaluation parameters**

Geological re-study of the area of the indicated sheets in the Teniz
depression with the purpose of studying Upper Paleozoic deposits and
determining their prospects for the discovery of copper deposits.
Total area of re-study: 37,720 km².

**45**

---

**II. Geological Objectives, Sequence and Methods of Their Solution.**

**1.** Compilation of a geological map at a scale of 1:200,000 meeting current requirements and representations, for which it is necessary to:

a) carry out detailed stratigraphic subdivision of Upper Paleozoic deposits;

b) using facies analysis, perform correlation of cross-sections of Upper Paleozoic deposits within major structures, tying them to reference sections;

c) study the variation in composition of Upper Paleozoic sequences depending on their position within various structures;

d) study or assess the process of formation of the Upper Paleozoic cover in dependence on the basement structure;

e) conduct new collections of paleontological remains for refinement of the age of geological formations;

f) study the position of cupriferous sandstones within the stratigraphic section;

g) analyze the lithologo-facies and structural regularities of the formation of cupriferous sandstones for the development of forecast characteristics.

The stated objectives will be addressed by means of geological traverses, well drilling in combination with various types of sampling and chemical-analytical work.

---

**III. Expected Results and Timeframe for Completion of Work.**

As a result of the work, a geological map meeting current requirements and a copper forecast map will be compiled.

The final report will be submitted in the IV quarter of 1983, defended at the NTS of TsKTGU and distributed to VGF, RGF, TsKTGU, SKtGU, VSEGEI.

The following graphic appendices will be attached to the final report:

1. Geological map at scale 1:200,000, combined with a mineral resources map.
2. Forecast map at scale 1:200,000.
3. Correlation diagrams of Upper Paleozoic cross-sections.
4. Factual data maps at scale 1:200,000.
5. Tectonic scheme at scale 1:500,000.
