**Borissovskaya Group of Deposits**
Ore bodies (# 37,40) are located on sheet M-42-4, in 1.5-3.5 km to the northeast of S. Borisovka. They consist of two ore deposits situated 5.9 km apart. The first deposit # 37 was investigated by E.V. Sonovym in 1959 and reexamined by A.N. Ryabukhm in 1949-1950. The second deposit # 40 was discovered by the Atbassarskaya party (Dmitrovsky, 1974) during geological-geochemical surveys on a scale of 1:50,000. The Borissovskaya ore field is positioned along the Upper Cretaceous-Paleocene belt.

In structural relation, the sections are confined to the northwest-southwest trending Mariinskii synclinorium, where strata dip westward at angles of 4-6°.

The ore deposit (# 37) is confined to a duo of gently sloping layers of gray clay rocks. Among them, four limonite-bearing ore bodies are observed. By extent they are distributed over 300-700m, with possible copper content of 0.2-1.3%. The copper content in the oxidized ores is 1-3.9%.

With the aim of deeper study of deposit # 37 in 1969 (Dmitrovsky, 1970), test boreholes 1-60, 1-134 were drilled to depths of 220 and 301 m. Their corresponding copper content in the ores at depths of 128 and 223 m with median copper mineralization of 1.4 and 0.8% with a copper content: per spectral analysis U₁² and U₁⁶³. Deposit # 40 is situated 3.5 km south of the aforementioned (57). Here, ore mineralization in two clay rocks gray sandstone layers contains medium mineralization concentrated in sandy lenses extending over 600 m. This lense is closed by a gentle ore horizon. It is situated on two plates. The ore body in name is conglomeratic, and in the upper part is clastic. Mineralization is concentrated only in the clay rocks.
