The variegated sediments of the Kiyma Formation are characterized by wide distribution. They make up numerous sedimentary troughs and synclinal folds of higher orders. The formation section throughout the studied area is uniform: it consists predominantly of red-bed facies. The proportion of grey-colored rocks in the section amounts to 10–20%, and only for the lower Kiyma subformation does it increase in individual structures to 50%.

The main part of the copper occurrences of this level is confined to the Algabas, Kiyma, and Uakares synclinal troughs, where the Kiyma Formation is represented by an alternation of red-bed and grey-colored sandstones, siltstones, and limestones. Copper mineralization is confined to sandstones (ore occurrences of Uakarasu, Kiyma, Altynkazgan) and siltstones (Altynkazgan). A large number of mineralization points and secondary copper dispersion halos are localized in pelitomorphic limestones. The most abundant sulfide mineralization is recorded in sandstones enriched with organic remains. Ore bodies have a lens-shaped form and rapidly pinch out—

The mineralogical composition of the primary ores is, in general, uniform — it is chalcopyrite; pyrite, bornite, galena, fahlore (tetrahedrite/tennantite), and covellite occur less frequently.

Chalcopyrite is most commonly encountered in polished sections and constitutes approximately 1–5% of their area. It forms irregularly shaped inclusions, 0.004–0.26 mm in size. Chalcopyrite inclusions are distributed unevenly within the rock. They are noted in the form of a disseminated mass, discontinuous veinlets, and stringers irregular in width. Occasionally, chalcopyrite inclusions are confined to carbonized remnants. Rarely, it is noted in intergrowth with galena, bornite, and is replaced by covellite and bornite. In places, chalcopyrite partially heals fractures in the rock.

Pyrite is established in the form of very rare, irregularly shaped inclusions up to 0.064 mm in size, noted predominantly in intergrowth with chalcopyrite.

Bornite forms rare, irregularly shaped inclusions up to 0.25 mm in size, developing mainly after chalcopyrite. Occasionally, thin bornite inclusions are confined to carbonized plant remnants.

Galena occurs in isolated grains less than 0.03 mm in size in association with chalcopyrite and fahlore.

Fahlore forms rare, irregularly shaped inclusions ranging in size from 0.004 to 0.13 mm. It occurs in intergrowth with chalcopyrite and locally forms rims around chalcopyrite grains.

Covellite is observed in the form of thin platelets up to 0.16 mm in size. It forms thin marginal rims 0.008 to 0.018 mm wide around chalcopyrite segregations, partially filling fractures within it. It is also noted in bornite developing after chalcopyrite.

**The Ushkarasu copper occurrence** (Nos. 22, 23, 25) is located in the basin of the Ushkarasu River, on the area of sheet M-42-VII. It is composed of a sedimentary complex of the Nairaktinsky and Kiymin formations — red-colored and gray-colored sandstones, siltstones, and limestones. In structural terms, the ore occurrence is situated at the southwestern closure of the Ushkarasuy syncline. The eastern limb of the structure is complicated by a flexure. The rocks here have a submeridional strike and a general westward dip at angles of 6–10°, with sections up to 20°. On the western limb, rocks dip at angles of 2–3°; in the central and southern parts, the bedding is close to horizontal.

As a result of work by A.D. Gostev (1970–1971), ten small lenses with copper mineralization, separated from one another by distances of up to 5 km, were identified within the occurrence area. The mineralization is exposed at the surface by small quarries and is localized in gray-colored sandstones enriched with plant remains. The extent of the mineralized gray-colored sandstones exposed by quarries is 150 m (No. 25), 250–300 m (No. 23), and 500 m (No. 22), with a visible thickness of 20–30 m. Maximum copper contents reaching industrial concentration up to 4.5% are recorded in the oxidation zone exposed by ancient quarries. The oxidation zone is weakly developed, having an insignificant depth (in borehole G-8 — 105 m, in borehole G-1 — 23.0 m).

According to mineralogical analysis data from pan concentrates sampled from mineralized sections, chalcopyrite, chalcocite, bornite, covellite, and pyrite are observed together with malachite and azurite.
