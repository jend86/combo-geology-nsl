on the basis of Order No. 193 of 21 October 1981, conducted the acceptance of field materials of the Zapadno-Tenizskaya PSP.

The field assignment of the Zapadno-Tenizskaya PSP for the year 1981 provided for geological re-study at scale 1:200,000 on the area of map sheets M-42-II, II (western half), VII (eastern half), VII (western half), and the conduct of drilling operations on the territory of sheets M-42-XIV, XV.

The Zapadno-Tenizskaya PSP in 1981 carried out geological re-study of the above-mentioned map sheets; cross-sections of Lower Carboniferous and Upper Carboniferous deposits were studied from natural outcrops and borehole core, principally from third-party organizations; drilling operations were conducted. The volumes of completed work are summarized in the table.

The following materials were submitted for review by the commission:
1. Field geological maps on photoschemes at scale 1:100,000.
2. Maps of factual material on photoschemes at scale 1:100,000.
3. Correlation of Paleozoic cross-sections.
4. Geological cross-sections.
5. Field diaries.
6. Borehole documentation logs.
7. Geological columns for boreholes.
8. Well-logging diagrams.
9. Collections of rock samples and organic remains.

Having reviewed the submitted materials, the commission notes:

1. Field geological work was carried out using correct methodology; the materials obtained are characterized by high quality.

2. Field geological maps at scale 1:100,000 are drawn carefully and in detail. The level of detail frequently exceeds that of maps at scale 1:200,000.

3. Core from reference and prospecting-mapping boreholes of third-party organizations has been reviewed and carefully documented.

4. The shortfall in drilling volumes on the area of sheets N-42-51,52 is fully compensated by documentation of core from third-party organizations (reference borehole T-20 and prospecting-mapping boreholes of the Turgai Expedition).

5. Geophysical investigations were carried out in accordance with the project in all boreholes. Well-logging coverage is 100%; they were performed using a complex of methods. The applied complex of methods ensures lithological subdivision of the section along boreholes and identification of ore (sulfide) mineralization.

6. A shortcoming of the well-logging work is the absence of caliper logging in the complex, and the doubtful quality of the GTK-N diagrams in borehole 147 and GTK-S in borehole 146.

The Commission RECOMMENDS:

1. During office processing of materials and drafting of map layouts at scale 1:200,000, to differentiate fault dislocations by significance and, where possible, by depth of occurrence.

2. On the basis of study of the facies variability of Upper Paleozoic strata and structural peculiarities, it is necessary to attempt to identify–
