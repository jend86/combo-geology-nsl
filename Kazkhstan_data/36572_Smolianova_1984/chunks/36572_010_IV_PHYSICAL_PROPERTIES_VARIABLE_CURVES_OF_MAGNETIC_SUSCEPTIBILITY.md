The known magnetic clays have the same two peaks of density. Here, apparently, they are associated with known pelomite admixtures.

For sandy clays of the magnetic group, rough variation curves were constructed based on inclusion in the group of colored clay deposits. When constructing variation curves, density variation was observed at intervals of 0.05 g/cm³. Apparently, this is the minimum value of density variation that characterizes the nature of the field of one deposit.

Regarding magnetic susceptibility, here the division of breeds was carried out according to the methodology proposed by I.A. Berudokim: magnetic susceptibility from 0 to 100·10⁻⁶ ed.CGS—practically non-magnetic breeds; from 100 to 700—weakly magnetic; from 700 to 3000—magnetic; from 3000 to 12000—strongly magnetic; and above 12000·10⁻⁶ ed.CGS—very strongly magnetic breeds. Since variation curves were constructed for non-magnetic varieties, the grouping interval is taken through 10·10⁻⁶ ed.CGS.

The average value of physical parameters for each petrographic group was determined as the arithmetic mean from all samples. The results of the study of physical properties of the breed are presented in Table № 3 and presented in the form of variation curves (Figs. 3-6).

Besides, based on the determination of physical properties of mountain breeds, a diagram of the distribution of sorted density by size was constructed (Fig. 7). The average sorted density for each age group was calculated using the formula:

δ̄ ср.взв. = (h₁δ₁ + h₂δ₂ + ... + hₙδₙ)/H, where

h₁ — thickness of individual lithostratigraphic subdivision included in the group;
δ — density of lithostratigraphic subdivision;
H — total thickness of age group.

For individual stratigraphic subdivisions (D₁₋₂, D₂₋₃), the average density is calculated by regions.
