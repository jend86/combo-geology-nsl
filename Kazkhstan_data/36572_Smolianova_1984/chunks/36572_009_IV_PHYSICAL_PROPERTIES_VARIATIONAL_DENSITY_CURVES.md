CHAPTER IV. PHYSICAL PROPERTIES OF MOUNTAIN ROCKS AND METHODS FOR INTERPRETING GEOPHYSICAL ANOMALIES

1. Physical properties of mountain rocks

For meaningful and quantitative interpretation of geophysical anomalies, deciphering geophysical fields and linking them with specific complexes, mountain rocks must be assigned certain definitions of physical properties of intrusive rocks and sedimentary layers based on the results of preceding investigations.

To determine the physical properties of relations of upper Paleozoic Danubian-Tenisskian strata, laboratory investigation of 860 samples was undertaken, where samples selected from main horizons from oceans, samples relating to surface facies as indicated in significant sections and supported by measurements, which made it possible to obtain a general picture of the distribution of physical parameters on variable sections. For more complete representation of physical properties of all lithological varieties encountered in the region, Table 4 presents physical properties of samples obtained during work at scale 1:50,000 and during surveys. Density of mountain rocks was determined on a Samsonov densitometer. Error in control measurements was ± 0.015 g/cm³.

Magnetic susceptibility was measured with a JMS-2 device with measurement error around 10%.

Results of measurements of physical properties of mountain rocks are processed by methods of mathematical statistics (physical properties of mountain rocks and useful ores, Moscow "Nedra" 1976; geophysical search for ore deposits, Alma-Ata, 1970).

In the process of statistical processing of results of determining physical properties of rocks, one petrographic group or selection of combined one lithological composition was made. If the selection contained more than 25 samples, characteristic curves of density and magnetic susceptibility were drawn (figs. 3-6).
