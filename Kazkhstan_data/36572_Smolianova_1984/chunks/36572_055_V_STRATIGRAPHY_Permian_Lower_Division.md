Over the greater part of the studied territory, a grey-colored terrigenous-carbonate-argillaceous sequence of the Kayrakt Formation conformably overlies the deposits of the Torsakkansk Formation.

In the course of additional study, the section was re-described, collections of fauna, microfauna, and flora were made, and samples were taken for spore-pollen analysis. Throughout the entire studied territory, only along these rivers can the section of the Kairaktin suite be studied in natural outcrops. The section is undoubtedly a well-suited stratotypic section.

Deposits of the Kairaktin suite are distributed very widely across the studied territory: they form the limbs of nearly all the synclines identified within the depression, are exposed on the limbs of a number of anticlinal structures, and also form the cores of the Mailykul, Mendysh, Aryakpai, Taireksa, and Pritengiz domes.

The most significant outcrops of rocks of the Kairaktin suite at the surface occur within the area of sheet M-42-Ya, where they form a chain of domes on the Churumsa uplift and the limbs of the Aukelbekov and Boltykul synclines. The second equally significant area of development of the Kairaktin suite is the Ashilin syncline within the area of sheet M-42-XY and a series of structures in the interfluve of the Shebdar-Symtas within the territory of sheet M-42-XIV. Geological surveys at a scale of 1:50,000 have been carried out across all areas of widespread development of Kairaktin suite deposits, and the materials from these surveys were used in compiling geological maps at a scale of 1:200,000.

The outcrop quality of rocks of the Kairaktin suite over the greater part of the studied territory is satisfactory, except for the far west, within the area of sheet M-42-VII. Decipherability is good. As a rule, the rock mass of the Kairaktin suite is well identified on contact prints by the characteristic relief pattern. Throughout the suite, a ridge relief is characteristic, with a fairly regular alternation of ridges and inter-ridge depressions (Fig. 42). The ridges are composed of beds of limestones and sandstones; aeolian weathering of the rocks composing them is observed along their crests. The inter-ridge spaces are composed of siltstones, claystones, and less dense sandstones on a carbonate-argillaceous cement.

In the southern part of the studied territory, along strike, the lower and middle portions are replaced by the Teniz suite (sheet M-42-XU).

The Kayraktin suite is facially the most persistent of all Upper Paleozoic deposits. Over most of the studied territory it is composed exclusively of gray-colored siltstones, sandstones, argillaceous limestones, argillites, and marlstones. Only in the eastern half of the studied territory does it contain variegated rocks. Figure 43 presents a scheme of the distribution of various structural-facial zones with different types of suite cross-sections.

For the greater part of the area (Zone I), the cross-section studied along the Aymen and Maksy-Kayrakty rivers is characteristic, i.e., the stratotypic cross-section. Throughout this entire zone, exclusively gray coloration of the rocks is present; the lower boundary of the suite within it almost universally coincides with the establishment of stable gray coloration. The upper boundary is drawn at the appearance of red-colored interbeds within the otherwise continuous gray-colored cross-section, as well as at the appearance of coarser-grained, most commonly medium-grained sandstones in the overlying Kiimin suite. In this zone, both the upper and lower boundaries are apparently diachronous, since the lithological characteristics of the suite as a whole, as well as of the identified sub-suites, are persistent across the area.

The suite in this zone is divided into three sub-suites: lower Kayraktin, middle Kayraktin, and upper Kayraktin.

For the **lower Kayraktin sub-suite** (P₁kr₁), a terrigenous, carbonate-argillaceous-terrigenous composition is characteristic. In the sub-suite cross-section, fine- and very fine-grained gray-green sandstones predominate, with thin plant detritus along bedding planes, frequently with abundant dissemination of pyrite, sometimes chalcopyrite, pale covellite, and less commonly covelline. Wavy-laminated textures are frequently noted in the sandstones. Siltstones play a lesser role in the cross-section. Common, especially in the lower parts of the sub-suite, are interbeds of gray pelitomorphic argillaceous limestones with chalcopyrite dissemination. The limestone interbeds are thin. Their thickness most frequently does not exceed 0.3–0.5 m.

The **middle Kayraktin sub-suite** (P₁kr₂) has a more carbonate composition. The lower boundary of the sub-suite throughout the entire zone, as well as in Zones II and III, is drawn at the base of the first stromatolitic limestone in the cross-section.

The subswamp is characterized by a varied set of limestones in terms of textural and structural features. Widely developed here are clayey limestones of massive texture and horizontally bedded, breccia-like and breccia-like-shelly limestones, oolitic limestones, white marls, dense calcareous siltstones or silty limestones. In the cross-section of the subswamp, limestones occur in approximately equal proportions with siltstones and fine-grained sandstones. Oolitic limestones are frequently encountered in the roof of the package, which sometimes transition into peculiar stromatolitic limestones with inclusions of black chert nodules, or into fine-crystalline limestones with dendrites of manganese and nests of white crystalline calcite. The latter are most commonly developed at the transition to Zone II.
