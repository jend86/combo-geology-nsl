DANIAN STAGE OF THE SECOND TIER

The Danian stage is used in the Mangystau region for work in a wider distribution area, more clearly characterizing the first stage. According to residents and inhabitants of both upper Danian gas-bearing complexes and encountered below among prolific petroliferous and phosphorite-bearing strata. There is wide distribution of strata, among which north-eastern localities are distinguished. In the early description of the development, the picture is presented in the form of young protruding blocks with good raised ridges. The natural composition ranges from small to medium. Multiple relationships between the second tier localities, located in the Ustyurt Plateau massif, allow E.I. Serik to follow the following sequence of opening layers from older to younger and younger to older: 1) gritty porphyry, 2) granitoids, 3) dioritic porphyrites, 4) micromonzonites, 5) spessartites, 6) microdiorites and granodiorite porphyrites, relationships between which are established, 7) diorite spessartite-porphyries (Serik, 1963).

GRANITE-PORPHYRIES are reddish-gray, gray-and-light-red and light-pink-red rocks with microsparry main mass among which two equalities are distinguished: 1) with the content of corrugations up to 20-40% and 2) with the content of corrugations less than 5%. In corrugations appear useful shale and quartz, in part type absent biotite. Number of days from 1.5m to 5.0m.

GRANODIORITES differ from granite-porphyries in granitic-red (pinkish spheroidal) structure of main mass and, in parts, smaller whole [illegible]. Corrugations in granodiorites contain large tabular calcium plagioclase II, resin, common quartz and biotite. Granodiorites have no independent body, as in central parts of composite dikes.

ANORTHOSITE AND QUARTZITE ANORTHOSITIC PORPHYRITES - yellowish-gray and tonalite-colored rocks with micro-, thin-layered main mass. Grain-sizes (35-40%) are limited by plagioclase and common quartz. Number of dikes of colonette from 10-200m to 5-6m, and rarely varies by the breadth of the table.

GRANITOIDS AND MICROLITHIC SERIES - a series of breeds of various generations of granite composition with a large general body and only large serum. Grain size from 2 to 5 m.

MIARODIORITES - greenish-gray breeds practically not containing mica minerals, according to the classification of P.N. Serkh belonging to their analogs of plagioclase and leuctonite. Grain size of days ~ 0.2-0.5 m.

GRANODIORITE-PORPHYRY - reddish-gray and greenish-gray breeds with toned mammozerization of feldspar mass. Among the accompanying rock, plagioclas (biotites to 8-4 cm) is widely developed. The quantity of days of granodiorite-porphyry from 15-30 to 25 m. They show directional prostration: 50-90° and 270-310°.

NATROID SKYALIT-PORPHYRY - in the areas of studied territory of unknown. They are described P.N. Serkh northern. in the area of p. Sokh, Balkhashskii.
