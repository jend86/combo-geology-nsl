To the north of the Ailandinskaya syncline is situated the **T r e t y a k o v s k a y a s y n c l i n e**. It is isometric in plan. Its central part, as in the Ailandinskaya syncline, is composed of strata of the Kairaktin suite, having very gentle dip angles not exceeding 4–5°. According to seismic survey data, the thickness of the epi-Caledonian cover in the core of the syncline is 1.6–1.7 km, while the base of the second structural level is situated at a depth of 700 m. The latter fact is confirmed by drilling. Borehole K-3 intersects the base of the upper member of the Upper Tersakkan subformation at a depth of 628 m; consequently, the base of the suite may be at a depth of approximately 750 m.

To the east-southeast, the Tretyakovskaya syncline is separated from the Lvovskaya syncline by the Proletarsky saddle, and to the south by the Sovetsko-Proletarsky saddle from the Ailandinskaya syncline.

The north-eastern limb of the syncline is complicated by a flexure and longitudinal conformable faults; one of these hosts the Prirechnoye copper ore occurrence.

In gravity-magnetic fields, the Tretyakovskaya syncline is mapped by negative anomalies; the Caledonian basement of the syncline is represented by terrigenous Ordovician deposits.

**LVOVSKAYA SYNCLINE** is located between the Tretyakovskaya and Vladimировskaya synclines and is separated from the first by the Proletarsky saddle, and from the second by the Poltavsky saddle. The syncline is slightly elongated in the meridional direction; its core is composed of red-colored carbonate-terrigenous formation of the Klimin Suite, and the limbs by grey-colored carbonate-argillaceous formation of the Keyraktin Suite. Dip of strata on the limbs is 5–7°. Toward the central parts of the syncline, the dip angles flatten to 2–3°.

In the geophysical fields over the deposits of the Lvovskaya syncline, a negative gravity anomaly and a local increase in the intensity of the magnetic field are observed. A granodiorite intrusion is traced within the basement of the syncline.

**VLADIMІROVSKAYA SYNCLINE** is the last in the chain of synclinal structures along the north-western limb of the Teniz Depression. Its axis is oriented in the north-western direction. This orientation is controlled by the Zerende Fault, which runs along the north-eastern limb of the syncline, and by the Novgorodsky Fault, manifested as a flexure on the south-western limb of the syncline.

The syncline is not a uniform and consistent structure. It is subdivided into two parts: southern and northern. While the northern part of the syncline is elongated, as noted above, in the north-western direction, the southern part is slightly elongated in the sublatitudinal direction, conformably with the Atbasar Uplift. The most downwarped parts of the syncline are displaced in the northern part toward the Novgorodsky flexure, and in the southern part toward the Atbasar Uplift, although near the uplift the Upper Paleozoic sequences begin to pinch out rapidly.

Along the north-eastern limb of the Vladimиrovskaya syncline, a zone of nearshore-shallow marine deposits of the Lower Carboniferous is observed, with pinch-out of either the Spassk Suite, or the Dalnensky horizon and the Kypsak Suite, or reduction in thickness of the Krasnoselskaya Suite. Pinch-out of the Kirey Suite and the lower part of the Tersakkan Suite is also observed here. To the east and north-east, the southern part of the Vladimиrovskaya syncline passes into the isolated Konurskaya syncline.

From the southeast, the Vladimirovskoye trough is bounded by the **Marinovskoye brachyanticline**, in the core of which rocks of the Caledonian basement crop out, and which here, in the northeast of the depression, begins the chain of anticlines composing the Atbasarskoye uplift. This chain then passes into the anticlinal structures of the Nurumseyskoye uplift. Both uplifts together separate the previously described synclinal structures of the northwestern limb of the depression from its inner parts.

The **Marinovskoye**, **Toberkolskoye**, **Zhabayskoye**, and **Magdalinovskoye brachyanticlines**, forming the Atbasarskoye uplift, have a sublatitudinal orientation of fold axes and together form a system slightly elongated in the northeast direction. In the gravity field they are defined by positive anomalies of northeast strike. In the magnetic field — indistinct negative anomalies.

In the core portions of nearly all anticlinal structures, rocks of the Caledonian basement crop out; on the limbs — rocks of the sub-platform cover. The latter frequently exhibit reduced thicknesses near the crests and occasionally pinch out. Most commonly the Kireyskaya suite pinches out; frequently, especially on the lower limbs, it is replaced together with the Kipchakskoye suite facially by red-colored coarse-grained sandstones of the undivided lower–middle Carboniferous, with shell detritus in the lower part of the sequence. This indicates, apparently, intensive uplift of the Atbasarskoye uplift at the early–middle Carboniferous boundary. Dip angles on the fold limbs vary from 5–6° to 20–35°; the fold limbs are frequently complicated by faults. From the southwest, the Atbasarskoye uplift is bounded by the Zhamankayraktinskiy fault, along which the uplift zone is displaced to the south. The first structure identified in the system of the Nurumseyskoye uplift is the **Bunyirkolskoye anticline**, the axis of which is elongated in a sublatitudinal direction. The anticline is situated in a block between major faults of northwest strike: between the Vaksy-Talsayskoye fault to the west and the Vostochno-Bunyirkol'skiy fault to the east.

In the physical fields, a negative magnetic anomaly of intensity up to 200 nT and a positive gravity anomaly are mapped. The Caledonian basement is represented by a terrigenous-siliceous formation of the Ordovician and by deposits of the Bratolyubovskaya series. From the southeast, along the Maksy-Talsaisky fault, the **South Shunkirkol Anticline** adjoins the Shunkirkol anticline, the axis of which is elongated along the fault. If the core of the Shunkirkol anticline is composed of the Bratolyubovskaya series and Devonian rocks, then the core of the South Shunkirkol anticline is composed of the continental red-colored formation of the Tereakkanskaya suite, while the limbs are composed of the gray-colored and variegated carbonate-argillaceous formation of the Keiraktinsky suite. The fold has a box-like character: in its crestal parts the dip of beds is gentle at 3–4°, while on the limbs it is steeper at 10–15 to 20°. Its northeastern and southwestern limbs are complicated respectively by the Taldysaisky and Tairaksa-Taldysaisky flexures, which correspond in the basement to zones of deep faults. The South Shunkirkol anticline corresponds in the basement to an uplift, which is penetrated in its core by boreholes at depths of 100–200 m. On the limbs of the Shunkirkol and South Shunkirkol anticlines, rocks of marine formations are sometimes absent or are represented in significantly reduced thicknesses. Continental deposits of the Tereakkanskaya suite lie here directly on the basement. In the physical fields, the South Shunkirkol anticline is identified by a positive magnetic anomaly and a negative gravity field; the basement of the anticline is composed of an intrusive massif of granodiorites.

To the east and northeast of these two anticlinal structures, two synclinal structures are identified: the **Sipinsky Syncline** and the **Kolutonskaya Mulda** — more precisely, its northwestern limb. **Z h e b a y s k a y a b r a c h y a n t i c l i n e** – a large structure elongated in the sublatitudinal direction, located at the confluence of the Iman and Zhebay rivers. In the core of the structure, Caledonian folded formations crop out; the limbs are composed of Carboniferous system deposits. On the northern limb of the anticline, an unconformable dip of the Tersakkanskaya suite beds onto the Lower Carboniferous deposits and basement rocks is observed. Dip angles on the fold limbs do not exceed 10°; most commonly they are no more than 6°. From the north, northeast, and possibly from the south, the anticline is bounded by large fault dislocations. A transverse diagonal fault of northeast strike cuts the fold into two parts. The existence of faults from the north and northeast is established by geological data and interpretation of aerial photographic materials. From the south, the presence of a fault is confirmed by geophysical data; specifically, along the southern limb of the anticline, the Novoaleksandrovskaya fault zone has been identified in the basement depression based on the character of the magnetic and gravity fields.

In the physical fields, the Zhebayskaya anticline is expressed by positive gravity values and negative magnetic anomalies of sublatitudinal strike.

The axis of the Burumsaiskoye uplift is oriented in the northeast direction and coincides with the general orientation of the Niiminsk group of troughs. At the surface, the Burumsaiskoye uplift is mapped as a chain of domal structures (northeast to southwest): Burumsaissk dome, Eastern or Shortembaissk dome, Artykpaissk dome, and Mendybassk dome. In physical fields, the Burumsaissk dome is delineated by a negative gravimagnetic anomaly. The basement of the dome is composed of a terrigenous-sedimentary sequence of the Ordovician. The Shortembaissk dome is mapped by a gradient zone of the gravimetric field and a positive magnetic anomaly. The Artykpaissk dome is confined to a gradient gravimagnetic zone. The Mendybassk dome is mapped by a negative magnetic anomaly and a positive gravimetric one. The basement is composed of deposits of the Bratolyubovsk series. The cores of all domal structures are composed of rocks of the Kairaktiinsk suite, and the limbs — of rocks of the Kiiminsk and Shoptykul suites. The dip of strata on the limbs does not exceed 5–6°, and in the axial parts 2–3°.

**D o m e K e n a c h a t** is located in the southwestern corner of sheet M-42-Ya. The name is retained from earlier usage, although this anticlinal structure is not strictly a dome structure. Most likely it is an anticlinal structure of the structural-nose type, adjoining the Obalykol'skaya anticline to the south and separating the Ushkarasuiskaya syncline from the Symtasskaya. The anticline is composed in its core of rocks of the Kairaktinian suite, and on its limbs — by deposits of the Kiiminskian and Shoptykul'skian suites. The plunge of the anticline is in the northwest direction; its southeastern extension of the fold is hidden beneath the thick cover of Cenozoic deposits. The southwestern limb of the fold is steeper than the northeastern one. The dip of strata on it is at an angle of 12–15°, up to 20°, while on the northeastern limb it does not exceed 4–5°. In the magnetic field over the dome a positive magnetic anomaly of intensity 50–100 nT is recorded, and in the gravity field — a gradient zone. The Caledonian basement of the dome is composed of an intrusive massif underlain, apparently, by deposits of the Proterozoic group.

To the southeast of the Ienachat dome and to the south of the Mendinsky dome lies the **Symtassk trough**. It is located at the boundary of sheets M-42-YU and M-42-XIV. The trough has relatively simple outlines of the southern limb and more complex outlines with crenulated protrusions of the northern limb. Its form approximates an isometric shape with a weakly expressed migration of the fold axis. The core is composed of rocks of the Kiiminian suite, and the limbs by members of the Kairaktinian suite. In this structure, a well-expressed facies replacement of the predominantly red-colored lower Kiiminian sub-suite by a gray-colored sequence is observed, which in the Ashalinian trough cannot be separated from the Kairaktinian. The Symtassk and Belesaisky troughs are separated by the Belesaisky–Symtassk saddle; to the northeast, the Symtassk trough transitions into the Aukelbek trough, and at their boundary there is, apparently, an insignificant step along the so-called Tersakkano fault in the basement of the depression, which in the structure of the platform cover manifests itself only as such steps even without complicating flexures, or rotations of structures along it. In the gravity field, the Symtassk and Bimsaisky troughs are delineated by the western gradient zone of a positive anomaly **Δg**, which maps the block uplift of the Caledonian basement. In the magnetic field, the Symtassk trough is delineated by low-contrast sign-variable anomalies.

The Caledonian basement of the troughs is composed predominantly of volcanogenic-terrigenous deposits of the Bratolubovskoye series.

To the south of the Ienachat dome, as well as of the Symtassk and Aukelbekskoye troughs, lies a zone of more intensive dislocations: here the number of folds increases sharply, but their dimensions decrease; both flexures and extended fault disruptions are most clearly manifested.
