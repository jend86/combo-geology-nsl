As noted above, the deposits of the classical horizon are composed of a complex of terrigenous and carbonate breeds. Terrigenous breeds are represented in the main sandstones.

Peochanniks, as a rule, light-gray, sometimes "milky" appearance (in vein sequences). From fine-grained (0.08-0.25mm) to coarse-grained (more than 1mm). Usually unequal in composition of mixed granulometric composition (small-

The degree of cementation of the seams is varied, often it, according to available data, increases to the west from the incoming edge of the Shipak trough. Okatannost is also varied, usually not higher than medium. Often the degree of okatannost is associated with the detrital genetically-regenerational forms. According to the composition of cementing seams, two large varieties can be distinguished: fine-grained and coarse-grained sandstones with predominantly uniform grain content of quartz and feldspar and properly coarse sandstones, in which quartz content can significantly exceed 50% of cement seams. Lito-dike cementing seams compose more or less substantial fraction (up to 15%) of quartz and feldspar cements. Among other examples noted are: okatyannye biopelites, rudaceous seams, chlorite, chert, hydroglauconite (?). The character of cementation and cement composition varies within the limits of one object. Prevails: cementation wadding, regeneration, intergranular and penetrative. According to composition cement is glinisto-carbonate, glinisto-silicate, glinisto-oledinistic, cleanly carbonate. Characteristic wide development of duolocal carbonate-dolomite cement, in which calcite fills pores (sometimes up to peneolithic cementation), and dolomite forms fine chemical incrusts in these pores or in pores filled with clay matter. Thus rare type encountered is glinisto-anhydritic cement.

Spicular limestones noted in the core of borehole 06. These are, typically, light-colored, finely-grained, under the microscope zeolitic porous. Dimensions of spicules 0.04-0.08 mm in cross-section and up to 0.8mm in length. The majority of the spicule fauna is replaced (chiefly monocrystalline calcite), but numerous are filled with primary cement. Frequently in the spicules there is a central canal. The cement is porous, crystalline calcite. A characteristic uneven burial of loosely packed spicules and their position relative to calcite (signs of replacement concurrent with cementation). Individual pores are filled with crystalline cement.

Dolomite limestone, described in the pores of the Cassino horizon from boreholes 15 and 150. These, in general, are fine-grained or biodetrital limestone deposits, which contain fairly large (up to 1cm) fragments of fine pelitic-morph calcite with previously visible structure features (samples 15-749.0; 150-220.0). Small cavities in these samples appear as thin channels, surrounded in cross-section. Filled with calcite of crystalline light color. In biodetrital limestones secondary calcite occurs in fracture fillings and in shell replacements.
