2. Methodology of interpretation of geographical data

The interpretation of geographical materials was based on representations of the geological structure of the region, differentiation of rock-forming complexes by area and magnetic susceptibility, data on seismic reflecting boundaries, as well as information on the relationship of physical fields with structural-tectonic elements.

Geological interpretation of geographical anomalies bore both qualitative and quantitative character.

Quantitative calculations were conducted with the aim of studying the deep structure of structural elements of the foundation (sampling of samples along profile ΔQ), and also for studying the representation of deeply buried objects (quantitative interpretation of local gravitational anomalies).

In the process of field work, the results of generalization of seismic materials from the report of the Kzyl-Orda party for 1960-1983 were used, materials on theme 306 TsKNZ KASIMCA for 1983, published maps of scale 1:500000-1:50000.

For studying the deep structure of the region, materials from profile geophysical work performed by the central party of UGP were used.

In structural-tectonic relation, the studied area refers mainly to the Teniz depression, which is bounded to the north by the Sarabu-Teniz uplift, to the south - by the Kocheteuskii brachyanticline.

Interpretation of geological-geophysical materials was conducted in three successive stages:

1. Assessment of the scope of work in the regional plan.
2. Qualitative interpretation of geophysical materials.
3. Quantitative interpretation of geophysical materials.

In the first stage, intercompartmental geological and geophysical maps were studied. In the Teniz depression, the character of gravitational and especially magnetic fields is substantially related to the composition of the foundation, and in connection with this the extension of gravitational and magnetic anomalies represents the extension of folded structures of the foundation.

For the northern part of the area of geological investigation, a characteristic north-southwest trending anomaly was established, for the eastern - a southwestern, for the central - a north-northeastern.

The large amount of geological information about Upper Paleozoic sediments, insufficient for geological interpretation of deep horizons, complicates the interpretation of geophysical fields.

As a result of qualitative interpretation of geophysical anomalies, several regional areas were identified, which showed gradients of force and dynamic character changes in the physical field. The steep gradients of the gravitational field noted the sharply differentiated boundaries of fault-block complexes along the line, which coincide in the main with the zones of deep blocks. Deep blocks were identified mainly by the anomaly type of "gravitational step," which is also shown on maps as zones of increased gradients. The deep blocks in the examined territory are covered by long-distance lateral offsets and in the structures of the upper tier, as blocks, are not fixed, but appear as folds in the development of structures.

In the Caledonides of the Teniz Depression, according to geophysical data, deep blocks of northeast, northwest, east-west, and submeridional extension are fixed.

Smaller blocks were identified in accordance with the location of anomalies, according to the character of the field.

Analysis of physical properties of rock samples from deep boreholes demonstrates that the largest maximum values of rock density surround the block-like uplifts of the Paleozoic foundation. Maximum values of rock density manifest relationships of zeolitic and breccia series, with an average density of 2.7 g/cm³.

Concerning the dense boundary coinciding with the edge along the indicated series, the nearest boundary in this area was mentioned above.
