In assessing the prospects for copper exploration in the variegated formation of the Spassk suite, it should be noted that the copper-bearing capacity of the rocks of this suite has been insufficiently studied. To date, they have been studied only by isolated boreholes at depth, although ore bodies with industrial copper contents have been established in the Spassk Formation sequences. In this connection, the copper-bearing potential of this stratigraphic level requires further study.

**Ore occurrence Spasskoye No. 33** is located on the left bank of the Kabai River, 500 m to the east of the village of Spasskoye. It was discovered by V.M. Popov and V.A. Shlyagin in 1929. Prospecting-evaluation work on the ore occurrence was carried out in 1940 (Bogamazov, 1940). Seven prospecting boreholes were drilled and a geological map at a scale of 1:2000 was compiled. As a result of this work, the ore occurrence did not receive a final evaluation. In 1949–50, evaluation work was continued by A.M. Rykov. Trenches and prospecting boreholes to a depth of 125.0 m were driven. As a result of the work carried out, A.M. Rykov concluded that the Spasskoye ore occurrence had low prospectivity. In 1969, I.A. Aktanov continued prospecting-evaluation work. On the flanks of the ore occurrence and along the dip of the productive member, 15 prospecting boreholes were drilled. No industrial copper contents were established, and the author concluded that this object was unpromising. In 1976–80, the Tselinograd GRE (Pavadsky, 1980) drilled three prospecting boreholes on the ore occurrence to depths of 123, 200 and 501 m. No ore intersections were identified.

The area of the ore occurrence is composed of sediments of the Spassk Formation. Structurally, the ore occurrence is located on the southern limb of the Labei Uplift, complicated by higher-order folds. The mineralization is confined to the north-western limb of a third-order synclinal fold, composed of red-colored and grey-colored sandstones and conglomerates. The mineralization is exposed by trenches, pits and boreholes. At the base

To the south of the Spasskoye ore occurrence, a profile of prospecting boreholes was drilled by the Tselinogradskaya Geological Exploration Expedition. Boreholes П-302, 303 intersected the Spassky horizon at various depths, but no copper mineralization was identified in the arkosic sandstones. The low productivity of the deposits of the Spassky suite, according to data from V.A. Pavadsky (1980), is conditioned by the predominance of red-colored rocks in its section. The low productivity of the grey-colored sandstones and conglomerates of the Spassky suite is confirmed by data from borehole No. 169, drilled by the Zapadno-Tenizskaya party 6 km to the northeast of the Spasskoye ore occurrence. In samples collected from all lithological varieties of rocks of the Spassky suite, spectral analysis shows that the copper content varies from 0.002 to 0.015%. The maximum copper content — 0.03% (interval 216.0–218.0 m) — was recorded in dark-grey silty sandstones.

According to data from work carried out in 1949–50, the copper reserves at the Spasskoye ore occurrence total 6469 t, which is of no practical interest.

The Middle Carboniferous (Kireysky) stratigraphic level is identified on the basis of the development of copper mineralization in the upper part of the Kipshak and Kireysky formations. Copper mineralization is confined to the deposits of the Kireysky formation at the boundary with the Tersakkansk and in the transition zone of the Kireysky formation deposits to Lower Carboniferous formations. The mineralization is associated with grey sandstones, siltstones, and limestones and is represented by disseminated impregnation. The copper-bearing character of the Kireysky formation is, possibly, due to its poor state of study, less well understood than the Tersakkan, and its prospects at the present time are not determined. According to our data, the Kireysky formation in its lithologo-facial characteristics is analogous to the Taskuduk formation, but is, possibly, not isochronous with it.

A.A. Arutyomov (1978, 1980) considers it a full analogue of the Taskuduk formation, with which in the Dzhezkazgan depression the large copper deposits of Saryoba, Itauze, Kipshakpai, and Karashomek are associated. Therefore he highly evaluates the prospects of the Kireysky formation in the Teniz depression. We consider that if the northern part of the studied territory has no prospects for copper exploration at this stratigraphic level, its south-western part at this level is poorly studied even at the Kenevsk occurrence.

Ore bodies of lens-shaped form extend along strike for the first hundreds of meters. The copper content in primary ores ranges from 0.1 to 0.4%, in oxidized ores — from 0.1 to 0.5%, rarely reaching 1.0%. Within the distribution of the Kireysky formation deposits, the following copper occurrences have been identified: Kireyskoe, Prirechnoye, Nabdarskoye, and Kipshak.

**Ore Occurrence Nabdar No. 4** is located on the right bank of the Nabdar stream (M-42-XIV), 7.8 km northwest of elevation mark 398.0 m. Prospecting work within the ore occurrence was carried out by the Tersakken party of PSE (Kolesnik, 1967–1969).

The ore occurrence is composed of deposits of the Kireysky formation and is confined to the closure of a third-order anticlinal fold. Dip angles of the rocks on the fold limbs are 17–20°. At the ore occurrence, trenches have exposed two horizons of cupriferous sandstones with oxidized copper mineralization. The cupriferous sandstones form ore bodies of lens-shaped form, concordant with the enclosing rocks.

**Upper Carboniferous (Tersakkan) Stratigraphic Level**

The Upper Carboniferous stratigraphic level is associated with the deposits of the Tersakkanskaya suite. They are widely distributed. The Tersakkanskaya suite, in terms of lithological-facies characteristics, thickness, and copper content, is not uniform across various parts of the area. In the northern and central parts of the described territory, the suite has a reduced thickness, whereas in the eastern and southern parts its thickness reaches maximum values.

The lithological composition of the suite, as well as its thickness, is variable. The ratio of grey-coloured to red-coloured rocks varies from 1:2 to 1:20. The structural characteristics, composition, texture, and spatial position of the sediments under consideration make it possible to assign them to alluvial, alluvial-lacustrine, diluvial, and deltaic facies. In the northern part of the explored territory, large copper occurrences and numerous mineralization points are confined to the upper Tersakkan sub-suite.

The absence of rich copper-ore deposits in the sediments of the Tersakkansk Suite is probably due to an insufficient quantity of copper entering the sedimentation area — firstly, and secondly, due to such conditions in the sedimentation area that led to the dispersion of copper throughout the entire rock sequence. Data from predecessors (126, 129) and results of work by the West-Teniz Party show that the sediments of the stratigraphic level under consideration do not contain significant concentrations of copper. The average copper content in them is at the level of clarke values.

According to data from V.S. Domarev (1962), sedimentational concentrations of copper can accumulate only through erosion of more ancient, copper-enriched rocks. Consequently, at the stratigraphic level under consideration, during the sedimentation stage, copper contents in the sediments were probably insignificant. During the diagenetic stage, plant remains played the leading role in copper mobilization, but they too are insufficient in the sediments of the Tersakkansk Suite. The accumulation of copper in the Tersakkansk Suite possibly also depended on climatic conditions. During the period of formation of the cupriferous sandstones, the climate was probably arid. It led to the enrichment of rocks with gypsum and calcite in the cement of the rocks. The latter are probably unfavorable for the accumulation of copper ores.

As indicated by A.I. Perelman (1962) and Yu.V. Bogdanov (1973), large concentrations of copper form under alkaline conditions in reducing environments, while under oxidizing conditions in an alkaline environment, regional dispersion of copper occurs. The latter is probably characteristic of the conditions of formation of the rocks of the Tersakkansk Suite. Analysis of geochemical materials showed that the background copper content in the rocks of the Teniz Depression is above clarke values according to Vinogradov, which, apparently, confirms the stated hypothesis.

A.A. Arustamov et al. (1980) correlate the sediments of the Tersakkansk (Vladimirovsky) and Dzhezkazgan suites, considering them to be similar. In the northern part of the studied territory, they apparently have greater similarity with the sediments of the Western Urals. This similarity was noted in its time by K.I. Satpaev (1954), studying the geology and metallogeny of the Atbasar-Tersakkansk ore district. Most recently, the same conclusion was reached by V.A. Peshadsky (1980).

Thus, the mineralization associated with the variegated formation of the Tersakkanian suite in the northern part of the investigated territory is evidently analogous to the Pre-Ural metallogenic type (Table No. 60). The deposits of the Tersakkanian suite, distributed in the southern part of the investigated area, are represented by coarse-grained sandstones and conglomerates with subordinate interbeds of siltstones. They form positive structures of high orders. The productivity of the Tersakkanian suite has been studied in detail here within the Kenen anticline. Rocks with rich copper mineralization, according to the data of A.D. Gostev (1969), have been established in the lower part of the section of the Tersakkanian suite. According to his data, the Kenen copper occurrence, confined to the deposits of the Tersakkanian suite, is localized in deltaic facies, which brings it closer by this characteristic to the well-studied Zhezkazgan deposit. The deposits of the Tersakkanian suite, distributed in the south of the investigated territory, by their stratigraphic position and lithological-facial characteristics are analogues of the Zhezkazgan suite. A certain similarity is also established in the tectonic position of the mineralization. The majority of ore bodies confined to the Tersakkanian suite, like the ore deposits in the Zhezkazgan suite, are localized on the limbs of anticlines. These data place the deposits of the variegated formation of the Tersakkanian suite in the south of the studied area in the category of prospective for copper exploration of the Zhezkazgan type, which should be taken into account when planning exploration work. The deposits of the Tersakkanian suite favorable for copper localization are distributed within the map sheets M-42-XIV and XV and to the east of them.
