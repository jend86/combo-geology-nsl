The territory on which the pear survey was conducted covers

According to administrative division, it belongs to the Tseliograd

Both the upper reaches and the mouths of these rivers are located in the studied territory.
There are extremely many lakes in the studied territory ,
and the lake basins are divided into three types: open lakes,
dried- up lakes, and old lakes.
Of the lakes of the open type, the most famous
is the bitter- salty lake Tengiz. Two significant
and approximately equal-sized lakes Kirey and Kilshak with rivers of
the same name are located nearby, these lakes are more saline,
completely dry out in dry years, and white salt deposits form
on the surface of the lake vats. The rest of the lakes have
brackish waters and sizes not exceeding 3-4 meters across. They
are overgrown with reeds and kamish.
The dried-up lakes are closed, flat basins, covered with
dense, sharply defined green, red, grassy vegetation, the
largest of which are Mailikol, Konen, Shoptykul in the south
of the district, in the north - the number of such lakes
is extremely large, they most often do not have their own
formations.
The climate of the described area is sharply continental, with hot, dry
summers and rather harsh winters. The average temperature in March is
+240, in January -140, in winter the temperature often drops to -40
°, in summer it exceeds +30 °. There are sharp diurnal fluctuations in
temperature. Precipitation is low, especially in summer, from 270-300
mm in the south to 400-450 mm in the north of the region.
The studied territory extends from the semi-desert zone in the
Central Asian region to the forest-steppes in the north, in the north
vegetation is, respectively, characteristic of the forest-steppe
and steppe zones. The pine forests of Balkanino and Voznesenka
are glued here; numerous birch and aspen groves and mixed copses.
By the present day, forests are gradually disappearing, the middle
part is occupied by a forested steppe, vast areas of which are used
for arable land, hayfields and pastures, In the south vegetation typical
of dry steppes and semi-deserts; tipchak, kovyl, solyanka; only
along the rivers there are thickets of reeds and stunted couscous
