Authors: Smolyaninova E.M.,
Naumenko V.V. et al.

The West-Teniz Party of the Central Geological Prospecting Expedition of PGO "Tsentrkazgeologiya" in 1978–1984 conducted comprehensive geological-geophysical investigations, including gravimetric surveying along individual profiles over the area of sheets M-42-II, III, VII, VIII, IX, XIV, XV in a volume of 717.4 line-km at a spacing of 200 m.

A base gravimetric network was not established. Each profile was worked from an individual point, which served as the starting point for accounting for zero-point drift. Reduction of observations to a state reference level was not carried out. The survey was performed at a conventional level.

Routine gravimetric surveying was carried out with a single gravimeter using a single-pass method with motor transport. The duration of traverse legs did not exceed 2 hours.

Gravimeters GR-K2 were used in the work. Quality control was performed by independent observations and comprised 12% of the total work volume.

Topographic-geodetic work was carried out in accordance with the "Basic Regulations for Topographic-Geodetic Support of Geological Prospecting Operations," issued 1974, and the "Instructions for Gravimetric Prospecting," issued 1980, and consisted of laying out profiles using the theodolite traverse method at precision 1:500 with distance measurement by 50-metre wire in one direction. Elevations were obtained by engineering levelling.

The achieved accuracies were: base network ±0.08 mGal (design ±0.15 mGal), horizontal position ±8 m (design ±40 m), point elevations ±0.04 m (design ±0.70 m), gravity anomalies ±0.08 mGal (design ±0.20 mGal).

As a result of the work performed, maps of gravity anomalies in the Bouguer reduction at an intermediate layer density of 2.67 g/cm³ were compiled.

The following remarks apply to the submitted report:

**43** **16**

1. The necessary information on the calibration of the gravimeter used in the work is absent.

2. Contradictory data exist regarding the obtained errors in the report text, graphic appendices, and the control statement.

3. In the future, all profile observations must be referenced to the national level. The work area is covered by surveys at scales 1:50,000 – 1:200,000. Where it is impossible to tie to state control points of Class II, tying to points of previously completed surveys is permitted.

4. The design accuracy of the planned and elevational positioning of points is not indicated. Of the 14 theodolite traverses presented, 3 traverses (21%) are tied with inadmissible relative errors, and 4 traverses (28%) have an inadmissible traverse length; therefore, the root-mean-square error of planned position calculated from traverse misclosures does not correspond to the actual value. Nothing is stated regarding control observations. The root-mean-square error of coordinate and elevation determination of points is characterized by control observations in the volume of 5–10% (see § 45 of the Instructions on Gravity Prospecting, 1980).
