Carboniferous-terrigenous deposits, conventionally comparable
to the Uluthu series of the Vendian age, are distributed in the south
of the territory within the Kirei Vistup. They are exposed on two
neighboring but disconnected sites, the first of which is located in the
northwestern part of the core of the Kipshak-Tersakkan anticline (M-42-xU),
and the second is north of the city of Kuadyr. Within the studied
territory, there is an inconsistent recritification of the Vendian strata by
volcanites of the Lower-Middle Devonian and red-colored terrigenous
trapping of the Dairin formation of the Upper Devonian; unclear
contact with volcanites of the Western Cambrian age and, more
often, intrusive and tectonic contacts with granitoid massifs of the
Krykkuk complex of Late Ordovician age and the Borovsk complex of
Silurian-Early Devonian age, the outcrop of the described formations,
especially in the shale parts of the section, is weak. Decryption
is poor. The rocks were transformed by the processes of regional
metamorphosis of low ridges and, partially, contact metamorphosis
