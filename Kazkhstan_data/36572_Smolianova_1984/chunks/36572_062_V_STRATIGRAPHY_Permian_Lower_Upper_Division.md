**Lower – Upper Division**

**KIIMINSKAYA SUITE**

The Kiiminskaya suite, like the Kayraktinskaya suite, is a valid stratigraphic subdivision of the Teniz Depression, which was defined by N.V. Litvinovich in 1954. [illegible]. The name is derived from the village of Kiima, to the northwest of which, along the Zhaksy-Kayrakty River, the suite's section was first described. The territory of sheet M-42-II is considered the stratozone of the Kiiminskaya suite, where the deposits of the Kiiminskaya suite have the best exposure on the studied area.

In the stratozone, the suite section can be studied in sufficient detail from outcrops along the Zhaman-Kayrakty River and to the northwest of the village of Kiima, along the Zhaksy-Kayrakty River. Moreover, in the cliffs of the Zhaman-Kayrakty River, the character of the suite's deposits can be understood better, in our opinion, than in the stratotypic section, since the latter is represented mainly by alluvial debris, and only in places are the suite's deposits exposed in it by numerous quarries.

In recent years, many exploratory boreholes have been drilled in the Kiiminskaya syncline in the area of the stratotypic section, and the cores of most of them have been documented during further study. Suite sections from borehole cores are shown on drawing 49, sheets 1, 2.

Deposits of the Kiiminskaya suite are widely developed across the studied territory; they form either the axial parts or the limbs of all synclinal structures. In many structures the suite's deposits are isolated, which makes correlation of the suite's sections across the entire area of the depression difficult. The upper boundary of the suite is particularly difficult to correlate.

Deposits of the Kiiminskaya suite are well recognized on photographic materials by their smoothed and less pronounced ridge-relief forms, in comparison with the relief of the Kayraktinskaya suite. The deposits of the Kiyma Formation conformably overlie the deposits of the Kayrakty Formation and show very gradual transitions from one formation to the other. This is particularly well visible in the cliffs of the Zheman-Kayrakty River. Gradual transitions are no less evident in boreholes that penetrate this boundary, for example, in reference boreholes T-14B, 04 and in numerous exploration boreholes.

During geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000, different authors in different areas drew the lower boundary of the formation differently: some drew it at the persistent appearance of red-colored beds, others at their first occurrence, and therefore it was often drawn at different levels even on adjacent map sheets. Its sliding position in the section is apparently often associated with purely subjective factors: the methodology of route description, consideration or disregard of the one-sided exposure of the formation's beds, and the degree of detail of the route description.

During further study in all sections and across the entire territory of the stratozone, the formation boundary was drawn at the first appearance of red-colored beds within the continuous grey-colored section of the Kayrakty Formation. The lower part of the Kiyma Formation section, where the content of grey-colored beds was still significant, was assigned to the lower Kiyma Subformation (P₁₋₂ km₁), while the upper part of the formation section, where the beds were predominantly red-colored, was assigned to the upper Kiyma Subformation (P₁₋₂ km₂).

The upper boundary of the formation, especially in the stratozone, is very poorly expressed, since the overlying Shoptykul Formation is also red-colored. The upper boundary of the Kiyma Formation is taken as the appearance of "porphyritic" limestones at the base of the Shoptykul Formation. In our view, this boundary is more distinctive not in the stratozone, but in the Aukelbek and Achilino synclines.

We do not have full confidence in the isochronous nature of the upper boundary of the Kiyma Formation across the entire area.

Based on the character of the sections in the studied area, several structural-facies zones are identified, the locations of which are shown in Fig. 57.

The Kiiminsk Formation throughout the entire studied area is divided into two subformations: the lower Kiiminsk and the upper Kiiminsk.

**L o w e r K i i m i n s k S u b f o r m a t i o n (P₁₋₂ km,)** is characterized by a transitional character of the section between the Kairaktin Formation and the overlying upper Kiiminsk subformation. Its distinguishing feature is the equal or approximately equal content of grey-coloured and red-coloured rocks in the section. Apparently, for this reason, pervasive copper mineralization is characteristic of it.

The lower boundary of the subformation, as of the formation as a whole, is drawn in the stratozone (Zone II and part of Zone III) at the first appearance of red-coloured rocks above the continuous grey-coloured section of the Kairaktin Formation. In the Vladimirovskoye and Archalinskaya synclines, its lower boundary is also drawn at the first appearance of red-coloured rocks in the section.

In the Aukelbekskoye syncline, on its northern, eastern and southeastern limbs (Zone III, Fig. 57), the lower part of the upper Kairaktin subformation is red-coloured; therefore, the lower boundary of the lower Kiiminsk subformation is drawn here not at the first appearance of red-coloured rocks following the Kairaktin Formation, but at the second.

In the Achilinsk syncline (Zone IV in Fig. 57), the lower boundary of the lower Kiiminsk subformation is not defined at all in the section. In this structure, mapping the lower Kiiminsk subformation separately from the upper member of the upper Kairaktin subformation is not considered possible. Both of them in the Achilinsk syncline, being predominantly grey-coloured, as is clearly visible in the section along reference-parametric borehole 04 (Drawing 49, Sheet 3), contain interbeds of identical nodular limestones. These limestones are dense. At the surface, limestone beds form ridges, and therefore both members are interpreted together based on the character of the relief. Both rock members form a single pattern on aerial photographic materials.

On the geological maps proposed in the report, a rock package is distinguished in the Archilinsk syncline, combining the upper member of the upper Kayraktinsky sub-suite and the lower Kiyminsky sub-suite (P₁kr₃² + P₁₋₂km₁).

The upper boundary of the sub-suite has a more distinct lithological definition, as will be noted above.

The thickness of the sub-suite shows considerable variation from 95 m to 295 m (Table 32). This change in thickness is apparently explained by the instability of the lower boundary.

Within the sub-suite, fine-grained and medium-to-fine-grained lilac sandstones alternate with fine-grained lilac and greenish-grey sandstones and siltstones. In the upper part of the sub-suite, in some synclines, interbeds of dark-grey nodular limestones with calcite veinlets appear. Therefore, in some structures the lower Kiyminsky sub-suite is divided into two members:

P₁₋₂ km₁¹ – lower – essentially terrigenous,
P₁₋₂ km₁² – upper – with limestone interbeds.

Such subdivision of the sub-suite into members has been carried out on the south-eastern limbs of the Shoptykul and South Kiyminsky synclines, and in the Aukelbek and Symtas synclines.

In the Vladimirovsky syncline, such enrichment of the upper part of the lower Kiyminsky sub-suite with limestone interbeds does not occur. In the Archalinskaya syncline, limestones are present at the top of the sub-suite, but they are not nodular, rather they are clayey pelitomorphic, similar to those in the upper part of the Kayraktinsky suite, but a rock member enriched in limestones is not distinguished here.

**U p p e r K i y m i n s k y s u b - s u i t e** (P₁₋₂ km₂) differs from the lower Kiyminsky sub-suite by the stable red-coloured staining of the rocks, the presence of thick interbeds of lilac medium-grained, medium-to-fine-grained sandstones, the appearance of "crypto-porphyritic" limestones in the suite section, and the presence of "porphyritic" concretions and segregations in the upper half of the sub-suite.
