The Teminskaya anticline, within the boundaries of which exploratory drilling was conducted, is geologically well studied, although not completely: the north-eastern and south-western parts of the anticline—at a scale of 1:50000, the central and eastern parts—at a scale of 1:200000. This was evident from the attached cartographic materials. The work carried out on them did not indicate any new information.

The investigation of the Teminskaya anticline consists of disconnected historical reports. First information about the area refers to mid-nineteenth century and bears a strictly geographical character. The geological structure of the region was first studied by mining engineer N.A. Shipinskii, who participated in 1815 in the military expedition of General Lyubanov. The information obtained from him was published by G. Spasskim in 1820.

At the end of the XIX century a new stage of geological investigations of the eastern regions of Russia began, associated with the construction of the Trans-Siberian Railway. Geologists of the Caucasus-Siberian geological expedition, A.A. Bomerov and A.A. Krasnopolskii, were the first to compile geological maps of the region at a scale of 1:420000. As a result of these works, an important clarification was made regarding the fact that all productive oil and gas-bearing rocks of southeastern Kazakhstan belong to the Jurassic.

The following stage of field search and prospecting work in the region dates to the early years when the young Soviet Republic began to actively develop oil-bearing regions and to conduct intensive search activities. The region was first examined by geological expeditions headed by N.I. Kassin, I.S. Bykov, A.I. Pavkin, N.S. Smirnov, N.T. Moskovchenko, M.S. Tolstoguzov, B.A. Popov, N.S. Matskogo. Among the researchers of the structure of paleogeogeography and history of geological development of all Kazakhstan, P.I. Bykov provided a sketch-structure for the Teminskaya anticline. No reconnaissance work in the Kegn, Konchagan, Mugojsk and other areas conducted by leading researchers lost their relevance by the present time.

The following stage of planimetric geological survey and mapping began at the end of the 1940s. Study of the region was in three parallel directions: serious research and investigation work on the issues of oil and gas potential of the Torgai Depression; large collection of geological data by MPI and MU staff under the supervision of A.I. Popov; conducting planimetric geological survey in area 1:200000; and continuing and expanding investigations of medicinal mineral springs.

For the first time in solving problems in the region, geological methods were applied, recognizing the need for comprehensive study of questions, images, regional structural plan. With this goal, drilling work was conducted with emphasis on boreholes in deep sections.

For solving questions related to geological mapping, detailed technical work on stratigraphy, lithology of silicoclastic and paleontological associations of the central Kazakhstan was carried out.

In 1965, geological survey of the entire territory of the region was completed at 1:200000, and in 1965 a detailed map of this region was completed at 1:200000 scale across the entire surveyed territory. Using geological maps of the 1:200000 scale sheets K-42-I (Badygin B..., 1955), L-42-I (Badygin B..., 1955), K-42-IV (Abokhin N..., 1958), L-42-IV (Karpachev N..., 1964), K-42-VIII (Druzhinin A..., 1960), K-42-IX (Shakarov A..., 1959), K-42-XI (Vinnichenko O..., 1960), K-42-XIII (Nazarovich O..., 1962), K-42-XXXV (Badygin B..., 1963), K-42-XXXV (Kuznev N..., 1964) served as the basis for this report.

Questions of stratigraphy of the region in development stages during detailed reconnaissance work were addressed in numerous studies. In addition, questions were resolved regarding stratigraphy of upper and lower Paleocene sediments of individual stratum.

In the present report, on the basis of the scheme for subdividing paleozoic-neozoic deposits, we accept this scheme.

Stratigraphic subdivision of paleozoic-neozoic continental deposits has great significance in connection with their petroliferous potential. So, in explanatory notes and geological maps at scale 1:200000, issues of conformability of stratigraphic schemes according to the meridian, in our opinion, should receive increased attention, and in published works and reports of a later period, there are many examples of earlier researchers' reports drawn up according to the south-eastern and northern parts of the square and remaining more suitable for stratigraphic structures of individual researchers.
