Another important parameter characterizing the petrochemistry of the rocks of the Krykkuldunskiy complex is the Na₂O/K₂O ratio (wt. %). A diagram illustrating the SiO₂ vs. Na₂O/K₂O ratio is presented in Figure No. 79. Analysis of the diagram yields the following: a) all rocks of the Krykkuldunskiy complex, for which analyses were available to the authors, belong to the potassium-sodium series and are characterized by Na₂O/K₂O ratios in the range of 0.6–2.2; b) quite definite differences exist between the alkali ratios in rocks of different massifs. Thus, the greatest "sodicity" is characteristic of the granitoids of Ishimskaya Luka, composing the Boryakolskiy and Derzhavinskiy intrusions; the values of the Na₂O/K₂O ratios for rocks of these plutons lie in the interval 1.2–2.12. For rocks of the Karatasskiy and Bulandy-Akkol'skiy intrusions, on the contrary, relatively low values of this indicator are characteristic, falling in the interval 0.6–1.1. These differences in the alkali character of the rocks are apparently related to the different geological positions of the massifs and, most probably, depend on the composition of the enclosing strata. However, the available data do not permit a more detailed discussion of the resolution of this question.

The degree of aluminosity of the rocks is determined by the aluminosity coefficient al' = Al₂O₃ / (Fe₂O₃ + FeO + MgO). Analysis of Diagram No. 78 shows that all the studied rocks belong to the highly aluminous (1 < al' < 2) and very highly aluminous (2 < al' < 10) varieties, with the greater part having a coefficient al' in the interval 1–4. A certain increase in aluminosity is observed in the transition from rocks of intermediate composition to acidic ones. As in the case of the Na₂O/K₂O ratio, the value of al' is relatively higher for rocks of the Derzhavinskiy and Boryakolskiy massifs (2.5 < al' < 5.5) than for other plutons (Karatasskiy, Bulandy-Akkol'skiy: 1.1 < al' < 3.3).

The contents of other petrogenic oxides vary within fairly wide limits.

The principal distinction that should be noted is the higher values of the sum (Na₂O+K₂O) characteristic of the rocks of the described territory compared to the weighted-average values. It is noteworthy that the greatest similarity is established between the weighted-average values and the composition of rocks forming the massifs in the area of Mininskaya Luka (Borlikolsky, Derzhavinsk).

It is necessary to separately address the chemistry of rocks forming a series of small stocks and dikes within the Marinovskoe uplift and assigned by us to the undivided formations of the Krynkuduk complex. The compositions of these rocks are characterized by: a) high values of the sum Na₂O + K₂O (up to 8.84 wt. %), as a result of which all of them belong to the subalkaline series (Fig. 77), b) high values of the ratio Na₂O/K₂O (1.8–2.7), maintained independently of the SiO₂ content (Fig. 79). By alkalinity type, therefore, the described rocks differ both from the majority of formations of the Krynkuduk complex of the study area and from the published weighted-average compositions.

On the contrary, the composition of granitoids intersected by deep boreholes beneath the cover of Upper Paleozoic sediments (c-120/257; c-113/113.0; c-119/1.6.0; c-124) is extraordinarily close both to the composition of other rocks of the first and second phases of the Krynkuduk complex of the study area, and to the weighted-average values. This, together with other criteria (mineralogical composition, textural-structural features), is the principal criterion by which the described rocks are correlated with the formations of the Krynkuduk complex.

**Analysis of data indicating the age of the described intrusive formations**

**G e o l o g i c a l d a t a.** In the study area, the intrusions described are intruded by diverse Proterozoic and Lower Paleozoic sediments, the youngest of which are the formations of the Kubosandyrsk Formation of conditionally Lower Ordovician age, as well as the faunistically characterized deposits of the Akkayraktinsk Formation and the Nalimkolsk Series of Middle–Upper Ordovician age. On the other hand, rounded pebbles of the described intrusive rocks are known from conglomerates of the Lower–Middle and Middle–Upper Devonian.

**RADIOLOGICAL DATA.** In order to refine the age of the described formations, we collected 5 samples from granitoids of the second phase of the Krynkuduk complex (Sarsymbai massif, sample 7595) and from undifferentiated formations exposed by deep boreholes beneath the cover of Upper Paleozoic deposits of the Tenisskaya depression (samples C-113; C-119; C-124; C-120). Unfortunately, the samples cannot be considered satisfactory. The considerable scatter in age values is caused by the poor preservation of biotite, altered as a result of chloritization and ferruginization. The transformation of the rocks was apparently accompanied by significant leaching of potassium, along with which a disproportionate loss of argon occurred. As a consequence, the argon-to-potassium ratios are not an unconditional function of the elapsed time, and the quoted age figures should not be taken as the time of formation of the rock.

More reliable, apparently, are the absolute age figures cited by N.M. Prudkus and V.B. Taranin for the diorites and granodiorites of the Ishinskaya Luka area. According to these authors, the absolute age of the described formations is 460–440 Ma (Prudkus, 1968) and 460 Ma (V.B. Taranin).

**CORRELATION WITH ADJACENT REGIONS** is, apparently, the most convincing substantiation of the age of the described rocks. The validity of correlating them with the Krynkuduk complex of post-Ordovician age is currently accepted by the majority of researchers.
