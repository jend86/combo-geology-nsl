In the area of the Shabdar stream, on the ridge with the reference mark "Kairakti," from the upper part of the formation, slightly below the chert horizon, from greenish-gray sandstones, numerous casts of trunks and imprints of rachises and articulate-stemmed plants were collected. Of the latter, the following predominate: *Calamites suckowii* Brongh., *C. cistii* Brongh., *C. undulatus* Steinb., *Artisia approximata* (Brongh.) Corda. From the same observation points 7331 and 7136/3, from the upper part of the formation, *Artisia approximata* (Brongh.) Corda was also collected,

*Paracalamites* sp. (possibly *P.* sp. A, described from analogs of the Tentekskaya Formation of the Ekibastuz Basin) — a trunk section with silicified [illegible], *Calamites cistii* Brongh., *C. suckowii* Brongh.

According to the conclusion of L.A. Goganova, who identified the flora, the lower part of the Kirei Formation belongs to the Bashkirian Stage and is correlated with the deposits of the Dolinskaya Formation of the Karaganda Basin and the Karabaldinskaya Formation of the Ekibastuz Basin. The upper part of the formation, characterized by plants: *Lepidodendron* sp., *Calamites suckowii* Brongh., *C. cistii* Brongh., *Paracalamites* sp., *P.* sp. A, *Artisia approximata* (Brongh.) Corda — is correlated with the Akbidinskaya Formation of Ekibastuz, the Tentekskaya Formation (above T₅) of Karaganda, and belongs to the Moscovian Stage.

In the Kishsak section from borehole C-21 at a depth of 106 m, from the upper half of the suite, the following species of pollen and spores were identified: *Lycospora subtriquetra* Lub., *Vallatisporites ciliaris* Lub., *Remisporites pailopterus* Lub., *Calamospora microrugosa* (Jbr.) S.W.B., *Angulisporites punctulosus* Lub., *Gramisporites testiculatus* Lub., *Granulatisporites p. rofornis* Loose, *Cordaitina grandireticulata* Schat., *Diversisaccates macropterus* Lub., *Pinites* sp., *Azonaletes* sp. Forms typical of the Middle Carboniferous are noted in the spectrum. Approximately 30% of the total composition falls on pollen of cordaites and conifers. Pollen of *Pinites* sp. has one transverse fold on the body, which is observed in pollen from Upper Carboniferous deposits. According to the conclusion of N.I. Stukalova, this part of the section may be referred to the upper part of the Middle Carboniferous.

From reference borehole 01 at a depth of 1179.7 m, from deposits of the Lower Kireyskaya sub-suite (drawing 49, sheet 3), A.I. Lopatina identified single miospores: *Schopfipollenites indecorus* Lub., *Stenozonotriletes* sp., *Planisporites mollis* Lub., *Apiculatisporites* sp., *Crassispora ignorata* (Lub.) Oschir., *Punctatisporites glabbates* Lub. Spores are of poor preservation, characteristic of the Carboniferous. Spores of *Schopfipollenites indecorus* Lub. in the deposits of Central Kazakhstan begin to appear from the base of the Upper Karaganda sub-suite and its analogues, and are widely distributed in the Middle and Upper Carboniferous deposits.

During geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000, from the Kishsak reference section from a sandstone bed lying at the base of the Kireyskaya suite, V.M. Smolyaninova (201) collected plant remains, among which O.B. Meyен identified *Knorria* sp., *Lyginodendron* sp. with a structure of the *Dictyoxylon* type, and flattened problematic plant remains (axes?) with various ribbing.

Trunks of *Lyginodendron* with a structure of the *Dictyoxylon* type belong to pteridosperms from the lyginopterid group, especially characteristic of the Lower and partly Middle Carboniferous of Western Europe and North America. Lepidophytes of the *Knorria* preservation type of this type,

as found at this locality, may correspond to *Lepidodendron kirghizicum* Zal. Such lepidophytes occur in the lower–middle Carboniferous.

The rocks hosting these remains can, in the opinion of S.V. Meyen, be assigned to the interval lower Carboniferous (without the lower part) – middle Carboniferous (without the upper part).

The deposits of the Kireyskaya Formation conformably overlie the deposits of the Kypshanskaya Formation and show a gradual transition from the latter.

The upper beds of the Kypshanskaya Formation, based on plant remains and ostracod fauna, as noted above, have been assigned to the lower part of the Bashkirian Stage.

During thematic fieldwork conducted simultaneously with the additional study, to the east of the studied area, in exposures of the Kireyskaya Formation along the Kulan-Utpes River, plant remains were collected from its middle part, among which L.A. Toganova identified, in addition to the species listed above: **Calamites cruciatus Sternb., C. gigas Brongn., Radicites columnaris (Artis) Zeil.** *Calamites gigas* is characteristic of the upper Carboniferous – lower Permian deposits, but in the Northern Caucasus and in Central Asia its occurrences are also known in the upper part of the Moscovian Stage. **Calamites cruciatus Sternb.** is characteristic of the upper part of the middle Carboniferous and of the upper Carboniferous of the Donbas. **Radicites columnaris (Artis) Zeil.** is known from the C₂³ – C₂⁵ formations of the Donbas.

N.V. Litvinovich (1974) collected samples from the deposits of the Kireyskaya Formation on the Kirey River for microfauna determination. S.V. Mamutova identified from them: *Darwinula pseudoranikriniana* Busch., *D. prima* Busch., *D. lona* Busch., *D. turgida* Busch., *D. exceptiformis* Resch., *D. discriminata* Resch., *D. dolinskaja* Busch., *D. dolinskaja* var. *supora* Busch., *D. triangula* Busch., *D. donratschovi* Resch., *D.* aff. *certa* Busch. The age of the rocks hosting this ostracod assemblage is, in her opinion, middle Carboniferous. From samples collected during the additional study, ostracod fauna in this area was not recovered.

At the most recent stratigraphic conference in Alma-Ata (1971), a middle Carboniferous age was adopted for the Kireyskaya Formation.

The palaeontological material collected during the additional study confirms the correctness of this decision. The age of the Kireyskaya Formation is defined within the interval upper half of the Bashkirian Stage –

**Moscow Stage.**

The suite is correlated by flora with the deposits of the Dolinskaya and Tentekskaya suites of the Karaganda Basin and the Karabidainskaya and Akbidainskaya suites of the Zhilanbutas Basin.

By lithological composition, the Kireyskaya suite is correlated with the Taskudukskaya suite of the Dzhezkazgan depression. However, there is no full confidence in the synchronicity of accumulation of the Taskuduk and Kireyskaya suite sediments, since this is contradicted by finds of foraminifer fauna from the horizon of silicified limestones in the roof of the Taskudukskaya suite.

The foraminifer fauna from this horizon, in the opinion of K.R. Reitlinger (42, 43), may belong to the Namurian stage or to the lower levels of the Bashkirian; M.V. Vdovenko (42, 43) holds the same view regarding this fauna.

Data from measurements of physical properties of rocks of the Kireyskaya suite indicate low values of magnetic susceptibility on the order of 11–60·10⁻⁶ CGS units, and density ranges from 2.43 to 2.80 g/cm³, depending on the content of ferromagnetic material in the clastic fraction.

The weighted mean density of suite deposits is 2.62 g/cm³, practically indistinguishable from the weighted mean of the Upper Paleozoic (δ weighted mean = 2.63 g/cm), therefore the observed local gravity anomalies above the deposits of the Kireyskaya suite reflect deeper objects.

An exception is constituted by the sandstones of the Kireyskaya suite, among which so-called magnetite sandstones are encountered with a magnetite content of up to 60%. Two-humped variation curves of density and magnetic susceptibility (Fig. 6) confirm the presence among the sandstones of the Kireyskaya suite of two groups: magnetic (χ mean = 310·10⁻⁶ CGS units) and non-magnetic (χ mean = 7·10⁻⁶ CGS units). Correspondingly, with increasing magnetism of the sandstones, i.e., increasing magnetite content, their density increases to 2.80 g/cm³ in individual samples up to 3.23 g/cm³.

However, due to the small dimensions of the lenses and interbeds of magnetite sandstones, they are not recorded in the gravity field; possibly their manifestation is through higher-order anomalies on detailed maps. On de-
