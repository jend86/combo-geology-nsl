Due to immeasurable dimensions of ore bodies, imperfectness of their extension and occurrence, small amounts and preservation of ores in them, ore prospecting in the Bogorod area should recognize Desperation.

A poikiletic group of rudoprovenance ores (24,25,37,38,39,42) is located 5 km to the northeast of the village of Spasskoye, on the left bank of the river Maban at the confluence of the Sarkyrany river. It was discovered by V.N. Uspovym in 1928, studied in detail in the process of geological-prospecting work on a scale of 1:50,000 of the Akbessarsk partition (Dmitrovsky, 1965).

The ore field follows the separation of the horsetail-occluding pedozem, represented by hornblende, series of sandstones and slerozinem with rare prolamine gray intrusive conglomerates and pelitic-morphic shale. In structural relation, ore occurrence is confined to monoclines, complicated by a disharmonic sever-east plunge. The amplitude of ore occurrence is 20-30m, the descent of ore is 8-10°.

Mineralization is localized in gray sandstones, aleurites, rarely in conglomerates and limestones. Mediumsupporting ores extend northward from them, forming ore bodies of linear form. Here there is no surface devastation visible, allowing one to observe the different extensions and power of ore bodies.

Part of them by extension extends to the east (large ore body on the edge of useful rock-bearing shows distinguished by outgoing mine deposits in 35,27,38,39,42).

One of the ore bodies, by extension extends to 1.95 km with small ruptures at an average power of 0.2m. The remaining ore bodies of linear form have a length of 150-500 cm. The power of ore bodies varies from 0.5 to 1 cm. The maximum power of the ore body is established in range 3 106 and is 1.35m.

Ore mineral assemblage is represented by malachite, azurite, rarely is not found chalcocite and chalkosite. As true ore minerals concentrated close to the residual stotoks, sometimes form pseudomorphism on them. The content of copper in shallow and boscosized ores ranges from 0.29 to 1.53%. With the goal of subsetting ore horizons on gauging in the north-western part of the Rudoproshchansky deposits of Komsomolskaya GGZ (Pavlodarski, 1953), well U-211 was drilled at 492 cm depth. A **proletarian group of copper ore occurrences** (4,7,8,12,15,17,19-22,26,28) is located on sheet M-42-8, on the left bank of the Ili River. Examination of the group of copper ore occurrences was reflected in the results of USSR-GSSRy work on a scale of 1:200,000 (Zvobchev, 1964) and 1:50,000 (Dmitrovsky, 1965). It unites thirty occurrences of copper ore: Proletarskoe # 4,7,12,15,17,19-22; Leninenovskoe # 3, Smirnovskoe # 26,28 and Prometeutochoe # 8. The Proletarian ore field is characterized by the Upper Cretaceous subsuite—alternation of gray, greenish-gray, brown sandstones and cosmosilicate mudstone layers. The significance in the section is held by pelitomorphic porphyritic minerals and mudstones, intrusive conglomerate intergrowths. In structural relation, sections are confined to the Proletarian anticline. Ore behavior is 4-6°.

Copper mineralization in the form of coatings, veinlets, impregnations in malachite and azurite is confined to denudated horizons of gray sandstones.

The vast majority of ore horizons consist of individual lenses and strata, mixed in extension. On daily ground surface, copper ore deposits extend in extent to tens and first hundreds of meters. Maximum probability of ore bodies extends from 400 to 950m, their power ranges from 0.2 to 0.9m. Content of copper in strata and boring boreholes according to spectral analysis data ranges from 0.4 to 1%, by chemical analysis it reaches 4.68%. For the purpose of verification of ore horizons on depth, one-hole wells were drilled.

Fifth, sixth and seventh ore bodies are confined to the upper part of the Upper Cretaceous pediment. The fifth ore body consists of light-colored greenish-gray sandstones, electrolytes and mica-bearing conglomerates. The fifth ore body extends for 2.5 km in length. The thickness of the ore body ranges from 0.4-3.0 m with copper and oxidized ores from 0.6 to 1.5 m. According to well A-2, it penetrates to a depth of 292.0-298.5 m, where gray sandstone is consolidated with a weakly cemented calcareous cement. The ore content, according to spectral analysis, does not exceed 0.1%. The sixth ore body is located in the interval above 55.0 m and extends for 2.5 km without visible mineralization. The ore body has a complex structure. It is represented by sandstones, electrolytes, ferruginous and medial-galenic conglomerates. Petrographic composition and skeletal distribution of fragmented plant debris in ore bodies resulted in uneven distribution of mineralization, represented by iron minerals, malachite and azurite. The thickness of ore body is 1.0 m. The copper content in oxidized ores, according to spectral analysis, reaches 2.39%. According to well A-2, located according to pediment at 1000 m from the exit of the sixth ore body, in the interval 154.0-168.0 m, there is a gray siltstone with sulfide mineralization. The copper content in rock probes, according to spectral analysis, does not exceed 0.02%.

The seventh ore body extends below the previous one, west of well No. 2 at a depth of 132.0-144.5 m and is represented by gray, dark-gray electrolytes with clusters of pyrite and chalcopyrite. The copper content in rock samples at the bottom of the probe does not exceed 0.04%; in oxidized ores it reaches 1.5%.

In 1986, on the Lorensky ore field of the Zelinograd State Geological Survey of the Karaganda Regional Geological Survey Department, well No. P-237 with a depth of 661.0 m was drilled. Sericitized, in intervals 86.2-97.5 m and 266.0-267.5 m, there is gray fine-grained sandstone with weak disseminated chalcopyrite and chalcocite. The copper content, according to spectral analysis, respectively amounts to 0.2 and 0.4%.
