On the southern slope of the Genisek ridge, the Dalnenskii horizon is complex, terrigenous clay with rare scattered known interlayers. The clay in the lower part is red-colored, in the upper part - variegated, with rare angular patterns. The power of the horizon at the Kokpektinsk uplift is 30-120 meters, at the Shademsai - 220 meters.

The Dalnenskii horizon is also exposed along the northern margin of the ridge, the character of the section is analogous to the aforementioned (see paragraph 49), but the power of the horizon here is sharply reduced and fluctuates from 10 to 80 meters.

Terrigenous rocks of the Dalnenskii horizon are represented by sandstones, siltstones and argillites.

Sandstones - rocks of gray, gray-green and lime-green color. Textures massive and porous. Siltstone from the sections of the Dalnenskii horizon of the eastern and northern regions are not distinguished from one another and have a number of common features. They are predominantly fine-grained, medium-fine-grained and medium-grained; distributed laminations also occur. The degree of roundness and sorting varies within wide limits. According to the composition of detrital minerals, siltstones are properly polymictitic. Rare lithoid interlayers - granites, glinite pores, volcanics - are found. Their quantity varies from 50 to 85% of the volume of detrital minerals. In the second location, in this section, there are, mainly argillites; in the northern - argillites in association with siltstone. The quantity of them fluctuates from 25 to 100-150%. Quartzose siltstone, as a rule, occurs in sandstones of the Dalnenskii horizon in a subordinate quantity - from 0% to 25% (rarely).

Noted in the detritus are ore minerals of rudely mineralized minerals, epidote, chlorite, and rarely - biotite. In sandstones of the northern regions of the ridge, as a rule, there occurs either another quantity of biogenic detritus, carbonate rocks.

Carbonate breeds of the distant horizon are represented exclusively by limestones. Among them two main ones stand out, as in the sections of that, and in the north of the Ipalias, groups: a) limestones with biomorphic remains and b) porphyritic limestones. Rare variety of limestones, noted only in northern sections, are crystalline limestones.

Limestones with biomorphic remains - not everywhere in the Teniz Spa were represented by series, light-series pelitomorphic distribution.

Cementing pelitomorphic carbonate constitutes 50%-70% of the breed. Structure of the cementing mass uniform or early homogeneous. Biomorphic remains presented, as usual, well preserved in racemized gastropods, ostracods, with shells of small bivalves under pedicels. Terrigenous admixture reaches up to 10%.

Similar in type and limestone distribution on the Teniz Spa northern section. They are noted in section r. Archar (sh.A-28a), and in the well sample 139 (gl.158/160.5). Insignificant distinction consists in the set of paleontological remains, which presented by predominant teeth of crinoids, breaking mussel colonies, shells of formanifera, holo remains and pelecopods are noted here. On the other side, in northern sections of the distant horizon, typically bioclastite limestones, composed mainly in main permatite of detrital matter (mainly in main cements of crinoids), cemented by ancrystalline calcium. In these breeds terrigenous admixture can reach 25%.

Clear boundary between limestones with biomorphic remains and porphyritic limestones is absent, since in sections of the distant horizon of the Teniz Spa limestones of first mobility are often noted with abundant porphyritic shells plainly.

Porphyritic limestones are distributed both in sections along the river and in northern parts of the Teniz Spa. Color of breed: gray, light-gray, greenish-yellow, yellowish. Frequently breeds patchy. Textures massive, comkovate, layered. Limestones are composed pelitomorphic, microcrystalline and very fine-crystalline calcium. Microstructures usually comkovate. Crimopelagic deposits are noted in size in s.Vladimirova (sh.B-15) and in the proper size on r.Lchian (sh.A-19). These are bright-red (s.Vladimirova) or pale-ocher (r.Lchian) monometallic breeds of massive texture. Under the microscope, they are almost completely lined with "particles" and "oriented" crystals of calcite. They contain a terrigenous admixture (to 15-20%), whereby the majority of grains are replaced by carbonate and their forms are established only along the clay crumbs, "penetrating" next to calcite. The genesis of the breed is unclear.
