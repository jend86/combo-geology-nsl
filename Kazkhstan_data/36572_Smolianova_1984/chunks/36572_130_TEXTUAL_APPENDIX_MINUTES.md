**HEARD:** Report by Smolyaninova E.M. on actual costs for the acquisition of aerial photographic materials for the West-Teniz PSP (Predictive Survey Program), on the required volume of sampling, and on the compilation of mineral resource maps during geological re-study at scale 1:200,000 for the western half of the Teniz Depression.

**1.** In preparing the cost estimate for the production of approximately-oriented photoplans at scale 1:100,000 for the West-Teniz PSP project, the following volumes were used:

Work area: 22,053 km²
Number of map sheets at scale 1:100,000 – 17 nom. sheets

Actual volumes are as follows:
Work area: 37,720 km²
Number of map sheets at scale 1:100,000 – 30 nom. sheets

Furthermore, the project did not account for the production of photoplans from various flight runs, including at scales 1:25,000 and 1:17,000, which requires the compilation of intermediate photoplans at scales 1:25,000 and 1:50,000.

II. In the project for the West-Teniz PSP, for 6,060 lin.m of mapping and 27,000 m of prospecting-mapping drilling, the sampling of only 500 core samples (500 lin.m) is planned.
Work experience has shown that during drilling of 4,457 lin.m of boreholes, 310 lin.m of core samples (255 samples) and 464 geochemical samples were collected. The latter were collected from red-colored varieties of samples and from gray-colored ones — without visible mineralization. In this case, the sampling interval for gray-colored varieties was up to 2 m, and for red-colored ones — up to 7 m.

For the remaining drilling volume, 1,600–1,500 lin.m of core sampling and 2,500–2,700 geochemical samples will be required. The deficient volume of core sampling amounts to 1,200–1,300 lin.m.

Collection of geochemical samples from borehole cores is not provided for in the project at all. A total of 2,700–3,000 specimens will be collected, with an interval of up to 5 m for red-colored varieties and up to 2 m for gray-colored ones.

III. According to the instructions for the compilation and preparation for publication of sheets of the State Geological Map of the USSR at scale 1:200,000, occurrences of mineral deposits are to be considered locations of mineral discoveries which, by their quality, meet the requirements of industry (or close to such), but due to limited reserves or insufficient study cannot be classified as deposits.

Based on current industrial requirements and the quality of copper-bearing sandstones, it is considered appropriate to plot on the mineral resources map occurrences with contents of 0.2–0.3% at a thickness of 1.5 m, or 0.3–0.45 m %.
