**Kyiminek svita**

The Kyiminek svita, like the Kabraktinsk, was subject to detailed stratigraphic examination of the Teniskey depression, which was identified by N.V. Litvinovich in 1964. The name is derived from the Kyiminek settlement, which is located to the north-northwest of the Laman-Kairakty river, which was previously described for the svita. In the context of the stratigraphic examination of the Kyiminek svita, the territory of sheet M-42-II, where the Kyiminek svita deposits have the best development in the studied area, is considered.

In the stratigraphic range of the svita with sufficient detail it is possible to study from the generalized sections of the Laman-Kairakty river and from the north-west of the Kyiminek settlement, along the Laman-Kairakty river. Moreover, in the sections of the Laman-Kairakty river, the character of the svita deposits, in our opinion, can be better than in the stratigraphic range, as the final representative, main forms, characteristic developments and only local manifestations of the svita rocks are represented in it in numerous facies variations.

In recent years, in the Kyiminek depression in the area of the stratigraphic range, many useful fossils, seeds of which the greatest part was concentrated in the process of sieving, were studied. The svita sections on the edge of the seams are given on sheet 49, sheets 1,2.

The distribution of Kyiminek svita deposits on the studied territory develops widely; they compose either separate parts, or the roof of all synclinal structures. In many structures, the deposits of the svita are arranged so that they establish a correlation of svita sections throughout the area of the depression. Particularly difficult to correlate is the upper boundary of the svita.
