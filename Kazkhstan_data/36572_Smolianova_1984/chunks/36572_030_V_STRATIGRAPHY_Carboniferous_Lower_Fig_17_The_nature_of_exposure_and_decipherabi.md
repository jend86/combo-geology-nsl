In addition, the deposits of the Ishim horizon are highlighted
in a detailed description of the distal support sections on the northern
wing of the depression. Along the section line, which was described
by careful collection of fauna, it is possible to draw a boundary between
the Ishim and Yagovkin horizons. This boundary runs in the middle
part of a monotonous undulating limestone pack. Sometimes
there are oolitic differences at this level.

When moving away from the area of the section description, due
to the lithological uniformity of the Ishim and Yagovkin horizons and
due to poor exposure, it is not possible to draw this boundary. Therefore
, the sediments of these horizons were mapped undifferentiated and
divided into the Krasnoselsky suite.

The thickness of the horizon in the supporting sections of the northern
wing of the depression is sharply reduced and amounts to 15-20 to 40 meters.
The section of the Ishim horizon in the south of the basin has
been poorly studied due to the shallow outcrop and needs further study.
The volume of the horizon, the nature of the structure of its discharge,
facies changes in area, lower and upper boundaries, and the distribution
of fauna are not fully clear.

*     *     *

The formations of the Ishim horizon are composed in
approximately equal proportions of terrigenous and carbonate rocks.
Terrigenous rocks are represented by sandstones, siltstones
and mudstones.

The sandstones and siltstones of
the Ishim horizon are gray, greenish-gray, and chalky-gray
rocks of various, mostly massive textures. Fine-grained
differences prevail, and the composition of clastic grains differs
sharply from the terrigenous rocks described above in the lower
carboniferus division. The sandstones of the Ishim horizon are
polymictic, with lithoid fragments strongly predominant. The total
content of quartz and feldspar fragments rarely exceeds 30%. As
a rule, there is an admixture of biogenic detritus particles. The
predominant type of cement is film-porous, clay with glauconite

and chlorite. In general, glaucolite-containing rock differences
are characteristic of the Ishim horizon in the south of the Teniz
Depression. The carbonate cemate (calcite) is less developed.
Epidote is present in cement as an aromatic mineral.
And they are black, very thin, massive rocks with
a comminuted separation. The admixture of pelitomorphic
carbonate is usually small, but it quite often contains
well-preserved organic residues.
Carbonate rocks are represented
exclusively by biomorphic-detrital limestones and containing
biomorphic oddities. Among them, there are differences similar
to those described in the Rusakovsky horizon section (marls,
marl limestones) and differences closer to those described in
the Simorinsky horizon, which are characterized by crystalline
cement and a sandy admixture rather than clay. Only polydetrite
differences (mossy-brachiopod-crinoid ) have been identified, as
in sandstones, glauconite is characteristic of individual layers of biomorphic-
detrital limestones.
In addition to aged limestone interlayers, small
(up to the first tens of centimeters) aggregations of fine
pelitomorphic carbonate enclosing biogenic remains, often very well
preserved (colonial corals, colonies of bryozoans, trilobites, etc.),
are noted among black siltstones. Very rarely, indistinct
porphyritic secretions accumulate in these strata.
