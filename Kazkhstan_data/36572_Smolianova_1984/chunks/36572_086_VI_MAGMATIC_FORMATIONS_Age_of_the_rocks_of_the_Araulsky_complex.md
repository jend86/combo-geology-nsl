**Age of the rocks of the Araulsky complex**

As noted above, within the studied territory, the granites of the Araulsky complex intrude the deposits of the Ulutau series and are in not entirely clear relationships with the rocks of the Krynkuduksky and Borovsky intrusive complexes.

– 305 –

A comparison with adjacent areas made by M.S. Grankin (1970) shows that these rocks are similar in mineral composition to the alaskite-like granites of the Kashurlin massif. These granites were identified by V.M. Shulga as belonging to the second phase of emplacement and the phase of supplementary intrusions of the Aralaul complex. Since a number of geological data and absolute age figures cited by V.M. Shulga (1969) for adjacent territories indicate a Silurian age for the alaskite-like granites described by him as the most probable, we, following M.S. Grankin, adopt this age for analogous rocks of the Karatas and Obalykol massifs.

The density of rocks of the Aralaul complex ranges from 2.55 g/cm³ to 2.60 g/cm³; magnetic susceptibility ranges from 10.10⁻⁶ CGS units to 166.10⁻⁶ CGS units.

In the magnetic field over the outcrops of the described intrusions, an insignificant increase in magnetic field intensity is observed.

In the gravity field, the Aralaul complex shows no clear expression.

**MAGMATIC FORMATIONS OF SILURIAN–EARLY DEVONIAN AGE**

---

With the Silurian–Early Devonian stage of magmatic activity, the formation of intrusive bodies of two magmatic complexes, differing in the composition of their constituent rocks, is associated within the studied territory: 1) the Ishim volcano-plutonic complex of alkaline effusives and intrusives, and 2) the Borovo granite complex.

**Petrochemical characteristics of the granites
of the Araulsky complex**

The authors had at their disposal one silicate analysis selected by the authors from the alaskite-type granites of the Obalshkolsky massif, and three analyses of similar rocks from the Karatasskiy massif, taken for comparison from the report of M.S. Grashin (1970). The small number of analyses permits only the most general conclusions to be drawn about the chemistry of the rocks described.

1. The rocks of the Araulsky complex belong to the acidic group, with the SiO₂ content exceeding 75% in all analyses.

2. From the diagram (Fig. No. 77), it is evident that, with respect to the relative content of SiO₂ and (Na₂O + K₂O), the granites described belong to the leucocratic family of the normal series and subalkaline, which correlates well with the quantitative mineral composition.

3. By the ratio Na₂O/K₂O, the granites described belong to the potassium-sodium series (Na₂O/K₂O = 1–0.7) with a distinct potassium specialization (Fig. 79).

4. The aluminosity coefficient of the rocks of the Karatasskiy massif is 3.5–3.6. Although, according to these values, the formations described fall within the range of highly aluminous rocks, as do the overwhelming majority of acidic rocks of the region, the indicated figures stand out from the general series, which distinguishes the leucocratic granites of the Karatasskiy massif from rocks of similar SiO₂ and Na₂O+K₂O content from other complexes (Borovskoy, Balkashinskiy) (Fig. 78).
