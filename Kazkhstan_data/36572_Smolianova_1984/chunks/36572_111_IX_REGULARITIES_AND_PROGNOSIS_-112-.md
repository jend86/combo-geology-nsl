I. ECONOMIC FEASIBILITY OF PLACEMENT OF MINERAL DEPOSITS.

Geological survey work at a scale of 1:200,000, as well as preceding geological, geophysical and geochemical investigations, made it possible to establish in the western part of the Teniz Depression the following regularities in the distribution of useful minerals (sheets No. 34-42) on the subplatform structure.

Coal

The main factor determining coal occurrence is the stratigraphic-structural affiliation of coal-bearing strata to the Cretaceous-Paleogene section. In general, this section should be examined in relation to coal. This is confirmed by the results of work on coal in the western part of the Western Siberian plate. Coal is not clear in the Cimperskaia anticline, where coal has not been found at depth. Depth of anthracite coal in these areas ranges from 0 to 500m.

BASIC SEARCH CRITERIA AND SIGNS. Based on data on xenomorphic deposits of ore minerals in the limits of the investigated territory, it was established that the most important criteria for searching for ore deposits are: stratigraphic, lithofacies-petrographic and structural control of mineralization.

1. The main metallogenic levels within the limits of the investigated territory are: upper Devonian, middle Carboniferous (Visean), middle Carboniferous (Serpukhovian) and upper Carboniferous (Gzhelian).

2. Lithostratigraphic control of ore mineralization occurs in conditions of maximum concentrations of copper and molybdenum-molybdenite (jointly in Cu, (possibly) and lead-zinc (Pb-Zn) facies.

3. Structural control is manifested clearly. All manifestations, secondary halos of copper and geochemical anomalies are located on large paleofaults and flexural bends. Structural control serves: as a determining factor in the distribution of ore mineralization within the region.

The most characteristic search signs and data of manifestations (location) within the region:

1. Fields of oxidized copper minerals.

2. Presence of manifestations and zones of mineralization.

3. Zones of dissemination of copper and accompanying elements, by results of lithochemical sampling. They, as a rule, coincide with crude ore outcrops on the surface.

4. Presence of residual deposits in alluvial deposits.
