**Tersakkansk Suite (C₃ tr)**

By decision of the Stratigraphic Conference of 1971 in the city of Alma-Ata, in the stratigraphic scheme of the Teniz Depression, above the Kireysk Suite, the Vladimirsk Suite is distinguished, with its stratotype on the northern limb of the Vladimirsk syncline, in the area of the settlements of Maksimovka–Vladimirka.

According to the "Stratigraphic Code," every suite distinguished as a local stratigraphic subdivision must have a stratotype.

For the middle–upper Carboniferous deposits, the section in the area of the settlement of Vladimirka cannot serve as the stratotype due to its incompleteness. Here, on the northern limb of the Vladimirsk syncline, deposits of the Kipchak Suite and the Kireysk Suite crop out in section, as well as a portion of the coarse-grained red-colored formations overlying the deposits of the Kireysk Suite. This thinning-out of part of the upper Paleozoic section in the Vladimirsk syncline was convincingly demonstrated during prospecting operations in the northern part of the depression by V.A. Nevadsky (1960). Having retained the Vladimirsk syncline's northern limb section as the stratotype section for the Vladimirsk Suite, he was compelled to distinguish, already within the Konursk syncline, the Konursk Suite below it — represented by coarse-clastic red-colored rocks — and, in the Tretyakovsk and Archalinsk synclines, to add the Ostrogorsk Suite beneath the Konursk Suite.

Having carefully studied the cores of all prospecting wells across the Vladimirsk, Konursk, Archalinsk, and Tretyakovsk synclines, and having tied the surface sections to the borehole sections, we arrived at the same conclusion regarding the absence, in the Vladimirsk reference section, of a portion of the upper Paleozoic deposits (fig. 49, sheet 4, 8).

The discrepancy in the volumes of the Vladimirsk Suite between the south and north of the Teniz Depression had been noted previously, both in archival and published works, but the nature of this discrepancy was assessed differently by various researchers: some believed that in the Vladimirsk reference section, beneath the Vladimirsk Suite, what is understood to be a single formation is an analogue of the Tersakkansk and Kireysk Suites taken together; while others believed it corresponds only to the uppermost Tersakkansk strata in the south of the depression. The latter viewpoint was most consistently defended by B.V. Dmitrovsky and A.P. Petrilak,

The essence of this substitution is not entirely clear. If O.V. Dmitrovskiy wished to emphasize the Permian age of the identified sequence, it would have been more correct, in accordance with the application of the right of priority, to retain the name of the suite and raise the question of revising its age as a whole.

If the inconsistency of the volume of the Vladimirovskoye suite to the south and north was being emphasized, then it was necessary to raise the question of establishing a new suite either only in the southern part of the trough, or throughout the entire trough. In the latter case, it was necessary to raise the question of the validity of the designated Vladimirovskoye suite, which is what is being done in the present report.

The description of the reference section of the Vladimirovskoye suite was made by V.M. Popov a long time ago, as early as 1931–32, in the "Report on the work of the Kokchetav Geological Prospecting Party," and was published in 1941 in the work "Cupriferous Sandstones of Central Kazakhstan." This section was never subsequently described in detail by anyone. Below is provided the complete description of the Vladimirovskoye reference section (after Popov, 1932) from bottom to top:

**1.** At the base lie contact hornfels forming a wide belt adjoining the granite massif. The width of the contact belt reaches 100 meters and more. These hornfels, apparently, were formed as a result of contact metamorphism of the granite intrusion on the argillaceous slates of the coal-bearing suite.

**2.** Above lies a thick suite of gray and gray-green sandstones . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . 600 m.

**3.** Greenish-gray, sometimes with brownish and yellowish tints, silicified marlstones and hornfels-like rocks . . . 70 m. Along strike, this suite pinches out and laterally grades into red sandstones and yellowish-green marlstones.

**4.** Coarse-grained gray sandstones, pinching out along strike to the west . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . 60 m.

Later, in 1954, V.M. Popov designates the ore-bearing (7) suite as the Vladimirovsky suite. Maps of the surface appended to the 1932 report allow one to precisely establish the lower and upper boundaries of the suite and tie them to the reference section compiled during the additional study process (Fig. 49, sheet 4). O.A. Mazarovich, and following him N.V. Dmitrovsky, assigned to the Vladimirovsky suite also the packet of the so-called "pepper" sandstones. The inclusion of this packet into the composition of the ore-bearing Vladimirovsky suite immediately violated the logical consistency of correlation of the red-colored continental strata of its southern depression and its northern part.

Since the Tersakkan suite, identified by G.E. Bykov (1935) in the southern depression, just as the Vladimirovsky suite in the north, began with the coarsest-grained red-colored sediments, while the underlying thin beds of variegated and greenish-grey colors were assigned by G.E. Bykov to the Lower Carboniferous. Later, the Kireyskaya suite was identified from the composition of the latter, and not from the composition of the Tersakkan suite, as stated in the "Stratigraphic Dictionary" (1977).

Initially, in the working field variant, the packet of "pepper" sandstones, which is very clearly distinguished in the section, was correlated by us (up to bed 33) with the Kymshakskaya suite, and in the interval of beds 33–43 with the Kireyskaya suite, all the more so because the reference reflecting boundary T₈, normally mapping the base of the Tersakkan suite, was identified in this section in a stratum close to the level of beds 43–45. Having conducted thorough collections of flora from beds 25, 33, 43, 45, we hoped to obtain confirmation of our correlations.

The conclusions obtained on flora, microfauna, spores and pollen did not allow us to identify in the north of the Vladimirovsky depression either the Ki-

Therefore, in the following field season we reviewed the cores of the exploration boreholes stored at the Tselinograd GRE, and we were compelled to abandon our initial concept and conclude that part of the sequence is absent from the Vladimirovsky reference section, that the red-colored continental deposits are incomplete within it, and that the Vladimirskaya suite identified in the north of the depression corresponds only to the upper part of the Tersakkansk sequence. The lower part of the red-colored Tersakkanskaya sequence in the north of the Tenizkaya depression corresponds to the Konurskaya and Ostrogorovskaya suites as identified by V.A. Papadakis, taken together. Therefore, if in accordance with the Code the validity of the Vladimirskaya suite is to be retained, while also preserving priority rights, then under the Vladimirskaya suite one should understand the upper Tersakkanskaya sub-suite identified by us.

Thus, the most complete section of red-colored continental deposits in the Tenizkaya depression is the section along the southern part of the depression. The locality in the basin of the Tersakkan River on the area of sheets M-42-XIV, XV is proposed as the stratotypic section for the interval of both the middle and upper Carboniferous.

From the stratigraphic scheme of the Tenizkaya depression it is proposed to abolish the Vladimirskaya and Orcaalinskaya suites, and between the deposits of the Kireyskaya and Kayraktinsky suites to distinguish in the stratigraphic section the Tersakkanskaya suite.

The Tersakkanskaya suite on the studied territory is distributed extremely widely. On the southern limb of the Tenizkaya depression it forms a wide belt of outcrops along the Kipshakskaya flexure from Lake Tengiz in the east to the Tersakkan River in the west. At the Tersakkan River its core forms the Kenenskaya anticline; in the north of sheet M-42-XV at the confluence of the Tersakkan and Mokinkty rivers it is exposed in the core of the Saryobinsky anticline. On the area of sheet M-42-XIV it forms a series of fold structures in the interfluve of the Tersakkan–Saytас rivers.

Here, in the southwestern part of the studied territory, the suite is well exposed; moreover, it has been penetrated by several key parametric boreholes, therefore this area (sheets M-42-XIV, XV) is proposed to be retained as the stratotype region of the Tersakkanskaya suite, where it is possible to select not only the stratotypic section of the suite but also to understand the character of its areal variation. It is convenient as a stratotype region also because the stratotype regions of the Kireyskaya and Kipshakskaya suites are likewise located here. Thus, all Carboniferous...

Along the northwestern limb of the Teniz depression, the suite is exposed somewhat worse: here, within the area of sheets M-42-YII, M-42-II, it forms isolated narrow strips of outcrops on the limbs of the Taldin, Shoptykul, and Kiymin synclines.

In the north of the studied territory, the Tersakkansk suite is exposed on the limbs of the Tretyakovsk, Vladimirovsк, Konursк, Archalinsk synclines, in the cores of the Poltavskoye, Proletskoye uplifts, and on the limbs of the Atbasarskoye uplift (sheets N-42-XXXVIII, XXXIY, M-42-II, IY).

In the central parts of the studied territory, the Tersakkansk suite crops out at the surface in the cores of the Kokpektin and Dzhunkarkolsk anticlines (M-42-IY).

Throughout the entire studied territory, as indeed throughout the entire Teniz depression, there are no exposures of the suite in vertical planes. Most often it is exposed in horizontal planes on the interfluves Figs. 21–24 of rivers and streams. All sections compiled from the surface do not give an idea of its characteristics, since selective exposure is typical for the suite: as a rule, coarse-clastic rocks are exposed, while thin terrigenous varieties are not exposed.

The suite is characterized by a ridge-and-hill relief, where lenses and beds of denser sandstones and conglomerates form small ridges and elongated hills oriented parallel to the strike of the rocks, with relative elevations of 1–3 m, rarely up to 5 m, covered by alluvial large slabs and rubble of sandstones or redeposited pebbles of conglomerates.

The decipherability of the suite varies in different structures. In the south of the studied territory, over the entire area of sheets M-42-XIY, XY, the lower boundary of the suite is clearly and unambiguously identified by photo-interpretation. On aerial photographs it is deciphered as a high, persistent along strike ridge, the first in the system of ridge relief Fig. 25 that characterizes the Tersakkansk suite, in contrast to the inexpressive low relief of the underlying Kireysk suite. When interpreting contact prints at a scale of 1:17,000, it is possible to distinguish ridges composed of conglomerates from sandy ridges. In the northern part of the studied area, deciphering characteristics have been studied less well due to the small size of the exposed sections. The lower boundary of the formation here does not have the same distinctiveness as in the south, since the formation lacks thick, persistent interbeds of conglomerates and coarse-grained sandstones. In the Tretyakovskaya syncline, the chert horizon at the boundary of the beds in the lower Tersakkan substage is deciphered very well.

In the south of the studied area, the formation everywhere overlies the deposits of the Kireyskaya formation. Various researchers characterize its lower boundary differently: some consider it conformable, others note significant angular unconformities. Study of the boundary in the southwest of the depression showed that there are no azimuthal discordances in the strike of the rocks of the Kireyskaya and Tersakkanskaya formations here. A certain angular discordance is observed only in the far east of the studied area, between Lake Kirey and Tengiz. Here the boundary is exposed by a trench excavated by the Dzhezkazgan HPS. In it, the dip angles of the rocks of the Kireyskaya and Tersakkanskaya formations in the zone of the Kipchakskaya flexure differ near the boundary by 8–10°. However, such discordance in angles near flexures has frequently been observed by us also in homogeneous sequences of the Upper Paleozoic.

In the central parts of the studied area (sheets M-42-VII, IX), the character of the lower boundary was observed only in boreholes. From these it is evident that the Kireyskaya formation sometimes drops out of the section or has a very insignificant thickness. The Tersakkanskaya formation lies either on the truncated Kireyskaya formation, or sometimes on more ancient formations. On the Munkirkol'skoye uplift it lies sometimes on the Kireyskaya formation, sometimes on Lower Carboniferous deposits, sometimes on the deposits of the Bretolubovskaya series, and sometimes on the granitoids of the Krykkuduksky complex (boreholes L-119, L-120, L-195, 118). The most complex for study and understanding is the lower boundary of the formation in the north of the investigated area: in the region of the so-called Atbasarskoe uplift and to the north of it, in the area adjacent to the Kokshetau horst. Here, significant reductions in the thicknesses of the Tersakkanskaya formation by a factor of 2–3 compared to the southwest are observed, as well as the dropping out of individual beds from the section. Thus, in the Vladimirovskoye syncline, approximately one quarter of the section of the lower part of the formation drops out. In connection with

It should be noted that the increase in coarse-clastic character of the terrigenous material in the deposits at the boundary of the Kirei and Tersakan formations nevertheless proceeds gradually where they have conformable occurrence. This is very well seen in the key boreholes T-20 (Fig. 49, sheet 1), O1 (Fig. 49, sheet 3), O15A (Fig. 49, sheet 5), in the cross-sections along the Tretyakovskaya and Archalinsk synclines (Fig. 49, sheets 4, 6). Intraformational conglomerates at the base of the formation occur, as a rule, in cross-sections where the Tersakan Formation overlies the most complete in section character Kirei Formation. Usually, they are observed on the limbs of cosedimentation synclinal structures and in their central parts. If the formation occurs on paleo-uplifts and has a reduced thickness, then at its base, as a rule, intraformational conglomerates are absent (Fig. 49).

The stratotypic section for the lower boundary of the formation can serve as the section along the Tersakan River, where it is drawn clearly and unambiguously at the base of the thick bed of boulder and large-pebble intraformational conglomerate. The character of this boundary throughout the studied territory was described in detail above in the description of the Kirei Formation.

The upper boundary of the formation throughout the entire territory is conventional, since the transition from the deposits of the Tersakan Formation to the deposits of the Kairktin Formation is very gradual. N.V. Litvinovich, who for a long time studied the Upper Paleozoic deposits of Kazakhstan, considers the main criterion in drawing the boundary between these formations to be the grey coloration of the rocks of the Kairktin Formation, the presence in it of carbonate interlayers, and the good sorting of terrigenous material in the latter. These criteria for the boundary between the Tersakan and Kairktin formations were adopted by us as well.

Their use in the process of mapping showed that they are not entirely reliable not only individually but even in combination.

Additionally, for mapping the upper boundary of the Tersakan Formation, we used the following criteria: textural and structural features of limestones, tracing of marker beds, and rhythmicity of the sequence. Despite this, there is no full confidence in the isochronous nature of the mapped upper boundary of the Tersakan Formation. We allowed for boundary migration across the area. Its character in individual structures will be discussed in more detail below, in the description of the Kayraktin Formation.

The section of the Tersakan Formation across the studied area is extremely variable; indeed, it is perhaps the most variable of all Upper Paleozoic sections. We distinguish several section types. Their areal distribution is shown in Fig. 26.

As the stratotypic (hypostratotypic) section of the Tersakan Formation, a section drilled by reference-parametric wells 01–03 is proposed, which fully penetrates the formation section. Exploration well 05 was used as an auxiliary well for correlation of sections with wells 01–03. This section is located within the zone of the former stratotypic section, but since it is based on boreholes, it provides a more complete characterization. The base of the Tersakan Formation is penetrated in well 01 at a depth of 810.3 m. According to VSP data from reference well 01, it is precisely at this level that the reference reflecting boundary T₂ occurs, which can be traced throughout the southwest of the studied territory.
