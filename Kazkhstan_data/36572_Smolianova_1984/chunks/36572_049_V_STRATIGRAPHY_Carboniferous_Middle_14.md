**Caprock Series (C₂ ky)**

The caprock series, in the nature of a local stratigraphic structure of the Tennessee suite, was first described by O.A. Lazarovich (1947) at a geological survey scale of 1:50000, on sheet L-42-D. Data are given below on the Kirghiz River, where the best exposure of the series is observed on a fluvial plain.

During geological mapping in the area of Lake Kirghiz and along the Kirghiz River, several markers were made for repeated clarification of the stratigraphic section. The markers showed that the upper section here consists entirely of clays. As O.A. Lazarovich notes, the name of the caprock series was assigned to it because of its lithofacies, exposed on the left bank of the Kirghiz River, where it was not preserved.

The homogeneity along the Kirghiz River gives the opportunity to compose only the most characteristic representation of the relationship of the series: it consists of two large sections in the cross-section, the upper part contains a large number of interlayers of pale-colored deposits with paleolithic traces; the series has fairly clear boundaries, for which an extensive paleoprobe and microprobe of the series along the Kirghiz River 1550-1800m.

Thus, a correctly generalized stratigraphic section of the folk suit to the present day. This section is more complete, if we were to require a revision of the stratigraphic sections of the suit with the output of the new "Stratigraphic Code of the USSR".

O.A. Lazarovich, as a head stratigraphist, made surveys along the Kirghiz River by points of wells. Only the names of the boundaries of two lower strata of the folk suit were established. The section along the Kirghiz River may be either a stratigraphic whole for the lower suite.
