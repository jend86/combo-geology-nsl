**Age of the granitoids of the Zerendinsky complex**

Geological data testifying to the age of the rocks of the Zerendinsky complex are as follows:

1. Within the studied area, granitoids of the Zerendinsky complex intrude Lower–Middle Ordovician deposits (area of v. Maksimovka, M-42-XXXIII).

This contact was described by many researchers (Babichev, 1963; Baidosenko, 1965). It was also observed by the authors of the report. Although the contact line itself is not exposed, the character of the changes in the host strata leaves no doubt as to its intrusive nature.

2. Pebbles of granodiorites were discovered in conglomerates of the Middle–Upper Devonian, exposed along the Konur River south of v. Ostrogorka.

3. Pebbles of granitoids of the Zerende complex are described from conglomerates of the Lower–Middle Devonian by V.A. Babichev, O.A. Mazarovich (1963) and G.N. Baidoshvili (1965) in areas immediately adjacent from the north and northwest to the survey territory.

Numerous determinations of the absolute age of rocks of the Zerende massif by the biotite potassium–argon method yield a significant scatter of figures – from 310 to 550 (Serykh, 1963; data from other authors fall within the same interval). We also selected a sample for absolute age determination from quartz monzodiorites of the second phase (No. 7851/84), analysis of which yielded a figure of 395 ± 8 Ma (the sample was analyzed at the Laboratory of Absolute Age of the GIN, Academy of Sciences of the Kazakh SSR).

Such scatter of age figures is explained by "significant and diverse metamorphic effects of activation processes to which the Kokchetav block was subjected in the Devonian, and which were accompanied by the emplacement of volcanics and ultraalkali granites." ("Geological map ……", 1981).

The distribution of absolute age figures by frequency of occurrence yields a distinct peak in the interval 440–450 Ma (Shulga, Chernopolsky, 1976), which does not contradict the geological data and allows one to speak of a Late Ordovician age of the described rocks as the most probable. This age of the granitoids of the Zerende complex is currently accepted by the majority of researchers.

The magnetic susceptibility of granodiorites of the Zerende complex reaches 4000·10⁻⁶ CGS units, density 2.65 g/cm³. The mean magnetic susceptibility of quartz diorites is 372·10⁻⁶ CGS units, density 2.76 g/cm³.

In gravimagnetic fields over the intrusions of the Zerende complex, negative gravity anomalies are observed, coinciding in plan with positive magnetic anomalies.

Granites are mapped by reduced magnetic and gravitational fields.
