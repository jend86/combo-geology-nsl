**CONCLUSION**

In conclusion, the following key points should be emphasized:

1. The party has completed virtually the entire planned volume of field work. The shortfall in the volume of exploration-mapping drilling was compensated by the study of core from third-party organizations. A large body of factual material was collected, making it possible to clarify, and in a number of cases to shed new light on, the geology of the region. During the office processing period, a synthesis of this material was carried out, conclusions were drawn regarding the geological structure of the studied territory, and objectives were outlined for further study of both the investigated area and the entire depression as a whole.

2. The target assignment of the party has been fulfilled.

3. On the submitted geological maps, a detailed subdivision of the Upper Paleozoic and Lower Carboniferous sequences has been carried out, and corrections have been introduced to the structure and age substantiation of the Proterozoic and Lower Paleozoic formations.

4. The attribution of the Bratoluybovskaya, Gershinskaya, and Nikolsko-Burdukskaya series to the Late Proterozoic is called into question. Certain data are presented attesting to their younger age.

5. Among the deposits of the Inisky horizon, two lithological members have been identified, differing also in the character of fossil organic remains. Further study of the faunal assemblages of the identified members will apparently make it possible to distinguish two sub-horizons within the Kipshak trough.

6. The discrepancy between the character of the section and the stratigraphic volume of the Beleutinskaya suite, previously defined for the Teniz Depression, and the volume and character of its section in the stratotypic section of the Sholakskaya mulde has been demonstrated. In connection with this, a new local stratigraphic subdivision has been defined for the Teniz Depression — the Kypshak suite, for which a Lower–Middle Carboniferous age has been proven on the basis of numerous plant remains. The suite is correlated by floristic remains with the Nadkaragandinskaya and Tentekskaya suites of the Karaganda Basin.

8. The Spasskaya and Krasnoselskaya formations have been identified as local stratigraphic subdivisions for the northern part of the Teniz depression, of Tournaisian and lower-middle Visean age, respectively.

9. It is proposed to identify the Tersakkansk formation as a local stratigraphic subdivision of the Teniz depression, the age of which, based on floristic remains and spore-pollen assemblages, is upper Carboniferous. Possibly, its uppermost beds are lower Permian, but no higher than the Sakmarian Stage.

10. For the Kireyskaya formation, a middle Carboniferous age has been proven on the basis of numerous floristic remains. The floral assemblage attests to its belonging to the Bashkirian–Moscovian Stage.

11. It has been demonstrated that the Archalinsk formation, previously identified as a local stratigraphic subdivision, is a facies equivalent of part of the Kayraktinsk formation. Its identification within the volume of the lower Kiiminsk [subdivision] is considered inadvisable.

12. The Teniz formation is identified as a local stratigraphic subdivision, which replaces the upper beds of the Tersakkansk and the greater part of the Kayraktinsk formation in the eastern part of the Teniz depression.

13. The volumes of the Kayraktinsk, Kiiminsk, and Shoptykul formations have been refined. Rich paleontological material has been collected to substantiate their ages.

14. The lithological composition of the formations has been studied, and on this basis an exhaustive characterization of the lithological features of each formation and sub-formation has been provided. The lateral inconsistency of rock units with thicknesses of less than 100 m has been demonstrated. The most unstable characteristic is the color of the rock.

15. A tectonic scheme has been compiled for the entire studied territory, on which, in addition to fold structures, the role of fault dislocations is also shown.

16. The dependence of the fold structure of the sub-platform cover on the block structure of the basement has been established.

17. Within the limits of the Gayrekovsky and Artyakpeysky domes, drilling operations have revealed new, unfortunately minor, copper ore occurrences.

18. Based on analysis of the spatial distribution of copper mineralizations in the studied territory, regularities in their distribution within the sub-platform cover of the Teniz depression have been identified. The most important of these are considered to be: the confinement of copper occurrences to seven stratigraphic levels; the localization of ore occurrences in the basal and roof portions of red-colored copper-bearing formations; the placement of the largest occurrences at the boundaries of facies transitions; the confinement of all large occurrences to long-lived regional fault zones, which on one hand control the facies, and on the other create zones of enhanced permeability favorable for redistribution and concentration of copper; the confinement of ore occurrences to the limbs of anticlines, which most often represent flanks of paleouplifts with unstable facies; strict lithological control of mineralization, expressed in the confinement of copper ores to gray-colored sandstones in cross-sections where their ratio to red-colored [rocks] approaches unity, in any case less than 1:10; the confinement of the largest occurrences to areas of the sub-platform cover where the ratio of underlying and overlying gray-colored sequences to the ore-hosting red-colored deposits likewise approaches unity.

19. Areas have been identified in which, in light of the established regularities of ore occurrence distribution, general prospecting work is recommended. In doing so, areas of varying priority have been delineated.
