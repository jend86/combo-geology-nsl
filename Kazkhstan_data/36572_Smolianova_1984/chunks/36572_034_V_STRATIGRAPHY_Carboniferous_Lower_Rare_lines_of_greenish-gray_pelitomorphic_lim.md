The carbonate rocks of the Dalnensky horizon are represented
exclusively by limestones. There are two main groups among them,
both in the sections of the CAA and the north of the basin: a)
limestones with biomorphic remains and b) porphyritic limestones.
A rare variety of limestones, found only in the northern
sections, are crystalline limestones.

Limestones with biomorphic remains
in the south of the Teniz depression are represented by gray,
light gray peltomorphic differences.

The cementing peltomorphic carbonate is 50-70%. breeds.
The structure of the cementing mass is clumped or evenly

Limestones of a similar type are also common in the north of
the Nizhny Novorod depression. They are marked in the section
of the Archaly river (section A-28"), in the core of well 158 (section
158/160,5). A slight difference lies in the set of faunal remains, which
are mainly represented by segments of crinoids, fragments of bryozoan
colonies, shells of foraminifera, although ostracods and pelecypods are
also noted here. On the other hand, in the northern sections of
the Dalnensky horizon, typical biodetrite limestones are composed
mainly of eroded detrital particles (mainly segments of
crinoids) cemented with clear-crystalline calcite. In these rocks
, the sandy terrigenous admixture can reach 25%.

There is no clear line between limestones with biomorphic
remains and porphyritic limestones, since limestones of
the first variety with numerous porphyritic accretions are very
often found in sections of the far horizon of the Teniz depression.

Porphyritic limestones are widespread
both in sections of the southern and northern parts of the Teniz Basin. The

micro-granular or very fine-grained calcite. Microstructures are
usually lumpy. Porphyritic secretions are crystalline growths with
