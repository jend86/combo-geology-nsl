Kipshak formation ( C12k,0 )

The sediments of the Kipshak formation come to the surface in the
limits of the Kipshak trough, on the wings of the Shabdara anticline,
and are also exposed by the support-parametric wells 017 and 015 drilled
in the core of the Kokpektin uplift, In addition, the sediments of the
formation come to the surface and are exposed by wells on the north-
western and northern wings of the depression.

The formation is represented by a coastal-marine carbonate-terrigenous
thickness, gray- colored in the roof, sometimes variegated.

The sediment cover is usually good, and the horizons of
limestones and sandstones are low, but well-defined in terms of-

These are ridges that can be traced over a long distance.
The decipherability of the sediments is also good, the photon of the
formation has a characteristic oolic. Usually, white stripes of varying intensity,
color, and width stand out on a gray background (Fig. 14, 18).
There are two podovites in the formation. The Nizhnekipshak
podovite is a coarse interlayer of siltstones, sandstones, low-mass
limestone interlayers and carbonaceous siltstones. The upper one is
characterized by a more subtle interbedding of sandstones and siltstones,
in the lower half of which there are often carbonaceous and low- thickness
limestone layers, and porphyritic ones in the roof.
Bundles are well identified during mapping, and
with good security they can be easily mapped.
The lower boundary of the formation in the south of the region is drawn
along the base of a powerful sandstone horizon with spherical separation, with remnants
of flora.
