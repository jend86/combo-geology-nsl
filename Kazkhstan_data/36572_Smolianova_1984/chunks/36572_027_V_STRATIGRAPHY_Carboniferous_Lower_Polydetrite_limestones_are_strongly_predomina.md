Polydetrite limestones are strongly predominant. They
are generally similar to those described among the rocks of the
Simorinsky horizon. Their distinctive feature is the frequent uneven
recrystallization of both detritus and cement particles. Significant
terrigenous sandy-siltstone admixture (up to 40%) is not uncommon,
the main components of detritus are crinoids, bryozoans, brachiopods
, pelicypods, less often ostracods, foraminifera, and quite
often detritus particles are silicified.

Spicule limestones were found in the core of well 06.
These are usually light dense, macroscopically granular rocks,
completely composed of single-ray sponge spicules. The spicules
are 0.04-0.08 mm across and up to 0.8mm long. Most of
the spicules are calcified (replaced by monocrystalline calcite),
but there are many made with primary silica. Sometimes
there is a central channel in the spokes. The cement is porous,
crystalline calcite, characterized by simultaneous extinction
of well- known spicules and pores with calcite (a sign of
synchro replacement with cementation). Individual pores are made
with redeposited silica.

The deposits of the Kassinsky horizon are much smaller
than in the Simorinsky horizon. All of them seem to
be of the metasomatic type and are mainly associated
with clay rocks. They are installed in the core of wells 06 and 150.
These are gray, dark gray rocks and fine-grained, usually with
mottled and lenticular-spotted textures. They almost do not boil
from hydrochloric acid. Dolomite, composing from 40% to 90% of the
rock , is micrograined (grain sizes up to 0.5Mm); forms
small cubic crystals. Dolomite contains clayey matter, a
few but diverse oiogenic detritus, silica,
and terrigenous fragments.

A rare type of rock found only in well 06 is
dolomite-anhydrite rocks (sedimentation), similar
to those described above.
