**Mineral Composition of Rocks of the Aramdul Complex**

**A l a s k i t e - l i k e l e u c o c r a t i c granites** are the main rock type of the Aramdul complex in the described area. Macroscopically these are pink, light, sometimes yellowish rocks, practically free of mafic minerals. The rock texture is medium-grained, medium- to fine-grained, rarely porphyritic. The microstructure is hypidiomorphic-granular, granitic.

The main rock-forming minerals are quartz, potassium feldspar, and plagioclase. The quantitative mineral composition of the rocks of the Obalikol massif, calculated from a single thin section (No. 4324/1), is as follows: quartz – 42.8%, K-feldspar – 29.9%, plagioclase – 27.3%. More detailed studies within the Karatas massif show that mineral contents may vary within fairly wide ranges (in the same order): 34–47%; 21–40%; 23–37% (Grankin, 1970).

Quartz is xenomorphic, with rounded grains ranging in size from 0.6 to 4 mm, usually united into large aggregates.

Potassium feldspar is strongly xenomorphic. It forms broad tabular crystals of various sizes (up to 0.7–1 cm in porphyritic varieties). Sizes are usually less than 5 mm.

Perthitized. Perthites are ribbon-like and wedge-shaped, often forming rims along which K-feldspar grains are in mutual contact.

Plagioclase – grains from 0.4 to 4 mm in size (usually 1–1.5 mm). Idiomorphic tabular crystals. Polysynthetic twins (albite-type) are well developed; measurements of which give the plagioclase number (oligoclase 25). In the central parts of the crystals, sericitization is present.

Biotite is represented by individual platy segregations, not encountered in all thin sections (0.1×0.3; 0.5×0.8 mm).

Accessory minerals are also rare.

These are mainly individual segregations of ore mineral (magnetite) and acicular crystals of apatite.

**V e i n r o c k s o f t h e f i r s t s t a g e** are represented by fine-grained alaskite-like granites, aplite-like granites, and fine-grained biotite-bearing granites.
