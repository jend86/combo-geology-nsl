For the commission's review, the following materials were submitted:

1. General geological maps on a scale of 1:100000.
2. Cores of actual material on a scale of 1:100000.
3. Summary of paleontological sections.
4. Geostratigraphic sections.
5. Paleontological data.
6. Journals documenting excavations.
7. Geostratigraphic columns for boreholes.
8. Lithostratigraphic diagrams.
9. Collections of samples and organic residues.

The review of submitted materials reveals:

1. Geological work was conducted methodically and properly; the obtained materials are characterized by high quality.
2. General geological maps at a scale of 1:100000 are executed carefully and in detail. The detail of mapping at a scale of 1:200000 is adequate.
3. Core sections are thoroughly documented; both lower and search-and-mapping wells of the lateral organizations are fully documented (search wells 1–20 and search-and-mapping wells of the Turanian expedition).
4. Incomplete coverage of archival work on sheets [pages] -42-51, 52; completeness of documentation by the lateral organizations (search-mapping wells 1–20 and search-and-mapping wells of the Turanian expedition).
5. Geological investigations were conducted in accordance with the program in all boreholes. Color correlation investigations at 100%; they were completed with a complex of methods. The applied complex of methods ensures lithological-stratigraphic analysis of sections and identification of ores (sulfide) mineralization.
6. Insufficient scope of field work is evident in incomplete completeness and general quality of diagrams ITK-4 on well 147 and ITK-C on well 146.
