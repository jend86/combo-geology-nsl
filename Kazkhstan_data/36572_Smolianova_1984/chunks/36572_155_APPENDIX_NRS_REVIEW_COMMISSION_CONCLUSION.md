**CONCLUSION**

of the working commission of the Kazakh Branch of the NRS VSEGEI at KazIMSe on the review of the report of the West-Teniz PSP of the Central Geological Prospecting Expedition of PGO "Tsentrkazgeologiya" on the geological re-examination of the western part of the Teniz Depression at scale 1:200,000 for 1978–1984.

*(authors: E.M. Smolyaninova, V.V. Naumenko, et al.)*

The commission, comprising S.B. Bakirov (chairman), A.B. Li, Yu.V. Dmitrovsky, and V.V. Yugin, having reviewed the report, the reviews by V.Ya. Koshkin and N.A. Kalban, the minutes of the NTS of PGO "Tsentrkazgeologiya" dated 26.11.84, the expert reviews of the Kazakhstan Branch of the NRS by V.B. Makedon and I.P. Benevolonsky, as well as the opinion of KazRNSP expert V.V. Ovchinnikov, states:

**I.** The work of the West-Teniz party was conducted in accordance with the program of comprehensive re-examination of the Teniz Depression, the primary objective of which is the general assessment of the copper-bearing potential of the Upper Paleozoic sequences of the region.

The re-examination at scale 1:200,000 was carried out in the northwestern and northern parts of the Teniz Depression, characterized by widespread sheet-like occurrences of Cenozoic deposits, which necessitated the execution of a large volume of mapping boreholes and geophysical data analysis.

As a result of the work, issues of the geological structure of the studied area were detailed in terms of stratigraphy, tectonics, and mineral resources; a detailed characterization of the lithological composition of the Upper Paleozoic and Cenozoic deposits was provided; the age dating of the identified suites of Carboniferous and Permian rocks was established on the basis of macro- and microfauna materials and spore-pollen assemblages. The authors emphasize the significance of the block structure of the basement in the formation of structures of the semi-platform cover.

Primary attention in the study of mineral resources was devoted to the assessment of the copper-bearing potential of the intermediate level of the Teniz Depression, with the identification of 7 stratigraphic levels with copper mineralization, of which the Upper Devonian–Carboniferous horizons were classified as prospective, and the Permian horizons as low-prospective. Areas were identified for conducting prospecting works of the first, second, and third priority.

6

II. The Commission considers it necessary to note a number of shortcomings:

1) The geological assignment is not appended to the report, which makes it difficult to assess the completeness and quality of the tasks carried out by the performers.

2) There is no correlation of the work performed with the results of deep geological mapping at scale 1:200,000, carried out in the eastern part of the Teniz Depression (Petrilyak, 1980), in particular, of the boundaries of Carboniferous and Permian subdivisions. The results of geological surveys at scale 1:50,000 conducted within the Teniz Depression have been insufficiently utilized.

3) The stratigraphy for the northern, northwestern, and central districts of the studied territory has been developed to an insufficient degree, where the disappearance in the section of the Rusakov horizon — marking the most extensive marine transgression in Central Kazakhstan — does not correspond to the available factual materials.

4) In the middle–upper Paleozoic section, the designation of suites under a single name for the entire Teniz Depression (Kypshak, Tersakan) is unjustified due to the sharp variability of their lithological composition and the stratigraphic assignment of the suites to different parts of various zones, conditioned by the block structure of the basement.

5) The results of large-scale gravimetric, magnetic, and high-precision aeromagnetic surveys of recent years have not been utilized; data on quantitative interpretation of magnetic anomalies are absent, and the geological interpretation of geophysical materials does not correspond to the specified map scale.

6) The calculation of forecast resources of mineral deposits has been performed and compiled at a low methodological level: there is no clear description and justification of the calculation parameters.

7) The delineation of prospective areas of the first, second, and third priority is not supported by figures for reserves of forecast categories. Recommendations for conducting prospecting operations are not correlated with drilling volumes, nor with the contours of the areas for which the forecast resource calculations are being conducted.

III. The Commission proposes to the Kazakh Branch of NRS:

1) To consider that the work on the geological further study of the western part of the Teniz Depression at scale 1:200,000 has been performed in a methodologically correct manner, at a high professional level. The compiled maps conform to the scale of 1:200,000.

7

2) To accept the report of the West-Teniz Party with a rating of "good", obligating the authors to supplement it prior to submission to the archives with:

a) borehole catalogues (or to provide the coordinates of the boreholes in the columns of the latter);

b) specialist conclusions on the age dating of lists of fossil organic remains.

3) In accordance with the conclusion of KazRNSP expert V.V. Ovchinnikov, the prognostic resources proposed by the authors for the studied territory under category P₃ are not to be approved.

4) To consider the question of the advisability of compiling a structural-metallogenic zoning map in reports on geological survey works.

5) To recommend PGO "Tsentrkazgeologiya" that, when drafting projects for geological-metallogenic works in the Teniz depression, the question of the advisability of identifying new suites be specifically examined, taking into account the results of previously conducted survey and thematic investigations.

# REVIEW

of the report of the West-Teniz Prospecting-Survey Party of PGO "Tsentrkazgeologiya" on the work carried out in 1978–84. "Geological re-study at scale 1:200,000 over the area of sheets M-42-XXXIII (I26-B,G; I37-B,G; I38); M-42-XXXIV (I28-G; I39; I40); M-42-XXXY (I29-B,G; I4I); M-42-II (3-B,G; 4; I5; I6); M-42-VI (7-A,B; 8-A,B); M-42-III; M-42-V (9); M-42-VII (26,38); M-42-VIII; M-42-IX (29,4I); M-42-XIV (5I,52,64 A,B); M-42-V"

**Authors:** E.M. Smolyaninova, V.V. Naumenko, A.M. Glukhov, L.P. Deriga, G.V. Filatova, and others.

---

The report consists of three volumes of text (354, 338, and 357 pp.) and two volumes of graphic appendices (109 drawings on 129 sheets).

The West-Teniz Prospecting-Survey Party in 1978–1984 conducted group geological re-study at scale 1:200,000 of the northern and western parts of the Teniz Depression, which formed part of a comprehensive programme for evaluating the copper-bearing potential of Upper Palaeozoic formations of this territory. Accordingly, during field investigations and in the report, primary attention was devoted to the study of Carboniferous–Permian terrigenous deposits and the copper mineralisation of the cupriferous sandstone formation associated with them.

The introduction presents information on the volumes of the principal types of work completed by the party. The party's work plan was largely fulfilled, with the exception of a shortfall of approximately 9,300 linear metres of prospecting-mapping drilling and profile geophysical work totalling 12,604 roubles, attributable to organisational difficulties and stipulated by the corresponding protocols.

The report lacks the party's geological assignment; at the same time it contains numerous interim protocols and acts, some of which could have been omitted.

In the chapter "Geological-Geophysical State of Knowledge," the results of preceding geological investigations are examined in detail, and in particular a critical analysis of existing concepts regarding the stratigraphy of the Carboniferous–Permian is carried out. At the same time, geophysi-

physical study has been examined very briefly (1.5 pages). This is perhaps the only section of the report that suffers from incompleteness and schematic description. Not considered, and not even mentioned in the list of references, are the early regional works of Kazgeofiztresta from the early 1950s (L.Ya. The appended catalogue of geophysical study essentially duplicates the list of archival literature and does not allow one to judge the representativeness and main results of the geophysical investigations.

A positive aspect of the investigations carried out by the party is the extensive use of geophysical materials from preceding surveys (MOV seismic exploration, gravity and magnetic field maps) and gravimetric and magnetometric observations along individual profiles that accompanied the field work. The data presented in the report attest to the good quality of the field geophysical material and to the accuracy of observations meeting the applicable instructional requirements. It should only be noted that the reference gravimetric network provided for in the project for profile surveying was not established, and accordingly the reduction of observations to the national datum was not carried out, and the survey was completed at a relative (conditional) datum level. Appended to the report are maps of gravity field elements and magnetic field at a scale of 1:500,000, which makes it difficult to compare them with the geological maps presented at a scale of 1:200,000. Both maps are highly schematic and do not make adequate use of materials from more detailed surveys, and in particular the high-precision aeromagnetic survey conducted by the Nikolayevsk aerogeological-geophysical expedition under the leadership of V.V. Yugin in the late 1970s – early 1980s.

Based on MOV seismic exploration data, a series of cross-sections and isohypse maps for three reflecting horizons have been constructed. The available seismic material does not permit more detailed subdivision, in connection with which the presented cross-sections suffer from oversimplification and schematism. Quantitative calculations were carried out along interpretation profiles using the well-known methodology taking into account petrophysical parameters; primarily the curves of 4 *[illegible]* loc. were interpreted.

It is unclear how the curves *W*₂₂ were used in the interpretation of individual profile segments, and moreover, the scale provided for these curves in mGal is erroneous. The expression "gravimagnetic field" should also be corrected.

The physical properties of rocks are examined very thoroughly in the report, primarily those of Upper Paleozoic formations. The density and magnetic susceptibility of Upper Paleozoic sedimentary rocks have been determined from a large number of core samples and statistically processed with calculation of weighted-mean values. The physical parameters of these strata presented are quite reliable and may be used as reference standards. It is only necessary to additionally correct the tables appended to the report and eliminate the errors contained therein. Of considerable interest is the information on the elevated magnetic susceptibility of individual cover rocks, and in particular of magnetite-bearing sandstones. Apparently, judging from the materials presented, siderite-bearing siltstones also occur within the investigated area; according to our determinations in the Karaganda Basin, these are associated with carbonaceous rocks and are likewise characterized by elevated magnetic susceptibility on the order of 200–300·10⁻⁶ CGS units. This question requires further study (distribution, thicknesses of rock strata with elevated magnetic susceptibility) in order to account for the role of sedimentary cover rocks in the structure of the magnetic field (especially during high-precision magnetic surveys). Unfortunately, in the expedition (as in other production organizations of Central Kazakhstan), determinations of such an important parameter as remanent magnetization have not been properly established, although instrumentation for mass measurements of this parameter can be acquired. The physical properties of Lower-Middle Paleozoic and Proterozoic rocks, which have limited distribution on the western periphery of the study area, are examined less thoroughly, mainly based on materials from outside organizations. When calculating weighted-mean density values for these formations, it is most appropriate to use as a basis the percentage ratio of the petrophysical rock varieties composing them. The data on ancient strata presented in the report require some refinements: noteworthy is the inconsistency among different authors regarding rocks similar in composition and age, and the inflated values of density and magnetic susceptibility for terrigenous rocks of the Ulutau series.

The main chapter of the report, occupying about half of its volume, is "Stratigraphy," with primary attention devoted to Upper Paleozoic formations. The description of older, pre-Famennian formations is provided mainly based on the work of predecessors; however, even in this section the authors present a very representative summary of existing concepts and provide their critical assessment. In particular, they raise a well-founded question regarding the younger (Early Paleozoic) age of the Bratolyubovskaya and Garshinsky suites, which is consistent with the rejuvenation of the age of the siliceous-diabase formation in other regions of Central Kazakhstan in connection with the latest finds of faunal remains, primarily conodonts.

A very complete, highly informative description is given of the quasi-platform formations of the Carboniferous–Permian. As was justifiably noted in the reviews and minutes of the preceding review, this section is distinguished by a high level of description, abundance, detail, and diversity of the presented material, and may be regarded both as a monographic synthesis and as a reference work. Rich paleontological material has been collected to substantiate the age of the Kayraktinsky, Shoptykul'sky, Kiyminskaya, Tersakkanskaya, and other suites. It should also be noted that the geological maps are of high quality, with high detail and precision in the subdivision of Upper Paleozoic and Lower Carboniferous sequences.

Sufficiently completely and competently, based mainly on the materials of predecessors, a description is given in the corresponding chapter of the report of the intrusive complexes (Zerendinsky, Krykkuduksky, Aralaul'sky, Ishimbaisky, Borovsky, Balkashinskiy), which have limited distribution on the western and northern flanks of the Teniz Depression.

The tectonic structure of the northern and western parts of the Teniz Depression and its framework is examined in the report at two levels: the quasi-platform cover and the Caledonian basement. On the tectonic scheme at scale 1:500,000, folded structures of the 1st and 2nd order are identified in this part of the depression; volcanogenic-sedimentary, sedimentary, and intrusive formations and fault zones are shown. The authors draw a well-founded conclusion regarding the dominant role of block movements of the basement with significant vertical displacement amplitudes along faults in the formation of the tectonic structures of the depression—

faults, expressed in particular in deep-seated folds of the quasi-platform cover. On the presented scheme it would be advisable to identify intermediate-order structures, which are discussed one way or another in the text, by combining individual muldes and synclines into larger subdivisions — syneclises or troughs (for example, the Kiiyminsky trough), and anticlines into anticlises or uplifts (of the Atbasar–Shunkyrkolsky uplift type). Additional information would be provided on the scheme by isohypses of the base of the quasi-platform cover, but, apparently, the available geophysical materials are insufficient for their construction.

On the scheme of the Caledonian basement structure of the depression at a scale of 1:500,000, deep-seated faults are identified, the blocks separated by faults are shown, and the presumed material composition of these blocks is indicated. The scheme under consideration will apparently be further developed as new factual material and broader coverage of the study area are obtained, but the very fact of compiling such a scheme merits approval. It should be noted that the legend to this scheme should more clearly reflect the tectonic position of the authors; doubt is raised by the identification of an independent Baikal folding episode with the assignment of the Kokchetav suite to it, which is regarded by the majority of researchers as a platform formation; the Bratolyubovskaya and Garshinsky suites should more correctly be considered (taking into account the Lower Paleozoic age assumed by the authors) as areas of early geosynclinal Caledonian folding (and not Epibaikal activation), etc. Apparently, on the scheme it would be advisable to combine individual small blocks into larger first-order blocks, which would to some extent reflect the general structural framework of the basement. In particular, according to geophysical data, it would be possible, in the reviewer's opinion, to tentatively delineate beneath the quasi-platform cover the continuation (with a bend in the north-easterly direction) of the Ulutau structures.

The overwhelming majority of the deep intrusive massifs identified on the scheme are regarded as intrusions of essentially granodioritic composition, tentatively of the Krykkuduk complex. It is suggested that some of them, marked by sharply expressed minima of the gravitational field, may be assigned to intrusions of essentially granitic composition of the Balkashinsky or Borovsky complexes.

In the section "Mineral Resources, Regularities of Their Distribution and Prognostic Assessment," the main focus is given to the mineralization of the cupriferous sandstone formation. In addition, polymetallic mineralization, silver ore occurrences, and among non-metallic mineral resources — rodusite, coal, and construction materials — are discussed more briefly. Appended to the report are mineral resource maps, forecast maps, and a catalog of mineral resources.

For completeness of description, it would be useful to briefly address the prospects for oil-and-gas potential and bauxite potential of the western half of the Teniz Depression, given the large volume of geological-geophysical investigations conducted here in this direction and the relevance of the problem.

Of particular interest is the identification, through geological prospecting work in the Kayraktin Suite of the Taldyk syncline, of a thick bed of calcareous siltstones with rodusite mineralization, similar to analogous occurrences of rodusite at Kumola and Ushbulak in the Dzhezkazgan Depression. The report provides substantiated recommendations for conducting prospecting work for rodusite on the northern limb of the Taldyk syncline.

The main volumes of prospecting and assessment work were carried out by the party to determine the copper-bearing prospects of the western half of the Teniz Depression. On the basis of the work performed, the principal factors controlling the distribution of copper occurrences of the cupriferous sandstone type were identified: stratigraphic, lithological-facies, structural, and geochemical. Seven stratigraphic levels were identified to which copper occurrences are confined. The confinement of mineralization to gray-colored sandstones at the top of red-colored formations on the limbs of anticlines was noted, as well as the placement of the largest occurrences at the boundaries of facies transitions in the zones of long-lived regional faults.

Mountain-sampling and drilling operations provided a preliminary assessment of a large number of copper ore occurrences, which, due to low concentrations of useful components and insignificant thickness of ore bodies, are classified as sub-economic, that is, the work confirmed the negative assessment of these occurrences given by K.I. Satpayev back in the 1950s. In conclusion, a prognostic-perspective assessment of the investigated territory is given

with calculation of forecast resources under category P₃. It is noteworthy that the areas of first-priority prospecting include sites of polymetallic and rodingite mineralization, while areas of copper mineralization have been assigned to the second and third priority levels.

It appears that the negative results of prospecting work have, to some degree, predetermined the lack of clarity in recommendations for the further continuation of investigations in the region under consideration. As regards geophysical investigations, certain prospects here would be offered by the latest advances in the field of seismic exploration in western Kazakhstan, which have been termed seismostratigraphy — employing the modern capabilities of seismic exploration (CDP method, computer processing) with the aim of studying the sequence of sedimentation, lithological composition, and features of the formation of sedimentary sequences (and, in particular, the identification of buried alluvial fans, paleodelta cones, reef structures, paleochannels, etc.) — as well as three-dimensional modeling with the drilling of parametric boreholes.

In the concluding chapter of the report, wireline logging investigations in boreholes using a complex of modifications — KS, PS, MEP, GK, GTK-S, DS — are examined; these provided assistance in the lithological subdivision of borehole sections with identification of ore intervals, primarily for intervals with poor core recovery. Geological columns together with wireline logging curves are presented for all drilled prospecting-mapping boreholes.

The submitted report on geological detailed study of the western half of the Teniz Depression at a scale of 1:200,000 is favorably distinguished by the comprehensiveness of investigations, the high quality of field work, and the high scientific level of data processing and presentation. The report is recommended for approval with a rating of **"excellent"**.
