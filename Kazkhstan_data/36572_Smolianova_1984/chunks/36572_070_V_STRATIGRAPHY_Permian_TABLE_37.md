The exit from the omission of the lower incision of the Ashinsky suite shows that the subtcultural flora is distributed on the studied territory in two subsections: lower subcultural and upper subcultural, which in turn in separate structures are subdivided into layers. In the lower subcultural subsection two layers are distinguished, and in the upper subcultural subsection - three layers.

The lower subcultural podsvita is distinguished by all structures, its lower boundary corresponds to the underlying formation and is described below. The upper boundary of the podsvita is conducted on the podsolite layer of greenish-gray sandstones with prolems and lineations of channeling and coarse-bearing sandstones, which are more coarse-grained and non-colored layer along the row.

The lower subcultural podsvita is characterized by the presence of "authigenic" minerals, the quantity of which is significantly increasing in the lower part of the podsvita. This part is especially clearly distinguished in the Ashinsky and Aukelbek mulyds. In the latter one it is isolated as a separate layer of the lower subcultural podsvita. In the Ashinsky suite this layer is clearly distinguished in sections, but does not map on the surface.

In contrast to the overlying upper Ashininsky podsvita, the lower subcultural podsvita (see Chart 49, Sheet 2) contains a larger quantity of interburrowed, medium-burrowed sandstones, practically in all sections.

In the lower subcultural podsvita in the Khiminsky group of mlyds, in contrast to the upper Ashininsky podsvita, seroserpent rows are observed. In the Ashinsky suite the section of the lower subcultural podsvita becomes more seroserpent than in the Khiminsky light, and in the Aukelbek suite the lower subcultural podsvita retains its red-colored nature to the upper subcultural podsvita.

The upper subcultural podsvita differs from the lower subcultural in the significant seroserpentity of the layer and individual layers. Seroserpent rows in this podsvita are more numerous than red-colored. The podsolite layer is difficult to determine, and in the cross-section where sections were made, there is a very thin re-laying of rows as in the light.

In the composition of the Shor-Shutkul Suite, terrigenous rocks do not differ from Kriminak and Karatinkinsk. However, they are distinguished only by better safety of clastic material, and even more than in Kriminak Suite, by the presence of relatively fine detritus. From the Karatinkinsk Suite they differ in that in them, even in sandstones, there is more clay remaining.

Carbonate rocks of the Shor-Shutkul Suite in its section differ from purely carbonate to clayey-carbonate and carbonate-clayey.

In the composition of the Suite there are crystalline fine-grained and flowering minerals, cemented by an aggregate of calcite with lenses of clay-hydromica composition.

Porphyroid minerals in the section of the Suite appear twice. In the basis of the Suite, "porphyroid" minerals have large porphyroid excretions in the form of rhombi, parallelograms, rectangles elongated and elongated-lenticular plaques with pyramidal endings. Their sizes up to 2.5-3 mm. The distribution of minerals "porphyroid" by section is uneven. Often they form openings, less often. Sometimes the layer is so saturated with the tribe that the individual sections of the layer sinter together, forming medial gneisses. Porphyroid excretions are visible both on the weathered surface and on fresh sections, and in the core of the aquifer. They under the microscope reveal lycocrystalline, and aggregative structure, but also in that and another case are filled with calcite. In the "porphyroid" excretions from the Aukulbek depression in singular porphyroid excretions, executed with a tonkocristalline calcite aggregate. According to the results of a rentgeno-structural analysis, minerals from the group of zeolites are detected - phillipsite, natrolite, analcime.

The species Ruflorla dernavini (Heub.) appears in the Urals in Artinskian deposits and reaches up to the tops of the Kungurian stage - Ufimian stage. In Siberia this species appears earlier, in Kazakhstan it is already found in the Sakmarian Suite.

The species Parascalamites decoratus is characteristic for the Lower Permian, but is also known in Ufimian and Kazakstanian deposits. The genus appears in the Lower Permian, but essentially characterizes the Upper Permian. According to S.B. Meleon, the return of deposits characterized by plant remains fragments is more frequent for the Upper Permian.
