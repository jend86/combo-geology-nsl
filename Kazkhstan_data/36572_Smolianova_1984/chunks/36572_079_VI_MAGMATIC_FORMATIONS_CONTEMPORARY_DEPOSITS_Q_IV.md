CONTEMPORARY DEPOSITS (Q IV)

Contemporary deposits by genesis are divided into surface and alluvial.
Among contemporary alluvial deposits one can identify two principal types: channel and terrace alluvium. Terrace alluvium always shows predominantly granular structure, in many cases in the overall and divisional allocation of channels.

The riverbed alluvium contains all the peculiarities associated with the hydrogeological regime of the rivers in the surveyed territory. Many rivers in the region migrate seasonally. The conditions of alluvial deposits on the bed of their valleys differ quite little from the floodplains. Rivers often do not have a morphologically expressed channel. These are determined by the steepness of the natural slope.

The composition and structure of the alluvium on one and the same river does not remain uniform at different sections of the valley. In the area of the lower course of the Ishim River, as in the contemporary channel, so in the abandoned riverbed, alluviums are characterized by interbedded sands with angular pebble proluvium, sometimes interspersed with dampened ancient clay. The upper horizons of the alluvium, both here and in the upper parts of the valleys, are distinguished by angular fragments, often darkened by surface weathering. Angular fragments are often cemented and occasionally cemented.

Contemporary alluvial deposits of the right tributaries of the Ishim River differ yet more markedly in composition from the alluvium of the Ishim River itself. In them, especially on small streams, angular fragments and suspended material predominate. The pebble porous material, in its upper portion consisting of coarse-grained gravel, and in its lower portion of coarser-grained gravel zone. Portions of proluvium, enriched with humus. The lower proluvium and sand layers occur only in the lower portion of the valley. On the rivers Daman-i, Lembi-Kairakty, Archaly, Konur, and Shilaidzhina, witnesses of the channel, glacially cut into separate gorges. On such contemporary alluvial sections, practically absent, together with the only small accumulations of small sandy and gravelly-pebble deposits on the river bottom. Such participation of practically absent contemporary alluvium is characteristic for the intersecting rubles of the Saryshagan river basin.

The bed of the Gerosukan River in the middle and upper course is characterized by coarse-grained alluvium. Its upper substitution is represented by slotted pockets with angular pebble cuttings with tightly ground prolong and multiply supported proluvium and lenses of sand and gravel. Lower riverbed horizons are characterized by different-sized pebbles and gravel. Large rocky stony deposits of alluvium acquire a more granular composition. Alluvial sands and silts constitute the bulk of its valley, and also cover the river bottom.

Contemporary lacustrine deposits are distributed across all higher elevations of the territory. Members of the Paleogene more widespread than those of other ages, and sometimes (on Lake Balkhash) Paleogene sediments occupy a continuous area. Young lacustrine deposits located in ancient river valleys. Young chestnut soils preserved near eroded slopes adjacent to the water table and include the upper arid steppe formations for supplementary supply of confined aquifers, or more often, in more or less sealed conditions in lakes. In the steppe zones and on the floors of descending lakes recent lake sediments are distributed. In most cases they predominate in terms of loose, recently consolidated sediments and organic matter content. Particularly characteristic of this role are lake sediments for populations having loose sediment cover, which predominantly represents granitoid and silt-like formations (in the Neogene-Quaternary strata). However, even in these conditions in the coastal zones of larger bodies of water more widespread sandy sediments are encountered, which are the result of obtaining coastal cliffs, coloring in sizes corresponding to the clay-sand role. In the sand-silt sediments observed on the shore of the studied region, everywhere, where near landslide deposits are located. On lake shores (opposite Lake Balkhash) coastal sandstone is weakly sorted or unsorted, often correctly sorted sands, forming moraines (up to 2 m) coastal shafts.

Contemporary lake sediments of this part of the region relate to widespread. First, silt sediments located on all lake shores. Third, sands, silt and pebbles are encountered in the dry season of part of the small saline lakes for dams of saline brine.

Paleozoic-Cenozoic lake in the lower strata of the continuous territory occupies a significant place in the dry season and against the upper part of the territory is strongly saturated with salt. In those water bodies in this period exhibits either arroyo boiling, regional groundwater supply, salt cake precipitation (or on separate plots saline-sodic soils and salt grass), or more often limestone silt.

Regarding the data for determining the volume of modern lake sediments: no. In large lake sediments it can reach considerable magnitude.
