represent white, yellow, red rocks with a waxy, glassy luster and a subconchoidal fracture. In fresh section they are light gray or yellowish, sometimes spotted with ferruginous pink or red streaks. Grain size 0.3–0.6 cm for sandstones, 0.8–1.2 cm for gravelites. The rocks represent an aggregate of quartz sub-rounded grains with siliceous-quartz, siliceous-ferruginous cement of the type of co-precipitation, regeneration, and pore-filling. On weathered surfaces they acquire an orange, yellow coloration and a distinctive varnish of desert tan type.

**Opaline rocks** — white, porcelain-like siliceous formations, massive, non-bedded.

The thickness of the Upper Oligocene deposits ranges from 0 to 40 m. The variation in thickness is explained both by uneven accumulation of the sandy-clayey sequence and by later repeated erosion events.

Since no fossil organic remains, with the exception of silicified tree trunks, have been found, the age of the sequence is determined by its stratigraphic position. It lies above the Chilantinskaya Formation of the Middle Oligocene and is overlain by Lower Miocene gypsiferous clays. Consequently, the age range of the studied deposits is strictly constrained: Middle Oligocene–Lower Miocene. In analogous deposits of the eastern part of the Tenizkaya Depression, where the described deposits can be traced without interruption, T.V. Pogodaeva (1959) determined a spore-pollen assemblage in which pollen of angiosperms predominates over pollen of gymnosperms and spores. Among angiosperms, pollen of broad-leaved trees dominates: Juglandaceae (9.5–25%), Fagus (0–20%); less common are Pterocarya, Carpinus, Acer, Castanea, Liquidambar and Fraxinus. Pollen of evergreen and subtropical plants constitutes 10%: Ilex, Nyssa, Magnoliaceae and Myrtaceae. Among conifers, pollen of Taxodium, Glyptostrobus, Cedrus, Pinaceae predominates. Pollen of herbaceous plants and spores occur only rarely. The assemblage, in her opinion, indicates an Upper Oligocene age for the deposits containing it.

Thus, the age of the above-described deposits, based on stratigraphic position and analogy with neighboring regions, is accepted as Upper Oligocene.
