The total thickness of the Tersakan Formation in the section amounts to 1055 m. The upper boundary of the formation in borehole C3 is identified with great difficulty; to determine it, correlation of the borehole section with the surface is necessary, which has been done during detailed study. The boundary of the upper Tersakan Formation is provisionally taken at a level 100–110 m below the stromatolitic limestone, encountered at a depth of 99 m. The stromatolitic limestone in this area is clearly and unambiguously mapped at the surface, and is clearly identifiable on photographic materials of all scales from detailed to space imagery and plans at a scale of 1:200,000. Parallel to it at the surface, a bed of brecciated limestones with ore mineralization is traced, along which, during geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000 (Smolyaninova, 1970), the lower boundary of the upper transitional unit of the Vladimirova Formation was drawn. This transitional unit is now, after study of the stratotype of the Kayrakt Formation, assigned by us to the Kayrakt Formation. The boundary between the Tersakan and Kayrakt formations is drawn along the horizon of brecciated ore-bearing limestones.

The cross-section of the Tersakkan Formation across the area is, as noted above, highly variable. Even within the reference region, it differs significantly between various structures, as vividly illustrated by the correlations on Drawing 49, Sheets 2, 3, where the cross-sections of the reference region are given for the Sarbobinskoye, Kenenskoye, Aksuyskaya anticlines, exploratory wells T-20, 01, 05, 03, 018, 019, and in the zone of the Kipshakskoye flexure. The cross-sections are easily read from the drawings; therefore, so as not to burden the report with their descriptions, we ask that they be examined on Drawing 49.

In the reference region, compared to the stratotypic cross-sections, the following is observed:

1. In the south-eastward direction – an increase in formation thickness, an increase in the role of conglomerates in the section, and a reduction in grey-coloured varieties.

2. In the north-eastward direction – a significant reduction in thickness, reduction of the role of conglomerates to a minimum, and an increase in the quantity of grey-coloured varieties in the upper part of the section.

3. In the westward and north-westward direction – a slight reduction in thickness, reduction of the role of conglomerates in the upper part of the section, and reduction of grey-coloured beds in the section to their complete disappearance.

4. In the northward direction – a reduction in thickness, reduction of the role of conglomerates in the section to zero, and a sharp increase in grey-coloured beds in the upper part of the formation. Such is the variation in composition of the formation in the reference region. The variation across the entire studied territory is illustrated by Fig. 26 and Drawing 49. For Zone II, the reference cross-section from well T-20 is characteristic. For Zone III – cross-sections from wells 135, M-45, 118 – for the Burunsayskoye uplift; all cross-sections along the southern and northern limbs of the Marinovskoye anticline, as well as along the north-eastern limb of the Taldykskoye syncline.
