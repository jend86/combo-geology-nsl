On map sheet M-42-XY, it is recommended to drill three deep boreholes for copper prospecting. Two boreholes are located on the northeastern limb of the Kenen anticline. The prospects of this structure are associated with deposits of the Tersakkansk suite, which are potential hosts of Dzhezkazgan-type mineralization. According to data from A.A. Gostev (1969), deltaic facies favorable for copper accumulation can be traced to the northeast of the Kenen ore occurrence. A flexure zone extending from southwest to northeast is also observed in this area. All these indicators suggest the possibility of intersecting industrial copper ores at greater depths with prospecting boreholes.

The first borehole is located 5.0 km to the northwest of borehole No. 05, in which industrial copper ores (0.89%) were intersected at a depth of 655.8 m. In the northeastern direction from borehole No. 05, according to data from B.P. Krasnovoron (1980), the cumulative thickness of grey-colored rocks increases in the productive part of the Tersakkansk suite section, which enhances the prospectivity of the Tersakkansk horizon for copper exploration to the northwest of borehole No. 05.

The second borehole is located along the profile of the first and is offset from it down-dip by 4.0 km. Here it is proposed to intersect two ore-bearing horizons. The first ore-bearing horizon is Lower Permian at the facies transition from grey-colored formations of the Kairaktin suite to the red-colored Tersakkansk suite. The second ore-bearing horizon is Upper Carboniferous (Tersakkansk–Dzhezkazgan type of mineralization). The depth of intersection of the base of the Tersakkansk suite, according to seismic survey data, is 1000.0 m (borehole No. 1) and 1500 m (borehole No. 2).

The following borehole is located in the southeastern part of map sheet M-42-XY, at the intersection node of a flexure fold of submeridional strike and a fault zone of sublatitudinal strike, established from seismic survey data. The recommended borehole, within the zone of tectonic disturbances and at greater depths, is expected to intersect sediments of deltaic facies, with which the rich copper ores at the Kenen copper occurrence are associated. According to seismic survey data, the deposits of the Tersakkansk suite favorable for copper localization are located at a depth of approximately 2000 m.
