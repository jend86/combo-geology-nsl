LITHOLOGICAL COMPOSITION OF DAIRSKOYE SUITE IS UNIFORM.
The main part of its section consists of conglomerate, gravels, pebbles and aleurolites. In addition, fine-grained sandstones and tuffaceous rocks are occasionally found.
CONGLOMERATES AND GRAVELS CHARACTERIZE ANCIENT AND MIDDLE SECTIONS OF THE SUITE. The rocks of this suite differ from the color of the cement deposition and vary from red to light-red and gray. The width of the section is characterized by a wide range of clastic material, interspersion of conglomerates of trachyte, gabbro, as well as porphyry. This compose grain-tuffaceous conglomerate composition. Arenitic and metamorphic rocks, bulkhanites of the base, middle and lower composition, products of disintegration of granitoids are present. According to the section, the grain composition becomes increasingly in the direction of increase of sedimentation content of bulkhanic deposits.

PEBBLES AND ALEUROLITES - rocks distributed throughout the suite of Dairskoye. The color of the pebbles varies from red to light-gray and nearly white. Granulometric spread: limonadipoliolite, cosoliolite. Characteristic is the separate distinctness. The size of bulkhanic pebbles varies - from small to coarse. Generally, weak sorting is widely distributed among rocks comprising the granulometric composition (fine-grained, coarse-grained and etc.). The degree of bulkhanic sediments like a rule is dense. DIAGENESIS IN THE CARBONIFEROUS. At an early stage of diagenesis, the rocks completely contain limestone (section in 1.50). In the upper parts of the section, 15-25% of the rocks, and sometimes up to 50%, especially in the upper parts of the section, undergo leaching. The petrogenetic composition of the rocks demonstrates that in the lower section, the rocks have a higher content of feldspar fragments. The chemical composition shows increased content of lithoid fragments, as well as increased feldspar content in the lower seams of carbonate material. The cement content of the rocks is increased due to the lack of substantial porosity in the rocks), siltstone, siderite.

Carbonate cement is characterized in terms of cement calcification, as well as in composition. It predominates in the form of calcite, hydro-silicate-chlorite cement, varying from light to semi-opaque. The porosity is not very widely distributed; semi-opaque and carbonate cement is characteristic. Carbonate is usually weakly crystalline, which corrodes fragments well. Regeneration cement in general is not characteristic, though it is required in certain cases.

Diagenesis has generally affected red and brown tones. It is characterized by poor separation of carbonate material, with visible precipitation in the cutting and porosity in the interstices. There is sharp variable dolomitization of the core and pore space in the sections. Cement marl, found in H, is often hydro-silicate-chloritic. Often noted is porosity, determined by the different composition of the cement (reduction in the cutting and iron content in the main section).

The reflections of the sulfiferous horizon emerge at the level of the top of the Kipchak suite. In addition, they are revealed by normal-parametric profiles O2 and O6 (sheet 49, sheet 10) drilled by the Dzezkazgan GRD in the northern part of the forecast area on the southeastern slope of Sardonkalyksk antikline.

The abundance of the sulfiferous horizon is good. The size is represented by small terrigenous deposits with lines of limestones and dolomites. The thickness of the beds usually 1-2 m, rarely 5-7 m. Their extension is 1-1.5 m, sometimes up to 2 km. Such a break pattern suggests that the output of the sulfiferous horizon breed on the surface usually forms a slightly elevated area where clearly defined high and low ridges and hills with sloping exits appear, and further with eluvial washings of limestone and dolomite remains.

The character of the exit of the breed on the surface also depends on the angle of dip of the layers. With a relatively shallow dip, the character of the ridge corresponds to a gentle shape. With a steeper dip, the character of the outputs is different: on the surface, one or two wide ridges are found, on which narrow strips of limestone and dolomite outcrops are visible.

The structure of Famenian deposits is also different. During a shallow dip, they give a characteristic series of photons with white streaks and shadows (fig. 13). During a steep dip, the photons are somewhat different: wide ridges are distinguished in the form of elongated light and dark stripes and white stripes on a sepia background (fig. 14). By photon, Famenian deposits are sharply distinguished from lower Paleozoic rocks of dairine thickness. From the exits of breeds of the simolitic horizon, they practically do not differ.

The total power of the horizon in this section is 192.3 m. On the southwestern flank of the Saryshagan anticline, in sections 02 and 06, the power of the sulfur-bearing horizon is significantly reduced. Here it reaches 40 m (cf. sheet 49, list 10). Attempts to identify analogues of the sulfur-bearing horizon in more northern regions of the Teniz basin were undertaken, unsuccessfully, by the preceding investigators. Thus, in the boundaries of the Obalkupskaya anticline (Zh-42-XIV) by Gaev V.V. and others (1980), to the Samesko department was attributed the entire sequence of sandstones and limestones, separated by squamines U-3c and U-2c. However, the definition of the fauna from this interval in the report contradicts such an interpretation of the age of the layers. Based on these definitions, they refer it to the lower department of the Turkic-Arudian layer (see corresponding section).

In the second region, where a sample showing evidence of phenomena of faunally mature age is distinguished as Cretaceous anticline. Here it was assigned to a bed of blue-green dolomitic limestones with argillites and limestones covered by G-I (Ranniku, 1960).

In a repeated sampling of faunistically material from section 015, covering the same interval of the section, as G-I, a complete fauna complex was identified, in the opinion of S.B. Namutovich, having an unambiguously early Cretaceous age. This fact, as well as the position in the section and the lithologic character of the layers, allow us to associate the data interval of the section of the Cretaceous anticline with the Simorinsk horizon of the cambrian coal system (see corresponding section). An analogical picture occurs also within the boundaries of the Shaidaro anticline. Summarizing the presented data, we can conclude that carbonate-terrigenous formations of the sulfur-bearing horizon are distributed within the boundaries of the described territory very limitedly, and the area of their distribution extends to the north and northwest further into the zone of the Mladinsko-Utusky flexure.
