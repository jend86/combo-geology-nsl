** LOWER DEPARTMENT

The lower department of the coal-bearing system within the studied territory is represented by a complex of marine, predominantly calcareous-carbonate and carbonate-terrigenous deposits. Its composition includes formations of Turanian and Liassic clays.

Turanian clay

The stratigraphic relationships of the Turanian clay are distinguished by the formation of the lower clay in the composition of Simorinian, Cassinian horizons and unbroken and intraformational deposits; the formation of the upper clay, lying on a rusackian horizon, is divided into two lithological packages. Moreover, for a significant part of the Teniz Depression, we distinguish unbroken clay deposits - an opescent suite.

According to the lithological composition of the breeds of the Simoronsky horizon, it is possible to subdivide it into three large groups: terrigenous, carbonate, and sulfate.

Terrigenous breeds, representing sandstones, siltstones, and argillites, play a subordinate role in the section of the Simoronsky horizon. They are confined, in the main, to lower parts (the Ippyaksky section). The isolated weight of them among described deposits increases in the northern direction (approximately equidistant 015).

Psammites and alevrolites are predominantly fine-grained. The sizes of clastic grains vary within the limits 0.05–0.28; 0.04–0.16; 0.15–0.8; 0.08–0.4; 0.2–1mm. The degree of sorting varies, ranging from fine to coarse. Uniformity of grains average to poor. Most favorably quartz grains are distributed in separate cases to 65-70% of clasts. Isolated spati and clasts of breeds, as a rule, poorly distinguished from the cementing substance. Examples: carbonate clasts, occasionally biogenics, oolites, chert.

Oolitic and oolitiferous rocks:

Oolitic and oolite-bearing artifacts form separate layers among rocks of the simorinian horizon in the northern regions of the Kishidzhonskaya depression.

Oolitic artifacts described from section 150. Rocks are gray, massive. Oolites consist of rocks of various forms and sizes: rounded, sized 0.15mm-0.35mm; elongated, 0.4mm-1.0mm, large up to 1 mm and more. They are arranged in radial-concentric structure, usually complete. The rock is usually composed of inorganic oooid: carbonate, sub-spar or ore.

In large oolites there can be several small free concentric nuclei, sometimes shell or agglomerized. Cement is fine-to-medium-grained, irregularly-distributed calcite.
