**2** | M-42-II | Podgorenskoye | M | Occurrences of kaolin clays at Podgorenskoye and Maksi-Kayraktinskoye are associated with the weathering crust developed over Ordovician deposits. The deposits are insignificant in size; the raw material is of low quality. Thickness of the productive bed is 34.4 m. Chemical analysis of the kaolin clays established: Al₂O₃ – 11.8–19.3%; Fe₂O₃ – 0.95–1.22%; TiO₂ – 0.59–0.66%. Kaolin yield 97–99%. Due to the low Al₂O₃ content, the raw material is unsuitable as a filler, but may be used in the ceramics industry. Area of the Podgorenskoye occurrence is 0.2 sq.km; projected reserves in category P₂ amount to 18.48 million tonnes. (Cheredov, 1983). The Maksikayraktinskoye kaolin occurrence is geologically identical to the one described above. | Investigated by A.E. Mikhailov (1959). The kaolin occurrence has not been studied in detail.
