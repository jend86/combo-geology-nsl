The magnetic susceptibility of sediments from the Nargala formation
is mainly 0-20.10 units.CIC, diabase 1200.10 units CIC.
The weighted average density (6 p. = 266 g/cm3)
is close to the weighted average density of the Ordovician,
with the exception of dibasic sills, whose average
density is 2.95 g/cm3. However, due to their small size, they
do not spill in gravimagnetic fields.

The outcrops, which are visible from the island of Kinervnm into the
cubasaday formation of the Upper Ordovician, are developed in the depths
of the Zarkannagachian basin, their outcrops occupy a small area to the
east and northeast of the city of Kuba Sadir (N-42-UP). The roof sag
of the diorite-granodnorite massif of the Late Ordovician age is layered
with them. The surface is strongly horn-lined and characterized by
weak outcrop and decipherability, the nature of the boundaries with
the underlying sediments is unclear, and higher elevation in the section
relative to the Akkairakta formation is established on the basis
of structural features.

There are contacts with rocks of the Shinsayoka and Taloai
formations of a tectolic nature. The above -mentioned column is
uncoordinated by Late Carboniferous and Cenozoic deposits.

The strata of the kubasadiroka svata are gray, greenish-gray,
fine-to-medium, coarse-grained sandstones, interspersed with
small-pebble polyanctic nonglomerates, and there are no traces of
dark gray weathered diabase pores of nrites (Taranche, Padusha,
et al. 1967). The thickness of the formation reaches
(Minervin, Babichov, Tenyakova, 1974).

The Upper Ordovician age of the formation is assumed conditionally,
based on its higher position in the section of the Nekeli Akkairaktin
formation and the presence of volcanites of the main composition,
which somewhat brings it closer to the deposits of the Kargalin
formation of the Kalmakkolsky synclinorium.

The sediments of the Kubasadir formation are mostly
non -magnetic (magnetic susceptibility is less than 10.10 CGS
Ed., for dnabaves 3180.10 CIC units).

The weighted average density of the formation is 2.66 g/cm³.
The density of diabases is 2.35 g/sl", however, due to the small
size in the field of pulling force, it does not appear on desks of a scale
of 1:200000.
