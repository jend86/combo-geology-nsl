**BORISOVKA GROUP OF COPPER MINERAL OCCURRENCES** (Nos. 37, 40) is located on map sheet M-42-II, 1.0–3.5 km to the south-southeast of the village of Borisovka. It consists of two copper mineral occurrences situated 3.5 km apart from each other. The first mineral occurrence No. 37 was investigated by V.M. Popov in 1939 and prospected by A.I. Rykov in 1949–1950. The second mineral occurrence No. 40 was discovered by the Atbasar Party (Dmitrovsky, 1970) during geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000. The Borisovka ore field is composed of deposits of the upper Torsak subformation.

In structural terms, the area is confined to the northwestern framing of the Marinovsky uplift, where the rocks dip monoclinally to the west at an angle of 4–6°.

The copper mineralization (No. 37) is confined to two horizons of grey-colored sandstones, among which four lens-shaped ore bodies are observed. Along strike they are traced for 300–700 m, with a thickness of 0.2–1.3 m. The copper content in oxidized ores is 1–3.9%.

With the aim of tracing the mineralization at depth, within occurrence No. 37 in 1969 (Dmitrovsky, 1970), boreholes A-60 and A-134 were drilled to depths of 230 and 301.0 m respectively. They intersected, at depths of 128 and 223 m, copper-bearing sandstones with thicknesses of 1.4 and 0.8 m with copper content by spectral analysis of 0.12 and 0.16%. Mineral occurrence No. 40 is located 3.5 km from the above-described one (No. 37). Here, the mineralization occurs in the same two horizons of grey sandstones. The copper mineralization is concentrated in three lenses, the most extensive of which is traced along strike for 600 m. This lens is exposed by a trench, which revealed a complex ore horizon. It is divided into two beds. In the lower one, the mineralized rocks are conglomerates, while in the upper one the mineralization is concentrated only in sandstones. The thickness of the lower ore interlayer is 0.65 m with a copper content of 0.2%, and the upper one — 0.35 m with a copper content of 1.61%. Based on data from previous workers and our own data, the group of copper occurrences (Nos. 37, 40) is **considered unpromising**.

It includes five copper ore occurrences, spaced 0.5 to 6.0 km apart from one another. One of them was discovered by an MGRI party in 1951 (Zlatogurova, 1952); the remainder were identified during the period 1966–1968 in the course of geological mapping at a scale of 1:50,000 by M.V. Dmitrovsky.

The host rocks of the Tretyakovsky ore field are grey fine- and medium-grained sandstones and conglomerates of the Upper Tersakan substage.

In structural terms, the Tretyakovsky group of copper occurrences is confined to the eastern limb of the Tretyakovsky syncline, where the strata dip westward at an angle of 3–6°.

As a result of detailed mapping in the Tretyakovsky ore field, five beds of grey sandstones with copper mineralisation have been established.

The first ore-bearing bed is confined to the lower portion of the Upper Tersakan substage and is represented by grey sandstones with carbonised plant remains. Rich accumulations of malachite and azurite are observed in the immediate vicinity of the plant remains. The ore-bearing bed can be traced along strike for 50 m at a thickness of 0.5 m. The copper content in the oxidised ores, according to spectral analysis, reaches 1.0%.

The second ore-bearing bed is situated at the same stratigraphic level as the one described above. Along strike it is located 3.5 km to the south-east of the first ore-bearing bed. The copper mineralisation can be traced along strike for 30 m at a visible thickness of 1.2 m. To the south the copper mineralisation disappears and reappears only 1.0 km away in the same grey sandstone. The copper content reaches 0.48%.

The third ore-bearing bed at the surface is defined by a secondary copper dispersion halo extending south-westward over a distance of 2.2 km. Within the halo, spotty copper mineralisation is observed, represented by malachite smears in grey sandstones.

The fourth ore-bearing bed occurs in the section above bed No. 3, represented by interbeds of grey sandstones and fine-pebble conglomerates enriched with carbonaceous detritus. The mineralisation can be traced for 300 m. The bed thickness is 1.5 m. The copper content in the oxidised ores, according to spectral analysis, amounts to 0.52%. On the basis of the foregoing, it follows that within the eastern flank of the Tretyakovskaya syncline, ore-bearing beds are distributed across the area extremely unevenly and are isolated from one another. Along dip they cannot, as a rule, be traced, but are facially replaced by red-coloured rocks, or the mineralization in cupriferous sandstones gradually disappears, passing into "barren" sandstones and siltstones.

All these facts unambiguously indicate the small dimensions of the ore deposits both in extent and in thickness, and the low copper grades in the ores.

**K e n e n s k a y a g r o u p o f c o p p e r o c c u r r e n c e s** Nos. 12, 13, 15 is located on both banks of the Tersakkан River and includes the ore occurrences of Tersakkан No. 13, Bidayak No. 15, Kenen No. 12, and a number of copper mineralization points identified by the Dzhezkazgan GRE of PGO "Tsentrkazgeologiya" (Gostev, 1969).

As a result of prospecting-exploration work by A.D. Gostev in 1967–1969, within the Kenen area, 14 horizons of grey-colored rocks with disseminated copper mineralization were established, separated by barren rock intervals, which determines the multi-tier nature of the Kenen occurrence. All of them are concentrated in the deposits of the lower Tersakkан sub-formation. In the upper Tersakkан sub-formation, grey-colored horizons are absent, and this part of the section is not prospective for further copper exploration. All ore-bearing grey-colored rock beds, in accordance with the general lenticular structure of the Tersakkан formation, are not strictly persistent and represent lenses of considerable strike extent with varying thickness.

In the investigated area, horizons 3–14 are exposed at the surface, which are intersected at various depths by boreholes. In the deepest boreholes (G-1-6, G-66, G-119, G-53, G-70, G-100, G-101, G-118, G-123, G-10, G-67, G-84, G-117), lower horizons 1–7 are also intersected, represented by medium-grained sandstones and siltstones with sparse mineralization of chalcopyrite. In the upper part of the lower Tersakkан sub-formation section, horizons 8–14 are intersected, which are lithologically composed of the same sediments as horizons Nos. 1–7.
