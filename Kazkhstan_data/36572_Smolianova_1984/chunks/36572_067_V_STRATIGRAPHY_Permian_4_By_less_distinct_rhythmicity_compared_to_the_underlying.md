4. By less distinct rhythmicity compared to the underlying Keymin Formation.

5. By textures characterizing quieter conditions of formation rock deposition.

6. By an abundance of organic remains.

The formation is exposed in the studied territory in isolated synclinal structures: in the Kiymin group of synclines to the northwest of the Shurumsa uplift, in the Aukelbek and Ashilin synclines to the southeast of it, and also in the Lodyzhenko–Kolutonskaya syncline. The lowest levels of the formation are exposed also along the Arzhaly River in the Archalin syncline.

The exposure of the formation is variable, but over the greater part of the studied area it is poor. The entire outcrop area of the Shoptykul Formation at the surface, with the exception of the Ashilin syncline and the southern limb of the Aukelbek syncline, has been ploughed up. Apparently, this explains why a more detailed subdivision of the formation has been carried out in the latter structures. It may be noted, however, that the section of the formation in the stratotype locality differs somewhat from the sections of the Aukelbek and Ashilin synclines by its red coloration, coarser clastic composition, and lesser saturation with limestones.

The lower boundary of the Shoptykul Formation is taken throughout the studied territory as the appearance in a continuous red-colored

-195-

in the section of two or three closely spaced beds of "apparently porphyritic" limestones, saturated with ostracod fauna, fish scales, bony fish remains, relicts of algae, and mud-feeder burrows. This boundary is particularly clearly established and interpreted on the surface in the Aukelbek and Ashilin synclines. It is also identified with reasonable confidence in reference-parametric borehole 04 at a depth of 77 m. The lower boundary is also recognized on the surface and in exploratory boreholes in the Yuzhno-Kiiminsk syncline (sheet M-42-YШ, boreholes M-19, 20), in the Shoptykul syncline (sheet M-42-YШ, boreholes Ch-333, Ch-405, Ch-410, sheet M-42-П, boreholes Ch-442, Ch-400). In reference-parametric borehole T-14B the lowermost beds of the formation are penetrated; core recovery over this interval is poor, and consequently the boundary is indistinct.

In the Ladyzhensko-Kolutensk syncline, neither the formation as a whole nor its lower boundary has been studied by drilling; moreover, the latter is almost everywhere overlain here by a cover of Cenozoic deposits. Identification of the formation in this structure, as well as its description, is based exclusively on eluvial debris and isolated surface exposures.

The upper boundary of the formation is nowhere established.

The most complete section of the formation is in the Ashilin syncline; the second most complete is the section of the Aukelbek syncline. In the Kiiminsk group of synclines only the lower part of the formation is represented, as shown in drawing 49, sheets 2, 3. The lithological composition of the formation in all sections can readily be read from the drawings; therefore below we provide a description only of the hypostratotypic section of the Shoptykul Formation from the Ashilin syncline.
