CHAPTER VIII. USEFUL FOSSILS

Various useful fossils have been discovered on the studied territory. The main role among them is played by honey, associated with Cretaceous deposits of the Ustyurt subformation. In connection with this, the main task of the prospecting work was to clarify the perspectives of honey-bearing potential of this formation; questions concerning the working of the Tengiz field were not examined in the report. Primary attention during prospecting was directed at evaluating the productivity of the field.

In addition to honey, within the limits of the upper Paleozoic deposits, manifestations of polymetals and silver have been identified. From German useful fossil occurrences in the upper Paleozoic deposits, manifestations of strrontium, roofing materials and structural materials were identified. From the upper hot Paleozoic useful fossils, no known manifestations of coal were identified on this territory.

Materials on useful fossils are presented on maps of useful fossil occurrences (references No. 24-32).

On them, manifestations of honey are shown depending on the intensity and location of mineralization, subdivided into rank-ordered and small manifestations of honey.

The manifestations on maps of useful fossils were measured and their detailed characteristics were given in catalogs of useful fossil occurrences (see below). Signs of honey manifestations identified as a result of surface survey of the surface are not shown on maps of useful fossils. On maps of useful fossils in the early rank-ordered manifestations of honey located in a narrow strip of outcrop, which had "weak" sanded horizontal layers with a content of honey 0.3 m and higher. To the signs of honey manifestations are attributed rank-ordered signs, subsurface outcrop, with honey content above 0.3 m and %.

# Kimmeridgian Group Coal-Bearing Deposits

The first signs of coal-bearing deposits in the research territory were established by A.M. Lavdin (1952). In 1954, Churubai-Nurina geologist of the trust "Mangystaugeologiya" (Mangystau-West, 1954) conducted work on coal in the north-eastern part (Sar-Donkali anticline). Four boreholes of 400-500m depth were drilled. Industrial coal seams were not discovered.

Coal deposits are associated with the Kimmeridgian suite, with its lower and upper shale subsuite, which are distinguished from the overlying and underlying Neocomian and undifferentiated Senonian coal-bearing deposits of the Mangystau coal basin.

The formation of coal-bearing deposits of the Kimmeridgian suite occurred in a near-marine-lagoonal setting, as evidenced both by the significant quantity of processes of marine fauna relics. The seams encountered are related to the Tithonian type.

The manifestations of coal are distributed among different structures. The northern group of deposits is located on the north-eastern flank (Sar-Donkali anticline), and the southern—on the northern flank (Imsheksk anticline). These groups form the Kimskoi coal-bearing region, including coal seams in Ves.10.19,21,22,23-26.

The coal basin of Kimsk coal-bearing region contains 6-45 coal seams, the majority of which are represented by coal organelles and semolina. All necessary significant seams are distributed in the north-western flank of the Sar-Donkali anticline. The most prominent and powerful coal horizon here is the seam "sloynyi," known as such due to its layered structure. It presents peculiar coal of seams of coal, coal organelles and "pustaki" (loose) series. The seam "sloynyi" is distributed at 2.95m. The quantity of it, 2.68, of which the local coal is 0.37m, coal organelles with bituminous coal 0.25m, loose organelles and gray-albite rock 1.38m. On the general and specific ranges of this seam, the reduction of manganese coal reaches 1.16m and 2.4m.

The coal seam "sloynyi" in terms of cement, a weakly metamorphosed and corresponds to mark "II" (Besedino, 1950).
