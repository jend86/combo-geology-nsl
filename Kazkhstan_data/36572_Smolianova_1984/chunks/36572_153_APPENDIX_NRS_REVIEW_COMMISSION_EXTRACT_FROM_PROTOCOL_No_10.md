**THE KAZFILIALA NRS NOTES:**

I. The work of the West-Teniz Party was carried out in accordance with

with the program of comprehensive additional study of the Teniz depression and the geological assignment, which can be considered fulfilled.

The geological additional study was conducted methodologically correctly, at a high professional level, with the use of a significant volume of drilling and analysis of geophysical data. As a result of the work, questions of the geological structure of the studied territory were elaborated in detail in terms of stratigraphy, tectonics, and mineral resources; a detailed characterization of the lithological composition of Upper Paleozoic and Cenozoic deposits was provided; and age dating of the identified formations of Carboniferous and Permian sequences was established.

An assessment of the ore-bearing potential of the studied territory was provided and, in particular, its copper-bearing potential. Recommendations on the direction of further prospecting and exploration work are given.

The compiled geological maps correspond in quality and detail to the scale of the work carried out. It should be noted, however, the presence of mismatches in the boundaries of Carboniferous and Permian deposits on these maps and the maps of the adjacent eastern part of the Teniz depression, compiled during the State Geological Map at scale 1:200,000 by the same PGO (Petrilyak, 1980).

**II. Shortcomings of the work:**

**1.** The geological assignment is not appended to the report.

**2.** In the Middle–Upper Paleozoic section, a number of formations (Kyshakskaya, Tersakkanskaya) are identified under a single name for the entire Teniz depression, which is unjustified due to the sharp variability of their lithological composition and the stratigraphic affiliation of the formations to different facies zones.

**3.** In the compilation of maps, the results of large-scale gravimetric, magnetic, and high-precision aeromagnetic surveys of recent years were insufficiently utilized.

**4.** The calculation of forecast resources of mineral deposits was performed and documented at a low methodological level, without a clear description and justification of the calculation parameters.

**5.** The identification of prospective areas of the first, second, and third priority is not supported by figures for reserves of forecast categories. Recommendations for conducting prospecting work are not linked to drilling volumes, nor to the outlines of the areas for which the forecast resource calculations are made.
