The variegated type of element distribution identified in the sediments of the Tersakkansk sequence is fully consistent with the regularities established by N.M. Strakhov (1962).

**MAIN EXPLORATION CRITERIA AND INDICATORS.** Based on data on the regularities of distribution of copper occurrences within the investigated territory, it was established that the most important criteria for the search of copper deposits are: stratigraphic, lithological-facies, and structural controls of mineralization.

1. The main metallogenic levels within the investigated territory are: Upper Devonian, Middle Carboniferous (Kireysky), Lower Carboniferous (Spassky), and Upper Carboniferous (Terekkhansky).

2. Lithological-facies control of mineralization is expressed in the confinement of maximum copper concentrations to shallow-marine (borehole No. O2, Spasskoye) and deltaic facies (Kenen).

3. Structural control is clearly expressed. All occurrences, secondary copper dispersion halos, and geochemical anomalies are located on the limbs of paleouplifts and flexural bends. Structural control is the determining factor in the distribution of copper mineralization in the region.

The most characteristic exploration indicators of copper occurrences (deposits) in the region:

1. Zones of development of cupriferous sandstones.

2. Presence of occurrences and points of mineralization.

3. Dispersion halos of copper and associated elements, based on results of lithogeochemical surveys. These, as a rule, coincide with outcrops of ore bodies at the surface.

4. Presence of plant remains in grey-colored rocks.

5. Irregular distribution of elements within the series of rocks: sandstone–siltstone–argillite.

As is known, the largest stratiform-type copper deposit in Kazakhstan is Dzhezkazgan, which serves as the reference standard for deposits of the cupriferous sandstone type in Kazakhstan, including for occurrences of the investigated region.

The principal factors controlling the localization of mineralization at the Dzhezkazgan deposit are:

1. Stratigraphic control of mineralization (Middle and Upper Carboniferous).

2. Mineralization is controlled by deep-seated faults.

**Polymetals**

Polymetallic (lead-zinc) occurrences are noted at the Permian, Upper Carboniferous (Tersakan) and Upper Devonian (Famennian) stratigraphic levels.

**1. Permian and Upper Carboniferous stratigraphic levels.** Lead-zinc mineralization in the stratigraphic section occurs either together with copper — at the Lower Kayrakty and Tersakan levels, or accumulates in the deposits of the Middle Kayrakty and Upper Kayrakty sub-suites, where it forms both copper-polymetallic and lead-zinc occurrences and mineralization points. High lead contents were identified at the Lower Kayrakty level (borehole No. 131 – 0.65%) and Tersakan (borehole No. 130 – 0.4–0.72%). In the deposits of the Middle Kayrakty and Upper Kayrakty sub-suites, according to data from boreholes No. A-1–4, lead and zinc contents vary from 0.1 to 0.3%. The Lower Kayrakty and Tersakan levels are of prospecting interest for polymetals.

**2. U p p e r D e v o n i a n (F a m e n n i a n) s t r a t i g r a p h i c l e v e l.**

The carbonate formation of the Famennian stage (sulciferous horizon) contains lead and zinc mineralization, localized in limestones and dolomites of the sulciferous horizon. Direct prospecting indicators are the presence of secondary halos of lead, zinc, and barium within the distribution area of the limestones and dolomites of the sulciferous horizon. Lead-zinc mineralization is localized in zones of development of silicified and limonitized rocks near large faults of westerly and north-westerly strike.

Silver occurrences occupy a special structural position. Unlike copper occurrences, they are located in the core parts of the Kiyminskaya, Aukelbek, and Simtasskaya synclines.

Rich accumulations of silver in limestones are controlled by sorption processes. Silver is capable of being sorbed by finely dispersed material — clays — and can also be reduced and precipitated from solutions by organic matter, which is abundant in pelitomorphic limestones. Taking into account the existence of industrial silver-bearing deposits of sedimentary genesis in the sandstones of the Cis-Urals region and the USA (Silver Reef), the deposits of the Kiyminskaya and Shoptykul'skaya formations should be considered prospective with respect to rich silver accumulations.

**Rodusite**

Rodusite occurrences are associated with deposits of the Lower Kairaktinskaya sub-formation and are confined to gray-colored pelitomorphic, fractured and brecciated marls and limestones. They are located in the marginal parts of the Taldykskaya syncline. The limbs of the syncline are composed of Kairaktinskaya formation deposits, folded into small folds complicated by low-amplitude faults. A clear spatial relationship is observed between rodusite mineralization and zones of fracturing and brecciation, which serve as reliable structural controls of rodusite ore formation.

The presence of significant rodusite accumulations in the rocks of the Kairaktinskaya formation determines their prospectivity for the search of rodusite deposits in the Teniz depression.
