**Petrochemical Characteristics of the Borovsky Complex Granites**

The authors had at their disposal 4 silicate analyses of samples collected from granites of the Borovsky complex during field traverses within the Sandyksui and Obalshkolsky massifs, as well as a number of analyses taken from the works of previous researchers. A review of the results of these analyses allows the following general conclusions to be drawn regarding the chemistry of the rocks of the Borovsky complex in the described territory (Table No. 55) (the following remarks relate primarily to the Sandyksui and Karatas massifs):

1. The SiO₂ content in the rocks varies within the range of 68%–76%, which confirms the presence among them of both normal and leucocratic granite varieties (Fig. 77).

2. The alkali content, the sum of which (Na₂O + K₂O) in the overwhelming majority of samples ranges from 7.0% to 8.6%, indicates the equal distribution among the described rocks of normal and subalkaline varieties. At the same time, the value (Na₂O + K₂O) shows no definite dependence on the SiO₂ content (Fig. 77).

3. The alkali ratio indicates a predominant prevalence of K₂O over Na₂O in the rocks of the complex (Na₂O/K₂O varies within the range of 0.7–1.0), and this is more consistent for granites of the Karatas massif (in the overwhelming majority of samples Na₂O/K₂O = 0.7) (Fig. 79).

4. By Al₂O₃ content, the described rocks belong to the category of highly aluminous (Fig. 78), with the value of the coefficient αl′ differing somewhat for rocks of different massifs. Thus, in granites of the Sandyksui intrusion it varies from 3.8 to 6.2 and shows a clear tendency to increase with increasing SiO₂ content in the rock.

In rocks of the Karatas (and possibly Obalshkolsky) intrusion, values of αl′ are more consistent (3.3–4.2) and the aforementioned tendency is practically not expressed. This is possibly due to the greater petrographic homogeneity of the rocks and the relatively low prevalence of leucocratic varieties among them.

5. It should also be noted that there is a relatively elevated CaO content (mainly ~2.0%) in the rocks of the Karatas massif compared with the rocks of the Sandyksui and Obalshkolsky massifs, as well as
