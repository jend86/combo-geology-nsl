The following materials were submitted for review by the commission:

1. Geological map of sheet M-42-V at scale 1:200,000.
2. Maps of factual materials for sheets M-42-V and M-42-XXX at scale 1:200,000.
3. Geological map of sheet M-42-XXX on photographic and topographic base at scale 1:50,000.
4. Field diaries.
5. Well documentation journals.
6. Geological columns for wells at scale 1:200.
7. Sampling journals.
8. Logging diagrams at scale 1:200.
9. Recultivation acts.
10. Temporary construction acts.

Having reviewed the submitted materials, the commission NOTES:

1. Geological re-mapping at scale 1:200,000 was conducted methodologically correctly. The compilation of the map corresponds to scale 1:200,000.

2. Upper Paleozoic deposits have been mapped and subdivided in detail; Lower Carboniferous red-colored deposits have been identified, the age of which is substantiated by fauna.

3. Since photomaterials at scale 1:100,000 for the entire studied area were of very poor quality, aerial photographs at scale 1:50,000 were partially used, and partially contact prints at scale 1:25,000.

4. The underfulfillment of prospecting-mapping drilling in the volume of 1544 l.m. on the area of sheet M-42-VII was reasonably replaced by documentation of wells from third-party organizations. In connection with this, the party overfulfilled the volume of core documentation from third-party organizations by 2323 m, which should be financed from the remaining budget allocation for drilling.

5. Well documentation was performed at a generally high level; however, in certain journals there is no well reference/tie-in data.

6. In wells 389 and 392, a complete logging suite was not performed (except GK) due to emergency condition of the wells.

7. Geological documentation of geological traverses is good. Descriptions of observation points are complete, sketches of the most interesting geological objects are present, and route-based conclusions are present throughout.

Having exchanged opinions, the commission **RECOMMENDS:**

1. To delineate, within the Molboldin trough, the boundaries of the Upper Paleozoic formations beneath the cover of Cenozoic deposits.

2. Using the materials of the Kustanai geological-geophysical expedition, to evaluate the area of sheet M–42–VII for rhodusite.

The commission **RESOLVES:**

1. To consider the geological assignment of the West-Teniz PSP for the year 1963 as fulfilled.

2. Field materials are accepted with a rating of **"good"**.
