Deposits of the Rusak horizon are widespread within the
Kipshak trough. In addition, these rocks come to the surface
on the krill of the Shabdara, Obalykul and Vostochno- Obalykul
anticlines, and Okvazhinai 015 was uncovered, and a well was drilled
at the Kokpektin uplift.
The outcrop of the Kipshak basin, the upper part of the
horizon, usually forms a high ridge, sharply defined in relief , which is
unambiguously defined and traced throughout the area of the Kipshak
trough and the south of the depression. This geomorphological
feature of the Rusakov horizon was previously noted by Litvinovich N.B.
(1969) and others.
The decipherability of sediments is good,
because marl and lime beds usually form well-defined
ridges of different heights, which give distinct white
stripes on aerial photographs (Fig. 13, 14).
The Rusakov horizon on the surface is mainly represented
by marls. Only in the west of the Kipshak trough is the lower part
of the section represented by gray organogenic-clastic rocks.

The deposits of the Rusakovsky and upper Kassinsky horizons were subjected to intense weathering, which resulted in the formation of a brightly colored weathering crust.

There are two bundles in the horizon section. The lower one is composed of interlacing marls, limestones, siltstones, and sandstones. The upper part is composed of a monotonous thickness of marls with rare interlayers of terrigenous rocks with a depth of less than one meter.

The lower boundary of the horizon is drawn along the base of oolithic limestone or calcareous sandstone with lenses and shallow interlayers of oolithic limestones. At this level, the fauna complex includes species typical of the Upper Turnian suborder.

The upper boundary of the Rusak horizon is well expressed geomorphologically in the form of a sharp decrease in relief and terrain is located at the foot of a high grad.

The deposits of the Rusakovsky horizon within the Kokpektinskaya and Shaodar anticlines are represented by organogenic- clastic crystalline and clayey limestones with layers of calcareous siltstones up to 2-3 m thick, less often sandstones. In the Shabad section, these deposits are characterized by pyritization in the form of films and inclusions of crystals, nodules and lenses of flints. A feature of this section is also the presence of numerous quartz secretions in the lower part of the horizon. The width of the burning umbrella here is l80m.

The Kokpekta section is characterized by limestones with siltstone and Aleuropean admixture with inclusions of sphalerite, galena, and rarely chalcopyrite. The width of the horizon here is 60m.
