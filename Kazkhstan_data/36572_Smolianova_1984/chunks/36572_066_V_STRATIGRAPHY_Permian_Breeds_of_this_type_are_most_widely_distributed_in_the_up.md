Breeds of this type are most widely distributed in the upper Kyrgyzian subunit.

Aleuroglinite rocks consist of gypsum-carbonate mass in 70-80% and terrigenous aleurgite admixture. Carbonate in these breeds is generally pelitomorphic or microcrystalline, well secured against gypsum composition, forms aggregates, crusts.

In many cases the breed is perforated by cemented rectangular fissures, creating cavities. Rarely such breeds are cemented.

In sandstones, aleurites of the upper productive formation, rarely observed fugogene admixture, especially much is marked in Aqshinian, Lukebutekin and Syganesk multiples.

Physical properties of rocks of cimmeridian deposits were studied on 197 samples.
The density of sandstones varies from 2.63 g/cm³ to 2.66 g/cm³, of claystones from 2.61 g/cm³ to 2.66 g/cm³. The average density of rocks is 2.64 g/cm³, practically not differing from the average density of Upper Paleozoic deposits; therefore, detecting cimmeridian deposits in the force field of gravity is not possible.

According to magnetic properties, all rocks of cimmeridian deposits are nonmagnetic; the magnetic susceptibility varied from 3 to 42.10⁻⁶ в CGS units.

Among sandstones, single weakly magnetic sandstones are encountered, the magnetic susceptibility of which reaches 150.10⁻⁶ en CGS units.
In the magnetic field above deposits of cimmeridian deposits, slightly expressed negative anomalies are observed.

The location of fossil vertebrates allowing assignment to the upper part of the Upper Devonian was established. The first finds of named vertebrates were made by N.V. Litvinovich in 1950. From them I.A. Efremov identified a fish and reptilian fauna.

In the location of fossiliferous horizon, an expedition of the Paleontological Institute of the USSR Academy of Sciences under the direction of B.D. Konkukov was organized in 1951. Numerous and varied vertebrate fossils were collected. Among the fossils identified by B.D. Konkukov and N.A. Efremov were determined as fossils: Labyrinthodontia, Batrachosauria, InorImosaulus satraevi Efr. and reptilians: Cotylosaurus, Diadectomorpha (?) gen.et sp.nov., Pelycosauria(?) gen.et sp.nov.

On the basis of the listed paleontological finds, an assignment by N.A. Efremov as Lower Permian was rejected.

At the fossiliferous location during the course of investigation, additional searches for unknown vertebrates were organized. Unfortunately, they did not yield new valuable finds, although individual fragments of bones and tusks were discovered.

During geological investigation of the deposits of the Kiyalinskyy series, a very large number of samples were collected for detailed analysis. The vast quantity of etches of samples proved unproductive. In individual samples, isolated spores and pollen were found, which cannot be sufficient evidence that they provide a Permian age, except that individual bone and tusk fragments were discovered.
