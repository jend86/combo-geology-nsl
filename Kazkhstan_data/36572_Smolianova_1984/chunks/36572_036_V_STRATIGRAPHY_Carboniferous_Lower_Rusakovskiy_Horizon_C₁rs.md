**Rusakovskiy Horizon (C₁rs)**

The exposures of the Rusakovskiy horizon are widely distributed within the Kippakskiy high. Besides this, these formations extend to the surface on the crests of the Shaldarkskiy, Obaykul'skiy, and Vostochno-Obaykul'skiy anticlinals, and broken by OUZ on the Bekektinskoye field.

The upper part of the horizon is developed along the plain, forms a well-marked crest in the Gryad, which was once defined and studied along the entire range of the Kipchakskiy high and its expansion. This geomorphological feature of the Rusakovskiy horizon was previously noted by Litvinovich N.V. (1969) and others.

The demigration of the horizon's core, i.e., the location of intercalations and sandstones, usually forms a good ridge expression of the gryad, which gives non-erodible deposits distinctive white and light gray colors (fig. 13, 14).

The Rusakovskiy horizon on the surface is presented by predominantly marl. Only in the western Kipchakskiy high the lower part of the section is represented by organic-detrital

crystalline rocks with deposits of rare earth elements in redbed formations of Paleozoic age. Paleozoic-Cretaceous sediments in the erosion zone are also found on the Badarok anticline.

The relationships of Russian and Upper Caspian horizons underwent intentional weathering, as a result of which an exhumed weathering crust formed.

Two packages are distinguished in the horizon section. The lower one, preceded by alternation of marls and sandstones, alerolites and sandstones. The upper stratum consists of a monotonous layer of marls with rare interlayers of sand terrigenous material less than one meter thick.

The lower boundary of the horizon is traced along the level of calitic limestone or calcareous-siliceous sandstone with dimensions and weak nodules. At this level in the complex fauna appear forms characteristic of the Upper Cretaceous sub-rus.

The composition of detrital grains is relatively uniform - quartz and feldspar predominate, while lithic fragments are present, predominantly as rock fragments. Besides this, in the capacity of admixtures, organogenic debris may be present, shell fragments of peritomorph bivalves, etc. The most widely distributed type of cement in sandstones is calcareous, followed by siliceous crystalline cement. In general, it should be noted that among sandy-arenaceous deposits of the Rubakovskiy horizont practically there are no truly terrigenous deposits - they are all more or less carbonaceous-terrigenous.

ARGILITES - dark-brown, brown, and sometimes blackish rocks with high clay-ness and water-resistance of texture. Widely distributed in the interstices of the Rubakovskiy horizont adjacent to carbonate deposits, forming a series of transition horizons. The evidence of this is that adjacent to the clayey material, which forms the main part of the pore in the argilites, there is carbonate in the form of peritomorphic shell, equally distributed throughout the deposit (to 30-40%); glauconite and detrital biogenic fragments and biomorphic remnants (up to 5-7% to 30%).

Free-form glauconite deposits, characteristic specifically for deposits of the Rubakovskiy horizont, are present as epiclastide argilites established in cores samples 015 and 150. The joints have dimensions 0.2x0.02mm; they are filled with cremnescent and secondary calcite, have frequently well-developed radial streaks. Prevalence up to 15-20% of deposits.

Spasokaya svita (C₁sp)

In the north-western and northern parts, within the basis of Lower Carboniferous domed layers, there is a noteworthy Tournaisian series, which has brought considerable attention to many researchers, as it adjoins a large developed zone between Spasokaya.

In 1951, during a description of the Spasokaya ore deposition by I.V. Sopko (1952), he identified this series of a genus, naming it spasokaya. The age of the svita he considered lower Tournaisian.

However, the spasokaya svita as the single stratigraphic subdivision of the Tournaisian did not find acceptance.

The name spasokaya svita is recorded in numerous reports on the northern part of the platform, but it was not used in the main, authoring works conducted field survey work. Thus spasokaya svita (Gormont) was identified in the works of Rybakov A.L. (1951), Khomchenko P.I. (1977), Namazov V.A. (1980), who attributed the svita to the lower Devonian, lower and upper Turge, respectively Lomchenko P.I., noted that the age of the svita could be both Tournaisian, and it, possibly, is an analogue of the Dainkovskaya svita.

Dmitriyevskii E.B. (1969) during field works on the Mashkhabai 1:50000 scale, assigned the series to the lower Turge.

As a result of our research, we came to the conclusion that for the northern part of the platform, this series should be identified in a svita, which should be named Spasokaya. To consider the stratigraphic range of the svita a section in the region of the Spasokoe village along the Adai River.
