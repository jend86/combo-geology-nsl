**Areas I–4B** are located in the southwestern part of sheet M-42-XY. Their geological structure is identical. Structurally, these areas are situated within individual tectonic blocks oriented from southeast to northwest. The prospects of the areas are associated with deposits of the sulfiferous horizon. Their outcrops are observed as individual blocks extending from southeast to northwest over a distance of 30.0 km.

The deposits of the sulfiferous horizon are represented by grey and dark-grey limestones and dolomites with subordinate interlayers of sandstones and siltstones. They are accompanied by secondary halos of lead and zinc dispersion. The extent of the halos varies from 0.6 to 6.0 km with lead contents of 0.003–0.01% and zinc 0.01%. Isolated points of lead and zinc with intensities of 0.03% are noted within the halo zones.

The area is very poorly studied.

The most important prerequisites for designating areas I–4B for polymetallic deposit prospecting are the similarity of its geological structure to that of area 5B of the first-priority prospecting category, and the ore occurrences of Barakbay and Kirey, identified by V.I. Zavrazhnov (1983), located in the southern parts of the area. The similarity of these

**Areas of the Third Priority**

**Area 2B** is located in the north-eastern part of sheet M-42-VII. The geological structure of the area includes grey-coloured and red-coloured sandstones, siltstones, and limestones of the Kairaktin and Tersakkан formations. The latter are penetrated by an exploratory borehole at a depth of 313.0–315.0 m. The prospects of the area are associated with the Lower Permian (Lower Kairaktin) and Upper Carboniferous (Tersakkан) copper-bearing horizons, which formed during the regressive stage of development of the paleobassein and are represented by facies of alluvial-lacustrine and lacustrine types.

The structural position of the area is favourable for the localization of copper. It is situated on the north-western limb of the Aukelbek syncline, complicated by the Artikpai and Mortenbai domes, which are separated by the North Artikpai syncline; here, between the Mortenbai dome and the North Artikpai syncline, a flexure zone of north-westerly strike is observed. On the north of the Mortenbai dome it joins with a flexure zone of north-easterly strike.

**Map Sheet N-42-XXXIII**

On map sheet N-42-XXXIII, it is recommended to drill two deep boreholes. The first borehole is intended to clarify the copper-bearing prospects of the Upper Carboniferous (Tersakkan) and Lower Carboniferous (Spassk) metallogenic levels. In structural terms, the borehole is located at the northwestern closure of the Vladimirovsky syncline, in the zone of influence of a flexure fold of northwestern strike. **Sheet area M-42-IV**

In the northeastern part of sheet area M-42-IV, it is recommended to drill one borehole. The objectives of the borehole are related to the prospects of the Kireysk and Spassk suites for copper. The deposits of the Kireysk suite on the southern margin of the Archalinsk trough have not been studied in sufficient detail. According to data from exploration boreholes, in the lower and middle parts of the Kireysk suite, interbeds of coarse-grained sandstones are noted and a significant increase in the local geochemical background of copper is observed, which predetermines the necessity of evaluating this area for copper. By borehole No. P-292, sandstones have been penetrated at this stratigraphic level, with copper content by spectral analysis of 0.4%. The most prospective for copper exploration in this part of the region are the deposits of the transgressive series of the Spassk suite. Elevated copper contents in the deposits of the Spassk suite have been established by borehole No. P-295. Here, the copper mineralization is confined to the base of the marine transgressive series and may possibly represent an analogue of Mansfeld-type ores.

**Area of Sheet M-42-II**

Within the southwestern part of the area of Sheet M-42-II, it is recommended to drill a borehole in the zone of the flexural bend of meridional strike. The objectives of the borehole are determined by the prospects for copper in the deposits of the Lower Carboniferous. By boreholes No. 4-403, 4-422, and 4-424, located 15, 20, and 30 km to the northeast of the recommended borehole, carboniferous deposits favorable for the localization of copper were opened at various depths. Mineralization was identified by borehole No. 4-424 at a depth of 108.5–109.5 m and is represented by poor disseminations of chalcopyrite and gelénite. The copper content is 0.1% and lead is 0.2%. The mineralization is confined to the base of the marine transgressive series and is probably an analog of the Spasskiy metallogenic level. The deposits of this level, favorable for the localization of copper, are expected to be opened at a depth of 400–500 m. In the profile of the first borehole, it is recommended to drill a second borehole, which is expected to open, in the zone of the flexural bend, the Upper Carboniferous (Tersakkanian) metallogenic level. The deposits of the Tersakkanian suite are represented by grey-colored and red-colored sandstones, siltstones, and rarely conglomerates. Secondary copper dispersion halos of intensity 0.01% are confined to the grey-colored varieties of the rocks. Richer copper mineralization in the deposits of the Tersakkanian suite was established in borehole No. 4-241, drilled in the northeastern part of the area of Sheet M-42-II. The average copper content, according to spectral analysis data, is 0.62% over a thickness of 3.4 m (Cheredov, 1983).

The data cited indicate that the grey-colored rocks of the Tersakkanian suite, favorable for the concentration of copper, may be found in the southwestern part of the area of Sheet M-42-II, where they have not been studied at depth. The estimated depth of intersection of the floor of the Tersakkanian suite, according to seismic survey data, is 500 m.

**Sheet Area M-42-II**

The borehole is located in the northeastern part of Sheet Area M-42-II. Its objectives are defined by prospects for silver in terrigenous sediments of the Lower–Middle Carboniferous. These are represented by grey and brown medium- to coarse-grained sandstones with subordinate interbeds of fine-grained sandstones and siltstones. At 1.0 km to the south-southeast of the recommended borehole, mapping borehole No. 160 was drilled, located in the zone of intersection of variously oriented flexural bends. Rocks with elevated silver content were encountered at depths of 33.0–42.0 m. Macroscopically, no mineralization was identified in the sandstones. Spectral analysis of spot sample No. 160/2 established silver at 15 g/t, over a thickness of 9.0 m.

Thus, taking into account the favorable structural setting (flexural bend zone), the presence of elevated silver contents in the rocks and their considerable thickness, as well as the shallow depth of the ore deposit, the area in the vicinity of the recommended borehole should be considered prospective for silver deposits. The depth of intersection of the mineralized rocks should be estimated in the range of 300–400 m.

**Sheet Area M-42-VII**

In Sheet Area M-42-VII, it is recommended to drill three boreholes. The objectives of the boreholes are defined by prospects for copper in the sediments of the Tersakkan Suite.

The first borehole is located 6 km west of Prognosis Area 28, on the western limb of the Churumсай anticline. By borehole No. 138, drilled by the West-Teniz Party, 12 km to the south-southeast of the recommended borehole, at the transition from grey-coloured facies to red-coloured facies, ores were identified with copper contents of 0.97%. On the basis of these data, the area in the vicinity of the recommended borehole is of interest for the exploration of industrial copper ores. As noted above, the copper-bearing horizon was established only in the base of the Lower Nizhneraktinsk subsuite. **Sheet Area M-42-IX**

One kilometer from forecast area IV, within the zone of the Taldysai flexure of northwest strike, it is recommended to drill a deep borehole. The objectives of the borehole are defined by the prospects for copper at two ore-bearing levels: the Lower Permian and the Upper Carboniferous (Chersak). Borehole No. 131 established copper (0.2%) and lead (0.65%) mineralization at the Lower Permian level; at the Upper Carboniferous (Chersak) ore-bearing level in this area, borehole No. 130 intersected copper-bearing sandstones with copper content up to 0.3%. Thus, taking into account the favorable structural position and the presence of anomalous copper and lead contents at the forecast levels, the possibility of intersecting rich copper and lead ores at greater depths cannot be excluded. The assumed maximum depth of intersection is 800 m.

**Sheet Area M-42-XIV**

Within the Kenenes anticline, it is recommended to drill two boreholes. The first borehole is located 1.6 km northwest of the profile of boreholes No. G-1-8, G-55, etc.; it is proposed to intersect and trace the ore horizons along strike and dip, identified as a result of prospecting and exploration work in 1967–1969 (Gostev, 1969). The maximum intersection depth favorable for the localization of copper in deltaic-type sediments is 1500 m.

The second borehole is to be drilled on the southeastern margin of the Nabdarsk brachyanticline, in the zone of tectonic dislocations of sublatitudinal strike. The prospects are associated with the deposits of the Kirei Formation, which within the Nabdarsk brachyanticline are copper-bearing. The possibility of discovering rich copper ores in this part of the area, which is characterized by a favorable tectonic setting, cannot be excluded. This tectonic setting possibly caused the redistribution of copper and its concentration at the Middle Carboniferous (Kirei) level. The depth of intersection of rocks favorable for copper localization is 700 m.
