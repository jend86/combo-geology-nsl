SIGNS OF COPPER ORE OCCURRENCE

Besides the above-mentioned copper-ore manifestations, molybdenum, aluminum, silver ore and gold, which are included in the catalog of useful minerals and which are the subject of past research, copper ore signs (point mineralization) are identified in the surface and subsurface, on both daily and kernel test bore holes.

Signs of copper ore occurrence are tied to various metallogenic levels and localized in serositic sandy shale, diorites and known rocks, crossed by argillified and varied altered zones. On the daily surface of this range, copper ore signs are manifested on the first meter, rarely ten meters at visible distance from 0.5 to 3.0M. Sometimes copper ore mineralization occurs in the form of individual points. Ore deposits are presented by cuprite, bornite, chalcocite and molybdenite, rarely malachite. Content of copper in oxidized ores, according to spectral analysis, sometimes reaches 1.0M.

In several traverses, ore-bearing pores are noted in C.1—1.0M. Copper ore mineralization is characterized by high unevenness of distribution. Content of copper in peripheral "ores", according to spectral analysis, ranges from thousand to 0.4M.

Signs of occurrence of malachite and chalcocite were identified by geological traverses. Polyminerallic mineralization is confined to middle and lower sub-zones and localization in gray known rocks, rarely in dioritic and sandstone rocks. Sometimes malachite mineralization is noted in association with copper. Porosity of ore-bearing pores, secret encrustations, ranges from 0.1 to 0.2M, rarely reaches 1.0M. Primary ores of useful mineral components distributed

According to prognosis maps of useful minerals distributed secondarily in weathering products, primary ores of copper, lead, zinc and silver, as well as anomalous concentrations of silver in individual lithochemical samples, have been identified. Ores of lead and zinc were examined earlier during the description of polymetallic mineralization and are not described here. Among the established ores, the largest quantity of them was attributed to the Tersek-Kenkinsky and Kimmyrktinsky subdistricts.

In the adjoining Dairik district, individual secondary ores by size of copper concentrate media 0.007%, located in gray arenaceous sandstones, have been established.

In the Tersektinskiy district, as in other stratigraphic subdivisions, ores of copper are closely associated with lead mineralization and are controlled, in the obvious strong serpentine sandstones, energides, and rare reddening. The largest quantity of ore-grade copper is concentrated within the limits of the Aukelbskoe, Kiiminskoe, Mailykolskoe and Achinskoe ore zones. The ore zones have a linear elongated form and are confined to the known Kiiminski and Shoptulski sites. The dimensions of ore zones vary from 400 to 5000m, the quantity of copper in the ore zones comprises 0.008–0.01%, rarely reaching 0.12%. Within the ore zones there are noted minerals with admixtures, sparse pyrite, rare disseminated malachite and azurite. A significant portion of ore-bearing copper located in the favorable geological environment in areas with ore-bearing copper mineralization received an assessment with the application of mining and prospecting work. The results are positive.

Ore zones of copper associated with Kiiminski and Shoptulski sites are confined to paleomorphic formations with low-grade copper mineralization. The poverty of these deposits, as was shown earlier, is low.

Silver is confined to limited areas by distribution. It occurs in the eastern part of sheet M-42-UE, where it forms a carbonate emanation of gray-black bituminous substance of the Kiiminski site. The area of ore zones comprises 5.0×2.0m and 8.0×3.0m. The content of silver in them comprises 0.01–0.3%. Silver was also noted in the upper Kiiminski deposit; three additional points with the inclusion of silver were established at palimorphic formations. The presence of ore-bearing silver indicates the reliability of the manifestation of the Kiiminski site of silver. This is confirmed by boring N 90, which was sunk in the non-large deep silver ore formation in quantities of 15 and 51 g/t. These data suggest the possibility of enrichment and deposits of Kiiminski site of industrial silver ore.
