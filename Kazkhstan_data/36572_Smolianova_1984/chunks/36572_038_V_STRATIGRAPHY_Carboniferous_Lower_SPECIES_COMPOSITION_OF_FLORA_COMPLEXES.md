Thus, the age of the described deposits is determined in the volume
of the Serpukhov Age of the Lower Carboniferous and part of the Bashkir
Age of the Middle Carboniferous. The age boundaries of the Kipshak
formation are wider than the boundaries of the Beleutinsky horizon in the
stratotypic section, which makes its separation legitimate.

In the north of the Teniz Depression, the age of the Kipshak
formation is confirmed by the determination of plant remains in sections
of the Konur and Archaly rivers (Table No. 22). In other places, it is
distinguished by its lithological similarity to the reference sections
and by its stratigraphic position between the faunistically characterized
deposits of the Dalnensky horizon and the Kirei formation. It should
be noted that in many cases this isolation is conditional, and
sometimes it is impossible, which leads to the need to isolate the
undifferentiated formations of the lower-middle carboniferous.

Lower - middle department.

The undifferentiated deposits of the lower-middle section of the coal
system are isolated on the ploughs of the 4-42-M sheet, on the wings
of the Marinovskaya and Taberkol anticlines according to the results of
drilling wells 152, 158 and very insignificant outputs to the daytime
surface.

On the daytime surface, they form small manes with
eluvial ruins of red, pinkish-red coarse-grained sandstones.
During geological surveys on a scale of I:50,000, these
deposits were attributed to the Vladimirovskaya formation.
Indeed , surface mapping allows you to do this. Only after
the wells were drilled, and red-colored coarse-grained
sandstones with shell detritus and crinoid segments were
discovered in them between the deposits of the Tersakkan
formation and the Dalnenski­y horizon , fragments of brachiopod
shells of indeterminate systematic affiliation were also discovered on the surface in the eluvium

The rock pack uncovered by well 158 in the range of 137-150.2
m. and well 152 in the range of 240-258.8 m was allocated to an
independent stratigraphic unit, the volume of which, apparently ,
corresponds to the reduced thickness of the deposits of the Kip-
Shak and Kirei formations.

The thickness of this rock pack is 18.8m.

Above it, to a depth of 226 m, lies another pack of red-
colored rocks with a thickness of l4 M, which, in its structural
and textural features, occupies an intermediate position between
the deposits of the Kirei and Tersakkan formations. At a depth of
226 m lies the first layer of small-pebble polymictic conglomerates
with a thickness of 0.2 m, above it begins a pack of rocks, which
is already attributed to the Tersakkan formation.

The origin of these rocks is probably related to the fact that
the Atbasar uplift in the late Lower - early Middle Carboniferous
began to rise intensively, forming a chain of islands on the

in which, in intra-island depressions, relief depressions

periodically flooded with murky waters, which deposited shell

were formed. Such formations had been observed earlier in the

The age of this rock pack is determined by its stratigraphic
position between the deposits of the Dalnensky horizon, whose
age is defined as the Upper Visean, and the Tersakkan
formation of the Upper Carboniferous age.

The physical properties have been studied from single samples, they do
