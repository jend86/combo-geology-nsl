THE ORDOVICIAN SYSTEM

The deposits of Ordovician age are located along
the northwestern, northern, and eastern margins of the Teniz
basin. Structurally, they are associated with three Caledonian
megastructures: the Kalmakkolsky synclinery, the Zharkannagachsky
anticlinory and the Stepnyaksky synclinery.

To a first approximation, the Ordovician section of the studied
territory is characterized by a three-membered structure.
The base is dominated by a complex of siliceous-terrigenous deposits, in which
volcanic rocks play a certain role. The middle part of the section is almost
universally represented by layers of terrigenous, gravelite-
sand-eleurolite sediments of rhythmic structure. The section is
crowned with coarse-grained terrigenous and volcanogenic formations

The proximity to various structural and facies zones, the
usually poor exposure of Ordovician sediments, the separation
of outcrops, the weak and unequal saturation of organic
sediments - all this led to the creation of a number of local
patterns of Ordovician sediments, which differ both for the
above-mentioned mega-structures and, in some cases, for isolated
areas within these structures. In accordance with these local patterns
of dissection of Ordovician sediments within the studied territory, the
