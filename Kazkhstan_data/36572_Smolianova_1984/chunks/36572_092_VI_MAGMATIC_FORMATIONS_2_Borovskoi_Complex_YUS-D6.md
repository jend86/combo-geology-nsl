2. Borovskoi Complex (YUS-D,6)

The main and subsidiary granites of the Borovskoi complex are distributed over the territory of the second sheet on the scale of late Hercynian granites of normal crustal thickness.

PETROCHEMICAL FEATURES OF GRANITOID ROCKS OF THE BOROVOE MASSIF

In accordance with the authors' plan, 4 specialized analyses of granitoid rocks of the Borovoe massif were carried out during the study of rare-metal mineralization in the Sandykulsky and Obalykulsky massifs, and a number of analyses, some of which are from works of previous researchers, were also collected. A compilation of the results of these analyses allows us to make the following general conclusions about the chemical composition of the rare-metal borovoe massif distributed throughout the region (see p. 55) (in addition to the main Sandykulsky and Karakuduk massifs).

1. The content of SiO₂ in granitoid rocks ranges within 65-75%; this explains their prevalence among them as normal, as well as leucocratic varieties of granitoids (fig. 77)

2. The content of alkalies, the sum of which (Na₂O+K₂O) in the majority of samples ranges from 7.03 to 8.6%, indicates a fairly even distribution among normal and subalkaline varieties. At the same time, potash (Na₂O+K₂O) does not show any definite dependence on the SiO₂ content (fig. 77)

3. The ratio of alkalies indicates the predominance of K₂O over Na₂O in the rock complex (Na₂O/K₂O ranges within 0.7-1.0), whereby it is more pronounced for the granitoids of the Karakuduk massif (in the majority of cases Na₂O/K₂O = 0.7) (fig. 79)
