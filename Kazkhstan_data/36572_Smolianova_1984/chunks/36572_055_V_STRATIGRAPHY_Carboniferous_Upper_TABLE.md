For the Kensai anticline, characteristic saline deposits in an interstratified sequence are noted. Twelve horizons of salt-bearing strata are distinguished in total, with each containing increased copper and germanium content.

For structures in the area between Syrym-Sadbor and for individual folds distributed between the Obshcheye-Chasovoludinsk and Mineevsk oil-bearing facies, characteristic features include a clear upper section with two subsiding zones and development of the upper Cretaceous subsidence over two periods.

For Todbyk fold, a characteristic and very effective upper saline deposit of upper Cretaceous age is distinguished. In this region, productive carbonate arc-grosing cements occur; these have not been found in other structures. Upper Cretaceous saline subsidence is characterized in this region by broad development of argillaceous cements in sandstones.

**Conglomerates** are distributed in the section over a wide range and reach significant thicknesses according to the section. In light of this, conglomerates, or, as they were previously called, "Old Red Sandstone" and conglomerates of younger deposits, deserve mention.

Conglomerate deposits in the studied territory are found in the lower part of the Paleozoic section, in the Pre-Cambrian and Cambrian anticlines, in protrusions to the Minsk flexure. In the northern part of this area, the distribution of conglomerates is limited by Saryosynsk and Akmolinsky anticlines. Conglomerates occur on the Punkaryolysk uplift and Kugumskaya anticline. In the Nenkidsk basin, they lie approximately at 0.5° elevation.
