The delineation of Minshak light sources was studied by many researchers over a number of years (Karashin, 1957; Abdulmanov, 1965; Minorgin, 1968; Markov, 1970). In certain sections of the light source, delineated along the Mangal river, the following naturally light-colored (gray, bluish-gray, light-gray) microparticle breeds are encountered: predominantly limestone, occasionally glauconite, cretaceous and lignite-cretaceous clays, rarely encountered are scattered coal debris.
