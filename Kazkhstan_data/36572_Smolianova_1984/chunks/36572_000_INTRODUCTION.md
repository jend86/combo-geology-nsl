In 1974-76, the Minister of Military Geology of the Kazakh SSR
developed a program for a comprehensive study of the Teniz Basin.
The main purpose of this comprehensive study should be a general
assessment of the copper content of the Upper Paleozoic deposits of this
region in order to create a foundation for general and detailed copper
prospecting. The complex of works also included the geological survey of
nasataba 1:200.000 in the lower part of the Teniz depression.

The party's technical assignment for 1978-04 provided
for the compilation of a geological map on a scale of 1:200,000,
meeting modern requirements and concepts. For this, it was
necessary to carry out a detailed stratigraphic division of Upper
Paleozoic sediments and correlate sections in individual
structures with reference to reference sections; to study the material
composition of Upper Paleozoic rocks and the dependence of structures
on of the Upper Paleozoic cover from the base structure together;
to conduct new analyses of fossil organic remains to clarify the age
of geological formations; to study the position in the section of
copper-colored sandstones and analyze the patterns of their placement
and formation to develop forward-looking estimates.

The tasks were solved with the help of geological surveys of
prospecting and mapping drilling, testing, studying the core of
third-party organizations, a thorough study of the materials of the
precursors, and the decoding of aerophotomaterials. In addition to the
materials of the Zapadno-Tenizskaya PSP, the report uses materials
from reference geological sections, which were obtained when
