## Kaolin

**2** | N–42–XXXIV | Gordeyevskoye | N₂
69°47′ N.L.
52°28′ E.L.

In the vicinity of settlements Mat, Ortakshil, Yana Mol, Gordeyevka, and Verolubovka, occurrences of kaolins are known on the day surface; they are exposed by quarries where a clayey weathering crust is observed, developed on sandstones, aleurolites of the Ordovician, and shales of the Kochetav Formation (Yana Mol), as well as on Ordovician granites of the upper sequence. Clays are variegated, rarely yellowish with a pale reddish tint with relic structures of parent rocks. Visible thickness 2–3 m. Chemical composition of kaolin clays is as follows (in %): SiO₂ — 60.85; Al₂O₃ — 17.33; MgO — 1.79 and CaO — 4.98. Clays may be used as molding and refractory materials.

Studied by V.A. Babichev (1970). **Prospects unclear.**
