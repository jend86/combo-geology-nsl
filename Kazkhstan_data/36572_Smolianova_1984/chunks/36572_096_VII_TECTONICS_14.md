The Ivanovsky anticline is located between the Turgaykovskaya and Diodimirovskaya anticlines and is separated from the first by a first-order synclinal structure, and from the second by the Boltayskaya syncline. The anticline has a symmetrical structure with an axis striking in a north-south direction, and sometimes with a slightly reddish carbonate-terrigenous formational Plimsollian structure, and with the corresponding carbonate-terrigenous formation of the Paleogene structure. The eastern flank does not exceed 5-7°, and the central part of the anticline's wings reaches 2-3°.

In the physical fields above the Ivanovsky anticline, there is no significant anomaly of force and slight increase in intensity of the magnetic field. In the foundation of the anticline, Turonian-Coniacian intrusion is observed.

The Diodimirovskaya anticline contains a cascade-shaped arrangement of synchronously developed tectonic structures in its latest stage. It is already oriented in a north-south direction in the north-eastern direction. As it is oriented in the Bereznikov section, which passes along the north-eastern flank of the anticline, and in the Novgorodsk section, which manifested itself in the form of a flexure in the western flank of the anticline.

The anticline is not a homogeneous and self-contained structure. It was divided into two parts: the northern and southern. If the northern part of the anticline tilts down, as it were, in the north-western direction, then the southern part tilts up in the sublatitude direction, according to the Atbasar concept. The southern part of the anticline is limited in the north part to the Novgorodsk flexure, and in the south to the Atbasar uplift, where the dip angles beneath the Church pericline are quickly established.
