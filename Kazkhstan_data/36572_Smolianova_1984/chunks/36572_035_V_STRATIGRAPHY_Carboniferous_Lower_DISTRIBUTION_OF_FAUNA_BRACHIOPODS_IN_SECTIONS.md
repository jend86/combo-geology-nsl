The fossil fauna contradicts the age determinations of the rocks. From the packing of the rocks it is determined *Spirifer sp. (Sf. altenuatus Sow.)*, which is a relatively early angular form and occurs sporadically in the lower part of the Turneian stage (Budnikenko, 1971). The appearance of brachiopods collected in the upper part indicates that genera of fauna not earlier than the lower Turneian existed. Somewhat larger by size, the collections of brachiopods are characteristic of the upper Tournaisian.

In fact, this packing is an analog of the Cassian and partially Simorinian horizon of the lower comb facies. It should be noted that the fauna characteristic of the Simorinian horizon in this section is not encountered.
