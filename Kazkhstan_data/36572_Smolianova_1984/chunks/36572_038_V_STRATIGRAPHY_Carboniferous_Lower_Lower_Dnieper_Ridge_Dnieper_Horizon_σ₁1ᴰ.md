**Lower Dnieper Ridge. Dnieper Horizon (σ₁1ᴰ)**

The Dnieper horizon emerges on the surface within the limits of the Kaltakskoe depression and its southwestern slope and disappears under the Quaternary deposits in the central part of the Kopetkinskoe depression.

The erosional nature of the horizon outlet. At the place of its emergence on the surface, a lowering in the relief is usually observed in relation to the ridges with the underlying grids and often the outlet of the wedge of mergelts and limestones.

Deformation also follows a poor pattern (fig. 17).

The discontinuous separation of the Dnieper horizon sections on the surface is not completely explained. Therefore it is described fragmentarily: the lower part on the right bank, the upper part on the left bank of the Ishim River.

In the Kaltakskoe depression, in the section on the surface and in boreholes, the Dnieper horizon is subdivided into two stratigraphic suites: lower - carbonate and upper - carbonate-terrigenous.

The lower boundary of the horizon is drawn along the roof of printed mergelts, in the upper part of which there are changes of Pozdurevskii-Rannekvashnii complex of fauna.

This boundary is unambiguously determined throughout the area of the Kaltakskoe depression and its southwestern slope.

The location of the section is given on the surface (above-below, fig. 13,18)

**Argillites** – black, very fine, massive varieties with skeletal separateness. Admixture of pelitomorphic carbonate, as is customary, is present. Veining, occasionally, closes quite often organic remnants of good preservation.

**Carbonate rock types** are represented exclusively by biomorphic-detrital and biomorphic sediments. Among them are distinguished varieties analogous to those described in the section "Rugakovsky horizon" ("mergels", "merged limestones") and varieties closer to oolitic horizon, for which crystalline cement and no glauconite are characteristic, and sandy subordinate varieties (chalk-brachiopod-crinoid). As in sandstones, for individual layers of biomorphic-detrital limestone types, glauconite is characteristic.

In addition to the described limestone types, among black aleurolites are noted small (up to 1-2 tenths of a centimeter) occurrences of thin pelagic carbonate enclosing biogenic remnants, frequently very good preservation (colonial corals, colonial bryozoans, trilobites, etc.).
