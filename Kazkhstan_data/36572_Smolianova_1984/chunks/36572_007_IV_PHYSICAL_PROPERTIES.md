2. Methods of interpretation of geophysical data

The interpretation of the geophysical materials is based on ideas
about the geological structure of the area, the differentiation of rocks
of the folded complex in terms of density and magnetic susceptibility,
data on seismic reflecting boundaries, as well as information
about the relationship of physical fields with structural and tectonic
elements.

The geological interpretation of geophysical anomalies
was both qualitative and quantitative.

Quantitative calculations were carried out in order to study the
deep structure of the structural elements of the foundation (selection
of the section according to the Io curve), as well as to obtain an
idea of the depth of the drying objects (quantitative interpretation
of local gravimagnetic anomalies).

In the course of the camera work, the results of generalization
of seismic materials from the report of the Yuzhno-Tenizskaya
Party for 1980-1983, materials on the topic 306 KAZIMSA PRICE
for 1983, flat maps of scale 1:500000-1:500000 were used.

To study the deep structure of the work area , the materials
of specialized hephysical works performed by the Central
Party of the CGPE were used.

Structurally and tectonically, the area under study belongs
mainly to the Teniz depression; it is bounded from the south by the
Sarysu-Teniz uplift, from the north by the Kokchetav block, and
from the west by the Karkgach acticlinium.

The interpretation of geological and geophysical materials was carried
out in three successive stages:

I. Assessment of the area of work in the regional plan.
2. Qualitative interpretation of geophysical materials.
3. Quantitative interpretation of geophysical materials...

At the first stage, small-scale geological and geophysical maps were studied.
In the Teniz depression, the nature of gravitational and especially magnetic
fields is influenced by the material composition of the
foundation and therefore the extension of gravitational and magnetic anomalies
reflects the extension of the folded structures of the

foundation.

The northwestern extension of the anomalies
is typical for the northern part of the geological survey area

, the western one for the southern part, and the northeastern one for the central part.

The large depth of the Upper Paleozoic sediments and insufficient
geological information about the deep structure of the work area
make it difficult to interpret the geophysical fields.
As a result of the qualitative interpretation of the geophysical
anomalies, areas composed of rocks of a certain composition were
identified . The boundaries of the regions were determined by zones
of gravity gradients and lines of abrupt changes in the nature
of physical fields, with increased gradients of the gravitational
field marking steeply falling boundaries of heterogeneous
rock complexes, which coincide mainly with zones of deep
faults. Deep faults were distinguished mainly by anomalies of
the "gravity step" type, which are also expressed on gravity
maps as zones of elevated gradients. The deep faults in the
studied area are overlain by Late Paleozoic deposits and in the
structures of the Upper ethane, as faults, they are almost not fixed
, but manifest themselves as axes of folds, in the reversal of structures.
According to geophysical data, deep faults of the
northeastern, northwestern sublatitudinal and submeridional strike
are recorded in the Caledonian basement of the Teniz Depression.
Smaller faults were distinguished by the displacement of the axes of correlation
of anomalies, by the change in the nature of the field.
An analysis of the physical properties of the rocks of the
area suggests that large gravity maxima in the studied area
reflect block uplifts of the Caledonian basin basement. The
maximum values of gravity are caused by the deposits of the
