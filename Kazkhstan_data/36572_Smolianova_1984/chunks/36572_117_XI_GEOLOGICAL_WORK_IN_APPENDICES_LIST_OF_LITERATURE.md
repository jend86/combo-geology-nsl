CONCLUSION

In conclusion, the following main points should be emphasized:

1. The first stage was completed practically with the full volume of planned work. The shortfall in the volume of search-and-prospecting drilling was compensated by the study of cores from the side walls of boreholes. A large amount of factual material was collected, which made it possible, in a number of cases, to clarify the geological structure of the area. Chamber processing of this material was carried out, from which conclusions were drawn on the geological structure of the surveyed territory and notes were made on further research as a subsequent stage, both in the whole region.

2. The stated goal of the first stage has been achieved.

3. Detailed calculations were made on the aforementioned geological maps of upper paleozoic and lower mesozoic strata, corrections were introduced in the structure and subsidence of upper proterozoic and lower paleozoic formations.

4. Strata under consideration are attributed to the later proteroic, riphean and nikolsko-burulsk series. Some data have been brought together concerning their greater age.

5. Among the rocks of the imsky genesis, identified and described on strike and in structural organic sediments, previously unknown comple[x] of fauna was isolated from individual seams, which made it possible to predict the location of the kalmaksky delta two stages ago.

Based on an analysis of the distribution of lead mineralization on the studied territory, the main patterns of its distribution in the subformation section of the Cretaceous period have been identified. The most significant of them are: the irregularity of lead mineralization in relation to stratigraphic levels; localization of ore deposits in the foundation and lower parts of red-colored sedimentary formations; the concentration of major ore bodies on larger fault transitions; the irregularity of all major ore bodies in a long-acting regional zone of ruptures, which on one side controls the position of faults, and on the other creates areas of potential mineralization; the favorable distribution and concentration of ores; the irregularity of ore deposits in peripheral anticlines, which are often associated with subsurface facilities; the role of geological control of ore bodies, expressed in the irregularity of ore deposits in the heart of sand rocks, where their correlation with red-colored problems becomes closer; in some cases worse than Pb-Zn; the irregularity of major ore bodies in the participant of the subformation section, where the placement of weakly mineralized and permeable heart zones with enclosed ore-bearing mineral relationships also applies to the single.
