3). Section along borehole 4–439 (Cheredov N.G., 1981) of deposits of the second floodplain terrace of the Ishim River on sheet M–42–41:

Loam, grayish-brownish; from a depth of 0.4 m acquires a brown tint and gradually transitions into light reddish-brown loams, sometimes with traces of horizontal lamination, with admixtures of sandy-gravelly material. The loams are strongly carbonatized, contain lenses and nests of finely crystalline gypsum — 7.0 m.

Clay, gray-yellow, strongly sandy, heavy, dense, calcareous, strongly ferruginized with nests (up to 1 cm) of limonite — 2.5 m.

Clay, light-gray, viscous, semi-lithified, strongly calcareous, with coatings and stains of black manganese oxides, with rare remains of flora and shell fragments of mollusks — 5.3 m.

Sandy clay, light-gray, viscous, semi-lithified, calcareous, with coatings and stains of manganese oxides, contains (up to 10%) variably-grained sandy material predominantly of quartz composition. Within the rock — spots of ferruginization. In the lower part of the layer, coarse-gravelly and fine-pebbly material: quartz, flint, limonite — 0.7 m.

Gravel sand, coarse-grained, poorly sorted, quartzose — 0.8 m.

Gravel sand of bright-yellow color, poorly sorted, predominantly quartzose. Cementing mass — clay, thin, non-carbonated. In the lower part of the layer, large pebbles of strongly weathered sandstones of yellow-gray color appear — 3.2 m.

The age of the alluvial deposits is determined by spore-pollen assemblages, mollusks, and fossil bones.

According to the conclusions of R.A. Tereshchenko, the spore-pollen assemblages from borehole D-48, outcrop D-12, a sand quarry on the left bank of the Ishim River near the bridge on the Atbasar–Ladyzhinka road, and in the cliffs of the eastern outskirts of the village of Magdalinovka (Table [illegible]) reflect conditions of a moist, cold climate of the dark taiga type (D–12a), or forest-steppe in the same moist cold climate, or steppe in a hot dry climate (4,5). All spectra, in her opinion, indicate a Middle Pleistocene age of the deposits of the period of the Samarovian glaciation in the north of Western Siberia.

In addition, near the village of Magdalinovka, teeth of large mammals were found by V.V. Dmitrovsky in 1976. According to the determination of V.S. Kozhamkulova, the teeth belonged to *Equus caballus fossilis*. The age of the enclosing clays is, in her opinion, Middle Pleistocene.

In the sand quarry near the bridge over the Ishim River on the Atbasar–Ladyzhinka road, mollusks were collected in the same year: *Valvata cristata* Müll., *Pupilla muscorum* (L.), *Pisidium* sp., *Radix peregra* Müll., *Planorbis planorbis* L., *Spiralina vortex* L., *Gyranlus laevis* Ald.

According to the conclusion of K.A. Yadzhina, the listed species are most characteristic of Middle–Upper Quaternary deposits.
