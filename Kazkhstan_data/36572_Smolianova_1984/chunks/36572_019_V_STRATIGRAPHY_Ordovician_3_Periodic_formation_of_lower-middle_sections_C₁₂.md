Paragraph 03.04.

ORDOVICIAN SYSTEM

The Ordovician sediments of the Ordovician age distributed in the north-western, northern and eastern framing of the Torgay depression. In structural plan they are confined to three megastructures: Kalmykkolskoe anticlinorium, Daralinskoe anticlinorium and Styoniskoe anticlinorium.

In the first approximation, for the development of Ordovician sediments characterized by layered structure. The basis is composed of predominantly clastic-terrigenous sediments, in which the determining role is played by turbidite breeds. The middle part of the section almost everywhere consists of members of terrigenous, gravel-psammitic-silty-mudstone sediments of rhythmic structure. Fissures are developed in coarse-fragmentary terrigenous and sand-mud formations.

The similarity and differences of structural-facies composition, general similarity of the boundaries of Ordovician sediments, distribution of fossil, weak and secondary mineralization of the rock organism of the section - all this made it necessary to create a number of local schemes of distribution of Ordovician sediments of Ordovician, differing as for characteristic features of their structure and in individual cases, for working out parts inside these structures, and in accordance with the data of regional framework of Ordovician sediments distribution in the territory of the scientific work, the following divisions are distinguished:

1. Sediments of the lower section. Kumskaya suite - O₁ km

2. Sediments of the lower-middle sections. Tasoblinskaya (O₁₋₂ ts ) and Gulskinskaya (O₁₋₂ gl ) suites.
