**Middle–Upper Division.**

Red-colored terrigenous deposits of the undifferentiated middle–upper division of the Carboniferous system are identified on the studied territory in the Zholboldin syncline. In the adjacent Oksanov syncline, based on the results of drilling borehole "A," both the Kireyskaya and Tersakkan suites have been identified. The lower boundary of the latter in borehole "A" is drawn at the base of a thick bed of deformation conglomerates at a depth of 365 m (Fig. 49, sheet 6). The cross-section of the upper Paleozoic deposits through the Zholboldin syncline, compiled from the results of drilling boreholes 391, 392 and from outcrops in the channel of the Zholbolda River, does not contain such a thick bed of conglomerates.

Outcrop exposure in the area of the Zholboldin syncline is so poor that it provides no information about the structure of the Tersakkan sequence. It was assumed that in the Zholboldin syncline it rests on the Lower Paleozoic basement. In borehole 392, beneath deposits of middle–upper Carboniferous age, variegated–red-colored deposits of the undifferentiated Lower Carboniferous with a rare fauna of atyrids and a chert horizon in the middle part are encountered.

The cross-section compiled for the Zholboldin syncline is correlated with the section of the Oksanov syncline, but the bed of polymictic conglomerates by which the boundary between the Kireyskaya and Tersakkan suites could be drawn is absent there. Therefore, we identify in the Zholboldin syncline a sequence of undifferentiated middle–upper Carboniferous.

Throughout the entire formation, intraformational conglomerates are characteristic, indicating, along with the carbonaceous and ferruginous nature of the sediments, continental conditions of formation of the rocks of the Tersakkansk formation and numerous reworking episodes of the rocks, especially on the flanks of consedimentation anticlines.

8. The formation is extremely facially variable; lacustrine, fluvial, proluvial–alluvial deposits are observed. Along with the variability of the formation's composition, there also occurs significant variation in thicknesses in various structures and their individual parts. As a rule, thicknesses decrease toward the crestal parts of anticlines and increase in the cores of synclinal structures.

9. The formation has a lenticular character, which manifests at two orders: regional and local. Various textures are characteristic of the formation, indicating varying dynamics of the environment and its variability in time and space.

10. The petrographic composition of the clastic fraction is confidently correlated with the rocks of the framing of the depression.

Based on magnetic susceptibility, the main body of the Tersakkansk formation deposits is practically non-magnetic, or weakly magnetic.

The magnetic susceptibility of individual samples reaches 800–1000 × 10⁻⁶ SI units; the average magnetic susceptibility does not exceed 100–200 × 10⁻⁶ SI units. The density of rocks of the Tersakkansk formation

varies from 2.58 g/cm³ for conglomerates and sandstones to 2.63 g/cm³ for siltstones; the weighted average density is 2.61 g/cm³.

Thus, in gravity fields the rocks of the described formation are not mappable. However, on detailed gravity surveys, slight gravity lows will apparently be observed over the thick deposits of the Tersakan Formation.

According to seismic data, the thickness of the Tersakan Formation in the study area does not exceed the first hundreds of meters. The greatest thicknesses (up to 2000 meters) of the Tersakan Formation are observed in the center and northeast of sheet M-42-XV and on sheet N-42-XXXII (up to 500–700 m).

The age of the Tersakan Formation is determined by its stratigraphic position, as well as by finds of flora, spore-pollen assemblages, and microfauna.

The Tersakan Formation overlies the deposits of the Kirei Formation, whose age has been determined by flora as late Bashkirian – Moscovian. The Tersakan Formation is overlain by deposits of the Nairak Formation, whose age, based on finds of fish, microfauna, flora, and spore-pollen assemblages, is defined as early Permian.

In reference-parametric borehole 01, from the lower intervals of the Tersakan Formation, spores and pollen were identified by A.M. Lopatina from samples collected at depths of 785, 779, 770.5, and 769 m. The palynological material is very sparse; only in samples from depths of 779 and 750.5 m was it possible to count 50 and 100 forms respectively.

In preparations with single spores and pollen, form-genera characteristic of Carboniferous deposits predominate: *Punctatisporites*, *Calamospora*, *Lycospora*, *Vallatisporites*, *Remijsporites*, *Ichopfipollenites*. Pollen of conifers was encountered singly. In samples where it was possible to count 50–100 forms, the species composition of the spectra is fairly diverse. In quantitative terms, spores constitute from 22% to 33%, while pollen accounts for a considerably high percentage. Spores are represented by the same form-genera as noted above. Among pollen, pollen of ancient conifers has been identified: *Florinites grandis* (Lub.) Dibn., *Platysaccus podocarpiformis* Lub., *Limitisporites* sp. They constitute 18–44% and are distributed in middle–upper Carboniferous deposits. The samples contain form-genera also characteristic of Permian depo-

Thus, based on stratigraphic position, the age of the Tersakkanian Formation is upper Carboniferous. The floristic remains, microfauna, and spore-pollen assemblages do not allow refinement of the formation's age. Therefore, we accept the age of the formation as upper Carboniferous, since in the lower third of the Kayraktinian Formation section up to 30% of upper Carboniferous species of microfauna are still present, and the palynocomplex of the Kayraktinian Formation gives an upper Carboniferous – Permian age for its deposits practically up to the middle of the formation. In this case, we allow that the very top of the Tersakkanian Formation — the upper member of the upper Tersakkanian submember — may also have a lower Permian age.

apparently, with the deposits of the Dzhezkazgan suite of the Dzhezkazgan depression. It is possible that the upper horizons of the Tersakkansk suite correlate with the very lowest levels of the Zhidelesian suite.

The palynocomplexes of the Tersakkan suite do not allow its correlation with the Tentek suite. They reveal certain similarities with the palynocomplexes of the Shekhan suite. However, at present there is insufficient material for a confident correlation of the Tersakkan suite with the Shekhan suite. They reveal only features of lithological similarity. The question of the correlation of the Tersakkan suite with the suites of the Karaganda basin requires further study.
