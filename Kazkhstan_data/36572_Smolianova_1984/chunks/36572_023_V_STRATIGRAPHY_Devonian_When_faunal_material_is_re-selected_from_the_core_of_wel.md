The formations of the Sulciferian horizon are composed of a complex
of terrigenous and carbonate rocks: sandstones, acheurolites,

limestones and dolomites.
Sandstones and vleuroliths are usually confined to the lower
parts of the described thickness. The color of the rocks ranges from mottled in
the uplifted parts of the horizon to gray in various shades. The
textures are mostly massive, less often spotted. The sandstones are
fine- grained and medium-grained. The sorting of the clastic grains is average
to poor; the roundness is usually weak. The composition of clastic
grains changes naturally in the direction north from the southern
side of the Kipshak trough. Thus, the Kipshak River section is
characterized by polymictic differences in composition: quartz 30%-40%,
plagioclases and kalishpates 30%-40%, lithoid fragments 20%-30%.
In the section uncovered by boreholes 02 and 06, the sulciferous
limestone is already significantly quartz, feldspars account for
about 15% here, and rock fragments form an inconspicuous
admixture. The cement of rocks is mainly porous,
film-porous by the nature of cementation. Clay in composition
(hydro-micaceous, hydro-micaceous-chlorite); regenerative

cementation of indentation and dissolution and determines the
"draining" appearance of the soil. Calcite cement is typical for
siltstone deposits. Calcite accounts for 30%-40% of the rock volume
and the sites have a kilitic appearance. Then he will mix the clastic
grains of the feldspar. An increase in the carbonate content leads
to the formation of rocks of mixed composition, transitional to pure

The carbonate rocks of the Sulciferian horizon are divided
into two large groups: limestones and dolomites, between which
a wide variety of transitional differences are possible.
Both sexes are most widespread within the
southern side of the Kipshak trough. In the northern direction
(wells 02, 06), their interlayers practically disappear, replaced
by terrigenous and dolomitic facies. According to our data, two groups of
limestones can be distinguished in the section of the southern wing of the
Kipshak syncline (according to the classification of G.I. Teodorovich
(1950): normally granular limestones and normally granular-
thick limestones with biomorphic detritus remains.
Normally granular limestones are mainly represented by
pelitomorphic differences (the size of carbonate grains is less than
0.005 mm). The structure is uniformly grained. Their distinctive
