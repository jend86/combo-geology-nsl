**CHAPTER VIII. MINERAL RESOURCES**

Various mineral resources are represented within the studied territory. The primary role among them is played by copper, associated with red-bed deposits of the Epicaledonian sub-platform cover. In connection with the fact that the main objective of the follow-up study was to assess the copper-bearing potential of this cover, questions relating to the metallogeny of the framing of the Teniz depression are not addressed in this report. The primary focus of the follow-up study was directed toward evaluating the copper-bearing potential of the depression.

In addition to copper, occurrences of polymetals and silver have been identified within the extent of Upper Paleozoic deposits. Among non-metallic mineral resources in the Upper Paleozoic deposits, occurrences of strontium, rodusite, and building materials have been identified. Among solid combustible minerals, occurrences of coal are known within the area.

Materials on mineral resources are presented on mineral resource maps (drawings No. 24–32).

On these maps, copper occurrences, depending on the intensity and scale of mineralization, are divided into ore occurrences and copper occurrence indicators.

Ore occurrences on the mineral resource maps are numbered and their brief characteristics are given in the mineral resource catalogues (table ). Copper occurrence indicators identified as a result of bulk sampling of the surface were not numbered on the mineral resource maps. On the mineral resource maps, in the category of copper ore occurrences, boreholes are also shown that penetrated "blind" ore horizons with copper content of 0.3 wt. % and above. Ore-bearing beds intersected by boreholes with copper content below 0.3 wt. % are classified as copper occurrence indicators.
